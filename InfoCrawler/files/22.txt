<p><b>Karen Spärck Jones</b> <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_British_Academy" title="Fellow of the British Academy">FBA</a> (26 August 1935 – 4 April 2007) was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> computer scientist.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karen_Sp%C3%A4rck_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Karen Ida Boalth Spärck Jones was born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. Her father was Owen Jones, a lecturer in chemistry, and her mother was Ida Spärck, a <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> who moved to Britain during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. They left Norway on one of the last boats out after the German invasion in 1940.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1">[1]</a></sup> Spärck Jones was educated at a grammar school in Huddersfield and then <a href="/wiki/Girton_College,_Cambridge" title="Girton College, Cambridge">Girton College, Cambridge</a> from 1953 to 1956, reading History, with an additional final year in Moral Sciences (philosophy). She briefly became a school teacher, before moving into Computer Science. During her career in Computer Science, she campaigned hard for more women to enter computing.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1">[1]</a></sup> She was married to fellow Cambridge computer scientist <a href="/wiki/Roger_Needham" title="Roger Needham">Roger Needham</a> until his death in 2003. She died 4 April 2007 at <a href="/wiki/Willingham,_Cambridgeshire" title="Willingham, Cambridgeshire">Willingham</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cambridgeshire" title="Cambridgeshire">Cambridgeshire</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karen_Sp%C3%A4rck_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She worked at the Cambridge Language Research Unit from the late 1950s,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> then at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">Cambridge's</a> <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_University_Computer_Laboratory" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambridge University Computer Laboratory">Computer Laboratory</a> from 1974, and retired in 2002, holding the post of Professor of Computers and Information, which she was awarded in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-1">[1]</a></sup> She continued to work in the Computer Laboratory until shortly before her death. Her main research interests, since the late 1950s, were <a href="/wiki/Natural_language_processing" title="Natural language processing">natural language processing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Information_retrieval" title="Information retrieval">information retrieval</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-doi10.1108.2Feb026526_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-doi10.1108.2Feb026526-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> One of her most important contributions was the concept of <a href="/wiki/Inverse_document_frequency" class="mw-redirect" title="Inverse document frequency">inverse document frequency</a> (IDF) weighting in information retrieval, which she introduced in a 1972 paper.<sup id="cite_ref-doi10.1108.2Feb026526_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-doi10.1108.2Feb026526-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-idf_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-idf-8">[8]</a></sup> IDF is used in most search engines today, usually as part of the <a href="/wiki/Tf-idf" class="mw-redirect" title="Tf-idf">tf-idf</a> weighting scheme.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>There is an annual <a href="/wiki/British_Computer_Society" title="British Computer Society">BCS</a> lecture named in her honour.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2017, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Huddersfield" title="University of Huddersfield">University of Huddersfield</a> renamed one of its campus buildings in her honour. Formerly known as Canalside West, the Spärck Jones building houses the University's School of Computing and Engineering.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span id="Karen_Spärck_Jones_Award"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Karen_Sp.C3.A4rck_Jones_Award">Karen Spärck Jones Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karen_Sp%C3%A4rck_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Karen Spärck Jones Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>To commemorate her achievements, the Karen Spärck Jones Award was created in 2008 by the <a href="/wiki/British_Computer_Society" title="British Computer Society">BCS</a> and its Information Retrieval Specialist Group (BCS IRSG), which is sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Research" title="Microsoft Research">Microsoft Research</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The recipients are:</p>
<ul>
<li>2016, <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Teevan" title="Jaime Teevan">Jaime Teevan</a></li>
<li>2015, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jordan_Boyd-Graber&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jordan Boyd-Graber (page does not exist)">Jordan Boyd-Graber</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Emine_Yilmaz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Emine Yilmaz (page does not exist)">Emine Yilmaz</a></li>
<li>2014, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryen_White&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ryen White (page does not exist)">Ryen White</a></li>
<li>2013, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eugene_Agichtein&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Eugene Agichtein (page does not exist)">Eugene Agichtein</a></li>
<li>2012, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Diane_Kelly(computer_scientist)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Diane Kelly(computer scientist) (page does not exist)">Diane Kelly(computer scientist)</a></li>
<li>2011, No award was made</li>
<li>2010, <a href="/wiki/Evgeniy_Gabrilovich" title="Evgeniy Gabrilovich">Evgeniy Gabrilovich</a></li>
<li>2009, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirella_Lapata&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mirella Lapata (page does not exist)">Mirella Lapata</a></li>
</ul>
