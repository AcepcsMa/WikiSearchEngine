<p><b>Authority control</b> is a way of associating a unique identifier to articles on Wikipedia. This is useful to disambiguate different items with similar or identical headings, as well as to establish a single standard title for an item that is commonly known by two or more titles. When used, authority control data links can be found near the bottom of Wikipedia pages, linking to bibliographical records on worldwide <a href="/wiki/Library_catalog" title="Library catalog">Library catalogs</a>. Authority control is often used on biographical articles because it is quite common for more than one person to share the same name. It is commonly used in other subject material, as well.</p>
<p>Authority control enables researchers to search more easily for pertinent information on the subject of an article, without needing to disambiguate the subject manually. For example, authority control is used on music articles so that the information in the article can be easily cross-referenced with the popular <a href="/wiki/MusicBrainz" title="MusicBrainz">MusicBrainz</a> database. This enables many media applications to display information from the relevant Wikipedia article when a song is played.</p>
<p>More generally, <a href="/wiki/Authority_control" title="Authority control">authority control</a> is a method of creating and maintaining index terms for bibliographical material in a library catalogue. The links produced by the <a href="/wiki/Template:Authority_control" title="Template:Authority control">authority control template</a> on Wikipedia go to authority control data in worldwide library catalogues. As an example, the Wikipedia authority control information for <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Graham_Bell" title="Alexander Graham Bell">Alexander Graham Bell</a> looks like this:</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/WorldCat" title="WorldCat">WorldCat</a> is a global cooperative <a href="/wiki/Union_catalog" title="Union catalog">union catalog</a> which itemizes the collections of 72,000 libraries in 170 countries and territories. What follows are four authority control records: <a href="/wiki/Virtual_International_Authority_File" title="Virtual International Authority File">Virtual International Authority File</a> (VIAF), the second is the <a href="/wiki/Library_of_Congress_Control_Number" title="Library of Congress Control Number">Library of Congress Control Number</a> (LCCN), then the <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Name_Identifier" title="International Standard Name Identifier">International Standard Name Identifier</a> (ISNI), and last the GND is the <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Authority_File" title="Integrated Authority File">Integrated Authority File</a> (in German).</p>

<p>Supported authority files on the English Wikipedia include, among others:</p>
<ol>
<li><a href="/wiki/Integrated_Authority_File" title="Integrated Authority File">Integrated Authority File</a> (<i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Gemeinsame Normdatei</span></i> or GND)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Library_of_Congress_Control_Number" title="Library of Congress Control Number">Library of Congress Control Number</a> (LCCN)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Virtual_International_Authority_File" title="Virtual International Authority File">Virtual International Authority File</a> (VIAF)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/LIBRIS" title="LIBRIS">LIBRIS</a> by the <a href="/wiki/National_Library_of_Sweden" title="National Library of Sweden">National Library of Sweden</a> (SELIBR)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/ORCID" title="ORCID">Open Researcher and Contributor ID</a> (ORCID)</li>
<li>Outdated: <a href="/wiki/Name_Authority_File" title="Name Authority File">Name Authority File</a> (<i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Personennamendatei</span></i> or PND), now part of GND</li>
<li>Outdated: <a href="/wiki/Corporate_Bodies_Authority_File" title="Corporate Bodies Authority File">Corporate Bodies Authority File</a> (<i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Gemeinsame Körperschaftsdatei</span></i> or GKD), now part of GND</li>
<li>Outdated: <a href="/wiki/Subject_Headings_Authority_File" title="Subject Headings Authority File">Subject Headings Authority File</a> (<i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Schlagwortnormdatei</span></i> or SWD), now part of GND</li>
</ol>
