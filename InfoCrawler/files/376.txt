<p><b>Sigismund of Luxemburg</b> (15 February 1368 – 9 December 1437) was <a href="/wiki/Prince-elector" title="Prince-elector">Prince-elector</a> of <a href="/wiki/Margraviate_of_Brandenburg" title="Margraviate of Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a> from 1378 until 1388 and from 1411 until 1415, <a href="/wiki/King_of_Hungary" title="King of Hungary">King of Hungary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Croatia_(medieval)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingdom of Croatia (medieval)">Croatia</a> from 1387, <a href="/wiki/King_of_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="King of Germany">King of Germany</a> from 1411, <a href="/wiki/King_of_Bohemia" class="mw-redirect" title="King of Bohemia">King of Bohemia</a> from 1419, <a href="/wiki/King_of_Italy" title="King of Italy">King of Italy</a> from 1431, and <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a> for four years from 1433 until 1437, the last male member of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Luxemburg" class="mw-redirect" title="House of Luxemburg">House of Luxemburg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Sigismund von Luxembourg was the leader of the last West European Crusade - the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Nicopolis" title="Battle of Nicopolis">Crusade of Nicopolis</a> of <a href="/wiki/1396" title="1396">1396</a> to liberate <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> and save <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Turks</a>. Afterwards he founded the <a href="/wiki/Dragon_Order" class="mw-redirect" title="Dragon Order">Dragon Order</a> to fight the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_people" title="Turkish people">Turks</a>. He was regarded as highly educated, spoke several languages (among them; French, German, Czech, Hungarian, Italian, and Latin) and was an outgoing person who also took pleasure in the <a href="/wiki/Tournament_(medieval)" title="Tournament (medieval)">tournament</a>. Sigismund was one of the driving forces behind the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Constance" title="Council of Constance">Council of Constance</a> that ended the <a href="/wiki/Western_Schism" title="Western Schism">Papal Schism</a>, but which in the end also led to the <a href="/wiki/Hussite_Wars" title="Hussite Wars">Hussite Wars</a> that dominated the later period of Sigismund's life.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, Sigismund was the son of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_IV,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles IV, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles IV</a>, and of his fourth wife, <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_of_Pomerania" title="Elizabeth of Pomerania">Elizabeth of Pomerania</a>, the granddaughter of <a href="/wiki/King_of_Poland" class="mw-redirect" title="King of Poland">King</a> <a href="/wiki/Casimir_III_of_Poland" class="mw-redirect" title="Casimir III of Poland">Casimir III of Poland</a>, and the great-granddaughter of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_rulers_of_Lithuania" title="List of rulers of Lithuania">Grand Duke of Lithuania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gediminas" title="Gediminas">Gediminas</a>. He was named after <a href="/wiki/Saint_Sigismund_of_Burgundy" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Sigismund of Burgundy">Saint Sigismund of Burgundy</a>. From Sigismund's childhood he was nicknamed the "ginger fox" (<i>liška ryšavá</i>) in the <a href="/wiki/Lands_of_the_Bohemian_Crown" title="Lands of the Bohemian Crown">Crown of Bohemia</a>, on account of his hair colour.</p>

<p>King <a href="/wiki/Louis_the_Great" class="mw-redirect" title="Louis the Great">Louis the Great of Hungary and Poland</a> always had a good and close relationship with Emperor Charles IV, and Sigismund was <a href="/wiki/Betrothal" class="mw-redirect" title="Betrothal">betrothed</a> to Louis' eldest daughter, <a href="/wiki/Mary,_Queen_of_Hungary" title="Mary, Queen of Hungary">Mary</a>, in 1374, when he was six years old. Upon his father's death in 1378, young Sigismund became <a href="/wiki/Margrave_of_Brandenburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Margrave of Brandenburg">Margrave of Brandenburg</a> and was sent to the Hungarian court, where he soon learnt the Hungarian language and way of life, and became entirely devoted to his adopted country. King Louis named him as his heir and appointed him his successor as <a href="/wiki/King_of_Hungary" title="King of Hungary">King of Hungary</a>.</p>
<p>In 1381, the then 13-year-old Sigismund was sent to <a href="/wiki/Krak%C3%B3w" title="Kraków">Kraków</a> by his eldest half-brother and guardian <a href="/wiki/Wenceslaus,_King_of_the_Romans" class="mw-redirect" title="Wenceslaus, King of the Romans">Wenceslaus, King of Germany and Bohemia</a>, to learn <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a> and to become acquainted with the land and its people. King Wenceslaus also gave him <a href="/wiki/Neumark" title="Neumark">Neumark</a> to facilitate communication between Brandenburg and Poland.</p>
<p>The disagreement between Polish landlords of <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Poland" title="Lesser Poland">Lesser Poland</a> on one side and landlords of <a href="/wiki/Greater_Poland" title="Greater Poland">Greater Poland</a> on the other, regarding the choice of the future King of Poland, finally ended in choosing the Lithuanian side; the support of the lords of Greater Poland was not enough to give Prince Sigismund the Polish crown. Instead, the landlords of Lesser Poland gave it to Mary's younger sister <a href="/wiki/Jadwiga_I_of_Poland" class="mw-redirect" title="Jadwiga I of Poland">Jadwiga I of Poland</a>, who married <a href="/wiki/Jogaila" class="mw-redirect" title="Jogaila">Jogaila</a> of <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Lithuania" title="Grand Duchy of Lithuania">Lithuania</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="King_of_Hungary">King of Hungary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: King of Hungary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:765572.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/765572.jpg/220px-765572.jpg" width="220" height="109" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/765572.jpg/330px-765572.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/765572.jpg/440px-765572.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="248" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

Gold coin of Sigismund of Hungary with his coat of arms (right), and the image of the King Saint <a href="/wiki/Ladislaus_I_of_Hungary" title="Ladislaus I of Hungary">Ladislaus I of Hungary</a> (left).</div>

</div>
<p>On the death of her father in 1382, his betrothed, Mary, became queen of Hungary and Sigismund married her in 1385 in Zólyom (today <a href="/wiki/Zvolen" title="Zvolen">Zvolen</a>). The next year, he was accepted as Mary's future co-ruler by the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Gy%C5%91r_(1386)" title="Treaty of Győr (1386)">Treaty of Győr</a>. However, Mary was captured, together with her mother, <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_of_Bosnia" title="Elizabeth of Bosnia">Elizabeth of Bosnia</a>, who had acted as regent, in 1387 by the rebellious <a href="/w/index.php?title=House_of_Horvat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="House of Horvat (page does not exist)">House of Horvat</a>, Bishop <a href="/wiki/Paul_Horvat" title="Paul Horvat">Paul Horvat</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ma%C4%8Dva" title="Mačva">Mačva</a>, his brother <a href="/wiki/John_Horvat" title="John Horvat">John Horvat</a> and younger brother Ladislav. Sigismund's mother-in-law was strangled, while Mary was liberated.</p>
<p>Having secured the support of the nobility, Sigismund was crowned <a href="/wiki/King_of_Hungary" title="King of Hungary">King of Hungary</a> at <a href="/wiki/Sz%C3%A9kesfeh%C3%A9rv%C3%A1r" title="Székesfehérvár">Székesfehérvár</a> on 31 March 1387.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Having raised money by pledging <a href="/wiki/Margraviate_of_Brandenburg" title="Margraviate of Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a> to his cousin <a href="/wiki/Jobst_of_Moravia" title="Jobst of Moravia">Jobst, margrave of Moravia</a> (1388), he was engaged for the next nine years in a ceaseless struggle for the possession of this unstable throne. The central power was finally weakened to such an extent that only Sigismund's alliance with the powerful Czillei-Garai League could ensure his position on the throne.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It was not for entirely selfless reasons that one of the leagues of barons helped him to power: Sigismund had to pay for the support of the lords by transferring a sizable part of the royal properties. (For some years, the baron's council governed the country in the name of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Crown" class="mw-redirect" title="Holy Crown">Holy Crown</a>). The restoration of the authority of the central administration took decades of work. The bulk of the nation headed by the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Garai" class="mw-redirect" title="House of Garai">House of Garai</a> was with him; but in the southern provinces between the <a href="/wiki/Sava" title="Sava">Sava</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Drava" title="Drava">Drava</a>, the Horvathys with the support of King <a href="/wiki/Tvrtko_I_of_Bosnia" title="Tvrtko I of Bosnia">Tvrtko I of Bosnia</a>, Mary's maternal uncle, proclaimed as their king <a href="/wiki/Ladislaus,_king_of_Naples" class="mw-redirect" title="Ladislaus, king of Naples">Ladislaus, king of Naples</a>, son of the murdered <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_Hungary" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles II of Hungary">Charles II of Hungary</a>. Not until 1395 did <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_II_Garay" class="mw-redirect" title="Nicholas II Garay">Nicholas II Garay</a> succeed in suppressing them. Mary died heavily pregnant in 1395.</p>
<p>To ease the pressure from Hungarian nobles, Sigismud tried to employ foreign advisors, which was not popular, and he had to promise not to give land and nominations to other than Hungarian nobles. However, this was not applied to <a href="/wiki/Stibor_of_Stiboricz" title="Stibor of Stiboricz">Stibor of Stiboricz</a>, who was Sigismund's closest friend and advisor. On a number of occasions, Sigismund was imprisoned by nobles, but with help of the armies of Garai and Stibor of Stiboricz, he would regain power.</p>

<p>In 1396 Sigismund led the combined armies of Christendom against the Turks, who had taken advantage of the temporary helplessness of Hungary to extend their dominion to the banks of the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a>. This crusade, preached by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Boniface_IX" title="Pope Boniface IX">Pope Boniface IX</a>, was very popular in Hungary. The nobles flocked in the thousands to the royal standard, and were reinforced by volunteers from nearly every part of Europe, the most important contingent being that of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_France" title="Kingdom of France">French</a> led by <a href="/wiki/John_the_Fearless" title="John the Fearless">John the Fearless</a>, son of <a href="/wiki/Philip_II,_Duke_of_Burgundy" class="mw-redirect" title="Philip II, Duke of Burgundy">Philip II, Duke of Burgundy</a>. Sigismund set out with 90,000 men and a flotilla of 70 galleys. After capturing <a href="/wiki/Vidin" title="Vidin">Vidin</a>, he camped with his Hungarian armies before the fortress of <a href="/wiki/Nicopolis,_Bulgaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Nicopolis, Bulgaria">Nicopolis</a>. Sultan <a href="/wiki/Bayezid_I" title="Bayezid I">Bayezid I</a> raised the siege of Constantinople and, at the head of 140,000 men, completely defeated the <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a> forces in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Nicopolis" title="Battle of Nicopolis">Battle of Nicopolis</a> fought between the 25 and 28 September 1396. Sigismund returned by sea and through the realm of <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Zeta" title="Principality of Zeta">Zeta</a>, where he ordained a local <a href="/wiki/Montenegrins_(ethnic_group)" class="mw-redirect" title="Montenegrins (ethnic group)">Montenegrin</a> lord <a href="/wiki/%C4%90ura%C4%91_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Đurađ II">Đurađ II</a> with the islands of <a href="/wiki/Hvar" title="Hvar">Hvar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kor%C4%8Dula" title="Korčula">Korčula</a> for resistance against the Turks; the islands were returned to Sigismund after Durad's death in April 1403.</p>
<p>The disaster in Nicopolis angered several Hungarian lords, leading to instability in the kingdom. Deprived of his authority in Hungary, Sigismund then turned his attention to securing the succession in <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Germany" title="Kingdom of Germany">Germany</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bohemia" title="Kingdom of Bohemia">Bohemia</a>, and was recognized by his childless half-brother <a href="/wiki/Wenceslaus,_King_of_the_Romans" class="mw-redirect" title="Wenceslaus, King of the Romans">Wenceslaus IV</a> as Vicar-General of the whole Empire. However, he was unable to support Wenceslaus when he was deposed in 1400, and <a href="/wiki/Rupert_of_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Rupert of Germany">Rupert of Germany</a>, Elector Palatine, was elected <a href="/wiki/King_of_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="King of Germany">German king</a> in his stead.</p>

<p>On his return to Hungary in 1401, Sigismund was imprisoned once and deposed twice. In 1401 Sigismund helped an uprising against Wenceslaus, during the course of which the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bohemia" title="Kingdom of Bohemia">Bohemian</a> king was taken prisoner, and Sigismund ruled Bohemia for nineteen months. He released Wenceslaus in 1403. In the meantime, a group of Hungarian noblemen swore loyalty to the last Anjou monarch, <a href="/wiki/Ladislaus_of_Naples" title="Ladislaus of Naples">Ladislaus of Naples</a>, putting their hands on the relic of Saint Ladislas of Hungary in Nagyvárad. Ladislaus was the son of the murdered <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_Hungary" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles II of Hungary">Charles II of Hungary</a>, and thus a distant relative of the long dead King <a href="/wiki/Louis_I_of_Hungary" title="Louis I of Hungary">Louis I of Hungary</a>. Ladislaus captured <a href="/wiki/Zadar" title="Zadar">Zadar</a> in 1403, but soon stopped any military advance. This struggle in turn led to a war with the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Venice" title="Republic of Venice">Republic of Venice</a>, as Ladislaus had sold the <a href="/wiki/Dalmatia" title="Dalmatia">Dalmatian</a> cities to the Venetians for 100,000 ducats before leaving for his own land. In the following years Sigismund acted indirectly to thwart Ladislaus' attempts to conquer central Italy, by allying with the Italian cities resisting him and by applying diplomatic pressure on him.</p>
<p>Due to his frequent absences attending to business in the other countries over which he ruled, he was obliged to consult Diets in Hungary with more frequency than his predecessors and institute the office of Palatine as chief administrator while he was away.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1404 Sigismund introduced the <i>Placetum Regium</i>. According to this decree, <a href="/wiki/Papal_bull" title="Papal bull">Papal bulls</a> could not be pronounced in Hungary without the consent of the king.</p>
<p>During his long reign the royal castle of Buda became probably the largest Gothic palace of the late Middle Ages.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="King_of_Croatia">King of Croatia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: King of Croatia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In about 1406 Sigismund married Mary's cousin <a href="/wiki/Barbara_of_Celje" class="mw-redirect" title="Barbara of Celje">Barbara of Celje</a> (Barbara Celjska, nicknamed the "<a href="/wiki/Messalina" title="Messalina">Messalina</a> of Germany"), daughter of Count <a href="/wiki/Hermann_II_of_Celje" class="mw-redirect" title="Hermann II of Celje">Hermann II of Celje</a>. Hermann's mother Katarina Kotromanić (of the House of <a href="/wiki/Kotromanic" class="mw-redirect" title="Kotromanic">Kotromanic</a>) and Mary's mother Queen Elizabeta (<a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_of_Bosnia" class="mw-redirect" title="Elisabeth of Bosnia">Elisabeth of Bosnia</a>) were sisters, or cousins who were adoptive sisters. Tvrtko I was their first cousin and adopted brother, and perhaps even became heir apparent to Queen Mary. Tvrtko may have been murdered in 1391 on Sigismund's order<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p>Sigismund managed to establish control in <a href="/wiki/Slavonia" title="Slavonia">Slavonia</a>. He did not hesitate to use violent methods (see <a href="/wiki/Bloody_Sabor_of_Kri%C5%BEevci" title="Bloody Sabor of Križevci">Bloody Sabor of Križevci</a>), but from the river Sava to the south his control was weak. Sigismund personally led an army of almost 50,000 "crusaders" against the <a href="/wiki/Croats" title="Croats">Croats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bosnians" title="Bosnians">Bosnians</a>, culminating with the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Dobor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Battle of Dobor (page does not exist)">Battle of Dobor</a> in 1408, a massacre of about 200 <a href="/wiki/Nobility" title="Nobility">noble</a> families.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Order_of_the_Dragon">Order of the Dragon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Order of the Dragon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Sigismund founded his personal order of knights, the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Dragon" title="Order of the Dragon">Order of the Dragon</a>, after the victory at Dobor. The main goal of the order was fighting the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>. Members of the order were mostly his political allies and supporters. The main members of the order were Sigismund's close allies <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_II_Garay" class="mw-redirect" title="Nicholas II Garay">Nicholas II Garay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hermann_II_of_Celje" class="mw-redirect" title="Hermann II of Celje">Hermann II of Celje</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stibor_of_Stiboricz" title="Stibor of Stiboricz">Stibor of Stiboricz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pippo_Spano" title="Pippo Spano">Pippo Spano</a>. The most important European monarchs became members of the order. He encouraged international trade by abolishing internal duties, regulating tariffs on foreign goods and standardizing weights and measures throughout the country.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="King_of_the_Romans">King of the Romans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: King of the Romans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the death of King <a href="/wiki/Rupert_of_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Rupert of Germany">Rupert of Germany</a> in 1410, Sigismund – ignoring the claims of his half-brother Wenceslaus – was elected as successor by three of the <a href="/wiki/Prince-elector" title="Prince-elector">electors</a> on 10 September 1410, but he was opposed by his cousin <a href="/wiki/Jobst_of_Moravia" title="Jobst of Moravia">Jobst of Moravia</a>, who had been elected by four electors in a different election on 1 October. Jobst's death 18 January 1411 removed this conflict and Sigismund was again elected king on 21 July 1411. His <a href="/wiki/Coronation" title="Coronation">coronation</a> was deferred until 8 November 1414, when it took place at <a href="/wiki/Aachen" title="Aachen">Aachen</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anti-Polish_alliances">Anti-Polish alliances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Anti-Polish alliances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On a number of occasions, and in 1410 in particular, Sigismund allied himself with the <a href="/wiki/Teutonic_Knights" class="mw-redirect" title="Teutonic Knights">Teutonic Knights</a> against <a href="/wiki/Jogaila" class="mw-redirect" title="Jogaila">Władysław II</a> of Poland. In return for 300.000 ducats he would attack <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Poland_(1385%E2%80%931569)" title="Kingdom of Poland (1385–1569)">Poland</a> from the south after the truce on St. John's Day, 24 June expired. Sigismund ordered his most loyal friend <a href="/wiki/Stibor_of_Stiboricz" title="Stibor of Stiboricz">Stibor of Stiboricz</a> to set up the attack on Poland. Stibor of Stiboricz was of Polish origin and from the main line of the powerful <a href="/wiki/Clan_of_Ostoja" class="mw-redirect" title="Clan of Ostoja">Clan of Ostoja</a> that had also been against choosing Jagiello as King of Poland. With the support of Sigismund, Stibor become one of the most influential men in late medieval Europe, holding titles as <a href="/wiki/Duke" title="Duke">Duke</a> of <a href="/wiki/Transylvania" title="Transylvania">Transylvania</a> and owning about 25% of modern-day <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>, including 31 castles of which 15 were situated around the 406&#160;km long <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A1h" title="Váh">Váh</a> river with surrounding land that was given to him by Sigismund. In the diplomatic struggle to prevent war between Poland-Lithuania, which was supported by the Muscovites, and the Teutonic Knights, Sigismund used Stibor's fine diplomacy to gain financially. The Polish side appointed several negotiators and most of them were also from the Clan of Ostoja, distant relations of the Stibors. However, those "family meetings" could not prevent the war and an alliance of twenty-two western states formed an army against Poland in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Grunwald" title="Battle of Grunwald">Battle of Grunwald</a> in July 1410. Stibor attacked then <a href="/wiki/Nowy_S%C4%85cz" title="Nowy Sącz">Nowy Sącz</a> and burned it to the ground, but after that he returned with his army back to the <a href="/wiki/Beckov_Castle" title="Beckov Castle">Beckov Castle</a>. After the Polish-Lithuanian victory in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Grunwald" title="Battle of Grunwald">Battle of Grunwald</a>, the Teutonic knights had to pay a huge sum of silver to Poland as reparation and again, through diplomacy of his friend Stibor, Sigismund was able to borrow all this silver from King Władysław II of Poland on good conditions. In the light of facts about the diplomatic work of Stibor and the Clan of Ostoja that was following the politics of King Sigismund, one can question whether Sigismund actually joined the anti-Polish alliance.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Council_of_Constance">Council of Constance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Council of Constance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Meister_der_Chronik_des_Konzils_von_Konstanz_001.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Meister_der_Chronik_des_Konzils_von_Konstanz_001.jpg/220px-Meister_der_Chronik_des_Konzils_von_Konstanz_001.jpg" width="220" height="321" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Meister_der_Chronik_des_Konzils_von_Konstanz_001.jpg/330px-Meister_der_Chronik_des_Konzils_von_Konstanz_001.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Meister_der_Chronik_des_Konzils_von_Konstanz_001.jpg/440px-Meister_der_Chronik_des_Konzils_von_Konstanz_001.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2024" data-file-height="2955" /></a>

<p>From 1412 to 1423 Sigismund campaigned against the <a href="/wiki/Venetia_(region)" class="mw-redirect" title="Venetia (region)">Venetians</a> in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. The king took advantage of the difficulties of <a href="/wiki/Antipope_John_XXIII" title="Antipope John XXIII">Antipope John XXIII</a> to obtain a promise that a <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Constance" title="Council of Constance">council</a> should be called in <a href="/wiki/Constance" class="mw-redirect" title="Constance">Constance</a> in 1414 to settle the <a href="/wiki/Western_Schism" title="Western Schism">Western Schism</a>. He took a leading part in the deliberations of this assembly, and during the sittings made a journey to <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_France" title="Kingdom of France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Burgundy" title="Kingdom of Burgundy">Burgundy</a> in a vain attempt to secure the abdication of the three rival popes. The council ended in 1418, solving the Schism and — of great consequence to Sigismund's future career — having the <a href="/wiki/Czech_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Czech people">Czech</a> religious reformer, <a href="/wiki/Jan_Hus" title="Jan Hus">Jan Hus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Execution_by_burning" class="mw-redirect" title="Execution by burning">burned at the stake</a> for <a href="/wiki/Heresy" title="Heresy">heresy</a> in July 1415. The complicity of Sigismund in the death of Hus is a matter of controversy. He had granted him a <a href="/wiki/Safe_conduct" title="Safe conduct">safe-conduct</a> and protested against his imprisonment; and the reformer was burned during his absence.</p>
<p>It was also at this Council that a cardinal ventured to correct Sigismund's <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> (he had construed the word <i>schisma</i> as feminine rather than neuter). To this Sigismund replied, in Latin:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>I am king of the Romans and above grammar.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>This utterance prompted historian <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Carlyle" title="Thomas Carlyle">Thomas Carlyle</a> to give him the nickname "Super Grammaticam" from the response that he had given ("Ego sum rex Romanus et super grammaticam").<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>An alliance with England against France, and a failed attempt, owing to the hostility of the princes, to secure peace in Germany by a league of the towns, were his main acts during these years. Also, Sigismund granted control of the <a href="/wiki/Margraviate_of_Brandenburg" title="Margraviate of Brandenburg">Margraviate of Brandenburg</a> (which he had received back after Jobst's death) to <a href="/wiki/Frederick_I,_Elector_of_Brandenburg" title="Frederick I, Elector of Brandenburg">Frederick I</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hohenzollern" class="mw-redirect" title="Hohenzollern">Hohenzollern</a>, burgrave of <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> (1415). This step made the Hohenzollern family one of the most important in Germany.</p>
<p>Sigismund began to shift his alliance from France to England after the French defeat at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Agincourt" title="Battle of Agincourt">Battle of Agincourt</a>. The Treaty of Canterbury (August 15, 1416) culminated diplomatic efforts between <a href="/wiki/Henry_V_of_England" title="Henry V of England">Henry V of England</a> and Sigismund and resulted in a defensive and offensive alliance against France. This, in turn, led the way to the resolution of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Schism" title="Western Schism">papal schism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hussite_Wars">Hussite Wars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Hussite Wars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Albrecht_D%C3%BCrer_082.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Albrecht_D%C3%BCrer_082.jpg/220px-Albrecht_D%C3%BCrer_082.jpg" width="220" height="408" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Albrecht_D%C3%BCrer_082.jpg/330px-Albrecht_D%C3%BCrer_082.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Albrecht_D%C3%BCrer_082.jpg/440px-Albrecht_D%C3%BCrer_082.jpg 2x" data-file-width="792" data-file-height="1468" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Albrecht_D%C3%BCrer_082.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Portrait of Emperor Sigismund, painted by <a href="/wiki/Albrecht_D%C3%BCrer" title="Albrecht Dürer">Albrecht Dürer</a> after the emperor's death</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1419, the death of <a href="/wiki/Wenceslaus,_King_of_the_Romans" class="mw-redirect" title="Wenceslaus, King of the Romans">Wenceslaus IV</a> left Sigismund titular <a href="/wiki/King_of_Bohemia" class="mw-redirect" title="King of Bohemia">King of Bohemia</a>, but he had to wait for seventeen years before the <a href="/wiki/Czechs" title="Czechs">Czech</a> <a href="/wiki/Estates_of_the_realm" title="Estates of the realm">Estates</a> would acknowledge him. It was then, and remained till much later, in question whether Bohemia was a hereditary or an elective monarchy, especially as the line through which Sigismund claimed the throne had accepted that the Kingdom of Bohemia was an elective monarchy elected by the nobles, and thus the regent of the kingdom (Čeněk of Wartenberg) also explicitly stated that Sigismund had not been elected as reason for Sigismund's claim to not be accepted. Although the two dignities of King of the Romans and King of Bohemia added considerably to his importance, and indeed made him the nominal temporal head of <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christendom</a>, they conferred no increase of power and financially embarrassed him. It was only as <a href="/wiki/King_of_Hungary" title="King of Hungary">King of Hungary</a> that he had succeeded in establishing his authority and in doing anything for the order and good government of the land. Entrusting the government of Bohemia to <a href="/wiki/Sofia_of_Bavaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Sofia of Bavaria">Sofia of Bavaria</a>, the widow of Wenceslaus, he hastened into <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Hungary</a>.</p>
<p>The Bohemians, who distrusted him as the betrayer of <a href="/wiki/Jan_Hus" title="Jan Hus">Hus</a>, were soon in arms; and the flame was fanned when Sigismund declared his intention of prosecuting the war against heretics. Three campaigns against the <a href="/wiki/Hussites" title="Hussites">Hussites</a> ended in disaster although the army of his most loyal ally <a href="/wiki/Stibor_of_Stiboricz" title="Stibor of Stiboricz">Stibor of Stiboricz</a> and later his son <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stibor_of_Beckov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stibor of Beckov (page does not exist)">Stibor of Beckov</a> could hold the Hussite side away from the borders of the Kingdom. The Turks were again attacking Hungary. The king, unable to obtain support from the German princes, was powerless in Bohemia. His attempts at the diet of <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> in 1422 to raise a mercenary army were foiled by the resistance of the towns; and in 1424 the electors, among whom was Sigismund's former ally, <a href="/wiki/Frederick_I,_Elector_of_Brandenburg" title="Frederick I, Elector of Brandenburg">Frederick I of Hohenzollern</a>, sought to strengthen their own authority at the expense of the king. Although the scheme failed, the danger to Germany from the Hussites led to the Union of Bingen, which virtually deprived Sigismund of the leadership of the war and the headship of Germany.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Final_years">Final years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Final years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1428 Sigismund led another campaign against the Turks, but again with few results. In 1431 he went to <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a> where on 25 November he received the <a href="/wiki/Iron_Crown_of_Lombardy" title="Iron Crown of Lombardy">Iron Crown</a> as King of Italy; after which he remained for some time at <a href="/wiki/Siena" title="Siena">Siena</a>, negotiating for his <a href="/wiki/Coronation" title="Coronation">coronation</a> as emperor and for the recognition of the Council of Basel by Pope <a href="/wiki/Eugenius_IV" class="mw-redirect" title="Eugenius IV">Eugenius IV</a>. He was crowned emperor in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> on 31 May 1433, and after obtaining his demands from the Pope returned to Bohemia, where he was recognized as king in 1436, though his power was little more than nominal. Shortly after he was crowned, Pope Eugenius began attempts to create a new anti-Ottoman alliance.<sup id="cite_ref-Buda2_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Buda2-10">[10]</a></sup> This was sparked<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> by an <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Revolt_of_1432%E2%80%9336" class="mw-redirect" title="Albanian Revolt of 1432–36">Albanian revolt</a> against the Ottomans, which had begun in 1432. In 1435, Sigismund sent <a href="/wiki/Fruzhin" title="Fruzhin">Fruzhin</a>, a Bulgarian nobleman, to negotiate an alliance with the Albanians. He also sent Daud, a pretender to the Ottoman throne, in early 1436.<sup id="cite_ref-Islami4_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Islami4-11">[11]</a></sup> However, following the defeat of the rebels in 1436, plans for an anti-Ottoman alliance ended.<sup id="cite_ref-Islami4_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Islami4-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sigismund died on 9 December 1437 at <a href="/wiki/Znojmo" title="Znojmo">Znojmo</a> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Znaim</i></span>), <a href="/wiki/Moravia" title="Moravia">Moravia</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>), and as ordered in life, he was buried at <a href="/wiki/Nagyv%C3%A1rad" class="mw-redirect" title="Nagyvárad">Nagyvárad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Hungary</a> (today <a href="/wiki/Oradea" title="Oradea">Oradea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>), next to the tomb of the king Saint <a href="/wiki/Ladislaus_I_of_Hungary" title="Ladislaus I of Hungary">Ladislaus I of Hungary</a>, who was the ideal of the perfect monarch, warrior and Christian for that time and was deeply venerated by Sigismund.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> By his second wife, <a href="/wiki/Barbara_of_Celje" class="mw-redirect" title="Barbara of Celje">Barbara of Celje</a>, he left an only daughter, <a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_of_Luxembourg" class="mw-redirect" title="Elisabeth of Luxembourg">Elisabeth of Luxembourg</a>, who was married to <a href="/wiki/Albert_II_of_Germany" title="Albert II of Germany">Albert V, duke of Austria</a> (later German king as Albert II) whom Sigismund named as his successor. As he left no sons his line of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Luxembourg" title="House of Luxembourg">House of Luxembourg</a> became extinct on his death.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_and_issue">Family and issue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Family and issue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sigismund married twice but had little luck in securing the succession to his crowns. Each of his two marriages resulted in the birth of one child. His <a href="/wiki/Childbirth" title="Childbirth">first-born</a> child, probably a son, was born prematurely as a result of a horse riding accident suffered by Queen <a href="/wiki/Mary_of_Hungary" class="mw-redirect" title="Mary of Hungary">Mary of Hungary</a> when she was well advanced in pregnancy. Mother and child both died shortly after the birth in the hills of <a href="/wiki/Buda" title="Buda">Buda</a> on 17 May 1395. This caused a deep succession crisis because Sigismund ruled over <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Hungary</a> by right of his wife, and although he managed to keep his power, the crisis lasted until his second marriage to <a href="/wiki/Barbara_of_Celje" class="mw-redirect" title="Barbara of Celje">Barbara of Celje</a>. Barbara's only child, <a href="/wiki/Born_in_the_purple" title="Born in the purple">born in the purple</a> on 7 October 1409, probably in the castle of <a href="/wiki/Visegr%C3%A1d" title="Visegrád">Visegrád</a>, was <a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_of_Luxembourg" class="mw-redirect" title="Elisabeth of Luxembourg">Elisabeth of Luxembourg</a>, the future <a href="/wiki/Queen_consort" title="Queen consort">queen consort</a> of Hungary, Germany and Bohemia. Queen Barbara was unable to give birth to any further issue. <a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_of_Bohemia_(1409%E2%80%931442)" class="mw-redirect" title="Elisabeth of Bohemia (1409–1442)">Elisabeth of Bohemia</a> was thus the only surviving legitimate offspring of Sigismund.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hungarian_affiliations">Hungarian affiliations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Hungarian affiliations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Sigismund was known to speak fluent <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>, wore Hungarian style royal clothes and even grew his beard in the Hungarian fashion.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He also spent huge amounts of money during his reign to rebuild the Gothic castles of <a href="/wiki/Buda_Castle" title="Buda Castle">Buda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Visegr%C3%A1d" title="Visegrád">Visegrád</a> in the Kingdom of Hungary, ordering the transportation of materials from Austria and Bohemia.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>His many affairs with women led to the birth of several <a href="/wiki/Legend" title="Legend">legends</a>, as the one that existed decades later during the reign of the King <a href="/wiki/Matthias_Corvinus" title="Matthias Corvinus">Matthias Corvinus</a> of Hungary. According to this, <a href="/wiki/John_Hunyadi" title="John Hunyadi">John Hunyadi</a> was Sigismund's illegitimate son. Sigismund gave a ring to the boy's mother when he was born, but one day in the forest a raven stole it from her, and the ring was only recovered after the bird was hunted down. It is said that this incident inspired the coat of arms of the <a href="/wiki/Hunyadi_family" title="Hunyadi family">Hunyadis</a>, and later also appeared in the coat of arms of Matthias "Corvinus".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sigismund adopted the Hungarian reverence for Saint <a href="/wiki/Ladislaus_I_of_Hungary" title="Ladislaus I of Hungary">Ladislaus I of Hungary</a>, who was considered to be an ideal Christian knight at that time. He went on pilgrimage several times to his tomb in Nagyvárad. Before Sigismund died, he ordered to be buried next to the king saint.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reformatio_Sigismundi">Reformatio Sigismundi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Reformatio Sigismundi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Reformatio_Sigismundi" title="Reformatio Sigismundi">Reformatio Sigismundi</a></div>
<p>The <i><b>Reformatio Sigismundi</b></i> appeared in connection with efforts to <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Reform" title="Imperial Reform">reform</a> the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> during the reign of Emperor Sigismund (1410–1437). It was presented in 1439 at the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Basel" class="mw-redirect" title="Council of Basel">Council of Basel</a>, published by an anonymous author, and referred to the injustice of the German rulers. It included a <a href="/wiki/Vision_(religion)" class="mw-redirect" title="Vision (religion)">vision</a> of Sigismund's about the appearance of a priest-king, Frederick, as well as plans for a wide <a href="/wiki/Reform" title="Reform">reform</a> of the monarchy and emperorship and the German empire.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles">Titles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Titles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Sigismund, by God's grace, Holy Roman Emperor; King of the Romans, of Hungary, Bohemia, Italy, Dalmatia, Croatia, Rama, Serbia, Galicia, Lodomeria, Cumania and Bulgaria; Prince of Silesia and Luxembourg; Margrave of Moravia, Lusatia and Brandenburg.</i><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancestors">Ancestors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Ancestors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
