<p><b>Königsberg in Bayern</b> is a town in the <a href="/wiki/Ha%C3%9Fberge_(district)" title="Haßberge (district)">Haßberge</a> district, in <a href="/wiki/Lower_Franconia" title="Lower Franconia">Lower Franconia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is situated 7&#160;km northeast of <a href="/wiki/Ha%C3%9Ffurt" title="Haßfurt">Haßfurt</a>, and 31&#160;km northwest of <a href="/wiki/Bamberg" title="Bamberg">Bamberg</a>.</p>
<p>The town is not to be confused with the most famous <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigsberg" title="Königsberg">Königsberg</a>, the former <a href="/wiki/East_Prussia" title="East Prussia">East Prussian</a> city of <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigsberg_in_Preu%C3%9Fen" class="mw-redirect" title="Königsberg in Preußen">Königsberg in Preußen</a> (since 1945 Kaliningrad, Russia), nor with <a href="/wiki/Neumark_(region)" class="mw-redirect" title="Neumark (region)">Königsberg in der Neumark</a> (since 1945 as <a href="/wiki/Chojna" title="Chojna">Chojna</a> in Poland). It was an exclave of <a href="/wiki/Saxe-Coburg_and_Gotha" title="Saxe-Coburg and Gotha">Saxe-Coburg and Gotha</a> until 1918, after having been a part of <a href="/wiki/Saxe-Hildburghausen" title="Saxe-Hildburghausen">Saxe-Hildburghausen</a>.</p>
