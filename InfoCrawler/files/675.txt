<p><b>San Carlos</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">[saŋ ˈkaɾlos]</a></span>) is the capital city of the <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> of San Carlos and of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_San_Juan_(department)" class="mw-redirect" title="Río San Juan (department)">Río San Juan</a> <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Nicaragua" title="Departments of Nicaragua">Department</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>.</p>
<p>The city proper has a population of roughly 12,174, while the city and surrounding communities (<i>Comarcas</i>) contain 37,461 as of 2005. San Carlos is positioned on the confluence of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Nicaragua" title="Lake Nicaragua">Lake Nicaragua</a> and the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_River_(Nicaragua)" title="San Juan River (Nicaragua)">San Juan River</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Solentiname_Islands" title="Solentiname Islands">Solentiname Islands</a> in Lake Nicaragua are part of the Municipality of San Carlos.</p>
