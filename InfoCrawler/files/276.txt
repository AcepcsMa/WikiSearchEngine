<p><b>Middle Franconia</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Mittelfranken</i></span>) is one of the three <a href="/wiki/Regierungsbezirk" title="Regierungsbezirk">administrative regions</a> of <a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is in the west of Bavaria and adjoins the state of <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a>. The administrative seat is <a href="/wiki/Ansbach" title="Ansbach">Ansbach</a> but the most populated city is <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Middle_Franconia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Ansbach" title="Principality of Ansbach">Principality of Ansbach</a></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Imperial_City_of_Nuremberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Imperial City of Nuremberg">Imperial City of Nuremberg</a></div>
<p>After the founding of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bavaria" title="Kingdom of Bavaria">Kingdom of Bavaria</a> the state was totally reorganised and, in 1808, divided into 15 administrative government regions (German: Regierungsbezirke (singular <a href="/wiki/Regierungsbezirk" title="Regierungsbezirk">Regierungsbezirk</a>)), in Bavaria called Kreise (singular: Kreis). They were created in the fashion of the French departements, quite even in size and population, and named after their main rivers.</p>
<p>In the following years, due to territorial changes (e. g. loss of <a href="/wiki/German_Tyrol" title="German Tyrol">Tyrol</a>, addition of the <a href="/wiki/Palatinate_(region)" title="Palatinate (region)">Palatinate</a>), the number of Kreise was reduced to 8. One of these was the <a href="/wiki/Rezatkreis" title="Rezatkreis">Rezatkreis</a> (Rezat District). In 1837 king <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_I_of_Bavaria" title="Ludwig I of Bavaria">Ludwig I of Bavaria</a> renamed the Kreise after historical territorial names and tribes of the area. This also involved some border changes or territorial swaps. Thus the district name of Rezatkreis changed to Upper Franconia.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Middle_Franconia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Next to the major city <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, the capital <a href="/wiki/Ansbach" title="Ansbach">Ansbach</a> and the former residene city <a href="/wiki/Erlangen" title="Erlangen">Erlangen</a> the cities of the <a href="/wiki/Romantic_Road" title="Romantic Road">Romantic Road</a> <a href="/wiki/Rothenburg_ob_der_Tauber" title="Rothenburg ob der Tauber">Rothenburg ob der Tauber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dinkelsb%C3%BChl" title="Dinkelsbühl">Dinkelsbühl</a> belong to the major tourist attractions. The <a href="/wiki/Lichtenau_Fortress" title="Lichtenau Fortress">Lichtenau Fortress</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rothenberg_Fortress" title="Rothenberg Fortress">Rothenberg Fortress</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hohenstein_Castle_(Middle_Franconia)" title="Hohenstein Castle (Middle Franconia)">Hohenstein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cadolzburg" title="Cadolzburg">Cadolzburg</a> belong to the most important castles of Middle Franconia. The <a href="/wiki/Franconian_Jura" title="Franconian Jura">Franconian Jura</a> and the northern valley of the River <a href="/wiki/Altm%C3%BChl" title="Altmühl">Altmühl</a> are among the scenic attractions.</p>
