<p><b>Erlangen</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈɛrlaŋən]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:De-Erlangen.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/27/De-Erlangen.ogg" class="internal" title="De-Erlangen.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>; <a href="/wiki/East_Franconian_German" title="East Franconian German">East Franconian</a>: <i>Erlang</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Middle_Franconia" title="Middle Franconia">Middle Franconian</a> <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is located north-west of <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> at the confluence of the river <a href="/wiki/Regnitz" title="Regnitz">Regnitz</a> and its large tributary, the <i>Schwabach</i>. Erlangen has more than 100,000 inhabitants.</p>
<p>As of 2015<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Erlangen&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> Erlangen is dominated by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg" title="University of Erlangen-Nuremberg">University of Erlangen-Nuremberg</a> and the numerous branch offices of <a href="/wiki/Siemens_AG" class="mw-redirect" title="Siemens AG">Siemens AG</a>, as well as a large research Institute of the <a href="/wiki/Fraunhofer_Society" title="Fraunhofer Society">Fraunhofer Society</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Max_Planck_Institute_for_the_Science_of_Light" title="Max Planck Institute for the Science of Light">Max Planck Institute for the Science of Light</a>. An event that left its mark on the city was the settlement of <a href="/wiki/Huguenot" class="mw-redirect" title="Huguenot">Huguenots</a> after the revocation of the <a href="/wiki/Edict_of_Nantes" title="Edict of Nantes">Edict of Nantes</a> in 1685.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Felix_Klein" title="Felix Klein">Felix Klein</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Erlangen_program" title="Erlangen program">Erlangen program</a> of 1872, considering the future of research in mathematics, is so called because Klein then taught at the University of Erlangen-Nuremberg.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erlangen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Erlangen was first mentioned in official records in 1002 under the name of <i>Villa Erlangon</i>. In 1361, the village was sold to <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Emperor</a>/<a href="/wiki/List_of_Bohemian_monarchs" title="List of Bohemian monarchs">King</a> <a href="/wiki/Charles_IV,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles IV, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles IV</a>. It became part of the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Czech Kingdom">Czech Kingdom</a>. Three years later, a city was built close to the village, which in 1374 was given its own <a href="/wiki/Coining_(mint)" title="Coining (mint)">mint</a>. In 1398, the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="Municipal rights">municipal rights</a> were confirmed by King <a href="/wiki/Wenceslaus_IV_of_Bohemia" title="Wenceslaus IV of Bohemia">Wenceslaus IV of Bohemia</a>. In 1402, the city came into the possession of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Hohenzollern" title="House of Hohenzollern">House of Hohenzollern</a> as part of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Principality_of_Brandenburg-Kulmbach&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Principality of Brandenburg-Kulmbach (page does not exist)">Principality of Brandenburg-Kulmbach</a> (from 1603 on <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg-Bayreuth" class="mw-redirect" title="Brandenburg-Bayreuth">Brandenburg-Bayreuth</a>), remaining under their rule until 1806. During the four year Napoleonic occupation, Erlangen was the capital of the so-called "Low County" (<i>Unterland</i>) of the principality, encompassing the area until <a href="/wiki/Neustadt_an_der_Aisch" title="Neustadt an der Aisch">Neustadt an der Aisch</a> and separated from the "High County" (Oberland) by a land corridor. In 1810 it became part of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bavaria" title="Kingdom of Bavaria">Kingdom of Bavaria</a>, together with the rest of former Brandenburg-Bayreuth.</p>
<p>While it was still part of Brandenburg-Bayreuth, the first French <a href="/wiki/Huguenot" class="mw-redirect" title="Huguenot">Huguenot</a> <a href="/wiki/Refugee" title="Refugee">refugees</a> arrived in Erlangen in 1686. Margrave <a href="/wiki/Christian_Ernst,_Margrave_of_Brandenburg-Bayreuth" title="Christian Ernst, Margrave of Brandenburg-Bayreuth">Christian Ernst</a> of <a href="/wiki/Margraviate_of_Brandenburg-Bayreuth" class="mw-redirect" title="Margraviate of Brandenburg-Bayreuth">Brandenburg-Bayreuth</a>, built a "new town" (Neustadt) for them. In 1706, the old town (just below the site of the annual <a href="/wiki/Bergkirchweih" title="Bergkirchweih">Bergkirchweih</a>) was almost completely destroyed by a fire, but soon rebuilt. In 1812, the old and new towns were merged into one.</p>
<p>In 1742, <a href="/wiki/Frederick,_Margrave_of_Brandenburg-Bayreuth" title="Frederick, Margrave of Brandenburg-Bayreuth">Frederick, Margrave of Brandenburg-Bayreuth</a>, founded a university for his royal seat of Bayreuth, but due to the rebelliousness of the local students, the university was transferred to Erlangen. Only later did it obtain the name of "Friedrich-Alexander-University" and become a <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussian</a> state university. Famous students of these times were <a href="/wiki/Johann_Ludwig_Tieck" class="mw-redirect" title="Johann Ludwig Tieck">Johann Ludwig Tieck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Heinrich_Wackenroder" title="Wilhelm Heinrich Wackenroder">Wilhelm Heinrich Wackenroder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Noether" title="Emmy Noether">Emmy Noether</a>.</p>
<p>Already during the Bavarian <a href="/w/index.php?title=Municipal_reform&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Municipal reform (page does not exist)">municipal reform</a> of 1818, the city was endowed with its own administration. In 1862, the canton administration Erlangen was founded, from which later arose the administrative district of Erlangen. In 1972, this district was merged with the administrative district of <a href="/w/index.php?title=H%C3%B6chstadt_(district)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Höchstadt (district) (page does not exist)">Höchstadt</a>. Erlangen became the capital of this newly founded district <a href="/wiki/Erlangen-H%C3%B6chstadt" title="Erlangen-Höchstadt">Erlangen-Höchstadt</a>. During this municipal reform, Erlangen was effectively enlarged considerably, thus in 1974 it had more than 100,000 inhabitants.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Points_of_interest">Points of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erlangen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Points of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg" title="University of Erlangen-Nuremberg">University of Erlangen-Nuremberg</a> (<i>Friedrich-Alexander-Universität</i>) was founded in 1742 by <a href="/wiki/Frederick,_Margrave_of_Brandenburg-Bayreuth" title="Frederick, Margrave of Brandenburg-Bayreuth">Frederick, Margrave of Brandenburg-Bayreuth</a>, in the city of <a href="/wiki/Bayreuth" title="Bayreuth">Bayreuth</a>, but was relocated to Erlangen the next year. Today, it features five faculties; some departments (Economics and Education) are located in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>. About 39,000 students study at this university, of which about 20,000 are located in Erlangen.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Botanischer_Garten_Erlangen" title="Botanischer Garten Erlangen">Botanischer Garten Erlangen</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Botanical_garden" title="Botanical garden">botanical garden</a> maintained by the university.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bergkirchweih">Bergkirchweih</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erlangen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bergkirchweih">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bergkirchweih" title="Bergkirchweih">Bergkirchweih</a> is an annual beer festival, similar to the <a href="/wiki/Oktoberfest" title="Oktoberfest">Oktoberfest</a> in <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> but smaller in scale. It takes place during the twelve days before and after <a href="/wiki/Pentecost" title="Pentecost">Pentecost</a> (that is, 49 days after Easter); this period is called the "fifth season" by the locals. The beer is served at wooden tables in one-litre stoneware jugs under the trees of the "Berg", a small, craggy, and wooded hill with old caves (beer cellars) owned by local breweries. The cellars extend for 21&#160;km (14 miles)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> throughout the hill (the "Berg") and maintain a constant cool underground temperature. Until <a href="/wiki/Carl_von_Linde" title="Carl von Linde">Carl von Linde</a> invented the electric refrigerator in 1871, this was considered to be the largest refrigerator in Southern Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The beer festival draws more than one million visitors annually. It features carnival rides of high tech quality, food stalls of most Franconian dishes, including bratwurst, suckling pig, roasted almonds, and giant pretzels.</p>
<p>It is commonly known by local residents as the "Berchkärwa" (pronounced "bairch'-care-va") or simply the "Berch", like in <i>"Gehma auf'n Berch!"</i> ("Let's go up the mountain!").</p>
<p>This is an outdoor event frequented and enjoyed by Franconians. Despite a relatively high number of visitors, it is not commonly known by tourists, or people living outside Bavaria.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_residents">Notable residents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erlangen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Notable residents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Though a small village for much of its history and now only a small city of only 100k inhabitants, Erlangen has made significant contributions to the world, primarily through its many Lutheran theologians, to its <a href="/wiki/University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg" title="University of Erlangen-Nuremberg">University of Erlangen-Nuremberg</a> scholars, and the <a href="/wiki/Siemens_AG" class="mw-redirect" title="Siemens AG">Siemens AG</a> pioneers in science and technology.</p>

<p>Among its noted residents are:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Johann_de_Kalb" title="Johann de Kalb">Johann de Kalb</a> (1721-1780), - Soldier, <a href="/wiki/War_of_Austrian_Succession" class="mw-redirect" title="War of Austrian Succession">War of Austrian Succession</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seven_Years%27_War" title="Seven Years' War">Seven Years' War</a>, Major General in the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>, namesake of many American towns</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Philipp_Ludwig_Statius_M%C3%BCller" title="Philipp Ludwig Statius Müller">Philipp Ludwig Statius Müller</a> (1725-1776), - zoologist, known to the classification of several new species, especially birds</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eugenius_Johann_Christoph_Esper" title="Eugenius Johann Christoph Esper">Eugenius Johann Christoph Esper</a> (1742-1810), - scientist, botanist, first to begin research into <a href="/wiki/Paleopathology" title="Paleopathology">Paleopathology</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Johann_Schweigger" title="Johann Schweigger">Johann Schweigger</a> - (1779-1857), chemist, physicist, mathematician, named "Chlorine", and invented the Galvanometer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_Friedrich_Schweigger" title="August Friedrich Schweigger">August Friedrich Schweigger</a> (1783-1921), - botanist, zoologist, known for taxonomy including the discovery of several turtle species</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Georg_Ohm" title="Georg Ohm">Georg Ohm</a> - (1789-1854), German scientist, famous for Ohm's Law regarding electric current, and the measurement unit <a href="/wiki/Ohm" title="Ohm">Ohm</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Karl_Heinrich_Rau" title="Karl Heinrich Rau">Karl Heinrich Rau</a> - (1792-1870), economist, published an influential encyclopedia of all "relevant" economic knowledge of his time</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Carl_Friedrich_Philipp_von_Martius" title="Carl Friedrich Philipp von Martius">Carl Friedrich Philipp von Martius</a> - (1794-1868), botanist, explorer, famous expedition into Brazil (1817-1820)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Adolph_Wagner" title="Adolph Wagner">Adolph Wagner</a> - (1835-1917), economist, founding proponent of Academic Socialism and State Socialism</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Paul_Zweifel" title="Paul Zweifel">Paul Zweifel</a> (1848-1927), - gynecologist, proved that the fetus was metabolically active, paving the way for new fetal research</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Emmy_Noether" title="Emmy Noether">Emmy Noether</a> - (1882-1935), mathematician, groundbreaking work on abstract algebra and theoretical physics</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fritz_Noether" title="Fritz Noether">Fritz Noether</a> (1884-1941), - mathematician, political prisoner, younger brother of <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Noether" title="Emmy Noether">Emmy Noether</a>, imprisoned in Soviet Russia</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ernst_Penzoldt" title="Ernst Penzoldt">Ernst Penzoldt</a> - (1892-1955), artist, famous German author, painter, and sculptor</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eduard_Hauser_(soldier)" class="mw-redirect" title="Eduard Hauser (soldier)">Eduard Hauser (soldier)</a> (1895-1961), - German officer, general in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>,</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Welker" title="Heinrich Welker">Heinrich Welker</a> - (1912-1981), theoretical physicist, made numerous inventions in the early electrical engineering fields</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Fleischmann" title="Rudolf Fleischmann">Rudolf Fleischmann</a> (1903-2002), - scientist, nuclear physicist, member of the <a href="/wiki/Uranium_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Uranium Club">Uranium Club</a>, theorist on isotope separation</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bernhard_Plettner" title="Bernhard Plettner">Bernhard Plettner</a> - (1914-1997), electrical engineer and Business Administration, CEO for Siemens AG (1971-1981)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Helmut_Zahn" title="Helmut Zahn">Helmut Zahn</a> (1916-2004), - scientist, chemist, one of the first to discover the properties of Insulin</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Walter_Krau%C3%9F" class="mw-redirect" title="Walter Krauß">Walter Krauß</a> (1917-1943), - Luftwaffe officer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hans_Lotter" class="mw-redirect" title="Hans Lotter">Hans Lotter</a> (1917-2008), - officer in World War II, escaped from POW camp and wrote memoirs about it</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Georg_Nees" title="Georg Nees">Georg Nees</a> - (1926-2016), Graphic Artist, expanded ALGOL computer language, pioneer in digital art and sculptures</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Elke_Sommer" title="Elke Sommer">Elke Sommer</a> - (born 1940), entertainer, Golden Globe Award winning actress from television and film, early Playboy playmate</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Heinrich_von_Pierer" title="Heinrich von Pierer">Heinrich von Pierer</a> - (born 1941), Business Administration, CEO for Siemens AG (1992-2005), advisor to numerous governmental figures</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Frey" title="Gerhard Frey">Gerhard Frey</a> - (born 1944), mathematician, worked on <a href="/wiki/Elliptic_Curve" class="mw-redirect" title="Elliptic Curve">Elliptic Curve</a> and helped prove <a href="/wiki/Fermat%27s_Last_Theorem" title="Fermat's Last Theorem">Fermat's Last Theorem</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Karl_Meiler" title="Karl Meiler">Karl Meiler</a> - (1949-2014), tennis player, moderately successful in Doubles Tennis in the 1970s.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Karlheinz_Brandenburg" title="Karlheinz Brandenburg">Karlheinz Brandenburg</a> - (born 1954), sound engineer, contributor to the invention of the format MPEG Audio Layer III, or <a href="/wiki/MP3" title="MP3">MP3</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Klaus_T%C3%A4uber" title="Klaus Täuber">Klaus Täuber</a> - (born 1958), footballer, played for several Bundesliga teams from the mid 1970s-1980s, managed at lower levels</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lothar_Matth%C3%A4us" title="Lothar Matthäus">Lothar Matthäus</a> - (born 1961), German Football legend, World Cup Winning Captain, Bayern Captain, first FIFA World Player of the Year</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Willi_Kalender" class="mw-redirect" title="Willi Kalender">Willi Kalender</a> - medical physicist, pioneer in CT Scan technology and research into numerous diseases</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_Teller" class="mw-redirect" title="Jürgen Teller">Jürgen Teller</a> - (born 1964), fine art and fashion photography, worked for numerous magazines and designers, often with <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rk" title="Björk">Björk</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hisham_Zreiq" title="Hisham Zreiq">Hisham Zreiq</a> - (born 1968), award-winning Palestinian Christian Independent filmmaker, poet and visual artist.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Peter_Wackel" title="Peter Wackel">Peter Wackel</a> - (born 1977), singer, with 6 albums and over 25 singles, he has a niche singing Schlager musik</li>
