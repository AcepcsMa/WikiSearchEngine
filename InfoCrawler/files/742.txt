<p><b>Pedro Rodríguez de la Vega</b> (18 January 1940 – 11 July 1971) was a Mexican <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_motor_racing" title="Grand Prix motor racing">Grand Prix motor racing</a> driver. He was the older brother of <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(Formula_One)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ricardo Rodríguez (Formula One)">Ricardo Rodríguez</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rodríguez was born in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, the second son of the marriage of Pedro Natalio Rodríguez and Concepción De la Vega, he had an older sister, Conchita, and three younger brothers: Ricardo, Federico (who was born dead) and Alejandro.</p>
<p>At 15, his father sent him to <a href="/wiki/Western_Military_Academy" title="Western Military Academy">Western Military Academy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Alton,_Illinois" title="Alton, Illinois">Alton, Illinois</a> in order to learn English and to get more discipline.<sup id="cite_ref-ref1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ref1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodríguez brothers raced bicycles and motorcycles, becoming Mexican national motorcycle champion in 1953 and 1954. He made his international debut in cars at <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">Nassau</a> in 1957 in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari" title="Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.</p>
<p>He married Angelina (<i>née</i> Dammy), in Mexico in 1961, although he had a girlfriend in England, Glenda Foreman, with whom he lived in <a href="/wiki/Bray,_Berkshire" title="Bray, Berkshire">Bray on Thames</a> in his latter years, but left no children.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Rodríguez always traveled with a <a href="/wiki/Mexican_flag" class="mw-redirect" title="Mexican flag">Mexican flag</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Gramophone_record" title="Gramophone record">record</a> of the national anthem because when he won the <a href="/wiki/1967_South_African_Grand_Prix" title="1967 South African Grand Prix">1967 South African GP</a> the organizers did not have the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_anthem" class="mw-redirect" title="Mexican anthem">Mexican anthem</a>, and instead played the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_hat_dance" class="mw-redirect" title="Mexican hat dance">Mexican hat dance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jo_Ram%C3%ADrez" title="Jo Ramírez">Jo Ramírez</a> was a very close friend to both Rodríguez as well as his younger brother Ricardo.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rodríguez began racing with bicycles at eight years old.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was a class winner in the Mexican Championship by 1950. He started racing a 125&#160;cc (7.6&#160;cu&#160;in) <a href="/wiki/Adler_(automobile)" title="Adler (automobile)">Adler</a> <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle" title="Motorcycle">motorcycle</a>, winning Mexico's national championship in 1952 and 1954.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1952, he entered a rally in a Ford, but achieved little.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He returned to racing full-time in 1955, at 15, entering a <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_XK120" title="Jaguar XK120">Jaguar XK120</a> or <a href="/w/index.php?title=Porsche_1600&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Porsche 1600 (page does not exist)">Porsche 1600</a>S in local contests.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the end of 1957, Rodríguez (who had been driving a <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet_Corvette_(C1)" title="Chevrolet Corvette (C1)">Chevrolet Corvette</a> in Mexico) and his brother entered the Nassau Speed Week competition, where the wild-driving elder brother wrecked his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari" title="Ferrari">Ferrari</a> <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_Monza#500_TR" title="Ferrari Monza">500 TR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 18-year-old Rodríguez shared a 500 TR at <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="24 Hours of Le Mans">Le Mans</a>, entered by U.S. importer <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Chinetti" title="Luigi Chinetti">Luigi Chinetti</a>, with José Behra, brother of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Behra" title="Jean Behra">Jean Behra</a>, as his co-driver; the car did not finish, after a radiator hose puncture.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Rodríguez came back every year to Le Mans, fourteen times in total, and won in <a href="/wiki/1968_24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="1968 24 Hours of Le Mans">1968</a>, co-driving with Belgian <a href="/wiki/Lucien_Bianchi" title="Lucien Bianchi">Lucien Bianchi</a>, sharing a <a href="/wiki/Ford_GT40" title="Ford GT40">Ford GT40</a> for the <a href="/wiki/John_Wyer" title="John Wyer">JW</a>–<a href="/wiki/Gulf_Oil" title="Gulf Oil">Gulf</a> team.</p>
<p>At the Rheims 12-hours in 1958, Rodríguez and Behra placed second in class (eighth overall) in their <a href="/wiki/Porsche_Carrera" title="Porsche Carrera">Porsche Carrera</a>, while Rodríguez came second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_250_TR" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrari 250 TR">Ferrari 250 TR</a> at Nassau at the end of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodríguez went to Europe to race starting in 1959, sharing a Porsche 1600 S with Leo Levine at the [[Nurbu{e{rgring 1000km]], which came in second in class (thirteenth overall).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He shared a 750&#160;cc (46&#160;cu&#160;in) <a href="/wiki/O.S.C.A." title="O.S.C.A.">O.S.C.A.</a> with his brother for Le Mans, which broke.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>At Cuba's 1960 Liberty <i>Grand Prix</i>, Rodríguez's 250TR followed <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a>'s winning <a href="/wiki/Maserati" title="Maserati">Maserati</a> <a href="/wiki/Maserati_Tipo_61" title="Maserati Tipo 61"><i>Tipo</i> 61</a> home, in second.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> At <a href="/w/index.php?title=1960_Sebring_12-Hours&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="1960 Sebring 12-Hours (page does not exist)">Sebring</a>, his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_156_F1" title="Ferrari 156 F1">156</a> failed to finish.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Rodríguez claimed seventh at the 1960 <a href="/wiki/Targa_Florio" title="Targa Florio">Targa Florio</a>, again in a 156, which spent time off the pavement as well as on.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He retired from that year's Nurbu[e]rgring 1000km, and from Le Mans.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1961, Rodríguez entered <a href="/wiki/Formula_Junior" title="Formula Junior">Formula Junior</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He returned also to Sebring, sharing a 250TR with his brother which suffered electrical trouble and came third.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The duo also failed to finish that year's Targa Florio or Nur 1000km, but did win the Paris 1000km.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> An ongoing duel with the works Ferraris at Le Mans, which ultimately resulted in engine failure only two hours from the end, attracted the attention of <a href="/wiki/Enzo_Ferrari" title="Enzo Ferrari">Enzo Ferrari</a>, who offered them <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> rides with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">his team</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Pedro declined, having "a motor business in Mexico City to run".<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite his refusal, Rodríguez kept racing, and in 1962 entered at Sebring, the Nurb, and Le Mans, but failed to finish each time.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> He won at <a href="/wiki/Bridgehampton_Race_Circuit" title="Bridgehampton Race Circuit">Bridgehampton</a>, in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_330_TRI/LM" title="Ferrari 330 TRI/LM">Ferrari 330 TRI/LM</a>, and shared a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_250_GTO" title="Ferrari 250 GTO">250GTO</a> with his brother to win the Paris 1000km, the second year in a row.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>After Ferrari refused to enter the 1962 <a href="/wiki/1962_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1962 Mexican Grand Prix">Mexican <i>Grand Prix</i></a>, the first to be held in Mexico, Rogriguez and his younger brother both found rides of their own. After his brother was killed in a horrific accident in practice, Rodríguez withdrew.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He considered retiring from racing. However, in 1963 he won the <a href="/wiki/Daytona_International_Speedway" title="Daytona International Speedway">Daytona</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Daytoha_Coninental&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Daytoha Coninental (page does not exist)">Continental</a> in a 250GTO entered by <a href="/wiki/North_American_Racing_Team" title="North American Racing Team">North American Racing Team</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> He came third at Sebring, sharing a 330TR/LM with <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> He failed to qualify at <a href="/wiki/1963_Indianapolis_500" title="1963 Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis</a>, in an <a href="/wiki/Aston_Martin" title="Aston Martin">Aston Martin</a>-powered <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cooper_T54&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cooper T54 (page does not exist)">Cooper T54</a>, but took part in his first <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One"><i>Grands Prix</i></a> in the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">works Lotus</a> at <a href="/wiki/Watkins_Glen_International" title="Watkins Glen International">Watkins Glen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Hermanos_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez">Magdalena Mixhuca</a>. Rodríguez failed to finish both times.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>For 1964, he again won the Daytona Continental, as well as the sports car Canadian <i>Grand Prix</i>, was second at the Paris 1000km, and third in the Bahamas <a href="/wiki/Tourist_Trophy" class="mw-disambig" title="Tourist Trophy">Tourist Trophy</a><sup class="plainlinks noprint Inline-Template" style="vertical-align:text-top;white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://dispenser.homenet.org/~dispenser/cgi-bin/dab_solver.py?page=Pedro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)&amp;editintro=Template:Disambiguation_needed/editintro&amp;client=Template:Dn"><span title="Link needs disambiguation. (July 2017)">disambiguation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In single-seater racing, he recorded a sixth in the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_156" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrari 156">Ferrari 156</a> at <a href="/wiki/1964_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1964 Mexican Grand Prix">Mexico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1965, his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_33" title="Lotus 33">Lotus 33</a>-Climax was fourth at the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Express" title="Daily Express">Daily Express</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Sivlerstone</a> Trophy, fifth at the <a href="/wiki/1965_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1965 United States Grand Prix">U.S. <i>Grand Prix</i></a> and seventh in the <a href="/wiki/1965_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1965 Mexican Grand Prix">Mexican <i>Grand Prix</i></a> in a Ferrari.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> He won the Rheims 12-Hours in a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ferrari_375P&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ferrari 375P (page does not exist)">Ferrari 375P</a> he shared with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jean_Guicher&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jean Guicher (page does not exist)">Jean Guicher</a>, and scored a third at the Candadian Sports Car <i>Grand Prix</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>He stood in for <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> with Lotus at the 1966 <a href="/wiki/1966_French_Grand_Prix" title="1966 French Grand Prix">French</a> and <a href="/wiki/1966_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1966 Mexican Grand Prix">Mexican</a> <i>Grands Prix</i>, falling out of fourth with oil system failure in the first and third with transmission trouble in the second.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> He also deputized for Clark in the <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> event at Rouen.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the start of the <a href="/wiki/1967_Formula_One_season" title="1967 Formula One season">1967 season</a>, Rodríguez won in only his ninth Grand Prix, at <a href="/wiki/1967_South_African_Grand_Prix" title="1967 South African Grand Prix">Kyalami</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Cooper manager <a href="/wiki/Roy_Salvadori" title="Roy Salvadori">Roy Salvadori</a> allowed Rodríguez to drive the practice car, over the objections of teammate <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Rindt" title="Jochen Rindt">Jochen Rindt</a>, who had demanded Rodríguez's car, with strong support from Rindt's close friend <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>. Rodríguez's smooth, consistent driving earned him victory after <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> had had a lengthy <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> and local <a href="/wiki/Privateer_(motorsport)" title="Privateer (motorsport)">privateer</a> <a href="/wiki/John_Love_(racing_driver)" title="John Love (racing driver)">John Love</a>'s Tasman Cooper needed a late fuel stop. Rindt, by contrast, retired the other Cooper-Maserati after 38 laps. Rodríguez drove a controlled season in 1967 as No. 2 to Rindt. Though usually slower than his teammate, he built up experience in the older and heavier <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T81" title="Cooper T81">T81</a>, while Rindt was given the improved <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T81B" class="mw-redirect" title="Cooper T81B">T81B</a> and later the brand new <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T86" title="Cooper T86">T86</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (December 2012)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A mid-season accident in a <a href="/wiki/Protos_(constructor)" title="Protos (constructor)">Protos</a>-Ford, at the Formula Two event at Enna, sidelined him for three <i>Grands Prix</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Rodríguez was only marginally slower than Rindt in the <a href="/wiki/1967_Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="1967 Dutch Grand Prix">Dutch Grand Prix</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> also the only other race in the season where the Coopers were competitive.</p>

<p>His performance at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Park_Zandvoort" title="Circuit Park Zandvoort">Zandvoort</a> earned Rodríguez a better drive with, <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a> in <a href="/wiki/1968_Formula_One_season" title="1968 Formula One season">1968</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Rodríguez proved himself excellent in the wet at Zandvoort and <a href="/wiki/Rouen-Les-Essarts" title="Rouen-Les-Essarts">Rouen</a> where he got his only <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> in F1 during the <a href="/wiki/1968_French_Grand_Prix" title="1968 French Grand Prix">French GP</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Lack of power meant he had to settle for second behind <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> in <a href="/wiki/1968_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1968 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian GP</a> at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Spa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/BRM_P133" title="BRM P133">BRM P133</a> faded through the year from lack of testing time after the death of <a href="/wiki/Mike_Spence" title="Mike Spence">Mike Spence</a>, who team's owners favoured.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Nevertheless, Rodríguez led the <a href="/wiki/1968_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1968 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> from <a href="/wiki/Chris_Amon" title="Chris Amon">Chris Amon</a> for 28 laps until he made a mistake and spun off.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> At the end of the year, despite Rodríguez's good performances, BRM team manager Sir <a href="/wiki/Louis_Stanley" title="Louis Stanley">Louis Stanley</a> released Rodríguez to the <a href="/wiki/Reg_Parnell" title="Reg Parnell">Parnell</a> BRM privateer team for.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Reg_Parnell_Racing" title="Reg Parnell Racing">Reg Parnell Racing</a> BRMs proved to have hopeless engines, and after <a href="/wiki/1969_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1969 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Rodríguez left and signed for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> for the remainder of the <a href="/wiki/1969_Formula_One_season" title="1969 Formula One season">1969</a> Grand Prix and sports car series.</p>
<p>Reentering F1 in the <a href="/wiki/1969_British_Grand_Prix" title="1969 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Rodríguez matched teammate Amon's pace in practice and led Amon by a whisker in the race. The uncompetitive <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312" title="Ferrari 312">312s</a> ran midfield until Rodríguez's car broke and Amon's engine blew for the second race in a row. Given the hopelessness of the 312 V12, the frustration of his drivers, and the slow progress with getting the new flat-12 F1 car ready, <a href="/wiki/Enzo_Ferrari" title="Enzo Ferrari">Enzo Ferrari</a> would rather have run two Italian drivers for the rest of the season, but the Brambilla brothers, <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Brambilla" title="Vittorio Brambilla">Vittorio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ernesto_Brambilla" title="Ernesto Brambilla">Ernesto</a>, proved too slow. So, Ferrari ran Rodríguez in the last four races of the season, in NART American racing colours for the North American races, but still, effectively, as a Ferrari works team. In the underpowered car, Rodríguez managed a fourth in 1968;<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> sixth in 1964,<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> 1967<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> and 1970;<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-48">[48]</a></sup> and seventh in 1965<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> and 1969;<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-48">[48]</a></sup> places in his six home races in Mexico, but Ferrari didn't offer him a ride for 1970.</p>
<p>BRM only offered him a ride in <a href="/wiki/1970_Formula_One_season" title="1970 Formula One season">1970</a> after <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> decided to leave to set up his own team at the last minute. For most of 1970, Stanley clearly favoured <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Oliver" title="Jackie Oliver">Jackie Oliver</a> as number one driver, perhaps partly in response to Stewart's opinion of Rodríguez and possibly because of his "old-boys' club" of Englishmen at the team.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> At <a href="/wiki/1970_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1970 Belgian Grand Prix">Spa</a>, Rodríguez won with his <a href="/wiki/BRM_P153" title="BRM P153">BRM P153</a> over the new <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> of Chris Amon by just 1.1 seconds and with an average speed of 149.94 <a href="/wiki/Miles_per_hour" title="Miles per hour">mph</a> (241.31 <a href="/wiki/Km/h" class="mw-redirect" title="Km/h">km/h</a>), then the highest average speed in the history of F1,<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Beltoise" title="Jean-Pierre Beltoise">Jean-Pierre Beltoise</a> got the third place in Matra.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>The power of the V12 engines was particularly suited to the fast circuits with few really slow corners, such as Spa, <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale Monza">Monza</a>, and to a degree <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands</a> and <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a>, and that was usually the case with the BRM, <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Weslake" title="Weslake">Weslake</a> engined cars. A strong drive at <a href="/wiki/1970_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1970 Canadian Grand Prix">St Jovite</a> saw him finish 4th. Only the need to pit in the last laps for fuel robbed him of a victory at <a href="/wiki/1970_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1970 United States Grand Prix">Watkins Glen</a>, the highest paying event of the year at the time, <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>50,000.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (December 2012)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The winner was <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a>, who got the first victory of his career in F1.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>After many years racing for Ferrari in the <a href="/wiki/World_Championship_of_Makes" class="mw-redirect" title="World Championship of Makes">World Championship of Makes</a> for sports cars, he signed for JW-Gulf-<a href="/wiki/Porsche_in_motorsport" title="Porsche in motorsport">Porsche</a> in <a href="/wiki/1970_World_Sportscar_Championship_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1970 World Sportscar Championship season">1970</a>. He became <a href="/wiki/1971_World_Sportscar_Championship_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1971 World Sportscar Championship season">two-time</a><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> world champion driver in the fearsome <a href="/wiki/Porsche_917" title="Porsche 917">Porsche 917</a> together with his co-driver <a href="/wiki/Leo_Kinnunen" title="Leo Kinnunen">Leo Kinnunen</a> (the sportscars series was run by teams in shifts).<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodríguez developed into one of the sport's great all-rounders, racing <a href="/wiki/Can-Am_Challenge_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Can-Am Challenge Cup">CanAm</a>, <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rallying" title="Rallying">rallies</a> and even becoming North American <a href="/wiki/Ice_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Ice Racing">Ice Racing</a> champion in 1970, invited by the Alaska Sports Car Club from <a href="/wiki/Anchorage" class="mw-redirect" title="Anchorage">Anchorage</a>, the race was in <a href="/wiki/Sand_Lake_(Anchorage)" title="Sand Lake (Anchorage)">Sand Lake</a>.</p>
<p>Rodríguez debuted in NASCAR at <a href="/wiki/Trenton_Speedway" title="Trenton Speedway">Trenton Speedway</a> in 1959, finishing 6th. At the 1963 <a href="/wiki/Firecracker_400" class="mw-redirect" title="Firecracker 400">Firecracker 400</a> he qualified 9th but retired after an engine failure. The Mexican finished 5th in the 1965 <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola_600" title="Coca-Cola 600">World 600</a>, his best result. At the <a href="/wiki/1971_Daytona_500" title="1971 Daytona 500">1971 Daytona 500</a> he finished 13th. His last NASCAR race was <a href="/wiki/Miller_High_Life_500_(Ontario)" class="mw-redirect" title="Miller High Life 500 (Ontario)">Miller High Life 500</a>, where he retired early with electrical issues<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodríguez drove a Ferrari 312 P Coupé in the CanAm round of Bridgehampton in 1969, finishing 5th. In 1970 he finished 3rd at Riverside and 5th at <a href="/wiki/Laguna_Seca_Raceway" class="mw-redirect" title="Laguna Seca Raceway">Laguna Seca Raceway</a> with a factory BRM P154.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/1971_Formula_One_season" title="1971 Formula One season">1971 Formula One season</a> could have seen him as a championship contender, with a <a href="/wiki/BRM_P160" title="BRM P160">BRM P160</a> being prepared by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Southgate" title="Tony Southgate">Tony Southgate</a>, and for once BRM had consistently good engines. BRM, however, was overextended, trying to run three, and later four, cars. Rodríguez challenged <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a> magnificently in the rain during the <a href="/wiki/1971_Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="1971 Dutch Grand Prix">Dutch Grand Prix</a>, and only just failed to win.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rodríguez was killed in an <a href="/wiki/Interserie" title="Interserie">Interserie</a> sports car race at <a href="/wiki/Norisring" title="Norisring">Norisring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, West Germany, on 11 July 1971. While he was driving for the lead, a slower car driven by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurt_Hild&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kurt Hild (page does not exist)">Kurt Hild</a> edged him into the wall and his prototype burst into flames. He died shortly after he was extracted from the wreck.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> Rodríguez was at the wheel of a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_512" title="Ferrari 512">Ferrari 512</a>M of <a href="/wiki/Herbert_M%C3%BCller_(racing_driver)" title="Herbert Müller (racing driver)">Herbert Müller</a> Racing, his friend and teammate at the <a href="/wiki/Targa_Florio" title="Targa Florio">Targa Florio</a> in 1971.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pedro Rodríguez was considered the best driver of his era in the wet.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> Along with <a href="/wiki/Jo_Siffert" title="Jo Siffert">Jo Siffert</a>, he was considered the bravest driver in motorsport, an example of this being the two touching through the then-very narrow and very dangerous Eau Rouge corner in the rain in their 917s at the start of the <a href="/wiki/1970_1000km_of_Spa-Francorchamps" title="1970 1000km of Spa-Francorchamps">1970 1000km of Spa-Francorchamps</a>.</p>
<p>After winning the <a href="/wiki/LMP2" class="mw-redirect" title="LMP2">LMP2</a> class at the <a href="/wiki/2013_24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="2013 24 Hours of Le Mans">2013 24 Hours of Le Mans</a>, the first class victory for a Mexican driver since Rodríguez, <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Gonz%C3%A1lez_(racing_driver)" title="Ricardo González (racing driver)">Ricardo González</a> recognized Pedro Rodríguez as his hero.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Race_result_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race_result-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commemoration">Commemoration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Commemoration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The first hairpin at <a href="/wiki/Daytona_International_Speedway" title="Daytona International Speedway">Daytona International Speedway</a> (the right-hand <a href="/wiki/Hairpin_turn" title="Hairpin turn">hairpin</a>) is named the Pedro Rodríguez curve.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1973 the Mexico City race track <a href="/wiki/Magdalena_Mixhuca_Sports_City" title="Magdalena Mixhuca Sports City">Magdalena Mixuhca</a>, where F1, <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" title="Champ Car">Champ Car</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nationwide_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="Nationwide Series">NASCAR</a> and other series race was renamed for him and Ricardo: <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Hermanos_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez">Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez</a> (Autodrome Brothers Rodríguez).</p>
<p>In July 2006, a bronze plaque was placed at the site of his crash in Nuremberg, a joint effort by <i>Scuderia Rodríguez</i> (the friends and family foundation) and the city authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> The <i>Scuderia</i> keeps alive the memory of both Rodríguez brothers, serving as register for Rodríguez memorabilia and cars, and certifying them. Its Secretary General, Carlos Jalife, published the Rodríguez brothers' biography in December 2006, with an English translation ready for sale <sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> in United States, Canada, and England which won the Motor Press Guild Book of the Year award in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
