<p><b>Duke Albert IV of Bavaria-Munich</b> (15 December 1447, <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> – 18 March 1508, Munich), (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Albrecht IV., der Weise, Herzog von Bayern</i></span>), from 1467 Duke of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria-Munich" title="Bavaria-Munich">Bavaria-Munich</a>, from 1503 Duke of the reunited <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_IV,_Duke_of_Bavaria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Albrecht_IV_Bayern_Fenster_BNM.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Albrecht_IV_Bayern_Fenster_BNM.jpg/220px-Albrecht_IV_Bayern_Fenster_BNM.jpg" width="220" height="322" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Albrecht_IV_Bayern_Fenster_BNM.jpg/330px-Albrecht_IV_Bayern_Fenster_BNM.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Albrecht_IV_Bayern_Fenster_BNM.jpg/440px-Albrecht_IV_Bayern_Fenster_BNM.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2015" data-file-height="2950" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Albrecht_IV_Bayern_Fenster_BNM.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Glass window „Duke Albert IV. and St. John“ from Prüll abbey, today in <a href="/wiki/Bavarian_National_Museum" title="Bavarian National Museum">Bavarian National Museum</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Albert was a son of <a href="/wiki/Albert_III,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Albert III, Duke of Bavaria">Albert III, Duke of Bavaria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anna_of_Brunswick-Grubenhagen-Einbeck" title="Anna of Brunswick-Grubenhagen-Einbeck">Anna of Brunswick-Grubenhagen-Einbeck</a>. After the death of his older brother <a href="/wiki/John_IV,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="John IV, Duke of Bavaria">John IV, Duke of Bavaria</a> he gave up his spiritual career and returned from <a href="/wiki/Pavia" title="Pavia">Pavia</a> to Munich. When his brothers Christoph and Wolfgang had resigned Albert became sole duke, but a new duchy Bavaria-<a href="/wiki/Dachau" title="Dachau">Dachau</a> was created from Bavaria-Munich for his brother Duke <a href="/wiki/Sigismund_of_Bavaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Sigismund of Bavaria">Sigismund</a> in 1467. After Sigismund's death in 1501, it reverted to Bavaria-Munich.</p>
<p>The marriage of <a href="/wiki/Kunigunde_of_Austria" title="Kunigunde of Austria">Kunigunde of Austria</a> to Albert IV, was a result of intrigues and deception, but must be counted as a defeat for emperor <a href="/wiki/Frederick_III,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Frederick III, Holy Roman Emperor">Frederick III</a>. Albert illegally took control of some imperial <a href="/wiki/Fief" title="Fief">fiefs</a> and then asked to marry Kunigunde (who lived in <a href="/wiki/Innsbruck" title="Innsbruck">Innsbruck</a>, far from her father), offering to give her the fiefs as a dowry. Frederick agreed at first, but after Albert took over yet another fief, <a href="/wiki/Regensburg" title="Regensburg">Regensburg</a>, Frederick withdrew his consent. On January 2, 1487, however, before Frederick's change of heart could be communicated to his daughter, Kunigunde married Albert. A war was prevented only by intermediation by the Emperor's son, <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Maximilian I, Holy Roman Emperor">Maximilian</a>. For Albert's wedding the <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%BCnwald_castle" class="mw-redirect" title="Grünwald castle">Grünwald castle</a> was extended in 1486/87 by Jörg von Weikertshausen. Albert finally decided to return territorial acquisitions in <a href="/wiki/Swabia" title="Swabia">Swabia</a> in 1492 to avoid a war with the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Habsburg">Habsburg</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Swabian_League" title="Swabian League">Swabian League</a>. He then also had to release Regensburg which had been reunited with Bavaria in 1486 and had to renounce <a href="/wiki/Further_Austria" title="Further Austria">Further Austria</a> when <a href="/wiki/Sigismund,_Archduke_of_Austria" title="Sigismund, Archduke of Austria">Sigismund, Archduke of Austria</a> tried to make it over to Albert.</p>
<p>After the death of the last duke of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria-Landshut" title="Bavaria-Landshut">Bavaria-Landshut</a> <a href="/wiki/George_of_Bavaria" class="mw-redirect" title="George of Bavaria">George</a> in 1503, Albert managed to reunite the whole of Bavaria in a dreadful <a href="/wiki/Landshut_War_of_Succession" class="mw-redirect" title="Landshut War of Succession">war</a> against George's heirs, the <a href="/wiki/Electoral_Palatinate" title="Electoral Palatinate">Palatinate</a> line of his <a href="/wiki/Wittelsbach" class="mw-redirect" title="Wittelsbach">Wittelsbach</a> family but had to transfer the most southern districts of Bavaria-Landshut to his brother-in-law Emperor Maximilian as compensation for his support: <a href="/wiki/Kufstein" title="Kufstein">Kufstein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kitzb%C3%BChel" title="Kitzbühel">Kitzbühel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rattenberg" title="Rattenberg">Rattenberg</a> passed to Maximilian in 1506 and were united with <a href="/wiki/German_Tyrol" title="German Tyrol">Tyrol</a>. For the Palatinate branch a new duchy of <a href="/wiki/Palatinate-Neuburg" title="Palatinate-Neuburg">Palatinate-Neuburg</a> was created.</p>
<p>To avoid any future division of Bavaria, Albert decreed the everlasting succession of the firstborn prince in 1506. Nevertheless, his oldest son and successor <a href="/wiki/William_IV,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="William IV, Duke of Bavaria">William IV, Duke of Bavaria</a> had to share his power from 1516 onwards with his younger brother <a href="/wiki/Louis_X,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Louis X, Duke of Bavaria">Louis X, Duke of Bavaria</a>. After the death of Louis in 1545, the edict became effective until the end of Bavarian monarchy in 1918.</p>
<p>Albert is buried in the <a href="/wiki/Munich_Frauenkirche" title="Munich Frauenkirche">Frauenkirche</a> in Munich.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_and_children">Family and children</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_IV,_Duke_of_Bavaria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family and children">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
