<p><a href="/wiki/Vice_Admiral" class="mw-redirect" title="Vice Admiral">Vice Admiral</a> <b>Sir Francis Drake</b> (<abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;"> 1540</span>&#160;– 28 January 1596<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>) was an English <a href="/wiki/Sea_captain" title="Sea captain">sea captain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Privateer" title="Privateer">privateer</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Elizabethan_era" title="Elizabethan era">Elizabethan era</a>. Drake carried out the second <a href="/wiki/Circumnavigation" title="Circumnavigation">circumnavigation</a> of the world in a single expedition, from 1577 to 1580, and was the first to complete the voyage as captain while leading the expedition throughout the entire circumnavigation. With his incursion into the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, he claimed <a href="/wiki/New_Albion" title="New Albion">what is now California</a> for the English and inaugurated an era of conflict with the Spanish on the western coast of the Americas,<sup id="cite_ref-Wallis1984_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wallis1984-4">[4]</a></sup> an area that had previously been largely unexplored by western shipping.<sup id="cite_ref-Soto2006_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Soto2006-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England" title="Elizabeth I of England">Elizabeth I</a> awarded Drake a <a href="/wiki/Knight" title="Knight">knighthood</a> in 1581. He was second-in-command of the English fleet against the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Armada" title="Spanish Armada">Spanish Armada</a> in 1588. He died of <a href="/wiki/Dysentery" title="Dysentery">dysentery</a> in January 1596,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> after unsuccessfully attacking <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>. Drake's exploits made him a hero to the English, but his <a href="/wiki/Privateer" title="Privateer">privateering</a> led the Spanish to brand him a <a href="/wiki/Pirate" class="mw-redirect" title="Pirate">pirate</a>, known to them as <i>El Draque</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> King <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">Philip II</a> allegedly offered a reward for his capture or death of 20,000&#160;<a href="/wiki/Ducat" title="Ducat">ducats</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Cummins1997_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cummins1997-8">[8]</a></sup> about £6 million (US$8 million) in modern currency.<sup id="cite_ref-Hanna2015_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hanna2015-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Birth_and_early_years">Birth and early years</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sfdrake42.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Sfdrake42.jpg/220px-Sfdrake42.jpg" width="220" height="219" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Sfdrake42.jpg/330px-Sfdrake42.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/57/Sfdrake42.jpg 2x" data-file-width="419" data-file-height="417" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sfdrake42.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Portrait_miniature" title="Portrait miniature">Portrait miniature</a> by <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Hilliard" class="mw-redirect" title="Nicholas Hilliard">Nicholas Hilliard</a>, 1581, reverse of "Drake Jewel", inscribed <i>Aetatis suae 42, An(n)o D(omi)ni 1581</i> ("42 years of his age, 1581 AD")</div>
</div>

<p>Francis Drake was born in <a href="/wiki/Tavistock,_Devon" class="mw-redirect" title="Tavistock, Devon">Tavistock, Devon</a>, England. Although his birth date is not formally recorded, it is known that he was born while the <a href="/wiki/Six_Articles" class="mw-redirect" title="Six Articles">Six Articles</a> were in force. His birth date is estimated from contemporary sources such as: "Drake was two and twenty when he obtained the command of the <i>Judith</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> (1566). This would date his birth to 1544. A date of c.1540 is suggested from two portraits: one a <a href="/wiki/Portrait_miniature" title="Portrait miniature">miniature</a> painted by <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Hilliard" class="mw-redirect" title="Nicholas Hilliard">Nicholas Hilliard</a> in 1581 when he was allegedly 42, the other painted in 1594 when he was said to be 53.<sup id="cite_ref-DNB_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DNB-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was the eldest of the twelve sons<sup id="cite_ref-ODNB04_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODNB04-12">[12]</a></sup> of Edmund Drake (1518–1585), a <a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> farmer, and his wife Mary Mylwaye. The first son was alleged to have been named after his <a href="/wiki/Godparent" title="Godparent">godfather</a> <a href="/wiki/Francis_Russell,_2nd_Earl_of_Bedford" title="Francis Russell, 2nd Earl of Bedford">Francis Russell, 2nd Earl of Bedford</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tudorplace_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tudorplace-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Froude_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Froude-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Because of religious persecution during the <a href="/wiki/Prayer_Book_Rebellion" title="Prayer Book Rebellion">Prayer Book Rebellion</a> in 1549, the Drake family fled from <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devonshire</a> into <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a>. There Drake's father obtained an appointment to minister the men in the King's Navy. He was ordained deacon and was made vicar of <a href="/wiki/Upnor" title="Upnor">Upnor</a> Church on the <a href="/wiki/Medway" title="Medway">Medway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Southey_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Southey-15">[15]</a></sup> Drake's father apprenticed him to his neighbour, the master of a <a href="/wiki/Barque" title="Barque">barque</a> used for coastal trade transporting merchandise to France.<sup id="cite_ref-Southey_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Southey-15">[15]</a></sup> The ship's master was so satisfied with the young Drake's conduct that, being unmarried and childless at his death, he bequeathed the barque to Drake.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (August 2017)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> <sup id="cite_ref-Southey_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Southey-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_family">Marriage and family</span></h2>
<p>Francis Drake married Mary Newman in 1569. She died 12 years later, in 1581. In 1585, Drake married Elizabeth Sydenham—born circa 1562, the only child of Sir George Sydenham, of <a href="/wiki/Combe_Sydenham" title="Combe Sydenham">Combe Sydenham</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> who was the <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_Somerset" class="mw-redirect" title="High Sheriff of Somerset">High Sheriff of Somerset</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> After Drake's death, the widow Elizabeth eventually married Sir <a href="/wiki/William_Courtenay_(died_1630)" title="William Courtenay (died 1630)">William Courtenay</a> of Powderham.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_at_sea">Career at sea</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Drake_Juwel.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Drake_Juwel.JPG/220px-Drake_Juwel.JPG" width="220" height="392" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Drake_Juwel.JPG/330px-Drake_Juwel.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Drake_Juwel.JPG/440px-Drake_Juwel.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1960" data-file-height="3488" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Drake_Juwel.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Drake Jewel, on loan at the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_and_Albert_Museum" title="Victoria and Albert Museum">Victoria and Albert Museum</a>, London</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1563, Drake, aged 23, made his first voyage to the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>, sailing with his second cousin, Sir <a href="/wiki/John_Hawkins_(naval_commander)" title="John Hawkins (naval commander)">John Hawkins</a>, on one of a fleet of ships owned by his relatives, the Hawkins family of <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>. He made three voyages with this fleet, attacking <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> towns and ships on the coast of <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> They then sailed to the <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Americas</a> and sold the captured cargoes of slaves to Spanish <a href="/wiki/Plantations" class="mw-redirect" title="Plantations">plantations</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Sauer1975_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sauer1975-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/John_Hawkins_(naval_commander)" title="John Hawkins (naval commander)">John Hawkins</a> is considered to have been the first English <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_slave_trade" title="Atlantic slave trade">slave-trader</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Hawkins only made only three such expeditions, the first in 1563, second in 1564 and the third expedition ending in the ill-fated 1568 <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_San_Juan_de_Ul%C3%BAa_(1568)" title="Battle of San Juan de Ulúa (1568)">incident at San Juan de Ulúa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1568 Drake was on his third expedition with the Hawkins fleet when, whilst negotiating to resupply and repair at a Spanish port in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, the fleet was attacked by Spanish warships, with all but two of the English ships lost. He escaped along with John Hawkins, surviving the attack by swimming.</p>
<p>Drakes hostility towards the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> is said to have started with this incident. Following the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_San_Juan_de_Ul%C3%BAa_(1568)" title="Battle of San Juan de Ulúa (1568)">defeat at San Juan de Ulúa</a>, Drake vowed revenge.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> He made two voyages to the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a>, in 1570 and 1571, of which little is known.</p>
<p>In 1572, he embarked on his first major independent enterprise. He planned an attack on the <a href="/wiki/Isthmus_of_Panama" title="Isthmus of Panama">Isthmus of Panama</a>, known to the Spanish as <a href="/wiki/Tierra_Firma" class="mw-redirect" title="Tierra Firma">Tierra Firme</a> and the English as the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Main" title="Spanish Main">Spanish Main</a>. This was the point at which the silver and gold treasure of <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> had to be landed and sent overland to the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>, where galleons from <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> would pick it up at the town of <a href="/wiki/Nombre_de_Dios,_Col%C3%B3n" title="Nombre de Dios, Colón">Nombre de Dios</a>. Drake left Plymouth on 24 May 1572, with a crew of 73 men in two small vessels, the <i>Pascha</i> (70 tons) and the <i>Swan</i> (25 tons), to capture Nombre de Dios.</p>
<p>His first raid was late in July 1572. Drake and his men captured the town and its treasure. When his men noticed that Drake was bleeding profusely from a wound, they insisted on withdrawing to save his life and left the treasure. Drake stayed in the area for almost a year, raiding Spanish shipping and attempting to capture a treasure shipment.</p>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:320px; padding: 10px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 100%; background-color: #ffe4b5;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: center;">
<div>The people of quality dislike him for having risen so high from such a lowely family; the rest say he is the main cause of wars.</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: center;"><small>— Gonzalo González del Castillo, letter to <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">King Philip II</a>, 1592</small><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>The most celebrated of Drake's adventures along the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Main" title="Spanish Main">Spanish Main</a> was his capture of the Spanish Silver Train at <a href="/wiki/Nombre_de_Dios,_Col%C3%B3n" title="Nombre de Dios, Colón">Nombre de Dios</a> in March 1573. He raided the waters around <a href="/wiki/Dari%C3%A9n,_Panama" class="mw-redirect" title="Darién, Panama">Darien</a> (in modern <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a>) with a crew including many French privateers including <a href="/wiki/Guillaume_Le_Testu" title="Guillaume Le Testu">Guillaume Le Testu</a>, a French buccaneer, and <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">African slaves (Maroons)</a> who had escaped the Spanish. Drake and tracked the Silver Train to the nearby port of Nombre de Dios. After their an attack on the richly laden mule train. Drake and his party found that they had captured around 20 tons of silver and gold. They buried much of the treasure, as it was too much for their party to carry, and made off with a fortune in gold.<sup id="cite_ref-Marley2008_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marley2008-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Konstam2011_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Konstam2011-28">[28]</a></sup> (An account of this may have given rise to subsequent stories of pirates and buried treasure.) Wounded, Le Testu was captured and later beheaded. The small band of adventurers dragged as much gold and silver as they could carry back across some 18 miles of jungle-covered mountains to where they had left the raiding boats. When they got to the coast, the boats were gone. Drake and his men, downhearted, exhausted and hungry, had nowhere to go and the Spanish were not far behind.</p>
<p>At this point Drake rallied his men, buried the treasure on the beach, and built a raft to sail with two volunteers ten miles along the surf-lashed coast to where they had left the flagship. When Drake finally reached its deck, his men were alarmed at his bedraggled appearance. Fearing the worst, they asked him how the raid had gone. Drake could not resist a joke and teased them by looking downhearted. Then he laughed, pulled a necklace of Spanish gold from around his neck and said "Our voyage is made, lads!" By 9 August 1573, he had returned to Plymouth.</p>
<p>It was during this expedition that he climbed a high tree in the central mountains of the <a href="/wiki/Isthmus_of_Panama" title="Isthmus of Panama">Isthmus of Panama</a> and thus became the first Englishman to see the Pacific Ocean. He remarked as he saw it that he hoped one day an Englishman would be able to sail it—which he would do years later as part of his circumnavigation of the world.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Drake returned to Plymouth after the raids, the government signed a temporary truce with King Philip II of Spain and so was unable to acknowledge Drake's accomplishment officially. Drake was considered a hero in England and a pirate in Spain for his raids.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span id="Circumnavigation_of_the_earth_(1577–1580)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Circumnavigation_of_the_earth_.281577.E2.80.931580.29">Circumnavigation of the earth (1577–1580)</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:502px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DRAKE_1577-1580.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/DRAKE_1577-1580.png/500px-DRAKE_1577-1580.png" width="500" height="209" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/DRAKE_1577-1580.png/750px-DRAKE_1577-1580.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/DRAKE_1577-1580.png/1000px-DRAKE_1577-1580.png 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="536" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:DRAKE_1577-1580.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A map of Drake's route around the world. The northern limit of Drake's exploration of the Pacific coast of North America is still in dispute. <a href="/wiki/Drake%27s_Bay" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake's Bay">Drake's Bay</a> is south of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Mendocino" title="Cape Mendocino">Cape Mendocino</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>With the success of the Panama isthmus raid, in 1577 <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England" title="Elizabeth I of England">Elizabeth I of England</a> sent Drake to start an expedition against the Spanish along the <a href="/wiki/Pacific" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific">Pacific</a> coast of the Americas. Drake used the plans that Sir <a href="/wiki/Richard_Grenville" title="Richard Grenville">Richard Grenville</a> had received the patent for in 1574 from Elizabeth, which was rescinded a year later after protests from Philip of Spain. He set out from Plymouth on 15 November 1577, but bad weather threatened him and his fleet. They were forced to take refuge in <a href="/wiki/Falmouth,_Cornwall" title="Falmouth, Cornwall">Falmouth, Cornwall</a>, from where they returned to Plymouth for repair.</p>
<p>After this major setback, Drake set sail again on 13 December aboard <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Hind" title="Golden Hind">Pelican</a></i> with four other ships and 164 men. He soon added a sixth ship, <i>Mary</i> (formerly <i>Santa Maria</i>), a Portuguese merchant ship that had been captured off the coast of Africa near the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Cape_Verde" title="Portuguese Cape Verde">Cape Verde Islands</a>. He also added its captain, Nuno da Silva, a man with considerable experience navigating in South American waters.</p>
<p>Drake's fleet suffered great attrition; he scuttled both <i>Christopher</i> and the <a href="/wiki/Flyboat" title="Flyboat">flyboat</a> <i>Swan</i> due to loss of men on the Atlantic crossing. He made landfall at the gloomy bay of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_San_Juli%C3%A1n" title="Puerto San Julián">San Julian</a>, in what is now <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. Ferdinand Magellan had called here half a century earlier, where he put to death some mutineers. Drake's men saw weathered and bleached skeletons on the grim Spanish <a href="/wiki/Gibbet" class="mw-redirect" title="Gibbet">gibbets</a>. Following Magellan's example, Drake tried and executed his own "mutineer" <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Doughty_(explorer)" title="Thomas Doughty (explorer)">Thomas Doughty</a>. The crew discovered that <i>Mary</i> had rotting timbers, so they burned the ship. Drake decided to remain the winter in San Julian before attempting the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan" title="Strait of Magellan">Strait of Magellan</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Execution_of_Thomas_Doughty">Execution of Thomas Doughty</span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Doughty_(explorer)" title="Thomas Doughty (explorer)">Thomas Doughty (explorer)</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DrakeStatueTavistock.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/DrakeStatueTavistock.jpg/170px-DrakeStatueTavistock.jpg" width="170" height="269" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/DrakeStatueTavistock.jpg/255px-DrakeStatueTavistock.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/DrakeStatueTavistock.jpg/340px-DrakeStatueTavistock.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1016" data-file-height="1606" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:DrakeStatueTavistock.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Bronze statue in Tavistock, in the parish of which he was born, by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Boehm" class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph Boehm">Joseph Boehm</a>, 1883.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On his voyage to interfere with Spanish treasure fleets, Drake had several quarrels with his co-commander Thomas Doughty and on 3 June 1578, accused him of witchcraft and charged him with <a href="/wiki/Mutiny" title="Mutiny">mutiny</a> and <a href="/wiki/Treason" title="Treason">treason</a> in a shipboard trial.<sup id="cite_ref-Coote_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coote-31">[31]</a></sup> Drake claimed to have a (never presented) commission from the Queen to carry out such acts and denied Doughty a trial in England. The main pieces of evidence against Doughty were the testimony of the ship's carpenter, Edward Bright, who after the trial was promoted to master of the ship <i>Marigold</i>, and Doughty's admission of telling <a href="/wiki/William_Cecil,_1st_Baron_Burghley" title="William Cecil, 1st Baron Burghley">Lord Burghley</a>, a vocal opponent of agitating the Spanish, of the intent of the voyage. Drake consented to his request of <a href="/wiki/Eucharist" title="Eucharist">Communion</a> and dined with him, of which <a href="/wiki/Francis_Fletcher_(clergyman)" title="Francis Fletcher (clergyman)">Francis Fletcher</a> had this strange account:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>And after this holy repast, they dined also at the same table together, as cheerfully, in sobriety, as ever in their lives they had done aforetime, each cheering up the other, and taking their leave, by drinking each to other, as if some journey only had been in hand.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Drake had Thomas Doughty beheaded on 2 July 1578. When the ship's chaplain Francis Fletcher in a sermon suggested that the woes of the voyage in January 1580 were connected to the unjust demise of Doughty, Drake chained the clergyman to a hatch cover and pronounced him excommunicated.</p>
<h3><span id="Entering_the_Pacific_(1578)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Entering_the_Pacific_.281578.29">Entering the Pacific (1578)</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sfec_goldenhind02crop.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Sfec_goldenhind02crop.jpg/220px-Sfec_goldenhind02crop.jpg" width="220" height="340" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Sfec_goldenhind02crop.jpg/330px-Sfec_goldenhind02crop.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Sfec_goldenhind02crop.jpg/440px-Sfec_goldenhind02crop.jpg 2x" data-file-width="664" data-file-height="1027" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sfec_goldenhind02crop.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A replica of the <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Hind" title="Golden Hind">Golden Hind</a></i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The three remaining ships of his convoy departed for the Magellan Strait at the southern tip of South America. A few weeks later (September 1578) Drake made it to the Pacific, but violent storms destroyed one of the three ships, the <i>Marigold</i> (captained by John Thomas) in the strait and caused another, the <i>Elizabeth</i> captained by <a href="/wiki/John_Wynter" title="John Wynter">John Wynter</a>, to return to England, leaving only the <i>Pelican</i>. After this passage, the <i>Pelican</i> was pushed south and discovered an island that Drake called <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Island_(Cape_Horn)" title="Elizabeth Island (Cape Horn)">Elizabeth Island</a>. Drake, like navigators before him, probably reached a latitude of 55°S (according to astronomical data quoted in <a href="/wiki/Richard_Hakluyt" title="Richard Hakluyt">Hakluyt</a>'s <i>The Principall Navigations, Voiages and Discoveries of the English Nation</i> of 1589) along the Chilean coast.<sup id="cite_ref-Wagner_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wagner-32">[32]</a></sup> In the Magellan Strait Francis and his men engaged in skirmish with local indigenous people, becoming the first Europeans to kill indigenous peoples in southern Patagonia.<sup id="cite_ref-Mateo_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mateo-33">[33]</a></sup> During the stay in the strait, crew members discovered that an infusion made of the bark of <i><a href="/wiki/Drimys_winteri" title="Drimys winteri">Drimys winteri</a></i> could be used as remedy against <a href="/wiki/Scurvy" title="Scurvy">scurvy</a>. Captain Wynter ordered the collection of great amounts of bark – hence the scientific name.<sup id="cite_ref-Mateo_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mateo-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite popular lore, it seems unlikely that Drake reached <a href="/wiki/Cape_Horn" title="Cape Horn">Cape Horn</a> or the eponymous <a href="/wiki/Drake_Passage" title="Drake Passage">Drake Passage</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Wagner_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wagner-32">[32]</a></sup> because his descriptions do not fit the first and his shipmates denied having seen an open sea. The first report of his discovery of an open channel south of <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego" title="Tierra del Fuego">Tierra del Fuego</a> was written after the 1618 publication of the voyage of <a href="/wiki/Willem_Schouten" title="Willem Schouten">Willem Schouten</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacob_le_Maire" class="mw-redirect" title="Jacob le Maire">Jacob le Maire</a> around Cape Horn in 1616.<sup id="cite_ref-Kelsey_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kelsey-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Drake pushed onwards in his lone flagship, now renamed the <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Hind" title="Golden Hind">Golden Hind</a></i> in honour of Sir <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Hatton" title="Christopher Hatton">Christopher Hatton</a> (after his <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a>). The <i>Golden Hind</i> sailed north along the <a href="/wiki/Pacific" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific">Pacific</a> coast of South America, attacking Spanish ports and pillaging towns. Some Spanish ships were captured, and Drake used their more accurate charts. Before reaching the coast of <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, Drake visited <a href="/wiki/Mocha_Island" title="Mocha Island">Mocha Island</a>, where he was seriously injured by hostile <a href="/wiki/Mapuche" title="Mapuche">Mapuche</a>. Later he sacked the port of <a href="/wiki/Valpara%C3%ADso" title="Valparaíso">Valparaíso</a> further north in <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, where he also captured a ship full of <a href="/wiki/Chilean_wine" title="Chilean wine">Chilean wine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Orich_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Orich-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Capture_of_Spanish_treasure_ships">Capture of Spanish treasure ships</span></h3>
<p>Near <a href="/wiki/Lima" title="Lima">Lima</a>, Drake captured a Spanish ship laden with 25,000&#160;<a href="/wiki/Spanish_dollar" title="Spanish dollar">pesos</a> of Peruvian gold, amounting in value to 37,000&#160;<a href="/wiki/Ducat" title="Ducat">ducats</a> of Spanish money (about £7m by modern standards). Drake also discovered news of another ship, <i><a href="/wiki/Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_la_Concepci%C3%B3n" title="Nuestra Señora de la Concepción">Nuestra Señora de la Concepción</a></i>, which was sailing west towards <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a>. It would come to be called the <i><a href="/wiki/Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_la_Concepci%C3%B3n" title="Nuestra Señora de la Concepción">Cacafuego</a></i>. Drake gave chase and eventually captured the treasure ship, which proved his most profitable capture.</p>
<p>Aboard <i>Nuestra Señora de la Concepción</i>, Drake found 80&#160;lb (36&#160;kg) of gold, a golden <a href="/wiki/Crucifix" title="Crucifix">crucifix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gemstone" title="Gemstone">jewels</a>, 13&#160;chests full of royals of plate and 26&#160;tons of silver. Drake was naturally pleased at his good luck in capturing the galleon, and he showed it by dining with the captured ship's officers and gentleman passengers. He offloaded his captives a short time later, and gave each one gifts appropriate to their rank, as well as a letter of safe conduct.</p>
<h3><span id="Coast_of_California:_Nova_Albion_(1579)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Coast_of_California:_Nova_Albion_.281579.29">Coast of California: Nova Albion (1579)</span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/New_Albion" title="New Albion">New Albion</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Drake_CA_1590.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Drake_CA_1590.jpg/250px-Drake_CA_1590.jpg" width="250" height="192" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Drake_CA_1590.jpg/375px-Drake_CA_1590.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2b/Drake_CA_1590.jpg 2x" data-file-width="486" data-file-height="373" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Drake_CA_1590.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Drake's landing in California, engraving published 1590 by <a href="/wiki/Theodor_de_Bry" title="Theodor de Bry">Theodor de Bry</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>After looting the Cacafuego, Drake turned north, hoping to meet another Spanish treasure ship coming south on its return from <a href="/wiki/Manila_galleon" title="Manila galleon">Manila to Acapulco</a>. Although he failed to find a treasure ship, Drake reputedly sailed as far north as the 38th parallel, landing on the coast of California on 17 June 1579. He found a good port, landed, repaired and restocked his vessels, then stayed for a time, keeping friendly relations with the <a href="/wiki/Coast_Miwok" title="Coast Miwok">Coast Miwok</a> natives. He claimed the land in the name of the <a href="/wiki/Trinity" title="Trinity">Holy Trinity</a> for the English Crown, called <i><a href="/wiki/Nova_Albion" class="mw-redirect" title="Nova Albion">Nova Albion</a></i>—<a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "New Britain". Assertions that he left some of his men behind as an embryo "colony" are founded on the reduced number who were with him in the <a href="/wiki/Maluku_Islands" title="Maluku Islands">Moluccas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>The precise location of the port was carefully guarded to keep it secret from the Spaniards, and several of Drake's maps may have been altered to this end. All first-hand records from the voyage, including logs, paintings and charts, were lost when <a href="/wiki/Whitehall_Palace" class="mw-redirect" title="Whitehall Palace">Whitehall Palace</a> burned in 1698. A bronze plaque inscribed with Drake's claim to the new lands – <a href="/wiki/Drake%27s_Plate_of_Brass" title="Drake's Plate of Brass">Drake's Plate of Brass</a> – fitting the description in his account, was discovered in <a href="/wiki/Marin_County" class="mw-redirect" title="Marin County">Marin County</a>, California but was later declared a hoax. Now a <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Landmark" title="National Historic Landmark">National Historic Landmark</a>, the officially recognised location<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> of Drake's <a href="/wiki/New_Albion" title="New Albion">New Albion</a> is <a href="/wiki/Drakes_Bay" title="Drakes Bay">Drakes Bay</a>, California.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Across_the_Pacific_and_around_Africa">Across the Pacific and around Africa</span></h3>
<p>Drake left the Pacific coast, heading southwest to catch the winds that would carry his ship across the Pacific, and a few months later reached the <a href="/wiki/Maluku_Islands" title="Maluku Islands">Moluccas</a>, a group of islands in the western Pacific, in eastern modern-day <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>. While there, <i>Golden Hind</i> became caught on a reef and was almost lost. After the sailors waited three days for convenient tides and had dumped cargo, they freed the barque. Befriending a sultan king of the Moluccas, Drake and his men became involved in some intrigues with the Portuguese there. He made multiple stops on his way toward the tip of Africa, eventually rounded the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a>, and reached <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone" title="Sierra Leone">Sierra Leone</a> by 22 July 1580.</p>
<h3><span id="Return_to_Plymouth_(1580)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Plymouth_.281580.29">Return to Plymouth (1580)</span></h3>
<p>On 26 September, <i>Golden Hind</i> sailed into Plymouth with Drake and 59&#160;remaining crew aboard, along with a rich cargo of spices and captured Spanish treasures. The Queen's half-share of the cargo surpassed the rest of the crown's income for that entire year. Drake was hailed as the first Englishman to circumnavigate the Earth (and the second such voyage arriving with at least one ship intact, after <a href="/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano" title="Juan Sebastián Elcano">Elcano</a>'s in 1520).<sup id="cite_ref-The_Drake_Jewel_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Drake_Jewel-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Queen declared that all written accounts of Drake's voyages were to become the Queen's secrets of the Realm, and Drake and the other participants of his voyages on the pain of death sworn to their secrecy; she intended to keep Drake's activities away from the eyes of rival Spain. Drake presented the Queen with a jewel token commemorating the circumnavigation. Taken as a prize off the Pacific coast of Mexico, it was made of enamelled gold and bore an African diamond and a ship with an ebony hull.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Drake_Jewel_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Drake_Jewel-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>For her part, the Queen gave Drake a jewel with her portrait, an unusual gift to bestow upon a commoner, and one that Drake sported proudly in his 1591 portrait by <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Gheeraerts_the_Younger" title="Marcus Gheeraerts the Younger">Marcus Gheeraerts</a> now at the <a href="/wiki/National_Maritime_Museum" title="National Maritime Museum">National Maritime Museum</a>, Greenwich. On one side is a state portrait of Elizabeth by the miniaturist <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Hilliard" class="mw-redirect" title="Nicholas Hilliard">Nicholas Hilliard</a>, on the other a <a href="/wiki/Sardonyx" class="mw-redirect" title="Sardonyx">sardonyx</a> cameo of double portrait busts, a regal woman and an African male. The "Drake Jewel", as it is known today, is a rare documented survivor among sixteenth-century jewels; it is conserved at the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_and_Albert_Museum" title="Victoria and Albert Museum">Victoria and Albert Museum</a>, London.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Drake_Jewel_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Drake_Jewel-38">[38]</a></sup></p>

Arms of Drake of Ash: <i>Argent, a <a href="/wiki/Wyvern" title="Wyvern">wyvern</a> wings displayed and tail nowed gules</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Vivian_1895.2C_p.292_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vivian_1895.2C_p.292-44">[44]</a></sup> The Drake family of Crowndale and Buckland Abbey used the same arms but the tail of the wyvern is not nowed (knotted)<sup id="cite_ref-Vivian_p.299_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vivian_p.299-45">[45]</a></sup></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>After receiving his knighthood Drake unilaterally adopted the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">armorials</a> of the ancient Devon family of Drake of <a href="/wiki/Ash,_Musbury" title="Ash, Musbury">Ash</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Musbury" title="Musbury">Musbury</a>, to whom he claimed a distant but unspecified kinship. These arms were: <i>Argent, a <a href="/wiki/Wyvern" title="Wyvern">wyvern</a> wings displayed and tail nowed gules</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-Vivian_1895.2C_p.292_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vivian_1895.2C_p.292-44">[44]</a></sup> and the crest, <i>a dexter arm Proper grasping a battle axe Sable, headed Argent</i>. The head of that family, also a distinguished sailor, Sir <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Drake" title="Bernard Drake">Bernard Drake</a> (d.1586), angrily refuted Sir Francis's claimed kinship and his right to bear his family's arms. That dispute led to "a box in the ear" being given to Sir Francis by Sir Bernard at court, as recorded by <a href="/wiki/John_Prince_(Totnes)" class="mw-redirect" title="John Prince (Totnes)">John Prince</a> in his "Worthies of Devon" (1697).<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Queen Elizabeth, to assuage matters, awarded Sir Francis his own coat of arms, <a href="/wiki/Blazon" title="Blazon">blazoned</a> as follows:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p><i>Sable a fess wavy between two pole-stars [Arctic and Antarctic] argent;</i> and for his crest, <i>a ship on a globe under ruff, held by a cable with a hand out of the clouds;</i> over it this motto, <i>Auxilio Divino;</i> underneath, <i>Sic Parvis Magna;</i> in the rigging whereof is hung up by the heels <i>a wivern, gules,</i> which was the arms of Sir Bernard Drake.<sup id="cite_ref-Campbell1828_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campbell1828-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The motto, <i>Sic Parvis Magna</i>, translated literally, is: "Thus great things from small things (come)". The hand out of the clouds, labelled <i>Auxilio Divino</i>, means "With Divine Help". The full achievement is depicted in the form of a large coloured plaster overmantel in the Lifetimes Gallery at Buckland Abbey<sup id="cite_ref-NationalTrust_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NationalTrust-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nevertheless, Drake continued to <a href="/wiki/Quartering_(heraldry)" title="Quartering (heraldry)">quarter</a> his new arms with the wyvern gules.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> The arms adopted by his nephew <a href="/wiki/Sir_Francis_Drake,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir Francis Drake, 1st Baronet">Sir Francis Drake, 1st Baronet</a> (1588–1637) of <a href="/wiki/Buckland_Abbey" title="Buckland Abbey">Buckland</a> were the arms of Drake of Ash, but the wyvern without a "nowed" (knotted) tail.<sup id="cite_ref-Vivian_p.299_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vivian_p.299-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span></h2>
<p>Drake was politically astute, and although known for his private and military endeavours, he was an influential figure in politics during the time he spent in Britain. Often abroad, there is little evidence to suggest he was active in Westminster, despite being a member of parliament on three occasions.</p>
<p>After returning from his voyage of circumnavigation, Drake became the Mayor of <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>, in September 1581<sup id="cite_ref-ODNB04_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODNB04-12">[12]</a></sup>. He became a member of parliament during a session of the 4th Parliament of <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I" class="mw-redirect" title="Elizabeth I">Elizabeth I</a><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup>, on 16 Jan 1581, for the constituency of <a href="/wiki/Camelford" title="Camelford">Camelford</a>, he did not actively participate at this point, and on 17 Feb 1581 he was granted leave of absence ‘for certain his necessary business in the service of her Majesty'.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>Drake became a member of parliament again in 1584 for <a href="/wiki/Bossiney_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Bossiney (UK Parliament constituency)">Bossiney</a><sup id="cite_ref-ODNB04_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODNB04-12">[12]</a></sup> on the forming of the 5th Parliament of Elizabeth I<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup>. He served the duration of the parliament and was active in issues regarding the navy, fishing, early American colonisation and issues related chiefly to <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>. He spent the time covered by the next two parliamentary terms engaged in other duties and an expedition to <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> He became a member of parliament for <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Plymouth (UK Parliament constituency)">Plymouth</a> in 1593.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> He was active in issues of interest to Plymouth as a whole, but also to emphasize defence against the Spanish.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Purchase_of_Buckland_Abbey">Purchase of Buckland Abbey</span></h2>
<p>In 1580 Drake purchased <a href="/wiki/Buckland_Abbey" title="Buckland Abbey">Buckland Abbey</a>, a large manor house near Yelverton in Devon, via intermediaries from Sir Richard Greynvile. He lived there for fifteen years, until his final voyage, and it remained in his family for several generations. Buckland Abbey is now in the care of the <a href="/wiki/National_Trust_for_Places_of_Historic_Interest_or_Natural_Beauty" title="National Trust for Places of Historic Interest or Natural Beauty">National Trust</a> and a number of mementos of his life are displayed there.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Great_Expedition">Great Expedition</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Boazio-Sir_Francis_Drakes_West_Indian_Voyage.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Boazio-Sir_Francis_Drakes_West_Indian_Voyage.jpg/220px-Boazio-Sir_Francis_Drakes_West_Indian_Voyage.jpg" width="220" height="169" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Boazio-Sir_Francis_Drakes_West_Indian_Voyage.jpg/330px-Boazio-Sir_Francis_Drakes_West_Indian_Voyage.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Boazio-Sir_Francis_Drakes_West_Indian_Voyage.jpg/440px-Boazio-Sir_Francis_Drakes_West_Indian_Voyage.jpg 2x" data-file-width="835" data-file-height="640" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Boazio-Sir_Francis_Drakes_West_Indian_Voyage.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map of Drake's Great Expedition in 1585 by <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Battista_Boazio" title="Giovanni Battista Boazio">Giovanni Battista Boazio</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>War had already been declared by Phillip II after the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Nonsuch" title="Treaty of Nonsuch">Treaty of Nonsuch</a>, so the Queen through <a href="/wiki/Francis_Walsingham" title="Francis Walsingham">Francis Walsingham</a> ordered Sir Francis Drake to lead an expedition to attack the Spanish colonies in a kind of <a href="/wiki/Preemptive_strike" class="mw-redirect" title="Preemptive strike">preemptive strike</a>. An expedition left Plymouth in September 1585 with Drake in command of twenty-one ships with 1,800 soldiers under <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Carleill" title="Christopher Carleill">Christopher Carleill</a>. He first attacked <a href="/wiki/Vigo" title="Vigo">Vigo</a> in Spain and held the place for two weeks ransoming supplies. He then <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Santiago_(1585)" title="Capture of Santiago (1585)">plundered Santiago</a> in the Cape Verde islands after which the fleet then sailed across the Atlantic, <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Santo_Domingo_(1586)" title="Battle of Santo Domingo (1586)">sacked the port</a> of <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cartagena_de_Indias_(1586)" title="Battle of Cartagena de Indias (1586)">captured the city</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cartagena_de_Indias" class="mw-redirect" title="Cartagena de Indias">Cartagena de Indias</a> in present-day Colombia. On 6 June 1586, during the return leg of the voyage, <a href="/wiki/Raid_on_St._Augustine" title="Raid on St. Augustine">he raided</a> the Spanish fort of <a href="/wiki/St._Augustine,_Florida" title="St. Augustine, Florida">San Augustín</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Florida" title="Spanish Florida">Spanish Florida</a>.</p>
<p>After the raids he then went on to find Sir <a href="/wiki/Walter_Raleigh" title="Walter Raleigh">Walter Raleigh</a>'s settlement much further north at <a href="/wiki/Roanoke_Colony" title="Roanoke Colony">Roanoke</a> which he replenished and also took back with him all of the original colonists before Sir Richard Greynvile arrived with supplies and more colonists. He finally reached England on 22 July, when he sailed into <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Portsmouth, England">Portsmouth, England</a> to a hero's welcome.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spanish_Armada">Spanish Armada</span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Armada" title="Spanish Armada">Spanish Armada</a></div>
<p>Encouraged by these acts Philip II ordered a planned invasion of England.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cadiz_raid">Cadiz raid</span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Singeing_the_King_of_Spain%27s_Beard" title="Singeing the King of Spain's Beard">Singeing the King of Spain's Beard</a></div>
<p>In another pre-emptive strike, Drake "singed the beard of the King of Spain" in 1587 by sailing a fleet into <a href="/wiki/Cadiz" class="mw-redirect" title="Cadiz">Cadiz</a> and also <a href="/wiki/A_Coru%C3%B1a" title="A Coruña">Corunna</a>, two of Spain's main ports, and occupied the harbours. He destroyed 37 naval and merchant ships. The attack delayed the Spanish invasion by a year.<sup id="cite_ref-Thompson_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thompson-56">[56]</a></sup> Over the next month, Drake patrolled the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian coasts</a> between <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cape_St._Vincent" title="Cape St. Vincent">Cape St. Vincent</a>, intercepting and destroying ships on the Spanish supply lines. Drake estimated that he captured around 1600–1700 tons of barrel staves, enough to make 25,000 to 30,000 barrels (4,800&#160;m<sup>3</sup>) for containing provisions.<sup id="cite_ref-Kraus_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kraus-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Defeat_of_the_Spanish_Armada">Defeat of the Spanish Armada</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DrakeMonumentTavistock.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/DrakeMonumentTavistock.jpg/250px-DrakeMonumentTavistock.jpg" width="250" height="188" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/DrakeMonumentTavistock.jpg/375px-DrakeMonumentTavistock.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/DrakeMonumentTavistock.jpg/500px-DrakeMonumentTavistock.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:DrakeMonumentTavistock.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Sir Francis Drake whilst playing bowls on Plymouth Hoe is informed of the approach of the Spanish Armada. Bronze plaque by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Boehm" class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph Boehm">Joseph Boehm</a>, 1883, base of Drake statue, Tavistock</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Loutherbourg-Spanish_Armada.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Loutherbourg-Spanish_Armada.jpg/250px-Loutherbourg-Spanish_Armada.jpg" width="250" height="192" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Loutherbourg-Spanish_Armada.jpg/375px-Loutherbourg-Spanish_Armada.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Loutherbourg-Spanish_Armada.jpg/500px-Loutherbourg-Spanish_Armada.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1263" data-file-height="971" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Loutherbourg-Spanish_Armada.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Armada" title="Spanish Armada">Spanish Armada</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Drake was <a href="/wiki/Vice_admiral" title="Vice admiral">vice admiral</a> in command of the English fleet (under <a href="/wiki/Charles_Howard,_1st_Earl_of_Nottingham" title="Charles Howard, 1st Earl of Nottingham">Lord Howard of Effingham</a>) when it overcame the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Armada" title="Spanish Armada">Spanish Armada</a> that was attempting to invade England in 1588. As the English fleet pursued the Armada up the English Channel in closing darkness, Drake broke off and captured the Spanish galleon <i>Rosario</i>, along with Admiral Pedro de Valdés and all his crew. The Spanish ship was known to be carrying substantial funds to pay the Spanish Army in the Low Countries. Drake's ship had been leading the English pursuit of the Armada by means of a lantern. By extinguishing this for the capture, Drake put the fleet into disarray overnight.</p>
<p>On the night of 29 July, along with Howard, Drake organised <a href="/wiki/Fire_ship" title="Fire ship">fire-ships</a>, causing the majority of the Spanish captains to break formation and sail out of <a href="/wiki/Calais" title="Calais">Calais</a> into the open sea. The next day, Drake was present at the <a href="/wiki/Naval_battle_of_Gravelines" class="mw-redirect" title="Naval battle of Gravelines">Battle of Gravelines</a>. He wrote as follows to Admiral Henry Seymour after coming upon part of the Spanish Armada, whilst aboard <i><a href="/wiki/English_ship_Revenge_(1577)" title="English ship Revenge (1577)">Revenge</a></i> on 31 July 1588 (21 July 1588 <a href="/wiki/Old_Style_and_New_Style_dates" title="Old Style and New Style dates">O.S.</a>):<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote>
<p>Coming up to them, there has passed some common shot between some of our fleet and some of them; and as far as we perceive, they are determined to sell their lives with blows.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The most famous (but probably apocryphal) anecdote about Drake relates that, prior to the battle, he was playing a game of <a href="/wiki/Bowls" title="Bowls">bowls</a> on <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Hoe" title="Plymouth Hoe">Plymouth Hoe</a>. On being warned of the approach of the Spanish fleet, Drake is said to have remarked that there was plenty of time to finish the game and still beat the Spaniards. There is no known eyewitness account of this incident and the earliest retelling of it was printed 37 years later.<sup id="cite_ref-Kelsey_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kelsey-34">[34]</a></sup> Adverse winds and currents caused some delay in the launching of the English fleet as the Spanish drew nearer,<sup id="cite_ref-Kelsey_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kelsey-34">[34]</a></sup> perhaps prompting a popular myth of Drake's cavalier attitude to the Spanish threat.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Drake-Norris_Expedition">Drake-Norris Expedition</span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/English_Armada" title="English Armada">English Armada</a></div>
<p>In 1589, the year after defeating the Armada, Drake and <a href="/wiki/John_Norreys" title="John Norreys">Sir John Norreys</a> were given three tasks: seek out and destroy the remaining ships, support the rebels in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, Portugal against King Philip II (then king of Spain and Portugal), and take the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a> if possible. Drake and Norreys destroyed a few ships in the harbour of <a href="/wiki/A_Coru%C3%B1a" title="A Coruña">A Coruña</a> in Spain but lost more than 12,000 lives and 20 ships.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This delayed Drake, and he was forced to forgo hunting the rest of the surviving ships and head on to Lisbon.<sup id="cite_ref-Kraus_57-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kraus-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Defeat_and_death">Defeat and death</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:BurialOfDrakeTavistockMonument.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/BurialOfDrakeTavistockMonument.jpg/220px-BurialOfDrakeTavistockMonument.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/BurialOfDrakeTavistockMonument.jpg/330px-BurialOfDrakeTavistockMonument.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/BurialOfDrakeTavistockMonument.jpg/440px-BurialOfDrakeTavistockMonument.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:BurialOfDrakeTavistockMonument.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Drake's burial at sea off <a href="/wiki/Portobelo,_Col%C3%B3n" title="Portobelo, Colón">Portobello</a>. Bronze plaque by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Boehm" class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph Boehm">Joseph Boehm</a>, 1883, base of Drake statue, Tavistock.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Drake's seafaring career continued into his mid-fifties. In 1595, he <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Las_Palmas" title="Battle of Las Palmas">failed to conquer</a> the port of Las Palmas, and following a disastrous campaign against Spanish America, where he suffered a number of defeats, he unsuccessfully attacked San Juan de Puerto Rico, eventually losing the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_San_Juan_(1595)" title="Battle of San Juan (1595)">Battle of San Juan</a>.</p>
<p>The Spanish gunners from <a href="/wiki/El_Morro_Castle" class="mw-redirect" title="El Morro Castle">El Morro Castle</a> shot a cannonball through the cabin of Drake's flagship, and he survived. He attempted to attack San Juan again, but a few weeks later, in January 1596, he died of <a href="/wiki/Dysentery" title="Dysentery">dysentery</a>, a common disease in the tropics at the time, while anchored off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Portobelo" class="mw-redirect" title="Portobelo">Portobelo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a>, where some Spanish treasure ships had sought shelter. Following his death, the English fleet withdrew.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>Before dying, he asked to be dressed in his full armour. He was buried at sea in a lead-lined coffin, near Portobelo. It is supposed that his final resting place is near the wrecks of two British ships, the Elizabeth and the Delight, scuttled in Portobelo Bay. Divers continue to search for the coffin.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_impact">Cultural impact</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:NPG_Drake.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/NPG_Drake.jpg/170px-NPG_Drake.jpg" width="170" height="278" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/NPG_Drake.jpg/255px-NPG_Drake.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/00/NPG_Drake.jpg 2x" data-file-width="306" data-file-height="500" /></a>

National Portrait Gallery, London.</div>

1591 portrait, also by Gheeraerts the Younger, wearing the "Drake Jewel" suspended from a strap, and displaying new arms<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the UK there are various places named after him, especially in <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>, Devon, where various places carry his name, including the naval base (<a href="/wiki/HMS_Drake" title="HMS Drake">HMS Drake</a>), <a href="/wiki/Drake%27s_Island" title="Drake's Island">Drake's Island</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Drake_Circus_Shopping_Centre" title="Drake Circus Shopping Centre">shopping centre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roundabout" title="Roundabout">roundabout</a> named <a href="/wiki/Drake_(ward)" title="Drake (ward)">Drake Circus</a>. <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Hoe" title="Plymouth Hoe">Plymouth Hoe</a> is also home to a statue of Drake.</p>
<p>In the United States <a href="/wiki/Drakes_Bay" title="Drakes Bay">Drakes Bay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sir_Francis_Drake_Boulevard" title="Sir Francis Drake Boulevard">Sir Francis Drake Boulevard</a> of <a href="/wiki/Marin_County,_California" title="Marin County, California">Marin County, California</a> are both named after him, as well as the high school in <a href="/wiki/San_Anselmo,_California" title="San Anselmo, California">San Anselmo, California</a>. The boulevard runs between Drakes Bay at <a href="/wiki/Point_Reyes" title="Point Reyes">Point Reyes</a> to <a href="/wiki/New_Albion#Point_San_Quentin.2C_San_Francisco_Bay.2C_California" title="New Albion">Point San Quentin</a> on <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay" title="San Francisco Bay">San Francisco Bay</a>. A large hotel in <a href="/wiki/Union_Square,_San_Francisco" title="Union Square, San Francisco">Union Square, San Francisco</a> also bears his name. Additionally, the <a href="/wiki/Sir_Francis_Drake_Channel" title="Sir Francis Drake Channel">Sir Francis Drake Channel</a> in the <a href="/wiki/British_Virgin_Islands" title="British Virgin Islands">British Virgin Islands</a> bears his name.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, where some theorize he may also have landed to the north of the usual site considered to be <a href="/wiki/Nova_Albion" class="mw-redirect" title="Nova Albion">Nova Albion</a>, various mountains were named in the 1930s for him, or in connection with Elizabeth I or other figures of that era, including <a href="/wiki/Mount_Sir_Francis_Drake" title="Mount Sir Francis Drake">Mount Sir Francis Drake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mount_Queen_Bess" title="Mount Queen Bess">Mount Queen Bess</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Hinde" class="mw-redirect" title="Golden Hinde">Golden Hinde</a>, the highest mountain on <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Island" title="Vancouver Island">Vancouver Island</a>.</p>
<p>Drake's will was the focus of a vast <a href="/wiki/Confidence_scheme" class="mw-redirect" title="Confidence scheme">confidence scheme</a> which <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Hartzell" title="Oscar Hartzell">Oscar Hartzell</a> perpetrated in the 1920s and 1930s. He convinced thousands of people, mostly in the American Midwest, that Drake's fortune was being held by the British government, and had compounded to a huge amount. If their last name was Drake they might be eligible for a share if they paid Hartzell to be their agent. The swindle continued until a copy of Drake's will was brought to Hartzell's mail fraud trial and he was convicted and imprisoned.<sup id="cite_ref-Rayner-Richard_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rayner-Richard-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
