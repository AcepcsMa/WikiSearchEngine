<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:black;"><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Society_Portal"><big>The Society Portal</big></span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #61C9E2; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:right;margin-left:1em">
<center>Cleric, knight and Peasant; example of <a href="/wiki/Feudalism" title="Feudalism">feudal societies</a></center>
<p><a href="/wiki/File:Cleric-Knight-Workman.jpg" class="image" title="Cleric, knight and Peasant; an example of feudal societies"><img alt="Cleric, knight and Peasant; an example of feudal societies" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Cleric-Knight-Workman.jpg/200px-Cleric-Knight-Workman.jpg" width="200" height="205" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Cleric-Knight-Workman.jpg/300px-Cleric-Knight-Workman.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Cleric-Knight-Workman.jpg/400px-Cleric-Knight-Workman.jpg 2x" data-file-width="656" data-file-height="672" /></a><br /></p>

<p>A <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> <b><a href="/wiki/Society" title="Society">society</a></b> is a group of people <a href="/wiki/Interpersonal_relationship" title="Interpersonal relationship">related</a> to each other through continued relations, or a large social grouping sharing the same geographical or virtual territory, same interests, subject to the same political authority and dominant cultural expectations. Human societies are characterized by patterns of relationships (<a href="/wiki/Social_relations" class="mw-redirect" title="Social relations">social relations</a>) between individuals who share a distinctive <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Institutions" class="mw-redirect" title="Institutions">institutions</a>. A given society may be described as the sum total of such relationships among its constituent members. In the <a href="/wiki/Social_sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Social sciences">social sciences</a>, a larger society often evinces <a href="/wiki/Social_stratification" title="Social stratification">stratification</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Dominance_hierarchy" title="Dominance hierarchy">dominance</a> patterns in subgroups.</p>
<p>In so far as it is <a href="/wiki/Collaborative" class="mw-redirect" title="Collaborative">collaborative</a>, a society can enable its members to benefit in ways that would not otherwise be possible on an individual basis; both individual and social (common) benefits can thus be distinguished, or in many cases found to overlap. A society can also consist of like-minded people governed by their own norms and values within a dominant, larger society. This is sometimes referred to as a <a href="/wiki/Subculture" title="Subculture">subculture</a>, a term used extensively within <a href="/wiki/Criminology" title="Criminology">criminology</a>: an organized group working together having a common interests, beliefs, or profession.</p>
<p>More broadly, a society may be described as an <a href="/wiki/Economic" class="mw-redirect" title="Economic">economic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Social" title="Social">social</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industrial</a> <a href="/wiki/Infrastructure" title="Infrastructure">infrastructure</a>, made up of a varied collection of individuals or subgroups. Members of a society may be from different <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_group" title="Ethnic group">ethnic groups</a>. A society can be a particular ethnic group, such as the <a href="/wiki/Saxons" title="Saxons">Saxons</a>; a <a href="/wiki/Nation_state" title="Nation state">nation state</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Bhutan" title="Bhutan">Bhutan</a>; or a broader cultural group, such as a <a href="/wiki/Western_culture" title="Western culture">Western society</a>. The word <i>society</i> may also refer to an <a href="/wiki/Organization" title="Organization">organized</a> <a href="/wiki/Voluntary_association" title="Voluntary association">voluntary association</a> of people for religious, benevolent, cultural, scientific, political, patriotic, or other purposes. A "society" may also be a group of <a href="/wiki/Social_organism" title="Social organism">social organisms</a> such as an <a href="/wiki/Ant_colony" title="Ant colony">ant colony</a>, or any <a href="/wiki/Cooperation" title="Cooperation">cooperative</a> aggregate such as, for example, in <a href="/wiki/Society_of_mind" class="mw-redirect" title="Society of mind">some formulations</a> of <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a>.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:black;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_article"><i>Selected article</i></span></h2>

<div style="border: 1px solid #61C9E2; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:left;margin-right:1em"><a href="/wiki/File:Europe_Balkans_Macedonia_geo.jpg" class="image" title="Map showing different regions that can be referred to as Macedonia"><img alt="Map showing different regions that can be referred to as Macedonia" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Europe_Balkans_Macedonia_geo.jpg/120px-Europe_Balkans_Macedonia_geo.jpg" width="120" height="101" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Europe_Balkans_Macedonia_geo.jpg/180px-Europe_Balkans_Macedonia_geo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Europe_Balkans_Macedonia_geo.jpg/240px-Europe_Balkans_Macedonia_geo.jpg 2x" data-file-width="726" data-file-height="609" /></a></div>
The <b><a href="/wiki/Macedonia_(terminology)" title="Macedonia (terminology)">definition of Macedonia</a></b> is a major source of confusion due to the overlapping use of the term to describe geographical, political and historical areas, languages and peoples. Ethnic groups inhabiting the area use different terminology for the same entity, or the same terminology for different entities. Geographically, no single definition of its borders or the names of its subdivisions is accepted by all scholars and ethnic groups. Demographically, it is mainly inhabited by four ethnic groups, three of which self-identify as <i>Macedonians</i>: One <a href="/wiki/Slavic_peoples" class="mw-redirect" title="Slavic peoples">Slavic</a> group does so at a national level, while another, <a href="/wiki/Bulgarians" title="Bulgarians">Bulgarians</a>, as well as a <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Greek</a> one do so at a regional level. Linguistically, the names and origins of the languages and dialects spoken in the region are a source of controversy. Politically, the use of the name Macedonia has led to a diplomatic dispute between <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">Republic of Macedonia</a>. Despite intervention from the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, the dispute is still pending full resolution.

<p><a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japanese</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">British</a> troops attack members of the Society of Righteous and Harmonious Fists ("Boxers") at Beijing Castle during the <b><a href="/wiki/Boxer_Rebellion" title="Boxer Rebellion">Boxer Rebellion</a></b> of 1899–1901. The Boxers, angered by foreign <a href="/wiki/Imperialism" title="Imperialism">imperialist</a> expansion into <a href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a> China, had engaged in <a href="/wiki/Looting" title="Looting">looting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arson" title="Arson">arson</a>, and killings of foreigners. The uprising was eventually put down by 20,000 troops from the <a href="/wiki/Eight-Nation_Alliance" title="Eight-Nation Alliance">Eight-Nation Alliance</a>.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:black;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_biography"><i>Selected biography</i></span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #61C9E2; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.2em"><a href="/wiki/File:Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg" class="image" title="Alcibiades"><img alt="Alcibiades" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg/120px-Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg" width="120" height="200" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg/180px-Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg/240px-Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1475" data-file-height="2460" /></a></div>
<b><a href="/wiki/Alcibiades" title="Alcibiades">Alcibiades</a></b> was a prominent <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athenian</a> statesman, orator, and general. The last famous member of an aristocratic family that fell from prominence after the <a href="/wiki/Peloponnesian_War" title="Peloponnesian War">Peloponnesian War</a>, he played a major role in the second half of that conflict as a strategic advisor, military commander, and politician. During the course of the Peloponnesian War, Alcibiades changed his allegiance on several occasions. In his native Athens in the early 410s&#160;BC, he advocated for an aggressive foreign policy, and was a prominent proponent of the <a href="/wiki/Sicilian_Expedition" title="Sicilian Expedition">Sicilian Expedition</a>, but fled to <a href="/wiki/Sparta" title="Sparta">Sparta</a> after his political enemies brought charges of sacrilege against him. In the years that he served Sparta, Alcibiades played a crucial role in Athens' undoing; the capture of <a href="/wiki/Decelea" title="Decelea">Decelea</a> and the revolts of several critical Athenian subjects occurred either at his suggestion or under his supervision. Once restored to his native city, however, he played a crucial role in a string of Athenian victories that eventually brought Sparta to seek a peace with Athens. He favored unconventional tactics, frequently winning cities over by treachery or negotiation rather than by siege. Alcibiades' military and political talents frequently proved valuable to whichever state currently held his allegiance, but his capacity for making powerful enemies ensured that he never remained in one place for long, and, by the end of the war that he had helped rekindle in the early 410s, his days of political relevance were a bygone memory.
