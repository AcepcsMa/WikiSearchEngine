<p>The <b>Rezatkreis</b>, between 1806 and 1837, was one of the 15 districts of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bavaria" title="Kingdom of Bavaria">Kingdom of Bavaria</a>. Its name meant in German the “District of the Rezat” because the <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A4nkische_Rezat" class="mw-redirect" title="Fränkische Rezat">Fränkische Rezat</a> River ran through it. It was the predecessor of the <i><a href="/wiki/Regierungsbezirk" title="Regierungsbezirk">Regierungsbezirks</a> Mittelfranken</i> (Administrative Regional District of <a href="/wiki/Middle_Franconia" title="Middle Franconia">Middle Franconia</a>). Its center was <a href="/wiki/Ansbach" title="Ansbach">Anspach</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rezatkreis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the years between 1806 and 1808, the Kingdom of Bavaria was divided into 15 (administrative) districts, whose names were taken from the local rivers. The <i>Rezatkreis</i> was bordered in the north by the Bavarian <i>Mainkreis</i> (<i>Obermainkreis</i> after 1817), in the east by the <i>Pegnitzkreis</i> and the <i>Altmühlkreis</i> (until 1810), in the south by the Bavarian <i>Oberdonaukreis</i> and in the west by the Württemberger <i>Jagstkreis</i>. In 1817, the number of districts was reduced from eight to seven (with the <i>Rheinpfalz</i>). The Pegnitzkreis and the northern part of the former <i>Altmühlkreis</i> (which had belonged since 1810 to the <i>Oberdonaukreis</i>) were added to the <i>Rezatkreis</i>. <a href="/wiki/Ansbach" title="Ansbach">Anspach</a> was the district capital [<i>Kreisstadt</i>]. At the request of King <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_I" class="mw-redirect" title="Ludwig I">Ludwig I</a>, the Territorial Reorganization (<i>Gebietsreform</i>) was made on 29 November 1837. Consequently, on 1 January 1838, the <i>Rezatkreis</i> became <i>Mittelfranken</i> [Upper Franconia], one of the seven current <i>Regierungsbezirke</i> of the Kingdom and later State of Bavaria.</p>
