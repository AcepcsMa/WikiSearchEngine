			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Diet of Nuremberg</h1>

<p>The <b>Diets of Nuremberg</b>, also called the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Diet_(Holy_Roman_Empire)" title="Imperial Diet (Holy Roman Empire)">Imperial Diets</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, took place at different times between the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> and the 17th century.</p>
<p>The first Diet of Nuremberg, in 1211, elected the future emperor <a href="/wiki/Frederick_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Frederick II, Holy Roman Emperor">Frederick II of Hohenstaufen</a> as German king.</p>
<p>At the Diet of 1356 the <a href="/wiki/Charles_IV,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles IV, Holy Roman Emperor">Emperor Charles IV</a> issued the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Bull_of_1356" title="Golden Bull of 1356">Golden Bull of 1356</a>, which required each <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a> to summon the first Imperial <a href="/wiki/Diet_(assembly)" title="Diet (assembly)">Diet</a> after his election at Nuremberg. Apart from that, a number of other diets were held there.</p>
<p>Important to <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestantism</a> (and the Turks)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="Please clarify the following statement or statements with a good explanation from a reliable source. (September 2017)">how?</span></a></i>]</sup> were the Diets of 1522 ("First Diet of Nuremberg"), 1524 ("Second Diet of Nuremberg") and 1532 ("Third Diet of Nuremberg").</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_1522_Diet_of_Nuremberg">The 1522 Diet of Nuremberg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diet_of_Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The 1522 Diet of Nuremberg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This Diet has become known mostly for the reaction of the papacy to the decision made on <a href="/wiki/Martin_Luther" title="Martin Luther">Luther</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Diet_of_Worms" title="Diet of Worms">Diet of Worms</a> the previous year. The new pope, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_VI" class="mw-redirect" title="Adrian VI">Adrian VI</a>, sent his nuncio <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Chieregati" title="Francesco Chieregati">Francesco Chieregati</a> to the Diet, to insist both that the <a href="/wiki/Edict_of_Worms" class="mw-redirect" title="Edict of Worms">Edict of Worms</a> be executed, and that action be taken promptly against Luther. This demand, however, was coupled with a promise of thorough reform in the Roman hierarchy, frankly admitting the partial guilt of the Vatican in the decline of the Church.</p>
<p>In the recess drafted on 9 February 1523, however, the German princes rejected this appeal. Using Adrian's admissions, they declared that they could not have it appear 'as though they wished to oppress evangelical truth and assist unchristian and evil abuses.'</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_1524_Diet_of_Nuremberg">The 1524 Diet of Nuremberg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diet_of_Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The 1524 Diet of Nuremberg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This Diet generally took the same line as the previous one. The Estates reiterated their decision from the previous Diet. The Cardinal-legate, Campeggio, who was present, showed his disgust at the behaviour of the Estates. On 18 April, the Estates decided to call 'a general gathering of the German nation', to meet at <a href="/wiki/Speyer" title="Speyer">Speyer</a> the following year and to decide what would be done until the meeting of the general council of the Church which they demanded.</p>
