<p><b>Alexander Gordon Shelley</b> (born 8 October 1979, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>) is an <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Conducting" title="Conducting">conductor</a>. He is currently chief conductor of the <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_Symphony_Orchestra" title="Nuremberg Symphony Orchestra">Nuremberg Symphony Orchestra</a> and music director of the <a href="/wiki/National_Arts_Centre_Orchestra" title="National Arts Centre Orchestra">National Arts Centre Orchestra</a> (NACO) in Ottawa, as well as principal associate conductor of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Philharmonic_Orchestra" title="Royal Philharmonic Orchestra">Royal Philharmonic Orchestra</a> (RPO).</p>
<p>The son of the pianists <a href="/wiki/Howard_Shelley" title="Howard Shelley">Howard Shelley</a> <a href="/wiki/OBE" class="mw-redirect" title="OBE">OBE</a> and Hilary Macnamara, Shelley learned piano from his mother and cello from his grandmother.<sup id="cite_ref-Duffy_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Duffy-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1992, he won a music scholarship to <a href="/wiki/Westminster_School" title="Westminster School">Westminster School</a> from The Hall School Hampstead. He studied cello with Timothy Hugh, Steven Doane and Johannes Goritzki at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Music" title="Royal College of Music">Royal College of Music</a> and at the <a href="/wiki/Robert_Schumann_Hochschule" title="Robert Schumann Hochschule">Robert Schumann Hochschule</a>, Düsseldorf respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-cbc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbc-2">[2]</a></sup> Master-classes with <a href="/wiki/Mstislav_Rostropovich" title="Mstislav Rostropovich">Mstislav Rostropovich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Janos_Starker" class="mw-redirect" title="Janos Starker">Janos Starker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aldo_Parisot" title="Aldo Parisot">Aldo Parisot</a> led him to France, Italy and North America. He was a member of the World Orchestra for Peace during the 2003 tour with <a href="/wiki/Valery_Gergiev" title="Valery Gergiev">Valery Gergiev</a>. He studied conducting with Professor Thomas Gabrisch<sup id="cite_ref-cbc_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbc-2">[2]</a></sup> in Düsseldorf and worked closely with Yan-Pascal Tortelier as his assistant conductor, among others with the <a href="/wiki/National_Youth_Orchestra_of_Great_Britain" title="National Youth Orchestra of Great Britain">National Youth Orchestra of Great Britain</a>.</p>
<p>In 2001<sup id="cite_ref-cbc_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbc-2">[2]</a></sup> he founded the Schumann Camerata, a chamber orchestra with whom he subsequently performed over 80 concerts. In 2005, he won first prize in the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Conductors_Competition" title="Leeds Conductors Competition">Leeds Conductors Competition</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In the same year, Shelley conceived the "440Hz" project, a series of concerts involving prominent German television and stage personalities, to attract young adults to the concert hall.<sup id="cite_ref-cbc_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbc-2">[2]</a></sup> Guest artists have included Konrad Beikircher, Gotz Alsmann&amp;Band, Die Wise Guys, Blank&amp;Jones, BASTA, Ensemble Six, Miki, Curse, Reen, Mellow Mark, Ono, Chima, Marlies Petersen and Ralf Bauer.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, Shelley became Artistic Director of the <i>Zukunftslabor</i> project of the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Kammerphilharmonie_Bremen" title="Deutsche Kammerphilharmonie Bremen">Deutsche Kammerphilharmonie Bremen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In September 2009, Shelley became the youngest ever Principal Conductor of the Nuremberg Symphony Orchestra, with an initial contract of four years. In 2011, his contract was extended to 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He is scheduled to conclude his Nuremberg tenure at the close of the 2016-2017 season.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2013, the NACO announced the appointment of Shelley as its next music director, as of the 2015-2016 season, with an initial contract of 4 years.<sup id="cite_ref-nac_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nac-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cbc_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbc-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In January 2015, the RPO named Shelley its principal associate conductor.<sup id="cite_ref-RPO_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RPO-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>His professional opera conducting debut was for <a href="/wiki/Royal_Danish_Opera" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Danish Opera">Royal Danish Opera</a> in 2008, conducting a production of <i>The Merry Widow</i>. Shelley's first album for <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Grammophon" title="Deutsche Grammophon">Deutsche Grammophon</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Hope_(violinist)" title="Daniel Hope (violinist)">Daniel Hope</a> and the Stockholm Philharmonic, was released in September 2014.</p>
<p>Shelley and his wife Zoe, a fitness model and personal trainer,<sup id="cite_ref-Duffy_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Duffy-1">[1]</a></sup> were married in July 2011 at <a href="/wiki/St_Paul%27s_Church,_Knightsbridge" title="St Paul's Church, Knightsbridge">St Paul's Church, Knightsbridge</a>. He is the <a href="/wiki/Dance4Life" title="Dance4Life">Dance4Life</a> Ambassador for Germany and patron of House meets Charity e.v.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
