			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Nazi architecture</h1>

A model of <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>'s plan for Berlin formulated under the direction of <a href="/wiki/Albert_Speer" title="Albert Speer">Albert Speer</a>, looking north toward the <a href="/wiki/Volkshalle" title="Volkshalle">Volkshalle</a> at the top of the frame.</div>

<p><b>Nazi architecture</b> is the architecture promoted by the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Third Reich</a> from 1933 until its fall in 1945. It is characterized by three forms: a stripped-down <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neoclassicism</a> (typified by the designs of <a href="/wiki/Albert_Speer" title="Albert Speer">Albert Speer</a>); a vernacular style that drew inspiration from traditional rural architecture, especially alpine; and a utilitarian style followed for major infrastructure projects and industrial or military complexes. Nazi ideology took a pluralist attitude to architecture; however, <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a> himself believed that form should follow function and wrote against "stupid imitations of the past".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The crowning achievement of this movement was to be <a href="/wiki/Welthauptstadt_Germania" title="Welthauptstadt Germania">Welthauptstadt Germania</a>, the projected renewal of the German capital <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> following the Nazis' victory in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. Speer, who oversaw the project, produced most of the plans for the new city. Only a small portion of the "World Capital" was ever built between 1937 and 1943. The plan's core features included the creation of a great neoclassical city based on an East-West axis with the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_victory_column" class="mw-redirect" title="Berlin victory column">Berlin victory column</a> at its centre. Major Nazi buildings like the <a href="/wiki/Reichstag_(building)" title="Reichstag (building)">Reichstag</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Volkshalle" title="Volkshalle">Große Halle</a> (never built) would adjoin wide boulevards. A great number of historic buildings in the city were demolished in the planned construction zones. However, with defeat of the Third Reich, the work was never started.</p>
