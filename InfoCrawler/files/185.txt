<p>The <b>2017 Central Mexico earthquake</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Terremoto de Puebla de 2017</i></span>) struck at 13:14&#160;<a href="/wiki/Central_Time_Zone" title="Central Time Zone">CDT</a> (18:14 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>) on 19 September 2017 with a magnitude estimated to be <a href="/wiki/Seismic_scale#Mw" title="Seismic scale"><span title="Moment mag. scale">M<sub>w</sub></span></a>&#160;7.1 and strong shaking for about 20 seconds, approximately 55&#160;km (34&#160;mi) south of the city of <a href="/wiki/Puebla_City" title="Puebla City">Puebla</a>. It caused damage in the Mexican states of <a href="/wiki/Puebla" title="Puebla">Puebla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morelos" title="Morelos">Morelos</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Mexico_City" title="Greater Mexico City">Greater Mexico City</a> area, including the collapse of more than forty buildings.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CNN_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-reforma_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reforma-4">[4]</a></sup> Although Mexico's <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Seismic_Alert_System" title="Mexican Seismic Alert System">SASMEX earthquake warning system</a> provided 20 seconds' advance warning in Mexico City, 324 people have been reported killed,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> 273 of which in Mexico City and surrounding areas,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and more than 4,600 injured.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The quake occurred on the 32nd anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/1985_Mexico_City_earthquake" title="1985 Mexico City earthquake">1985 Mexico City earthquake</a>, which killed about 10,000 people, commemorated with a national earthquake drill at 11&#160;a.m. local time, two hours before this earthquake struck.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Tagesschau_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tagesschau-9">[9]</a></sup> There had been an <a href="/wiki/2017_Chiapas_earthquake" title="2017 Chiapas earthquake">even larger earthquake</a> 650&#160;km (400&#160;mi) away off the coast of the state of <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a> twelve days earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-AP-Latest_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP-Latest-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Central_Mexico_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mexico_tectonic_plates.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Mexico_tectonic_plates.png/220px-Mexico_tectonic_plates.png" width="220" height="183" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Mexico_tectonic_plates.png/330px-Mexico_tectonic_plates.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Mexico_tectonic_plates.png/440px-Mexico_tectonic_plates.png 2x" data-file-width="717" data-file-height="596" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mexico_tectonic_plates.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Tectonic plates of Mexico. Visible in the image is most of the North American continent, along with Central America. Mexico is located in the lower middle part of the picture, to the right of the Cocos plate.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Mexico is one of the world's most seismically active regions, sitting atop several intersecting <a href="/wiki/Tectonic_plate" class="mw-redirect" title="Tectonic plate">tectonic plates</a>. The border between the <a href="/wiki/Cocos_Plate" title="Cocos Plate">Cocos Plate</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_American_Plate" title="North American Plate">North American Plate</a>, along the Pacific Coast of Mexico, creates a <a href="/wiki/Subduction_zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Subduction zone">subduction zone</a> that is able to generate large seismic events.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS-2">[2]</a></sup> Taken together with the activity along the edges of the <a href="/wiki/Rivera_Plate" title="Rivera Plate">Rivera</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> plates, this causes the country to experience an average of 40 earthquakes a day.<sup id="cite_ref-SSN_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SSN-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mexico City is <a href="/wiki/Lake_Texcoco" title="Lake Texcoco">built on a dry lakebed</a> with soft soil made up of sand and clay that amplify the destruction caused by a major earthquake.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Loose sediments near the surface have a decelerating effect on seismic shockwaves that is slowing them down from 1.5 miles per second to roughly 150 feet per second.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-13">[13]</a></sup> This leads the waves' amplitude to increase, while deeper and denser soil layers cause shockwaves to endure in the valley longer, thereby extending the length of the earthquake.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Less than two weeks before the Puebla earthquake, Mexico had been struck by an <a href="/wiki/2017_Chiapas_earthquake" title="2017 Chiapas earthquake">earthquake in Chiapas</a> on 7 September, which killed almost 100 people. Despite its close timing, the Puebla earthquake was not an <a href="/wiki/Aftershock" title="Aftershock">aftershock</a> of the Chiapas event, as the epicenters were 650&#160;km (400&#160;mi) apart.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The possibility of a <a href="/wiki/Remotely_triggered_earthquakes" title="Remotely triggered earthquakes">link between the earthquakes</a> was being investigated in the days after the second one. Big earthquakes can increase the long-term risk of seismic activity by transferring "static stress" to adjacent faults, but only at a distance of up to four times the length of the original rupture. In the 19 September earthquake static stress transfer was considered unlikely due to the distance between the earthquakes, in excess of the expected 400&#160;km maximum. "Dynamic triggering", with seismic waves propagating from one quake affecting other faults, may operate at much longer distances, but usually happens within hours or a few days of the triggering quake; a 12-day gap is hard to explain.<sup id="cite_ref-Witze2017_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Witze2017-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>19 September is designated as a day of remembrance for the <a href="/wiki/1985_Mexico_City_earthquake" title="1985 Mexico City earthquake">1985 Mexico City earthquake</a>, which killed approximately 10,000 residents. Every year at 11&#160;a.m., a national earthquake drill is conducted by the government through the use of public loudspeakers located throughout Mexico City.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The 2017 drill took place as scheduled, at 11&#160;a.m., around two hours before the central Mexico earthquake.<sup id="cite_ref-Tagesschau_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tagesschau-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/National_Seismological_Service" title="National Seismological Service">National Seismological Service</a> (SSN) of Mexico, the <a href="/wiki/Epicenter" title="Epicenter">epicenter</a> was located 12&#160;km (7.5&#160;mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Axochiapan" title="Axochiapan">Axochiapan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morelos" title="Morelos">Morelos</a>, and 120&#160;km (75&#160;mi) from Mexico City.<sup id="cite_ref-SSN_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SSN-11">[11]</a></sup> The earthquake was measured at a <a href="/wiki/Moment_magnitude_scale" title="Moment magnitude scale">magnitude</a> of 7.1, occurring at 13:14:40 <a href="/wiki/Central_Time_Zone" title="Central Time Zone">Central Daylight Time</a>, at a depth of 51&#160;km (32&#160;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-SSN_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SSN-11">[11]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Geological_Survey" title="United States Geological Survey">United States Geological Survey</a> (USGS) placed the epicenter 5&#160;km (3.1&#160;mi) ENE of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Raboso" title="San Juan Raboso">San Juan Raboso</a> and reported a measurement of VIII (Severe) on the <a href="/wiki/Mercalli_intensity_scale" title="Mercalli intensity scale">Mercalli intensity scale</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS-2">[2]</a></sup> While there was a report of strong shaking for about one minute, which is a long time for an earthquake,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> acceleration/velocity/displacement <a href="/wiki/Seismogram" title="Seismogram">seismograms</a> at <a href="/wiki/UNAM" class="mw-redirect" title="UNAM">UNAM</a> showed about 20&#160;seconds of strong shaking with a period of ≈1&#160;second.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> SSN reported a <a href="/wiki/Peak_ground_acceleration" title="Peak ground acceleration">peak ground acceleration</a> of 112&#160;cm/s<sup>2</sup> (0.114&#160;<a href="/wiki/Standard_gravity" title="Standard gravity"><i>g<sub>0</sub></i></a>) at the <a href="/wiki/Popocat%C3%A9petl" title="Popocatépetl">Popocatépetl</a> reporting station in Tlamacas, <a href="/wiki/State_of_Mexico" title="State of Mexico">Estado de México</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SSN_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SSN-11">[11]</a></sup> According to the USGS, the earthquake occurred on a moderately dipping <a href="/wiki/Normal_fault" class="mw-redirect" title="Normal fault">normal fault</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the bulletin of Mexico's <a href="/wiki/SASMEX" class="mw-redirect" title="SASMEX">SASMEX</a> earthquake warning system, 20 seconds' advance warning was given in Mexico City;<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> however the general experience in the capital was that the alarm and cellphone alerts started only a few seconds before, or during the quake,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>, although some people may have mistaken the alert for a continuation of the earlier drill.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> 25 of the early-warning seismic sensors detected the earthquake, and alerts were also provided to Oaxaca, Acapulco, Chilpancingo, and Puebla, with lead times stated to range from 12 to 48 seconds.</p>

<p>Within hours of the earthquake, at least 217 people had been reported killed.<sup id="cite_ref-AP-Latest_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP-Latest-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-segobpc-tweet_26-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-segobpc-tweet-26">[26]</a></sup> At least 71 people were killed in the state of <a href="/wiki/Morelos" title="Morelos">Morelos</a>, 86 in Mexico City, 43 in <a href="/wiki/Puebla" title="Puebla">Puebla</a>, 12 in the <a href="/wiki/State_of_Mexico" title="State of Mexico">State of Mexico</a>, four in the state of <a href="/wiki/Guerrero" title="Guerrero">Guerrero</a> and one in the state of <a href="/wiki/Oaxaca" title="Oaxaca">Oaxaca</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AP_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-segobpc-tweet_26-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-segobpc-tweet-26">[26]</a></sup> In Mexico City, 22 of the fatalities occurred at the Enrique C. Rébsamen school, where the bodies of 26 students and four instructors were pulled from the rubble; 30 students and 8 adults were still unaccounted for as of the evening of 19 September<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=2017_Central_Mexico_earthquake&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Monterrey_Institute_of_Technology_and_Higher_Education,_Mexico_City" title="Monterrey Institute of Technology and Higher Education, Mexico City">Mexico City campus of the Monterrey Institute of Technology and Higher Education</a> suffered damage, with at least 5 people killed and 40 injured.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>More than 800 people had been reported injured by the day after the earthquake, with more than 295 confirmed dead<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> as Mexico earthquake rescue efforts continue.</p>
<p>On September 25, death toll climbed to 324, with more than 4500 injured people.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Damage_and_aftermath">Damage and aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Central_Mexico_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Damage and aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Puebla, church steeples had toppled in the city of <a href="/wiki/Cholula,_Puebla" title="Cholula, Puebla">Cholula</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-mexibritain_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mexibritain-34">[34]</a></sup> and a church on the slopes of <a href="/wiki/Popocat%C3%A9petl" title="Popocatépetl">Popocatépetl</a> in <a href="/wiki/Atzitzihuacan" class="mw-redirect" title="Atzitzihuacan">Atzitzihuacan</a> collapsed during mass, killing 15 people.<sup id="cite_ref-mexibritain_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mexibritain-34">[34]</a></sup> A second church, which was built in the 17th century, fell in <a href="/wiki/Atzala" title="Atzala">Atzala</a> during a baptism, killing 11 people including the baby.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcmexi_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcmexi-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>At least 44 buildings collapsed in Mexico City due to the earthquake, trapping people inside, creating large plumes of dust, and starting fires.<sup id="cite_ref-mexibritain_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mexibritain-34">[34]</a></sup> At least 50 to 60 people were rescued by emergency workers and citizens.<sup id="cite_ref-AP_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYTimes_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes-36">[36]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AP_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP-28">[28]</a></sup> Several buildings caught fire.<sup id="cite_ref-CBC_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBC-37">[37]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Condesa" title="Condesa">Condesa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colonia_Roma" title="Colonia Roma">Roma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colonia_del_Valle" title="Colonia del Valle">del Valle</a> neighbourhoods were among those most affected in the zone: a building located on <a href="/wiki/Avenida_%C3%81lvaro_Obreg%C3%B3n" title="Avenida Álvaro Obregón">Álvaro Obregón Avenue</a> collapsed, and several buildings on <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Avenue,_Mexico_City" title="Amsterdam Avenue, Mexico City">Ámsterdam Avenue</a> suffered damage.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gas leaks were reported, along with "piles" of rubble from collapsed buildings.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTimes_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes-36">[36]</a></sup> Stock prices declined at the <a href="/wiki/Mexico_Stock_Exchange" class="mw-redirect" title="Mexico Stock Exchange">Mexico Stock Exchange</a> but recovered before trading was suspended.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Comisi%C3%B3n_Federal_de_Electricidad" title="Comisión Federal de Electricidad">Comisión Federal de Electricidad</a>, the national electric utility, reported that 4.78 million customers lost power in Guerrero, Morelos, Puebla, State of Mexico, Oaxaca, <a href="/wiki/Tlaxcala" title="Tlaxcala">Tlaxcala</a>, and parts of Mexico City—roughly 35% of the company's customers in those states. However, none of the <a href="/wiki/Power_station" title="Power station">generating stations</a> in the region sustained structural damage.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mexico_City_International_Airport" title="Mexico City International Airport">Mexico City International Airport</a> suspended operations while damage assessments took place, but reopened at 4:00 p.m.&#160;<a href="/wiki/Central_Time_Zone" title="Central Time Zone">CDT</a> (2100 UTC). 180 flights were cancelled or diverted during the closure.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CNN_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LATimes_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-44">[44]</a></sup> A plane carrying President <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Pe%C3%B1a_Nieto" title="Enrique Peña Nieto">Enrique Peña Nieto</a>, returning from touring damage in <a href="/wiki/Oaxaca" title="Oaxaca">Oaxaca</a> from the <a href="/wiki/2017_Chiapas_earthquake" title="2017 Chiapas earthquake">earlier Chiapas earthquake</a>, was diverted to <a href="/wiki/Santa_Luc%C3%ADa_Air_Force_Base" class="mw-redirect" title="Santa Lucía Air Force Base">Santa Lucía Air Force Base</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City_Metro" title="Mexico City Metro">Mexico City Metro</a> service was temporarily cancelled on several subway lines due to a power failure, but restored by 17:30, offering free service to stranded passengers.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Building evacuations also caused delays to <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City_Metrob%C3%BAs" title="Mexico City Metrobús">Metrobús</a> service in the city.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>The federal <a href="/wiki/Secretariat_of_the_Interior_(Mexico)" title="Secretariat of the Interior (Mexico)">Secretariat of the Interior</a> (SEGOB) declared a state of emergency for all 33 <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Morelos" title="Municipalities of Morelos">municipalities of Morelos</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-segob-morelos_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-segob-morelos-48">[48]</a></sup> for all 16 <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Mexico_City" title="Municipalities of Mexico City">boroughs of Mexico City</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-segob-cdmx_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-segob-cdmx-49">[49]</a></sup> and 112 of the 217 <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puebla" title="Municipalities of Puebla">municipalities of Puebla</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-segob-puebla_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-segob-puebla-50">[50]</a></sup> The declarations allow funds from the National Natural Disaster Fund (FONDEN) to be used during the emergency response phase.<sup id="cite_ref-segob-morelos_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-segob-morelos-48">[48]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-segob-cdmx_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-segob-cdmx-49">[49]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-segob-puebla_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-segob-puebla-50">[50]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Army" title="Mexican Army">Mexican Army</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Navy" title="Mexican Navy">Mexican Navy</a> deployed 3,000 active-duty troops to Mexico City through the <a href="/wiki/Plan_DN-III-E" title="Plan DN-III-E">DN-III-E and Plan Marina</a> emergency response plans. The troops were tasked with debris cleanup, <a href="/wiki/Search_and_rescue" title="Search and rescue">search and rescue</a>, and security missions. Additionally, the <a href="/wiki/SEDENA" class="mw-redirect" title="SEDENA">Secretariat of National Defence</a> moved eight helicopters to Mexico City, and activated 3 shelters in the affected areas.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>All Round of 16 matches of the <a href="/wiki/Copa_MX" title="Copa MX">Copa MX</a>, an association football cup competition, were suspended until further notice.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>The hashtag #FuerzaMéxico (Be strong, Mexico) was used on social media outlets.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Israel_Defense_Forces" title="Israel Defense Forces">Israel Defense Forces</a> sent a group of 50 search and rescue personnel including engineers, to help in the aftermath of the earthquake. The contingent had special dispensation to travel during the <a href="/wiki/Rosh_Hashanah" title="Rosh Hashanah">Rosh Hashanah</a> holiday, normally forbidden under religious law.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Japan_International_Cooperation_Agency" title="Japan International Cooperation Agency">Japan International Cooperation Agency</a> sent a disaster relief team of 72 search-and-rescue personnel, four search dogs and five tons of equipment with personnel from the Japan Disaster Relief Team, Tokyo Fire Department and the Tokyo Metropolitan Police's SAR officers.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_(Japan)" title="Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Japan)">Ministry of Foreign Affairs</a> of the country remarked that it was a show of thanks, as Mexico had sent a search team to help Japan during the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>Turkish state aid agency <a href="/wiki/Turkish_International_Cooperation_and_Development_Agency" title="Turkish International Cooperation and Development Agency">TİKA</a> sent humanitarian aid – including packages containing hygienic and medical supplies prepared in coordination with the Mexican Red Cross – to Mexico City and Xochimilco.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> TIKA also provided tools and equipment to be used in search-and-rescue efforts.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">US President</a> <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a> called Peña Nieto to offer condolences, while the White House has offered search and rescue assistance.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/U.S._Agency_for_International_Development" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Agency for International Development">U.S. Agency for International Development</a> deployed an urban search and rescue team from the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_County_Fire_Department" title="Los Angeles County Fire Department">Los Angeles County Fire Department</a> and experts from the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Foreign_Disaster_Assistance" title="Office of Foreign Disaster Assistance">Office of Foreign Disaster Assistance</a> to the affected regions.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
