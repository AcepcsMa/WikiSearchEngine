<p><b>Alexandre "Alex" Virot</b> (1890 – 14 July 1957) was a French <a href="/wiki/Sports_journalism" title="Sports journalism">sports journalist</a>. He won a <a href="/wiki/Art_competitions_at_the_1928_Summer_Olympics#Painting" title="Art competitions at the 1928 Summer Olympics">silver medal in painting</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1928_Summer_Olympics" title="1928 Summer Olympics">1928 Summer Olympics</a>. Virot died in a motorcycle accident whilst covering the <a href="/wiki/1957_Tour_de_France" title="1957 Tour de France">1957 Tour de France</a>, making him the only journalist to have been killed whilst covering the Tour.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Virot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In his youth, Virot was interested in sports and art courses, with a particular interest in <a href="/wiki/Antoine_Bourdelle" title="Antoine Bourdelle">Antoine Bourdelle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Eurosport_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eurosport-1">[1]</a></sup> Virot fought in the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="First World War">First World War</a> in the trenches and as a trained pilot,<sup id="cite_ref-Eurosport_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eurosport-1">[1]</a></sup> and also fought for the <a href="/wiki/French_Resistance" title="French Resistance">French Resistance</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TOB_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TOB-2">[2]</a></sup> He later drew for the Miroir des Sports, and was asked by the newspaper to cover the <a href="/wiki/1928_Summer_Olympics" title="1928 Summer Olympics">1928 Summer Olympics</a> in Amsterdam. Whilst there, he was awarded the silver medal in the painting event.<sup id="cite_ref-Eurosport_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eurosport-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a journalist, Virot reported on 22 editions of the <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France" title="Tour de France">Tour de France</a>. In 1932, Virot broadcast a <a href="/wiki/Summit_finish" class="mw-redirect" title="Summit finish">summit finish</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Col_d%27Aubisque" title="Col d'Aubisque">Col d'Aubisque</a>, the first live radio broadcast from a summit finish.<sup id="cite_ref-Eurosport_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eurosport-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Virot also covered football, boxing, motorsports and skiing, as well as the first French national lottery draw in 1934.<sup id="cite_ref-Eurosport_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eurosport-1">[1]</a></sup> During the 1930s, Virot also worked as a <a href="/wiki/War_reporter" class="mw-redirect" title="War reporter">war reporter</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Second_Italo-Ethiopian_War" title="Second Italo-Ethiopian War">Second Italo-Ethiopian War</a>, Virot interviewed Ethiopian King <a href="/wiki/Haile_Selassie" title="Haile Selassie">Haile Selassie</a>, and he also provided a live report of the German <a href="/wiki/Annexation" title="Annexation">annexation</a> of Austria, in defiance of Nazi <a href="/wiki/Censorship" title="Censorship">censorship</a> of the event.<sup id="cite_ref-Eurosport_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eurosport-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Virot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Virot died in a motorcycle accident whilst covering the <a href="/wiki/1957_Tour_de_France" title="1957 Tour de France">1957 Tour de France</a>, making him the only journalist to have been killed whilst covering the Tour.<sup id="cite_ref-Eurosport_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eurosport-1">[1]</a></sup> Virot was covering the <a href="/wiki/Bastille_Day" title="Bastille Day">Bastille Day</a> stage from Barcelona, Spain, to <a href="/wiki/Ax-les-Thermes" title="Ax-les-Thermes">Ax-les-Thermes</a>. Riding on a motorcycle with his driver, René Wagner, Virot had been providing a time check to cyclist <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Queheille" title="Marcel Queheille">Marcel Queheille</a>. His motorcycle veered off the track and crashed in a <a href="/wiki/Ravine" title="Ravine">ravine</a>. Virot fractured his skull and died on his way to hospital, and Wagner also succumbed to his injuries.<sup id="cite_ref-Eurosport_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eurosport-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TOB_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TOB-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
