<p><b>Nuremberg Zoo</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Tiergarten Nürnberg</i></span>) is a zoo located in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Reichswald&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nuremberg Reichswald (page does not exist)">Nuremberg Reichswald</a> ("imperial forest"), southeast of <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, Germany. With an area of 67 hectares (170 acres), approximately 300 animal species are kept by the zoo.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Zoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_Middle_Ages">In the Middle Ages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Zoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In the Middle Ages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The tradition of zoos in Nuremberg goes back to the Middle Ages. However the only indications of a zoo maintained by the local nobility, the Burgraves, take the form of certain place names, such as the Zoo Gate (<i>Tiergärtner Tor</i>) and the adjoining Zoo Square (<i>Tiergärtnertorplatz</i>). This preserve stretched as far as the so-called Johannisfelder (today: St. Johannis) and the Rohlederersgarten (today the site of the Klinikum Nord). One can only speculate about the use made of the park.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century">20th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Zoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 20th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tiergarten_Nbg_Weiherlandschaft.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Tiergarten_Nbg_Weiherlandschaft.JPG/220px-Tiergarten_Nbg_Weiherlandschaft.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Tiergarten_Nbg_Weiherlandschaft.JPG/330px-Tiergarten_Nbg_Weiherlandschaft.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Tiergarten_Nbg_Weiherlandschaft.JPG/440px-Tiergarten_Nbg_Weiherlandschaft.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2304" /></a>

<p>The Nuremberg Zoo was founded on 11 May 1912<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> on the site of the Bavarian State Exhibition at the Luitpoldhain. After <a href="/wiki/The_Nazis" class="mw-redirect" title="The Nazis">The Nazis</a> seized power, the zoo had to give way for the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_party_rally_grounds" title="Nazi party rally grounds">Reichsparteitagsgelände</a> at the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutzendteich&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dutzendteich (page does not exist)">Dutzendteich</a> and was closed in February 1939. In May 1939, the new zoo was opened in the Reichswald at the Schmausenbuck. It was almost completely destroyed after World War II and rebuilt at the end of the 1950s.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Today">Today</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Zoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Today">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:NBG-Big-Cat-House.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/NBG-Big-Cat-House.jpg/220px-NBG-Big-Cat-House.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/NBG-Big-Cat-House.jpg/330px-NBG-Big-Cat-House.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/NBG-Big-Cat-House.jpg/440px-NBG-Big-Cat-House.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2304" /></a>

<p>With 67 hectares (170 acres), it is one of the largest European zoos. Typical are large, natural enclosures, which are embedded in a landscape of forests and stone pits. More than one million visitors are counted every year. Many new enclosures, e.g., for gorillas, snow leopards and polar bears, were built in recent years.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Flocke" title="Flocke">Flocke</a>, a captive-born polar bear who was born at the zoo in December 2007, became a popular tourist attraction and an international celebrity after a controversial decision to remove her from her mother and raise her by hand. Flocke has since been "adopted" by <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Environment_Programme" title="United Nations Environment Programme">United Nations Environment Programme</a> chief <a href="/wiki/Achim_Steiner" title="Achim Steiner">Achim Steiner</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-patron_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-patron-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Animals">Animals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Zoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Animals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The zoo has <a href="/wiki/Asiatic_lion" title="Asiatic lion">Asiatic lions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Siberian_tiger" title="Siberian tiger">Siberian tigers</a>, which live in former stone pits. The inner cages of both large cat species are behind a rock face and can be visited only after entering the house through a tunnel. Other carnivores are <a href="/wiki/Snow_leopard" title="Snow leopard">snow leopards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polar_bear" title="Polar bear">polar bears</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syrian_brown_bear" title="Syrian brown bear">Syrian brown bears</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Maned_wolves" class="mw-redirect" title="Maned wolves">maned wolves</a>. The zoo is also successful in breeding <a href="/wiki/South_African_cheetah" title="South African cheetah">South African cheetahs</a>. Further attractions are <a href="/wiki/Common_bottlenose_dolphin" title="Common bottlenose dolphin">Common bottlenose dolphins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indian_rhinoceros" title="Indian rhinoceros">Indian rhinoceroses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malayan_tapir" title="Malayan tapir">Malayan tapirs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lowland_gorilla" class="mw-redirect" title="Lowland gorilla">lowland gorillas</a>, <a href="/wiki/California_sea_lion" title="California sea lion">California sea lions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manatee" title="Manatee">manatees</a>.</p>
<p>Many hooved mammals, like <a href="/wiki/Reticulated_giraffe" title="Reticulated giraffe">reticulated giraffes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Somali_wild_ass" title="Somali wild ass">Somali wild asses</a>, <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A8re_David%27s_deer" title="Père David's deer">Père David's deer</a>, <a href="/wiki/European_bison" title="European bison">European</a> and <a href="/wiki/American_bison" title="American bison">American bison</a>, <a href="/wiki/African_buffalo" title="African buffalo">African buffalo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plains_zebra" title="Plains zebra">plains zebras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkmenian_kulan" title="Turkmenian kulan">Turkmenian kulans</a> live in large enclosures, which are embedded in the forested, hilly landscape. Beautiful ponds are home to a variety of water birds.</p>

<p>"The Polar Bear Group" is a life-size, bronze animal sculpture in the Aqua Park of the Zoo, created by <a href="/wiki/Josef_Tabachnyk" title="Josef Tabachnyk">Josef Tabachnyk</a> in 2007.</p>
<p>The Group consists of a mother <a href="/wiki/Polar_bear" title="Polar bear">polar bear</a> with her young cubs in a playful pose, and is based on a flat plate from bright <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a>, which is reminiscent of an ice floe. The Group was created by the sculptor <a href="/wiki/Josef_Tabachnyk" title="Josef Tabachnyk">Josef Tabachnyk</a> and was installed in the zoo in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The measurements of the Polar Bear Group are 120x240x90 cm. Since November 2010, another bronze bear sculpture created by the same artist was installed in the entrance area of the zoo. It depicts a <a href="/wiki/Brown_bear" title="Brown bear">brown bear</a> lying on its back, playing with a slug.</p>

The bronze sculpture, The Tree of Life, 2012</div>

</div>
<p>"The Tree of Life" is a bronze sculpture also created by the artist Tabachnyk. It has been standing on public grounds of the Zoo since November 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The sculpture, when viewed from a distance, depicts a blossoming <a href="/wiki/Apple_tree" class="mw-redirect" title="Apple tree">apple tree</a>. Up close, one can see a few elements of the life cycle embedded in the various elements of the tree. In the tree’s roots and bark, faces, hands and other body parts are implied. Within the branches grows a young male figure, who appears to raise up the leaves and fruit of the tree. Additionally a female figure lies in the trees. At the top of the tree birds are leaving and flying up to heaven. The measurements of the sculpture are 270x190x95cm.</p>
<p>From below the generations are depicted. The ancestors, which are shown in the tree’s roots, bark and branches, create the foundation for the current generation. The current generation cares for and provides for the leaves and fruit, which depict the future generations.</p>
