<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:black;"><span class="mw-headline" id="The_History_Portal"><big>The History Portal</big></span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #C27E3A; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #EEDCC8;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<p><br /></p>
<div style="float:right;margin-left:1em">
<center><a href="/wiki/The_Historians%27_History_of_the_World" title="The Historians' History of the World">The Historians' History of the World</a></center>
<p><a href="/wiki/The_Historians%27_History_of_the_World" title="The Historians' History of the World"><img alt="The Historians' History of the World" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/The_Historians%27_History_of_the_World_-_Title_Page.jpg/200px-The_Historians%27_History_of_the_World_-_Title_Page.jpg" width="200" height="293" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/The_Historians%27_History_of_the_World_-_Title_Page.jpg/300px-The_Historians%27_History_of_the_World_-_Title_Page.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/The_Historians%27_History_of_the_World_-_Title_Page.jpg/400px-The_Historians%27_History_of_the_World_-_Title_Page.jpg 2x" data-file-width="855" data-file-height="1254" /></a><br /></p>
</div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/History" title="History">History</a></b> is the discovery, collection, organization, analysis, and presentation of information about past events. History can also mean a continuous, typically chronological record of important or public events or of a particular trend or institution. Scholars who write about history are called <a href="/wiki/Historian" title="Historian">historians</a>. It is a field of <a href="/wiki/Knowledge" title="Knowledge">knowledge</a> which uses a <a href="/wiki/Narrative" title="Narrative">narrative</a> to examine and analyse the sequence of events, and it sometimes attempts to objectively investigate the patterns of cause and effect that determine events. Historians debate the nature of history and its usefulness. This includes discussing the study of the discipline as an end in itself and as a way of providing "perspective" on the problems of the present. The stories common to a particular <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a> but not supported by external sources (such as the legends surrounding <a href="/wiki/King_Arthur" title="King Arthur">King Arthur</a>) are usually classified as <a href="/wiki/Cultural_heritage" title="Cultural heritage">cultural heritage</a> rather than as the "disinterested investigation" needed by the discipline of history. Events of the past prior to written record are considered <a href="/wiki/Prehistory" title="Prehistory">prehistory</a>.</p>
<p>Amongst scholars, fifth century BC <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Greek</a> historian <a href="/wiki/Herodotus" title="Herodotus">Herodotus</a> is considered to be the "father of history"; the methods of Herodotus along with his contemporary <a href="/wiki/Thucydides" title="Thucydides">Thucydides</a> form the foundations for the modern study of history. Their influence (along with other historical traditions in other parts of their world) has spawned many different interpretations of the nature of history which has developed over the centuries and are continuing to change. The modern study of history has many different fields, including those that focus on certain regions and those that focus on certain topical or thematic elements of historical investigation. Often, history is taught as part of primary and secondary education, and the academic study of history is a major discipline in university studies.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:black;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_article">Selected article</span></h2>

<div style="border: 1px solid #C27E3A; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #EEDCC8;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:left;margin-right:1em"><a href="/wiki/File:Skyraider_A-1H-J_1969_-_00000033_-_USAF.jpg" class="image" title="An A-1 Skyraider, similar in model to the aircraft used in the attack"><img alt="An A-1 Skyraider, similar in model to the aircraft used in the attack" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Skyraider_A-1H-J_1969_-_00000033_-_USAF.jpg/150px-Skyraider_A-1H-J_1969_-_00000033_-_USAF.jpg" width="150" height="100" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Skyraider_A-1H-J_1969_-_00000033_-_USAF.jpg/225px-Skyraider_A-1H-J_1969_-_00000033_-_USAF.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Skyraider_A-1H-J_1969_-_00000033_-_USAF.jpg/300px-Skyraider_A-1H-J_1969_-_00000033_-_USAF.jpg 2x" data-file-width="792" data-file-height="526" /></a></div>
The <b><a href="/wiki/1962_South_Vietnamese_Independence_Palace_bombing" title="1962 South Vietnamese Independence Palace bombing">1962 South Vietnamese Independence Palace bombing</a></b> in <a href="/wiki/Saigon" class="mw-redirect" title="Saigon">Saigon</a> was an <a href="/wiki/Airstrike" title="Airstrike">aerial attack</a> on February 27, 1962, by two dissident <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_Air_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="Vietnam Air Force">Vietnam Air Force</a> pilots, Second Lieutenant <a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_V%C4%83n_C%E1%BB%AD_(pilot)" title="Nguyễn Văn Cử (pilot)">Nguyễn Văn Cử</a> and First Lieutenant <a href="/wiki/Ph%E1%BA%A1m_Ph%C3%BA_Qu%E1%BB%91c" title="Phạm Phú Quốc">Phạm Phú Quốc</a>. The pilots targeted the <a href="/wiki/Independence_Palace" title="Independence Palace">Independence Palace</a>, the official residence of the President of South Vietnam, with the aim of assassinating President <a href="/wiki/Ngo_Dinh_Diem" title="Ngo Dinh Diem">Ngô Đình Diệm</a> and his immediate family, who acted as his political advisors.
<p>The pilots later stated that their assassination attempt was in response to Diệm's autocratic rule, in which he focused more on remaining in power than on confronting the <a href="/wiki/Viet_Cong" title="Viet Cong">Viet Cong</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Marxism%E2%80%93Leninism" title="Marxism–Leninism">Marxist–Leninist</a> guerilla army who were threatening to overthrow the South Vietnamese government. Cử and Quốc hoped that the airstrike would expose Diệm's vulnerability and trigger a general uprising, but this failed to materialise. One bomb penetrated a room in the western wing where Diệm was reading but it failed to detonate, leading the president to claim that he had "divine protection". With the exception of Diệm's sister-in-law <a href="/wiki/Madame_Ngo_Dinh_Nhu" class="mw-redirect" title="Madame Ngo Dinh Nhu">Madame Ngo Dinh Nhu</a>, who escaped with minor injuries, the Ngo family were unscathed; however, three palace staff died and another 30 were injured. Afterwards, Cử managed to escape to <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a>, but Quốc was arrested and imprisoned.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:black;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_biography">Selected biography</span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #C27E3A; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #EEDCC8;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:left;margin-right:1em"><a href="/wiki/File:Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg" class="image" title="Bust of Alcibiades"><img alt="Bust of Alcibiades" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg/125px-Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg" width="125" height="208" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg/188px-Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg/250px-Bust_Alcibiades_Musei_Capitolini_MC1160.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1475" data-file-height="2460" /></a></div>
<b><a href="/wiki/Alcibiades" title="Alcibiades">Alcibiades</a></b>, son of Clinias, from the deme of Scambonidae (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˌ/: secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="/æ/: 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/ɪ/: 'i' in 'kit'">ɪ</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="/aɪ/: 'i' in 'tide'">aɪ</span><span title="/./: syllable break">.</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">iː</span><span title="'z' in 'zoom'">z</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="grc" xml:lang="grc">Ἀλκιβιάδης Κλεινίου Σκαμβωνίδης</span>, <a href="/wiki/Transliteration_of_Greek_to_the_Latin_alphabet" class="mw-redirect" title="Transliteration of Greek to the Latin alphabet">transliterated</a> <i>Alkibiádēs Kleiníou Skambōnidēs</i>; <i>c.</i> 450–404 BC), was a prominent <a href="/wiki/History_of_Athens" title="History of Athens">Athenian</a> <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/statesman" class="extiw" title="wikt:statesman">statesman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orator" title="Orator">orator</a>, and <a href="/wiki/General_officer" title="General officer">general</a>. He was the last famous member of his mother's aristocratic family, the <a href="/wiki/Alcmaeonidae" title="Alcmaeonidae">Alcmaeonidae</a>, which fell from prominence after the <a href="/wiki/Peloponnesian_War" title="Peloponnesian War">Peloponnesian War</a>. He played a major role in the second half of that conflict as a strategic advisor, military commander, and politician.
<p>During the course of the Peloponnesian War, Alcibiades changed his political allegiance on several occasions. In his native Athens in the early 410s BC, he advocated an aggressive foreign policy, and was a prominent proponent of the <a href="/wiki/Sicilian_Expedition" title="Sicilian Expedition">Sicilian Expedition</a>, but fled to <a href="/wiki/History_of_Sparta" title="History of Sparta">Sparta</a> after his political enemies brought charges of <a href="/wiki/Sacrilege" title="Sacrilege">sacrilege</a> against him. In Sparta, he served as a strategic adviser, proposing or supervising several major campaigns against Athens. In Sparta too, however, Alcibiades soon made powerful enemies and was forced to defect to <a href="/wiki/Achaemenid_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Achaemenid Dynasty">Persia</a>. There he served as an adviser to the <a href="/wiki/Satrap" title="Satrap">satrap</a> <a href="/wiki/Tissaphernes" title="Tissaphernes">Tissaphernes</a> until his Athenian political allies brought about his recall. He then served as an Athenian General (<a href="/wiki/Strategos" title="Strategos">Strategos</a>) for several years, but his enemies eventually succeeded in exiling him a second time.</p>
