<p><b>SIGIR</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a>'s <b>Special Interest Group on Information Retrieval</b>. The scope of the group's specialty is the theory and application of computers to the acquisition, organization, storage, <a href="/wiki/Information_retrieval" title="Information retrieval">retrieval</a> and distribution of information; emphasis is placed on working with non-numeric information, ranging from natural language to highly structured data bases.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conferences">Conferences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Special_Interest_Group_on_Information_Retrieval&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Conferences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The annual international SIGIR conference, which began in 1978, is considered the most important in the field of information retrieval. SIGIR also sponsors the annual <a href="/wiki/Joint_Conference_on_Digital_Libraries" title="Joint Conference on Digital Libraries">Joint Conference on Digital Libraries</a> (JCDL) in association with <a href="/wiki/ACM_SIGWEB" title="ACM SIGWEB">SIGWEB</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Conference_on_Information_and_Knowledge_Management" title="Conference on Information and Knowledge Management">Conference on Information and Knowledge Management</a> (CIKM), and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Conference_on_Web_Search_and_Data_Mining&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="International Conference on Web Search and Data Mining (page does not exist)">International Conference on Web Search and Data Mining</a> (WSDM) in association with <a href="/wiki/SIGKDD" title="SIGKDD">SIGKDD</a>, <a href="/wiki/SIGMOD" title="SIGMOD">SIGMOD</a>, and <a href="/wiki/ACM_SIGWEB" title="ACM SIGWEB">SIGWEB</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Special_Interest_Group_on_Information_Retrieval&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The group gives out several awards to contributions to the field of information retrieval. The most important award is the <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Salton_Award" title="Gerard Salton Award">Gerard Salton Award</a> (named after the computer scientist <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Salton" title="Gerard Salton">Gerard Salton</a>), which is awarded every three years to an individual who has made "significant, sustained and continuing contributions to research in information retrieval". Additionally, SIGIR presents a Best Paper Award <sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> to recognize the highest quality paper at each conference. "Test of time" Award <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is a recent award that is given to a paper that has had "long-lasting influence, including impact on a subarea of information retrieval research, across subareas of information retrieval research, and outside of the information retrieval research community". This award is selected from a set of full papers presented at the main SIGIR conference 10-12 years before.</p>
