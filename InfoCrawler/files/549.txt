<p>The <b>Nuremberg Transport Museum</b> (<i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Verkehrsmuseum Nürnberg</span></i>) is based in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, Germany, and consists of the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Bahn_AG" class="mw-redirect" title="Deutsche Bahn AG">Deutsche Bahn's</a> own <b>DB Museum</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and the <b>Museum of Communications</b> (<i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Museum für Kommunikation</span></i>). It also has two satellite museums at <a href="/wiki/Koblenz" title="Koblenz">Koblenz-Lützel</a> (the <i><a href="/wiki/DB_Museum_Koblenz" class="mw-redirect" title="DB Museum Koblenz">DB Museum Koblenz</a></i>) and <a href="/wiki/Halle_(Saale)" title="Halle (Saale)">Halle</a> (<i>DB Museum Halle</i>). The Nuremberg Transport Museum is one of the oldest technical history museums in Europe.</p>
<p>In February 2007 the official name of the DB Museum became the Company Museum of the Deutsche Bahn AG (<i><span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Firmenmuseum der Deutschen Bahn AG</span></i>). It is a milestone on the European Route of Industrial Heritage (ERIH).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_DB_Museum">The DB Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Transport_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The DB Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Transport_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The forerunner of the present-day DB Museum was opened in 1899 as a royal <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bavaria" title="Kingdom of Bavaria">Bavarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Railway_museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Railway museum">railway museum</a> and it is therefore the oldest railway museum in Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Today it is a company museum belonging to the Deutsche Bahn and portrays, amongst other things, the history of the railways. The present building was built in 1925.<sup id="cite_ref-db-image-2008-85_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-db-image-2008-85-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 July 1996, the Deutsche Bahn AG (DB AG) took over the museum from the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Bundesbahn" title="Deutsche Bundesbahn">Deutsche Bundesbahn</a> for the symbolic purchase price of one <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Mark" title="Deutsche Mark">deutschmark</a>. At the same time Dr. Jürgen Franzke, previously head of the Museum of Industrial Culture (<i>Museums Industriekultur</i>) in Nuremberg, was appointed as the museum's chief. The DB AG planned at that time to invest 6 million DM up to the museum's centenary year.<sup id="cite_ref-etr-1996-1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-etr-1996-1-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-etr-1996-513_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-etr-1996-513-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>In the displays of historical railway vehicles are the following important exhibits:</p>
<ul>
<li>Parts of the royal train for the Bavarian king, <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_II_of_Bavaria" title="Ludwig II of Bavaria">Ludwig II</a></li>
<li>The streamlined locomotive, <a href="/wiki/DRG_Class_05" title="DRG Class 05">05 001</a></li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Trans-Europ-Express" class="mw-redirect" title="Trans-Europ-Express">TEE</a> diesel <a href="/wiki/Railcar" title="Railcar">railcar</a> from the 1950s</li>
<li>An original coal wagon from 1829 (on long-term loan from the <a href="/wiki/National_Railway_Museum" title="National Railway Museum">National Railway Museum</a>, <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a> from the <i>South Hetton</i> coal mine in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, the oldest surviving railway vehicle on the European mainland</li>
<li>The oldest <a href="/wiki/Steam_locomotive" title="Steam locomotive">steam locomotive</a> in Germany still preserved in its original state, the <i><a href="/wiki/Bavarian_B_V" title="Bavarian B V">Nordgau</a></i> (built in 1853)</li>
<li>The Bavarian <a href="/wiki/Bavarian_S_2/6" title="Bavarian S 2/6">S 2/6</a> <a href="/wiki/Express_train" title="Express train">express train</a> locomotive</li>
<li>The red <a href="/w/index.php?title=DRB_Class_E_19&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="DRB Class E 19 (page does not exist)">E 19 12</a> express train locomotive</li>
<li>A fragment of the <a href="/wiki/DRG_Class_SVT_877" title="DRG Class SVT 877">SVT 877</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Flying_Hamburger" class="mw-redirect" title="Flying Hamburger">Flying Hamburger</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Diesel_multiple_unit" title="Diesel multiple unit">diesel multiple unit</a>.</li>
<li>A mock-up of part of the traction coach of the <a href="/wiki/ICE_3" title="ICE 3">ICE&#160;3</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Some of the original vehicles are not in the museum itself, but in the wagon shed situated in the open area belonging to the museum on the opposite side of the street. The museum also owns a range of historical vehicles that can be used for special rail services.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Model_collection">Model collection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Transport_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Model collection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A further attraction of the museum is its collection of 160, 1:10 scale models occupying 1000 square metres of the museum. These models have been built over the years since the end of the 19th century and are very finely detailed. The first were made in 1882 by apprentices of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Bavarian_State_Railways" title="Royal Bavarian State Railways">Royal Bavarian State Railways</a> (<i>Königlich Bayerische Staats-Eisenbahnen</i>).</p>
<p>The museum also offers a walk-through tunnel, a <a href="/wiki/Level_crossing" title="Level crossing">level crossing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Railway_signal" title="Railway signal">signals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Railroad_switch" title="Railroad switch">points</a> that can be operated, models equipped with sound and light and simulators.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Model_railway">Model railway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Transport_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Model railway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DB-Museum_Moba.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/DB-Museum_Moba.JPG/220px-DB-Museum_Moba.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/DB-Museum_Moba.JPG/330px-DB-Museum_Moba.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/DB-Museum_Moba.JPG/440px-DB-Museum_Moba.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:DB-Museum_Moba.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Model railway in the Nuremberg Transport Museum</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Model_railway" class="mw-redirect" title="Model railway">model railway</a> covering an area of 80&#160;m² is used to demonstrate prototypical railway operations. During museum opening hours there is a ten-minute demonstration hourly, on the half-hour, with explanations of the key concepts of railway operating. The layout, built between 1960 and 1970, is worked using control panels with a total of some 5000 relays.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Library">Library</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Transport_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Library">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the main building of the DB Museum there is a library with about 40,000 titles on railway subjects. The <i>Präsenzbibliothek</i> can be visited by appointment free of charge on workdays. Postal loans are not possible.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Damage_following_the_fire_of_17_October_2005">Damage following the fire of 17 October 2005</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Transport_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Damage following the fire of 17 October 2005">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:BW_Gostenhof.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/BW_Gostenhof.jpg/220px-BW_Gostenhof.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/BW_Gostenhof.jpg/330px-BW_Gostenhof.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/BW_Gostenhof.jpg/440px-BW_Gostenhof.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1800" data-file-height="1200" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:BW_Gostenhof.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Remains of the burnt-out engines in the <a href="/wiki/Locomotive_shed" class="mw-redirect" title="Locomotive shed">locomotive shed</a> Gostenhof. In front the former <a href="/wiki/DB_Class_V_100" title="DB Class V 100">V 100</a> 1023 Hof engine.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On the evening of 17 October 2005 much of the <a href="/wiki/Roundhouse" title="Roundhouse">roundhouse</a> of the museum, less its steel frame, was burnt to the ground. Unlike the wagon shed, the locomotive shed was not located in the immediate vicinity of the museum and accessible to the public, but four kilometres away at the Nuremberg West <a href="/wiki/Bahnbetriebswerk" title="Bahnbetriebswerk">locomotive depot</a> in the suburb of Gostenhof. It was there that the DB Museum had stabled operational locomotives for which room could not be found in the museum.</p>
<p>A 1935 working replica of the <i><a href="/wiki/Adler_(locomotive)" title="Adler (locomotive)">Adler</a></i>, a locomotive from the <a href="/wiki/Bavarian_Ludwig_Railway" title="Bavarian Ludwig Railway">first German railway between Nuremberg and Fürth</a>, was badly damaged by the blaze. Apprentices and experienced specialists rebuilt the engine in the <a href="/wiki/Meiningen_Steam_Locomotive_Works" title="Meiningen Steam Locomotive Works">Meiningen Steam Locomotive Works</a>. After two years of reconstruction the <i>Adler</i> was once again ready for operations in October 2007 and returned to the Nuremberg Transport Museum on 23 November 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-adler_rekonstruiert_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adler_rekonstruiert-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Federal Government MPs, members of the DB AG management and the Bavarian minister-president, Beckstein, took part in the first journey of the restored <i>Adler</i> on 26 April 2008. A further, non-operational, 1953 replica is displayed in the museum.</p>
<p>Unfortunately the last surviving examples of the <a href="/wiki/Goods_train" class="mw-redirect" title="Goods train">goods train</a> locomotive, the <a href="/wiki/DRG_Class_45" title="DRG Class 45">DRG Class 45</a>, the electric <a href="/w/index.php?title=DRG_Class_E_75&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="DRG Class E 75 (page does not exist)">Class E 75</a> locomotive and other exhibits, especially locomotives, as well as numerous spare parts, also fell victim to the fire or were at least badly damaged.</p>
<p>A total of 24 historical engines and wagons went up in smoke. In the long term, the steam engines should be repairable, as should the E 75. The damaged diesel engines and railbuses, due to their light construction, were irreparable and were scrapped by July 2006. The burnt out and partly collapsed locomotive shed was torn down. Several locos were sold or loaned to railway museums for restoration. For example, number <a href="/wiki/DB_Class_23" title="DB Class 23">23 105</a>, the last steam engine bought by the Bundesbahn in 1959, was leased to the <a href="/wiki/South_German_Railway_Museum" title="South German Railway Museum">South German Railway Museum</a> (<i>Süddeutsches Eisenbahnmuseum Heilbronn</i>) in <a href="/wiki/Heilbronn" title="Heilbronn">Heilbronn</a> for visual restoration.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="DB_Museum_branch_in_Koblenz">DB Museum branch in Koblenz</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Transport_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: DB Museum branch in Koblenz">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the Lützel district of <a href="/wiki/Koblenz" title="Koblenz">Koblenz</a> is the <a href="/wiki/DB_Museum,_Koblenz" title="DB Museum, Koblenz">DB Museum, Koblenz</a>, a branch of the museum that accommodates a number of vehicles including several <a href="/wiki/DB_Class_103" title="DB Class 103">Class 103</a>, <a href="/wiki/DB_Class_E_10" title="DB Class E 10">110 and 113</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=DRG_Class_E_44&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="DRG Class E 44 (page does not exist)">E 44</a> and <a href="/wiki/DRG_Class_E_16" title="DRG Class E 16">E 16</a> electric express locomotives, as well as several coaches belonging to the Joseph Goebbels special train. In addition a restored <a href="/wiki/Prussian_T_3" title="Prussian T 3">Prussian T 3</a>, factory number 499 built by the <a href="/wiki/Maschinenfabrik_Christian_Hagans" title="Maschinenfabrik Christian Hagans">Maschinenfabrik Christian Hagans</a> in 1903 and stored under in accordance with strict heritage protection regulations, can be viewed. For decades it had been used as in a children's play park at Cologne Zoo.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> At present two of the steam locomotive exhibits damaged in the great fire at Nuremberg are also being restored there.</p>
<h2><span id="DB_Museum_branch_in_Halle_(Saale)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="DB_Museum_branch_in_Halle_.28Saale.29">DB Museum branch in Halle (Saale)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Transport_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: DB Museum branch in Halle (Saale)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The former locomotive shed in <a href="/wiki/Halle_(Saale)" title="Halle (Saale)">Halle (Saale)</a> is a branch of the Nuremberg Museum. On display there are several Deutsche Reichsbahn vehicles, in particular the <a href="/wiki/DRG_Class_03.10" class="mw-redirect" title="DRG Class 03.10">03 1010</a> steam locomotive and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=DR_Class_E_11&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="DR Class E 11 (page does not exist)">E 11 001</a> and <a href="/wiki/DRG_Class_E_18" title="DRG Class E 18">E 18 31</a> electric locomotives. The vehicles are looked after by the Halle shed.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Museum_of_Communications">Museum of Communications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Transport_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Museum of Communications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Museum_of_Communication,_Nuremberg,_Germany" title="Museum of Communication, Nuremberg, Germany">Museum of Communication, Nuremberg, Germany</a></div>
<p>The museum of communications in the Transport Museum came from the royal Bavarian postal museum and was integrated into the Transport Museum in 1902. It displays over 500 years of postal and telecommunication services history in Bavaria from the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> to the present. Major aspects covered are the development of postal transportation, post coach travel, telegraphy and telephony. Original vehicles, such as post coaches and motorised vehicles, are displayed, as well as technical equipment from old-fashioned teleprinters to modern news satellites.</p>
<p>Due to lack of space, several historical railway postal vehicles are located in the railway park at <a href="/wiki/Augsburg" title="Augsburg">Augsburg</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Charter_and_tourist_trains">Charter and tourist trains</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Transport_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Charter and tourist trains">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Nuremberg Transport Museum owns several trains, not all stabled in Nuremberg, that are used for tourist and charter services including:</p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Rheingold_Express" class="mw-redirect" title="Rheingold Express">Rheingold Express</a> with <a href="/wiki/DB_Class_103" title="DB Class 103">DB Class E 03</a> locomotives. Based in <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>.</li>
<li>The traditional Zwickau fast-stopping train (<i>Traditions-<a href="/wiki/Eilzug" title="Eilzug">Eilzug</a> Zwickau</i>), based in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>.</li>
<li>The Blue Gentian (<i>Blauer Enzian</i>) <a href="/wiki/Trans-Europ_Express" class="mw-redirect" title="Trans-Europ Express">TEE</a> express. Stabled in Berlin.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Bundesbahn" title="Deutsche Bundesbahn">Deutsche Bundesbahn</a> express, based in <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Düsseldorf">Düsseldorf</a>.</li>
<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Adler_(locomotive)" title="Adler (locomotive)">Adler</a></i> old-time replica locomotive with three open passenger wagons, stabled at Nuremberg.</li>
</ul>
