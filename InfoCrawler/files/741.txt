			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Norisring</h1>

<p>The <b>Norisring</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Street_circuit" title="Street circuit">street circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, on the former <a href="/wiki/Nazi_party_rally_grounds" title="Nazi party rally grounds">Nazi party rally grounds</a> (in <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de"><i>"Reichsparteitagsgelände"</i></span>) site of the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Party" title="Nazi Party">NSDAP</a> party conventions. As the city's German name Nürnberg would lead to confusion with the already famous <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a>, the old name Noris was chosen for the simple track which is nowadays approx. 2,300 metres (1.4&#160;mi) long.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norisring&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 18 May 1947, motorracing events around the remaining 360 metres (0.22&#160;mi) long grand stand (<i>Zeppelinhaupttribüne</i> or simply <i>Steintribüne</i>) are held on streets that are otherwise used for public traffic. Different configurations were used in the early years, including figure-8 layouts. Nowadays, the start-finish straight in front of the central grand stand leads to a right hand sweeper and the lefthand U-turn at <a href="/wiki/Grundig" title="Grundig">Grundig</a> tower, and back in a (now lefthand) sweeper to the Steintribüne, where the "Schöller-S" right-left chicane lead to the backstraight at its backside. Cars touch the outside wall often there. After a righthand kink, the lefthand U-turn of "Dutzendteichkehre" leads back to the main straight after a flat-out lefthand kink.</p>
<p>Due to its proximity of the lake "Dutzendteich" and its location inside a large city, the track and event is compared to the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> (<i>Fränkisches Monaco</i>).</p>
<p>The annual mid-July <i>Norisringrennen</i> is considered a highlight of the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Tourenwagen_Masters" title="Deutsche Tourenwagen Masters">Deutsche Tourenwagen Masters</a> series, as fans get closer to the action and the drivers than on modern venues.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fatalities">Fatalities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norisring&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fatalities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mexican racing driver <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)" title="Pedro Rodríguez (racing driver)">Pedro Rodríguez</a> died at Norisring in 1971 when the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_512" title="Ferrari 512">Ferrari 512</a> he was hired to drive (in championship events, he used to race a <a href="/wiki/John_Wyer" title="John Wyer">Gulf Racing</a>-<a href="/wiki/Porsche_917" title="Porsche 917">Porsche 917</a>) hit the bridge wall before <i>Schöller-S</i> and burst in flames. Afterwards, the track was shortened by moving the <i>Grundigkehre</i> U-turn closer in order to reduce corner speeds. In 2006, a memorial plaque was inaugurated at the site of the crash. Hungarian Formula 3 driver <a href="/wiki/Csaba_Kesj%C3%A1r" title="Csaba Kesjár">Csaba Kesjár</a> also died at Norisring in June 1988.</p>
