<p><b>Arnold Hans Weiss</b> (July 25, 1924 – December 7, 2010) was a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German-born</a> refugee from <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> who emigrated to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> where he became an intelligence officer working for the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Strategic_Services" title="Office of Strategic Services">Office of Strategic Services</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and played a key role in the discovery of the <a href="/wiki/Last_will_and_testament_of_Adolf_Hitler" title="Last will and testament of Adolf Hitler">last will and testament of Adolf Hitler</a>, dictated during the last days of the war in Europe and laying out the succession of leadership following his impending suicide as the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Berlin" title="Battle of Berlin">overtook Berlin</a> and encircled the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%BChrerbunker" title="Führerbunker">Führerbunker</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnold_Weiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> as <b>Hans Arnold Wangersheim</b> on July 5, 1924, he grew up in an orphanage after being placed there as a child following the divorce of his parents. A Jewish organization arranged for him to be sent to a family in <a href="/wiki/Janesville,_Wisconsin" title="Janesville, Wisconsin">Janesville, Wisconsin</a> when he was 13 years old.<sup id="cite_ref-GlobeObit_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GlobeObit-1">[1]</a></sup> Both of his parents escaped from Germany on their own, his father to <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> while his mother also emigrated to the U.S. He attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wisconsin%E2%80%93Madison" title="University of Wisconsin–Madison">University of Wisconsin–Madison</a> and changed his surname to match that of <a href="/wiki/Howard_Weiss" title="Howard Weiss">Howard Weiss</a>, a star football player for the school's football team.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTObit_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTObit-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_career">Military career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnold_Weiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Military career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Weiss served in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a> during World War II, but his training as a <a href="/wiki/Tail_gunner" title="Tail gunner">tail gunner</a> ended when the plane he was in crashed and severely injured his legs. His ability to speak the <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German language</a> led to his being recruited by the O.S.S. for service in Germany. Serving with the <a href="/wiki/Counterintelligence_Corps_(United_States_Army)" class="mw-redirect" title="Counterintelligence Corps (United States Army)">Counterintelligence Corps</a> in the months following the fall of Nazi Germany, Weiss questioned many members of the Nazi hierarchy in advance of their trials.<sup id="cite_ref-GlobeObit_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GlobeObit-1">[1]</a></sup> Weiss's unit was given the responsibility of confirming <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>'s death, and together with <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Trevor-Roper" title="Hugh Trevor-Roper">Hugh Trevor-Roper</a> located <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Zander" title="Wilhelm Zander">Wilhelm Zander</a>, who had been <a href="/wiki/Martin_Bormann" title="Martin Bormann">Martin Bormann</a>'s adjutant, using information that had been obtained by contacting members of his family. While being questioned, Zander described the circumstances in <a href="/wiki/Hitler%27s_Berlin_bunker" class="mw-redirect" title="Hitler's Berlin bunker">Hitler's Berlin bunker</a> in the last days before he committed suicide. Under interrogation, Zander disclosed that he had been sent out of the bunker in order to transport a series of documents, which included an attestation to Hitler's marriage to <a href="/wiki/Eva_Braun" title="Eva Braun">Eva Braun</a>. Also among the documents that were recovered was Hitler's last will and testament, which named Bormann as his <a href="/wiki/Executor" title="Executor">executor</a> and was later introduced as evidence during the <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_Trials" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuremberg Trials">Nuremberg Trials</a>. Weiss was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Commendation_Medal" title="Commendation Medal">Commendation Medal</a> for his role in "apprehending a personality high in the annals of the Nazi system". Another soldier, Herman Rothman, made a claim in a 2009 book that Hitler's will had been located months earlier than the date based on Weiss's account and a book by Hugh Trevor-Roper made no mention of Weiss or of his role in uncovering Hitler's will.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTObit_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTObit-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnold_Weiss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After returning to the United States after completing his military service, Weiss earned a degree in law at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wisconsin_Law_School" title="University of Wisconsin Law School">University of Wisconsin Law School</a> and worked for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_the_Treasury" title="United States Department of the Treasury">United States Department of the Treasury</a>, where he participated in the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Development_Bank" title="Inter-American Development Bank">Inter-American Development Bank</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTObit_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTObit-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>A resident of <a href="/wiki/Chevy_Chase,_Maryland" title="Chevy Chase, Maryland">Chevy Chase, Maryland</a>, Weiss died at the age of 86 due to <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rockville,_Maryland" title="Rockville, Maryland">Rockville, Maryland</a> on December 7, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTObit_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTObit-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
