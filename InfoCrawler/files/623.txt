<p>The <b>Nuremberg tramway network</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Straßenbahnnetz Nürnberg</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Transport_network" title="Transport network">network</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tram" title="Tram">tramways</a> forming part of the <a href="/wiki/Public_transport" title="Public transport">public transport</a> system in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, a city in the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany">federal state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, Germany.</p>
<p>The network is operated by <a href="/w/index.php?title=VAG_Verkehrs-Aktiengesellschaft_N%C3%BCrnberg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="VAG Verkehrs-Aktiengesellschaft Nürnberg (page does not exist)">VAG Verkehrs-Aktiengesellschaft Nürnberg</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/VAG_Verkehrs-Aktiengesellschaft_N%C3%BCrnberg" class="extiw" title="de:VAG Verkehrs-Aktiengesellschaft Nürnberg">de</a>)</span> (VAG), which is a member of the VGN (Verkehrsverbund Großraum Nürnberg or Greater Nuremberg Transport Network).</p>
<p>As of 2013<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Trams_in_Nuremberg&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the network consisted of five lines,<sup id="cite_ref-tramlines_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tramlines-1">[1]</a></sup> running on a total operational route length of 33 kilometres (21&#160;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-report_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-report-3">[3]</a></sup> The network carried 39.152 million passengers annually.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trams_in_Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first horse-drawn tramline opened in Nuremberg on 25 August 1881.<sup id="cite_ref-history1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history1-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Electrification came to the system when the first electric tramline opened on 7 May 1896.<sup id="cite_ref-history1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history1-4">[4]</a></sup> The entire system was electrified on 20 July 1898.<sup id="cite_ref-history1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history1-4">[4]</a></sup> Until the 1990s the <a href="/wiki/Track_gauge" title="Track gauge">track gauge</a> was <span class="nowrap">1,432&#160;mm</span> (<span class="nowrap">4&#160;ft&#160;<span class="frac">8<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>3</sup>⁄<sub>8</sub></span>&#160;in</span>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trams_in_Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Nuremberg tramway network operated on six lines until 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-report_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-report-3">[3]</a></sup> when two tram lines were joined.</p>
<p>The network is made up of 96 kilometres (60&#160;mi) of track,<sup id="cite_ref-facts_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-facts-2">[2]</a></sup> with a total route length of 37 kilometres (23&#160;mi),<sup id="cite_ref-facts_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-facts-2">[2]</a></sup> of which 33 kilometres (21&#160;mi)<sup id="cite_ref-facts_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-facts-2">[2]</a></sup> is operational route. Of the tram's operational route, 41% is tramline segregated from other traffic, with the other 59% representing trams operating in the street with other automobile traffic.<sup id="cite_ref-facts_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-facts-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_plans">Expansion plans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trams_in_Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Expansion plans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After a 2016 ballot measure in Erlangen, the section to am Wegfeld is planned to be extended to <a href="/wiki/Erlangen" title="Erlangen">Erlangen</a> and from there to <a href="/wiki/Herzogenaurach" title="Herzogenaurach">Herzogenaurach</a> in a roughly L-shaped line. Herzogenaurach previously had a connection to mainline rail, but it was shut down in the 1980s, leaving it one of southern Germany's biggest cities without a rail connection.</p>
