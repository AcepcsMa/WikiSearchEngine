<p><b>Central European Time</b> (<b>CET</b>), used in most parts of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and a few <a href="/wiki/North_African" class="mw-redirect" title="North African">North African</a> countries, is a <a href="/wiki/Standard_time" title="Standard time">standard time</a> which is 1&#160;hour ahead of <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">Coordinated Universal Time</a> (UTC). The <a href="/wiki/Time_offset" class="mw-redirect" title="Time offset">time offset</a> from <a href="/wiki/UTC" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC">UTC</a> can be written as <a href="/wiki/UTC%2B01:00" title="UTC+01:00">+01:00</a>. The same standard time, <a href="/wiki/UTC%2B01:00" title="UTC+01:00">UTC+01:00</a>, is also known as <b>Middle European Time</b> (MET, German: <a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/MEZ" class="extiw" title="de:MEZ">MEZ</a>) and under other names like <b>Berlin Time</b>, <b>Romance Standard Time</b> (RST), <b>Paris Time</b> or <b>Rome Time</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/15th_meridian_east" title="15th meridian east">15th meridian east</a> is the central axis for UTC+01:00 in the world system of <a href="/wiki/Time_zones" class="mw-redirect" title="Time zones">time zones</a>.</p>
<p>As of 2011, all <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_European_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="Member states of the European Union">member states of the European Union</a> observe summer time; those that during the winter use CET use <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">Central European Summer Time</a> (CEST) (or: <a href="/wiki/UTC%2B02:00" title="UTC+02:00">UTC+02:00</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daylight_saving_time" title="Daylight saving time">daylight saving time</a>) in summer (from last Sunday of March to last Sunday of October).</p>
<p>A number of African countries use <a href="/wiki/UTC%2B01:00" title="UTC+01:00">UTC+01:00</a> all year long, where it is called <a href="/wiki/West_Africa_Time" title="West Africa Time">West Africa Time</a> (WAT), <span class="citation-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">although <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> also use the term <i>Central European Time</i></span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>

<p>After World War II <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andorra" title="Andorra">Andorra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a> implemented CET.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Time_in_Portugal" title="Time in Portugal">Portugal</a> used CET in the years 1966–1976 and 1992–1996.</p>
<dl>
<dt>United Kingdom</dt>
</dl>
<p>The time around the world is based on <a href="/wiki/Universal_Coordinated_Time" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Coordinated Time">Universal Coordinated Time</a> (UTC) which is roughly synonymous with <a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Mean_Time" title="Greenwich Mean Time">Greenwich Mean Time</a> (GMT). From late March to late October, clocks in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> are put forward by one hour for <a href="/wiki/British_Summer_Time" title="British Summer Time">British Summer Time</a> (BST). Since 1997, most of the European Union aligned with the British standards for BST.</p>
<p>In 1968 there was a three-year experiment called <i>British Standard Time</i>, when the UK and <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a> experimentally employed <a href="/wiki/British_Summer_Time" title="British Summer Time">British Summer Time</a> (GMT+1) all year round; clocks were put forward in March 1968 and not put back until October 1971.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Central European Time is sometimes referred to as <a href="/wiki/Continental_time" title="Continental time">continental time</a> in the UK.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_countries">Other countries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_European_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other countries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Several African countries use <a href="/wiki/UTC%2B01:00" title="UTC+01:00">UTC+01:00</a> all year long, where it called <a href="/wiki/West_Africa_Time" title="West Africa Time">West Africa Time</a> (WAT), <span class="citation-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">although <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> also use the term <i>Central European Time</i>, despite being located in <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a></span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p>Between 2005 and 2008, <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> observed <a href="/wiki/Daylight_saving_time" title="Daylight saving time">daylight saving time</a>. <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a> also used CET during the years 1951–1959, 1982–1989, 1996–1997 and 2012–2013.</p>
<p>For other countries see <a href="/wiki/UTC%2B01:00" title="UTC+01:00">UTC+01:00</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Africa_Time" title="West Africa Time">West Africa Time</a>.</p>

<p>Legal, political and economic, as well as physical or geographical criteria are used in the drawing of time zones so official time zones rarely adhere to meridian lines. The CET time zone, were it drawn by purely geographical terms, would consist of exactly the area between meridians 7°30′ E and 22°30′ E. As a result, there are European locales that despite lying in an area with a "physical" UTC+1 time, actually use another time zone (UTC+2 in particular – there are no "physical" UTC+1 areas that employ UTC). Conversely, there are European areas that have gone for UTC+1, even though their "physical" time zone is UTC (typically), UTC−1 (westernmost <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>), or UTC+2 (e.g. the very easternmost parts of <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>). On the other hand, the people in Spain still have all work and meal hours one hour later than France and Germany even if they have the same time zone.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Following is a list of such "incongruences":</p>
<p>Historically <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a> maintained UTC+1 all year until the opening of the land frontier with Spain in 1982 when it followed its neighbour and introduced CEST.</p>

<h4><span id="Areas_between_22°30′_E_and_37°30'_E_(&quot;physical&quot;_UTC+2)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Areas_between_22.C2.B030.E2.80.B2_E_and_37.C2.B030.27_E_.28.22physical.22_UTC.2B2.29">Areas between 22°30′ E and 37°30' E ("physical" UTC+2)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_European_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Areas between 22°30′ E and 37°30' E (&quot;physical&quot; UTC+2)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The easternmost part of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">Republic of Macedonia</a>, including the city of <a href="/wiki/Strumica" title="Strumica">Strumica</a></li>
<li>The easternmost part of <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Pirot_District" title="Pirot District">Pirot District</a>, including the city of <a href="/wiki/Pirot" title="Pirot">Pirot</a>, and small easternmost parts of <a href="/wiki/Bor_District" title="Bor District">Bor District</a>.</li>
<li>The easternmost tips of <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>, bordering to the north and south respectively the Ukrainian <a href="/wiki/Transcarpathian_Oblast" class="mw-redirect" title="Transcarpathian Oblast">Transcarpathian Oblast</a> (<a href="/wiki/Zakarpattia_Oblast" title="Zakarpattia Oblast">Zakarpattia Oblast</a>), a bit to the east of <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A1s%C3%A1rosnam%C3%A9ny" title="Vásárosnamény">Vásárosnamény</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> – <a href="/wiki/Uzhhorod" title="Uzhhorod">Uzhhorod</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a> (both at 22°18′ E) line</li>
<li>The easternmost part of <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, including the cities of <a href="/wiki/Lublin" title="Lublin">Lublin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bia%C5%82ystok" title="Białystok">Białystok</a></li>
<li>The northeast of <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Norrbotten" title="Norrbotten">Norrbotten province</a>, including the cities of <a href="/wiki/Kalix" title="Kalix">Kalix</a> and <a href="/wiki/Haparanda" title="Haparanda">Haparanda</a></li>
</ul>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Petsamo.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Petsamo.png/220px-Petsamo.png" width="220" height="260" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Petsamo.png/330px-Petsamo.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Petsamo.png/440px-Petsamo.png 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="945" /></a>

Map of <a href="/wiki/Pechengsky_District" title="Pechengsky District">Petsamo area</a> in northern Finland/Soviet Union/Russia. The green area is the Finnish part of the <a href="/wiki/Rybachy_Peninsula" title="Rybachy Peninsula">Rybachi peninsula</a> (Kalastajasaarento) which was ceded to the Soviet Union after the <a href="/wiki/Winter_War" title="Winter War">Winter War</a>. The Red area is the <a href="/wiki/Rayakoski" title="Rayakoski">Jäniskoski</a>-Niskakoski area ceded to the USSR in 1947.</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>The northeast of <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, lying north of <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, roughly coinciding with the county of <a href="/wiki/Finnmark" title="Finnmark">Finnmark</a>; for instance <a href="/wiki/Vads%C3%B8_(town)" title="Vadsø (town)">Vadsø</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Finnmark" title="Finnmark">Finnmark</a>, has a longitude of 29°49′ E. Actually, the easternmost town in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vard%C3%B8_(town)" title="Vardø (town)">Vardø</a>, lies at 30°51′ E, which is so far east, so as to be east even of the central meridian of EET (UTC+2), i.e. east of <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a>. The sun reaches its highest point at 10:56 (when not DST).<br />
The <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a>–<a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Polish</a>-<a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarussian</a> border are the only places where CET (UTC+1/+2) borders Moscow time (UTC+3), resulting in a two hours time change (or one hour in summer) for the passenger crossing that border.</li>
<li>More so, there exists a "tri-zone" point (where UTC+1, UTC+2, and UTC+3 meet) at the <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>–<a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>–<a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> tripoint near <a href="/wiki/Muotkavaara" title="Muotkavaara">Muotkavaara</a>. Mental experiment when looking at this map: Go to the westernmost point of the red area (the <a href="/w/index.php?title=J%C3%A4niskoski-Niskakoski_area&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jäniskoski-Niskakoski area (page does not exist)">Jäniskoski-Niskakoski area</a>); this belongs to Russian jurisdiction, hence the time there is UTC+3. Then, take a northeastern (NE) direction (that is an <i>eastwards</i> direction); you will soon be crossing into <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finnish</a> territory, thus moving to the UTC+2 time zone. Continuing in that direction, you will eventually reach the <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>–<a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> border and enter <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, thus passing into the UTC+1 time zone. So, moving in a (north)<i>easterly</i> direction, you will be moving from UTC+3 to UTC+2 to UTC+1.</li>
</ul>
