<p><b>Central European Summer Time</b> (<b>CEST</b>), sometime referred also as Central European Daylight Time (CEDT), is the standard clock time observed during the period of <a href="/wiki/Daylight_saving_time" title="Daylight saving time">summer daylight-saving</a> in those European countries which observe <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Time" title="Central European Time">Central European Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC%2B01:00" title="UTC+01:00">UTC+1</a>) during the other part of the year. It corresponds to <a href="/wiki/UTC%2B02:00" title="UTC+02:00">UTC+2</a>, which makes it the same as <a href="/wiki/Central_Africa_Time" title="Central Africa Time">Central Africa Time</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_African_Standard_Time" title="South African Standard Time">South African Standard Time</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kaliningrad_Time" title="Kaliningrad Time">Kaliningrad Time</a> in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Names">Names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_European_Summer_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Other names which have been applied to Central European Summer Time are Middle European Summer Time (MEST), Central European Daylight Saving Time (CEDT), and Bravo Time (after the second letter of the <a href="/wiki/NATO_phonetic_alphabet" title="NATO phonetic alphabet">NATO phonetic alphabet</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Period_of_observation">Period of observation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_European_Summer_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Period of observation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 1996 European Summer Time has been observed between 1:00 UTC on the last Sunday of March and 1:00 on the last Sunday of October; previously the rules were not uniform across the European Union.<sup id="cite_ref-Myers_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Myers-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage">Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_European_Summer_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The following countries and territories use Central European Summer Time.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, regularly since 1974</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Andorra" title="Andorra">Andorra</a>, regularly since 1985</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, regularly since 1980</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, regularly since 1980</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a>, regularly since 1983</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>, regularly since 1983</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>, regularly since 1979</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> (metropolitan), regularly since 1980</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> (<a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_France" title="Metropolitan France">metropolitan</a>), regularly since 1976</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Daylight_saving_time_in_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Daylight saving time in Germany">Germany</a>, regularly since 1980</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a>, regularly since 1982</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, regularly since 1983</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, regularly since 1968</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein" title="Liechtenstein">Liechtenstein</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>, regularly since 1981</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, regularly since 1974</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a>, regularly since 1976</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a>, regularly since 1983</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, regularly since 1977</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, regularly since 1980</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, regularly since 1977</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">Republic of Macedonia</a>, regularly since 1983</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/San_Marino" title="San Marino">San Marino</a>, regularly since 1966</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, regularly since 1983</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a> regularly since 1979</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>, regularly since 1983</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> (except <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, which apply <a href="/wiki/Western_European_Summer_Time" title="Western European Summer Time">Western European Summer Time</a> instead), regularly since 1974</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, regularly since 1980</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, regularly since 1981</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican</a>, regularly since 1966</li>
</ul>
<p>CEST was used also in the years 1993–1995 in <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, 1998–1999 in <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a> and 2005–2008 in <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a>. In addition, <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a> used CEST during the years 1951–1959, 1982–1989, 1996–1997 and 2012–2013.</p>
