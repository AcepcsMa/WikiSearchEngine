<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="General_notices">General notices</span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Until 14 March 2018, as part of the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Autoconfirmed_article_creation_trial" title="Wikipedia:Autoconfirmed article creation trial">autoconfirmed article creation trial</a> (ACTRIAL), <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Autoconfirmed" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Autoconfirmed">autoconfirmed status</a> is required in order to create new articles.</li>
<li>The RFC discussion regarding <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:OUTING" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:OUTING">WP:OUTING</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikimedia_Foundation_statement_on_paid_editing_and_outing" title="Wikipedia:Wikimedia Foundation statement on paid editing and outing">WMF essay about paid editing and outing</a> (see more at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:Arbitration_Committee/Noticeboard/Archive_34#Response_to_the_Wikimedia_Foundation_statement_on_paid_editing_and_outing" title="Wikipedia talk:Arbitration Committee/Noticeboard/Archive 34">the ArbCom noticeboard archives</a>) is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:Conflict_of_interest/Archive_27#Investigating_COI_policy" title="Wikipedia talk:Conflict of interest/Archive 27">now archived</a>. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:Conflict_of_interest/Archive_27#Milieu_3" title="Wikipedia talk:Conflict of interest/Archive 27">Milieus #3</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:Conflict_of_interest/Archive_27#Milieu_4" title="Wikipedia talk:Conflict of interest/Archive 27">#4</a> received support; so did <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:Conflict_of_interest/Archive_27#Concrete_proposal_1" title="Wikipedia talk:Conflict of interest/Archive 27">concrete proposal #1</a>.</li>
<li>The gadget to opt-out search results from sister projects is <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Village_pump_(proposals)&amp;oldid=787619953#Disable.2Fopt-out_option_needed_for_sister_projects_search_results">created per discussion</a>. Registered users may go to <a href="/wiki/Special:Preferences/Gadgets#mw-prefsection-gadgets" title="Special:Preferences/Gadgets">user preferences setup</a>, find the opt-out option and select it.</li>
<li>Search results from selected sister projects—<a href="/wiki/Wiktionary" title="Wiktionary">Wiktionary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikibooks" title="Wikibooks">Wikibooks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikivoyage" title="Wikivoyage">Wikivoyage</a> (title matches only), <a href="/wiki/Wikiquote" title="Wikiquote">Wikiquote</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikisource" title="Wikisource">Wikisource</a>—are now activated. See more at the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Village_pump_(policy)/Archive_135#RfC:_sister_projects_in_search_results" title="Wikipedia:Village pump (policy)/Archive 135">archived RfC discussion</a> (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Village_pump_(technical)/Archive_155#Follow-up_to_the_RfC_for_sister_projects_in_search_results" title="Wikipedia:Village pump (technical)/Archive 155">short summary of results</a>).
<dl>
<dd><i><b>Correction:</b></i> There was "no consensus" to include search results from Wikibooks. Therefore, a <a href="https://phabricator.wikimedia.org/T168697" class="extiw" title="phab:T168697">Phabricator task</a> was filed to suppress those search results.</dd>
<dd><i><b>Update:</b></i> <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Village_pump_(policy)&amp;oldid=792670658#RfC:_Should_Wikibooks_pages_be_displayed_in_search_results">Another discussion</a> resulted in "no consensus" to include Wikibooks, but the task to suppress it from the cross-wiki search results was declined. A <a href="https://phabricator.wikimedia.org/T171803" class="extiw" title="phab:T171803">task to sort the sister projects snippets</a> was made instead.</dd>
</dl>
</li>
<li>Do you have need for a specialized script that doesn't exist yet, or have an idea for a cool new script or gadget? Post requests and ideas at <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:User_scripts/Requests" title="Wikipedia:User scripts/Requests">Wikipedia:User scripts/Requests</a></b>.</li>
<li>Free subscriptions to high-quality paywalled journals, newspaper archives, and online reference works are available for Wikipedia editors. For more information, see <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:The_Wikipedia_Library/Databases" title="Wikipedia:The Wikipedia Library/Databases">Wikipedia:The Wikipedia Library/Databases</a></b></li>

<h2 style="display: block; margin: 0 0 .75em; border: 0; padding: .1em .5em .5em; background-color: #ffebcd; font-weight: bold;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Tip_of_the_day">Tip of the day</span></h2>
<div role="complementary" aria-labelledby="totd-label" style="overflow: hidden; box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0.5em auto 0; border: solid medium #ADD8E6; -moz-border-radius: 15px; -webkit-border-radius: 15px; border-radius: 15px; width: 90%; padding: 1.4em 0.9em 1em 1.2em;">
<div id="totd-label" style="font-size: 90%;"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Tip_of_the_day" title="Wikipedia:Tip of the day">Tip of the day...</a></div>
<div class="floatright"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Simple_light_bulb_graphic.png/120px-Simple_light_bulb_graphic.png" width="120" height="125" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Simple_light_bulb_graphic.png/180px-Simple_light_bulb_graphic.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Simple_light_bulb_graphic.png/240px-Simple_light_bulb_graphic.png 2x" data-file-width="717" data-file-height="744" /></div>
<div class="center" style="width:auto; margin-left:auto; margin-right:auto;"><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>How to link to word definitions</b></span></div>
<p>Let's say you are writing an article and you want to create a link to the definition of a word the understanding of which is crucial to understanding the article. But in this hypothetical situation, you don't want to link to the Wikipedia article for that word because it would be overkill. Wikipedia has a sister project that presents hundreds of thousands of definitions. It's called <a href="/wiki/Wiktionary" title="Wiktionary">Wiktionary</a>. Here's how you would provide a link to the definition of "understanding":</p>
<p>both <b>[[wiktionary:understanding|]]</b> and <b>[[wikt:understanding|]]</b> will look like this:</p>
<dl>
<dd><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/understanding" class="extiw" title="wikt:understanding">understanding</a></dd>
</dl>
<p>and link to the definition of the word "understanding".</p>
<p>Notice that the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Tip_of_the_day/September_19,_2006" title="Wikipedia:Tip of the day/September 19, 2006">"pipe trick"</a> (|) was used in the links above.</p>
