			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Dudum siquidem</i></h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pope_Alexander_Vi.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Pope_Alexander_Vi.jpg/220px-Pope_Alexander_Vi.jpg" width="220" height="291" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Pope_Alexander_Vi.jpg/330px-Pope_Alexander_Vi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Pope_Alexander_Vi.jpg/440px-Pope_Alexander_Vi.jpg 2x" data-file-width="780" data-file-height="1031" /></a>

<p><i><b>Dudum siquidem</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "A short while ago") was a <a href="/wiki/Papal_bull" title="Papal bull">papal bull</a> issued by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Alexander_VI" title="Pope Alexander VI">Pope Alexander VI</a> on <span class="nowrap">26 September 1493</span>, one of the <a href="/wiki/Bulls_of_Donation" title="Bulls of Donation">Bulls of Donation</a> addressed to the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs" title="Catholic Monarchs">Catholic Monarchs</a> <a href="/wiki/Isabella_I_of_Castile" title="Isabella I of Castile">Isabella I</a> of <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">Castile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_Aragon" title="Ferdinand II of Aragon">Ferdinand II</a> of <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon" title="Crown of Aragon">Aragon</a> which supplemented the bull <i><a href="/wiki/Inter_caetera" title="Inter caetera">Inter caetera</a></i> and purported to grant to them "all islands and mainlands whatsoever, found and to be found, discovered and to be discovered, that are or may be or may seem to be in the route of navigation or travel towards the west or south, whether they be in western parts, or in the regions of the south and east and of India".<sup id="cite_ref-englishtext_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-englishtext-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dudum_siquidem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bull <i><a href="/wiki/Aeterni_regis" title="Aeterni regis">Aeterni regis</a></i> of 1481, delivered by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Sixtus_IV" title="Pope Sixtus IV">Pope Sixtus IV</a>, had confirmed the substance of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Alc%C3%A1%C3%A7ovas" title="Treaty of Alcáçovas">Treaty of Alcáçovas</a>, which itself had confirmed <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">Castile</a> in its possession of the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a> and had granted to Portugal all further new lands to be won by Christendom in Africa and the <a href="/wiki/East_Indies" title="East Indies">East Indies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Losreyescatolicos.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Losreyescatolicos.jpg/220px-Losreyescatolicos.jpg" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Losreyescatolicos.jpg/330px-Losreyescatolicos.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/ac/Losreyescatolicos.jpg 2x" data-file-width="424" data-file-height="278" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Losreyescatolicos.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs" title="Catholic Monarchs">Ferdinand and Isabella</a>, to whom the bull is addressed</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>At the beginning of the year 1492 the <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">Christian reconquest of Spain</a> was <a href="/wiki/Granada_War" title="Granada War">completed</a> by the capture of <a href="/wiki/Emirate_of_Granada" title="Emirate of Granada">Granada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Voyages_of_Christopher_Columbus" title="Voyages of Christopher Columbus">voyages</a> of <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a>, seeking a westerly route to the riches of the Indies, began in the summer of the same year, and on 12 October Columbus sighted land in the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a> which he believed to be part of <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He returned to Spain, with news of what he had found, in March 1493.<sup id="cite_ref-malam_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-malam-5">[5]</a></sup> Columbus's discoveries in the <i><a href="/wiki/Annus_mirabilis" title="Annus mirabilis">annus mirabilis</a></i> created a competitive frenzy between the two principal sea-powers of the day, Portugal, and the new <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish kingdom</a> based on Castile and Aragon.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>A papal bull delivered on 4 May 1493, <i><a href="/wiki/Inter_caetera" title="Inter caetera">Inter caetera</a></i>, attempted to divide the non-Christian world beyond Europe between Portugal and Spain, from the point of view of future conquests. Among much else, it states:<sup id="cite_ref-nativeweb_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nativeweb-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Among other works well pleasing to the Divine Majesty and cherished of our heart, this assuredly ranks highest, that in our times especially the Catholic faith and the Christian religion be exalted and be everywhere increased and spread, that the health of souls be cared for and that barbarous nations be overthrown and brought to the faith itself ...you should appoint to the aforesaid mainlands and islands worthy, God-fearing, learned, skilled, and experienced men, in order to instruct the aforesaid inhabitants and residents in the Catholic faith and train them in good morals.<sup id="cite_ref-nativeweb_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nativeweb-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>This papal command signalled the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Spanish colonization of the Americas">Spanish colonization</a> of much of the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a>. An important, although accidental, effect of the combination of this papal bull and of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Tordesillas" title="Treaty of Tordesillas">Treaty of Tordesillas</a> was that nearly all of the Pacific Ocean and the <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_west_coast_of_North_America" title="History of the west coast of North America">west coast of North America</a> (the existence of both of which was still unknown) were allotted to Spain. However, it is still unclear whether the pope was issuing, so far as he could, a gift of <a href="/wiki/Sovereignty" title="Sovereignty">sovereignty</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Feudalism" title="Feudalism">feudal infeodation</a> or investiture. Differing interpretations have been argued.<sup id="cite_ref-verzijl_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-verzijl-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Spanish are thought to have requested an extension to what they had already been granted by the pope because they foresaw that there were richer territories still to be discovered than those which were yet known.<sup id="cite_ref-davidson_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-davidson-9">[9]</a></sup> In August 1493 the Portuguese ambassadors <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pero_Diaz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pero Diaz (page does not exist)">Pero Diaz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rui_de_Pina" title="Rui de Pina">Rui de Pina</a> were in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> negotiating with the Spanish over several issues, including the Portuguese intention to send a fleet to the New World to capture the islands discovered by Columbus, when Ferdinand and Isabella sent an appeal to the pope which resulted in the granting of <i>Dudum siquidem</i>, dated 26 September.<sup id="cite_ref-davenport_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-davenport-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The day before, on 25 September, Columbus had sailed from <a href="/wiki/Cadiz" class="mw-redirect" title="Cadiz">Cadiz</a> on his second voyage of exploration, at the head of a fleet of seventeen ships containing some 1,200 people. Many of them were passengers who intended to make a new life in the Indies.<sup id="cite_ref-malam_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-malam-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Provisions">Provisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dudum_siquidem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Provisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Iberian_Union_Empires.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Iberian_Union_Empires.png/350px-Iberian_Union_Empires.png" width="350" height="162" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Iberian_Union_Empires.png/525px-Iberian_Union_Empires.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Iberian_Union_Empires.png/700px-Iberian_Union_Empires.png 2x" data-file-width="1357" data-file-height="628" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Iberian_Union_Empires.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Spanish (red) and Portuguese (blue) empires about 1600, not showing the unsettled areas claimed by Spain</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The bull <i>Dudum siquidem</i>, which is also known as the "extension of the donation", extending the land grants of <a href="/wiki/Inter_caetera" title="Inter caetera">Inter caetera</a>, foresees that "it may happen that your envoys and captains, or vassals, while voyaging towards the west or the south, might bring their ships to land in eastern regions and there discover islands and mainlands that belonged or belong to India".<sup id="cite_ref-englishtext_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-englishtext-1">[1]</a></sup> In view of that, the pope adds to his previous grants to Ferdinand and Isabella</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>...just as if in the aforesaid letters full and express mention had been made thereof, we do in like manner amplify and extend our aforesaid gift, grant, assignment, and letters, with all and singular the clauses contained in the said letters, to all islands and mainlands whatsoever, found and to be found, discovered and to be discovered, that are or may be or may seem to be in the route of navigation or travel towards the west or south, whether they be in western parts, or in the regions of the south and east and of India. We grant to you and your aforesaid heirs and successors full and free power through your own authority, exercised through yourselves or through another or others, freely to take corporal possession of the said islands and countries and to hold them forever, and to defend them against whosoever may oppose.<sup id="cite_ref-englishtext_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-englishtext-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Effect">Effect</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dudum_siquidem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Effect">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pope Alexander VI was himself a native of <a href="/wiki/Valencia" title="Valencia">Valencia</a> in Spain, and his bulls seem to have been intended to assist Spanish expansion more than that of Portugal. While he confirmed the concessions already granted to the Portuguese,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> the pope's <i>Dudum siquidem</i> increased the rights of Spain to the prejudice of Portugal.<sup id="cite_ref-davidson_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-davidson-9">[9]</a></sup> The specific mention of India caused consternation in Portugal, which mounted a diplomatic campaign aimed at damage limitation.<sup id="cite_ref-parry_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parry-12">[12]</a></sup> The pope refused to reconsider his position, so <a href="/wiki/John_II_of_Portugal" title="John II of Portugal">King John II of Portugal</a> negotiated directly with Ferdinand and Isabella, accepting <i>Inter caetera</i> as the starting point. This resulted in an agreement to move the boundary line established in <i>Inter caetera</i> 270 leagues further to the west, given effect as the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Tordesillas" title="Treaty of Tordesillas">Treaty of Tordesillas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-parry_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parry-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In later centuries <i>Dudum siquidem</i> was understood as giving Spain a free hand in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. It developed a long-term presence there, especially in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-davidson_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-davidson-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Texts">Texts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dudum_siquidem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Texts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the American historian <a href="/wiki/Frances_Gardiner_Davenport" title="Frances Gardiner Davenport">Frances Gardiner Davenport</a>, no copy of this bull has been found in the records of the <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican</a>, but in the <a href="/wiki/General_Archive_of_the_Indies" title="General Archive of the Indies">General Archive of the Indies</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> two original manuscripts of it survive, both with the <a href="/wiki/Papal_seal" class="mw-redirect" title="Papal seal">papal lead seal</a> attached, although not marked "Registrata", as is usual.<sup id="cite_ref-davenport_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-davenport-10">[10]</a></sup> Until these originals of the bull were revealed during the 20th century, there was some doubt about the authenticity of what had been quoted from it.<sup id="cite_ref-davidson_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-davidson-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
