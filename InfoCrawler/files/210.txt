<p><i><b>Golden Hind</b></i> was an English <a href="/wiki/Galleon" title="Galleon">galleon</a> best known for her privateering <a href="/wiki/Circumnavigation" title="Circumnavigation">circumnavigation</a> of the globe between 1577 and 1580, captained by <a href="/wiki/Francis_Drake" title="Francis Drake">Sir Francis Drake</a>. She was originally known as <i>Pelican</i>, but was renamed by Drake mid-voyage in 1578, in honour of his patron, <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Hatton" title="Christopher Hatton">Sir Christopher Hatton</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Crest_(heraldry)" title="Crest (heraldry)">crest</a> was a golden 'hind' (a female <a href="/wiki/Red_deer" title="Red deer">red deer</a>). Hatton was one of the principal sponsors of Drake's world voyage. There is one full-size replica in existence in London, on the south bank of the <a href="/wiki/Thames" class="mw-redirect" title="Thames">Thames</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Hind&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Golden_Hind_1581.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b8/Golden_Hind_1581.jpg/220px-Golden_Hind_1581.jpg" width="220" height="170" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b8/Golden_Hind_1581.jpg/330px-Golden_Hind_1581.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/b/b8/Golden_Hind_1581.jpg 2x" data-file-width="359" data-file-height="277" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Golden_Hind_1581.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<i>Golden Hind</i> at Deptford 1581</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1577, <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England" title="Elizabeth I of England">Queen Elizabeth</a> partly sponsored Sir Francis Drake as the leader of an expedition intended to pass around <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan" title="Strait of Magellan">Strait of Magellan</a> and to explore the coast that lay beyond. The queen's support was advantageous; Drake had official approval to benefit himself and the queen as well as to cause the maximum damage to the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spaniards</a>. This would eventually culminate in the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Spanish_War_(1585%E2%80%931604)" title="Anglo-Spanish War (1585–1604)">Anglo–Spanish War</a>. Before setting sail, Drake met the queen face-to-face for the first time and she said to him, "We would gladly be revenged on the King of Spain for divers injuries that we have received."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The explicit object was to "find out places meet to have traffic." Drake, however, acted as a <a href="/wiki/Privateer" title="Privateer">privateer</a>, with unofficial support from Queen Elizabeth.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Drake commissioned his flagship in Plymouth. The keel was registered in 1575. The majority of the work was done in 1576, and it was completed early the next year.<sup id="cite_ref-Sugden_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sugden-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OGH_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OGH-4">[4]</a></sup> She is described as a "mid 16th century warship during the transition from the carrack to the galleon," and displaced about 100 tons.<sup id="cite_ref-OGH_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OGH-4">[4]</a></sup> He first named his flagship <i>Pelican</i>, but renamed her <i>Golden Hind</i> on 20 August 1578<sup id="cite_ref-Famous_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Famous-5">[5]</a></sup> to honor his patron, Sir <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Hatton" title="Christopher Hatton">Christopher Hatton</a>, whose family crest was a golden hind (a female red deer).</p>
<p>He set sail in December 1577 with five small ships, manned by 164 men, and reached the <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> coast in early 1578.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 March 1579, now in the Pacific Ocean, off the coast of Ecuador, <i>Golden Hind</i> challenged and captured the Spanish galleon <a href="/wiki/Spanish_ship_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_la_Concepci%C3%B3n_(1570)" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish ship Nuestra Señora de la Concepción (1570)"><i>Nuestra Señora de la Concepción</i></a>. This galleon had the largest treasure captured to that date: over 360,000 pesos (equivalent to around £480m in 2017).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The six tons of treasure took six days to transship and included 26 tons of silver, half a ton of gold, porcelain, jewellery, coins and jewels.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 26 September 1580, Francis Drake sailed his ship into Plymouth Harbour with only 56 of the original crew of 80 left aboard, the ship was unloaded at Saltash Castle nearby where the treasure offloading was supervised by the Queens guards.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Over half of the proceeds went to the Queen and country and were used to pay off the annual debt in its entirety, Queen Elizabeth I herself went aboard <i>Golden Hind</i>, which was then permanently at Deptford on the Thames Estuary, where she had requested it be placed on permanent display as the first 'museum ship'. There she shrewdly asked the French ambassador to bestow a knighthood on Drake.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Her share of the treasure came to at least £160,000: "enough to pay off her entire government debt and still have £40,000 left over to invest in a new trading company for the <a href="/wiki/Levant" title="Levant">Levant</a>. Her return and that of other investors came to more than £47 for every £1 invested, or a total return of 4700%."<sup id="cite_ref-Waves_2004_p.94_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Waves_2004_p.94-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>After Drake's circumnavigation, <i>Golden Hind</i> was maintained for public exhibition at the dockyard at <a href="/wiki/Deptford" title="Deptford">Deptford</a>, London. The ship remained there from 1580 to approximately 1650 (some 45 years after Queen Elizabeth had died) before the ship eventually rotted away and was finally broken up. In 1668, the keeper of the stores at Deptford, one John Davies of Camberwell, had the best remaining timber of <i>Golden Hind</i> made into a chair which was presented to the <a href="/wiki/Bodleian_Library" title="Bodleian Library">Bodleian Library</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OGH_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OGH-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Oxford_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oxford-12">[12]</a></sup> where it remains (with a replica in the Great Hall, <a href="/wiki/Buckland_Abbey" title="Buckland Abbey">Buckland Abbey</a>, Devon, Drake's home and now maintained by the <a href="/wiki/National_Trust_for_Places_of_Historic_Interest_or_Natural_Beauty" title="National Trust for Places of Historic Interest or Natural Beauty">National Trust</a>).</p>
<p>A table, known as the cupboard (pronounced "cup-board"), in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Temple" title="Middle Temple">Middle Temple</a> Hall (in London) is also reputed to have been made from the wood of <i>Golden Hind.</i> Upon the cupboard is placed the roll of members of Middle Temple, which new members sign when they are called to the Bar. The ship's lantern was hung in the vestibule of Middle Temple Hall but was destroyed during the Second World War.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Replicas">Replicas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Hind&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Replicas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Replica_in_Essex">Replica in Essex</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Hind&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Replica in Essex">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A replica of <i>Golden Hind</i> was constructed at Peter Pan's Playground (now <a href="/wiki/Adventure_Island_(amusement_park)" title="Adventure Island (amusement park)">Adventure Island (amusement park)</a>), <a href="/wiki/Southend-on-Sea" title="Southend-on-Sea">Southend-on-Sea</a>, Essex. It was constructed from 1947 and opened in 1949 together with a waxworks. It was popular at first, but by 1992 attendances had dropped; rising maintenance costs together with the need for major renovation to the wooden structure caused its closure in 1997. The ship was replaced by a replica of Blackbeard's <i><a href="/wiki/Queen_Anne%27s_Revenge" title="Queen Anne's Revenge">Queen Anne's Revenge</a></i>. This ship was demolished in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<p>A tourist attraction replica of <i>Golden Hind</i> has been permanently moored in the harbour of the sea port of <a href="/wiki/Brixham" title="Brixham">Brixham</a> in Devon (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Golden_Hind&amp;params=50_23_48_N_3_30_46_W_type:landmark_region:GB-TOB"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">50°23′48″N</span> <span class="longitude">3°30′46″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">50.39667°N 3.51278°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">50.39667; -3.51278</span></span></span></a></span>) since 1963 following its use in the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Sir_Francis_Drake_(TV_series)" title="Sir Francis Drake (TV series)">Sir Francis Drake</a></i>, which was filmed in and around the bays of Torbay and Dartmouth. The replica ship used in the TV series cost £25,000 to construct and had no rear gallery or gun deck and was a converted fishing boat .<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The ship sank whilst under tow in 1987 to Dartmouth for restoration in heavy seas and could not be saved. A second replica, which is not full size, was completed in 1988 and stands in the harbour welcoming thousands of visitors annually. The current vessel is based on a steel barge and could never sail. It does not have a gun deck. <sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="London_replica_–_Golden_Hinde"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="London_replica_.E2.80.93_Golden_Hinde">London replica – <i>Golden Hinde</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golden_Hind&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: London replica – Golden Hinde">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Golden_Hinde_(1973)" title="Golden Hinde (1973)">Golden Hinde (1973)</a></div>
<p>A full-size reconstruction of the ship <i>Golden Hinde</i> was built by traditional handcraft in <a href="/wiki/Appledore,_North_Devon" class="mw-redirect" title="Appledore, North Devon">Appledore, North Devon</a> and launched in 1973. <i>Golden Hinde</i> was the result of three years research and construction. Since then she has travelled more than 140,000 miles (225,000&#160;km). She sailed from Plymouth on her maiden voyage in late 1974, arriving on 8 May 1975 <sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>. In 1979 she sailed to Japan to make the movie <i>Shogun</i>, after which she returned to the UK having completed a circumnavigation. Between 1981 and 1984, she was berthed in England and was established as an educational museum. In 1984–85 she sailed around the <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">British Isles</a> and then crossed the Atlantic to St Thomas in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>. In 1986, she passed through the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal" title="Panama Canal">Panama Canal</a> to sail on to <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a> where she was the main attraction in the Marine Plaza at Expo86. In 1987, she began a tour of US coastal cities spending two years on the Pacific coast. In late 1988 she passed back through the Panama Canal to continue port visits on the Gulf and East Coasts of the USA. In 1992 she returned home to the UK and spent the next four years visiting ports in Europe. Since 1996 she has been berthed at St Mary Overie Dock, in <a href="/wiki/Bankside" title="Bankside">Bankside</a>, <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Southwark" title="London Borough of Southwark">Southwark</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> where she is open to the public and hosts a range of educational programmes.<sup id="cite_ref-Devon_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Devon-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
