<p><b>Maximilian ("Maxl" or "Max") Morlock</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈmɔʁlɔk]</a></span>; 11 May 1925 in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> – 10 September 1994 in Nuremberg) was one of the most popular <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> players in the 1950s and early 1960s. In his time with the <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">West German national team</a>, he earned 26 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cap (football)">caps</a> and scored 21 goals. His position was that of an <a href="/wiki/Inside_right" class="mw-redirect" title="Inside right">inside right</a> forward.</p>
<p>In his youth he learned to play football at Eintracht Nürnberg. In 1940 he became a member of the then famous <a href="/wiki/1._FC_N%C3%BCrnberg" title="1. FC Nürnberg">1. FC Nürnberg</a>, debuting in the first team on 30 November 1941. Until 1964 he appeared more than 900 times in the first team of the so-called <i>Club</i> and scored about 700 goals. In 1948 and 1961 he led the team to German championships, in 1962 to the <a href="/wiki/DFB-Pokal" title="DFB-Pokal">German Cup</a>. 38 years old he even appeared 21 times in the <a href="/wiki/1963%E2%80%9364_Bundesliga" title="1963–64 Bundesliga">founding season</a> of the German <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He also was top scorer of the Oberliga Süd in 1950–51 and 1951–52.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>His first cap for the national team was in 1950, when he played instead of the injured <a href="/wiki/Fritz_Walter" title="Fritz Walter">Fritz Walter</a>. He was a member of the West German team that won their first <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/1954_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1954 FIFA World Cup">1954</a>. In the final match against <a href="/wiki/Hungary_national_football_team" title="Hungary national football team">Hungary</a> Morlock scored West Germany's first goal to start the comeback after going 2–0 down. He received his last cap in a friendly game against <a href="/wiki/Egypt_national_football_team" title="Egypt national football team">Egypt</a> in December 1958.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a player, Morlock's strengths were a sound technique coupled with fighting spirit. As a linkman he felt at home best between defense and attack, but he was also dangerous in front of the goal.<sup id="cite_ref-Bitter_p.323_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bitter_p.323-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Max Morlock died from <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a> in 1994, aged 69.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Max_Morlock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>In 1961 he was voted <a href="/wiki/German_Footballer_of_the_Year" class="mw-redirect" title="German Footballer of the Year">German Footballer of the Year</a> by the Association of German Sports Journalists.</li>
<li>In 1995, less than a year after his death, the <a href="/wiki/Town_square" title="Town square">square</a> in front of the Frankenstadion, home of the 1. FC Nürnberg, was renamed <i>Max-Morlock-Platz</i> in his honour. The stadium's <a href="/wiki/Address_(geography)" title="Address (geography)">postal address</a> is <i>Max-Morlock-Platz 1</i>.</li>
<li>In 2006, a majority of fans voted in favour of renaming the Frankenstadion itself into "<a href="/wiki/Max-Morlock-Stadion" title="Max-Morlock-Stadion">Max-Morlock-Stadion</a>", but the <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">city of Nuremberg</a> won a sponsorship deal with a local bank, which included renaming the stadium EasyCredit-Stadion after one of that bank's financial products. His name was finally used as the stadium's name in July 2017.</li>
</ul>
