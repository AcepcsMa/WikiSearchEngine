<p>This article lists the Margraves and Electors of <a href="/wiki/Margraviate_of_Brandenburg" title="Margraviate of Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a> during the period of time that Brandenburg was a constituent state of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>.</p>
<p>The Mark, or <i>March</i>, of Brandenburg was one of the primary constituent states of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>. It was created in 1157 as the <a href="/wiki/Margraviate_of_Brandenburg" title="Margraviate of Brandenburg">Margraviate of Brandenburg</a> by <a href="/wiki/Albert_the_Bear" title="Albert the Bear">Albert the Bear</a>, Margrave of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_March" title="Northern March">Northern March</a>. In 1356, by the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Bull_of_1356" title="Golden Bull of 1356">Golden Bull</a> of <a href="/wiki/Charles_IV,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles IV, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles IV</a>, the Margrave of Brandenburg was given the permanent right to participate in the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_election" title="Imperial election">election</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a> with the title of <a href="/wiki/Prince-elector" title="Prince-elector">Elector</a> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Kurfürst</i></span>).</p>
<p>The early rulers came from several different dynasties, but from 1415 Brandenburg and its successor states were ruled by the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Hohenzollern" title="House of Hohenzollern">House of Hohenzollern</a> for over 500 years. From 1618 onward, Brandenburg was ruled in <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">personal union</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Prussia" title="Duchy of Prussia">Duchy of Prussia</a>. The Hohenzollerns raised Prussia to a kingdom as the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a> in 1701, and from then on Brandenburg was <i>de facto</i> treated as part of the kingdom even though it was legally still part of the Holy Roman Empire. The titles of Margrave of Brandenburg and Elector of Brandenburg were abolished along with the Holy Roman Empire in 1806 (although they were one of the titles of the Kings of Prussia until 1918), and Brandenburg was formally integrated into Prussia. From 1871 to 1918 the Hohenzollerns were also the <a href="/wiki/German_Emperor" title="German Emperor">German Emperors</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Upper_Presidents_of_Brandenburg">Upper Presidents of Brandenburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_rulers_of_Brandenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Upper Presidents of Brandenburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1815 Brandenburg was constituted as the Prussian <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Brandenburg" title="Province of Brandenburg">Province of Brandenburg</a> without a sovereign ruler, but with <a href="/wiki/Oberpr%C3%A4sident" class="mw-redirect" title="Oberpräsident">Upper Presidents</a> appointed by the central Prussian government. The upper president carried out central prerogatives on the provincial level and supervised the implementation of central policy on the lower levels of administration.</p>
<ul>
<li>1815–1824: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Georg_Friedrich_Christian_von_Heydebreck&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Georg Friedrich Christian von Heydebreck (page does not exist)">Georg Friedrich Christian von Heydebreck</a> (1765–1828)</li>
<li>1825–1840: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Friedrich_Magnus_von_Bassewitz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Friedrich Magnus von Bassewitz (page does not exist)">Friedrich Magnus von Bassewitz</a> (1773–1858)</li>
<li>1840–1842: vacancy</li>

<li>1848–1849: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_von_Patow&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Robert von Patow (page does not exist)">Robert von Patow</a> (1804–1890), per pro</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Land_Directors_of_Brandenburg">Land Directors of Brandenburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_rulers_of_Brandenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Land Directors of Brandenburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 1875, with the strengthening of <a href="/wiki/Self-rule" class="mw-redirect" title="Self-rule">self-rule</a> within the provinces, the urban and rural <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Prussia" title="Districts of Prussia">counties</a> elected representatives for the provincial diets (<a href="/wiki/Provinziallandtag" class="mw-redirect" title="Provinziallandtag">Provinziallandtage</a>). These parliaments legislated within the competences transferred to the provinces. The provincial diet of Brandenburg elected a provincial executive body (government), the provincial committee (Provinzialausschuss), and a head of province, the land director (<a href="/wiki/Landesdirektor" class="mw-redirect" title="Landesdirektor">Landesdirektor</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Self-rule was abolished under the Nazi dictatorship.</p>
<ul>
<li>1876–1896: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Erdmann_Karl_Gerhard_von_Levetzow&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Albert Erdmann Karl Gerhard von Levetzow (page does not exist)">Albert Erdmann Karl Gerhard von Levetzow</a> (1827–1903), <a href="/wiki/German_Conservative_Party" title="German Conservative Party">German Conservative Party</a></li>
<li>1896–1912: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Otto_Karl_Gottlob_von_Manteuffel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Otto Karl Gottlob von Manteuffel (page does not exist)">Otto Karl Gottlob von Manteuffel</a> (1844–1913), German Conservative Party</li>
<li>1912–1930: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Joachim_von_Winterfeldt-Menkin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Joachim von Winterfeldt-Menkin (page does not exist)">Joachim von Winterfeldt-Menkin</a> (1865–1945)</li>
<li>1930–1933: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hugo_Swart&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hugo Swart (page does not exist)">Hugo Swart</a> (1885–1952)</li>
</ul>

<p><br />
After the defeat of Nazi Germany in the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>, Brandenburg, which had previously been merely a province of <a href="/wiki/Prussia_(state)" class="mw-redirect" title="Prussia (state)">Prussia</a>, re-emerged as a German <a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany"><i>Land</i></a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prime_Minister_of_Brandenburg.2C_1945.E2.80.931952">Prime Minister of Brandenburg, 1945–1952</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_rulers_of_Brandenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Prime Minister of Brandenburg, 1945–1952">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Karl_Steinhoff" title="Karl Steinhoff">Karl Steinhoff</a> (SPD/SED), 1945–1949.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Jahn" title="Rudolf Jahn">Rudolf Jahn</a> (SED), 1949–1952.</li>
</ul>
<p>After being abolished in a reorganization of the territories administered by the <a href="/wiki/German_Democratic_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="German Democratic Republic">German Democratic Republic</a> (East Germany), the <i>Land</i> Brandenburg was restored in the prelude to <a href="/wiki/German_reunification" title="German reunification">German unification</a> in 1990.</p>
