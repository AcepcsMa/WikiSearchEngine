<p>This is a list of places in <b>Germany</b> which have standing links to local communities in other countries, or in other parts of Germany (mostly across the former <a href="/wiki/Inner_German_border" title="Inner German border">inner German border</a>). In most cases, the association, especially when formalised by local government, is known as "<a href="/wiki/Town_twinning" class="mw-redirect" title="Town twinning">town twinning</a>" (though other terms, such as "partner towns" or "sister cities" are sometimes used instead), and while most of the places included are towns, the list also comprises villages, cities, districts, counties, etc. with similar links.</p>
<p>A few twin places have a historical <i>German name</i>, especially in bordering countries or areas with mixed languages.</p>
<p>For German-German twins there are abbreviated state names: <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a> (<b>BW</b>), <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a> (<b>BA</b>), <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> (<b>BE</b>), <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg" title="Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a> (<b>BB</b>), <a href="/wiki/Bremen_(state)" title="Bremen (state)">Bremen</a> (<b>BN</b>), <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a> (<b>HB</b>), <a href="/wiki/Hesse" title="Hesse">Hesse</a> (<b>HE</b>), <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a> (<b>LS</b>), <a href="/wiki/Mecklenburg-Vorpommern" title="Mecklenburg-Vorpommern">Mecklenburg-Vorpommern</a> (<b>MV</b>), <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a> (<b>NRW</b>), <a href="/wiki/Rhineland-Palatinate" title="Rhineland-Palatinate">Rhineland-Palatinate</a> (<b>RP</b>), <a href="/wiki/Saarland" title="Saarland">Saarland</a> (<b>SL</b>), <a href="/wiki/Saxony-Anhalt" title="Saxony-Anhalt">Saxony-Anhalt</a> (<b>SA</b>), <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a> (<b>SX</b>), <a href="/wiki/Schleswig-Holstein" title="Schleswig-Holstein">Schleswig-Holstein</a> (<b>SH</b>) and <a href="/wiki/Thuringia" title="Thuringia">Thuringia</a> (<b>TH</b>).</p>
