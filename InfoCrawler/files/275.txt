<p><i><b>Regierungsbezirk</b></i> (<small>pronounced</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ʁeˈɡiːʁʊŋsbəˌt͡sɪʁk]</a></span>, often abbreviated to <b>Reg.-Bez.</b>; English: <a href="/wiki/Administrative_district" class="mw-redirect" title="Administrative district">Administrative district</a>) is an administrative region at <a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany">federal state</a> level in Germany. The regional authority is called a <i>Regierungspräsidium</i> or <i>Bezirksregierung</i> (district government) and is headed by a <i>Regierungspräsident</i> (district president).</p>
<p>The <i>Regierungsbezirke</i> do not pass any legislation. Within the federal state authority, they act as a mid-level agency, concerned mostly with administrative decisions on a regional level for the affiliated rural or urban <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany">districts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Translations">Translations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Regierungsbezirk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Translations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><span lang="de">Regierungsbezirk</span></i> is variously translated as "governmental district",<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> "administrative district"<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> or "province",<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> with the first two being closest literal translations.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Regierungsbezirk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first <i>Regierungsbezirke</i> were established in the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bavaria" title="Kingdom of Bavaria">Kingdom of Bavaria</a> (1808) and in the course of the <a href="/wiki/Prussian_reforms" class="mw-redirect" title="Prussian reforms">Prussian reforms</a> between 1808 and 1816, when the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a> divided its <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Prussia" title="Provinces of Prussia">provinces</a> into 25 <i>Regierungsbezirke</i>; eventually Prussia had 37 such districts in 12 provinces. By <a href="/wiki/Unification_of_Germany" title="Unification of Germany">German unification</a> in 1871, the concept of Regierungsbezirke had been adopted by most <a href="/wiki/States_of_the_German_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="States of the German Empire">States of the German Empire</a>.</p>
<p>The <i>Regierungsbezirke</i> of North Rhine-Westphalia are in direct continuation of those created in the Prussian <a href="/wiki/Rhine_Province" title="Rhine Province">Rhine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Westphalia" title="Province of Westphalia">Westphalia</a> provinces in 1816. Similar entities in other states were initially named <i>Kreishauptmannschaft</i> (in Saxony) or <i>Kreis</i> (in Bavaria and <a href="/wiki/W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Württemberg">Württemberg</a>) (not to be confused with the present-day <i>Kreis</i> or <i>Landkreis</i> districts) or <i>province</i> in Hesse. In <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> the naming was unified to <i>Regierungsbezirk</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regierungsbezirke_by_state"><i>Regierungsbezirke</i> by state</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Regierungsbezirk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Regierungsbezirke by state">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Currently, only four large-area states out of 16 in total are divided into <i>Regierungsbezirke</i>; all others are directly divided into districts without mid-level agencies. Those four states are divided into a total of 19 <i>Regierungsbezirke</i>, ranging in population from 5,255,000 (<a href="/wiki/Duesseldorf_(region)" class="mw-redirect" title="Duesseldorf (region)">Düsseldorf</a>) to 1,065,000 (<a href="/wiki/Gie%C3%9Fen_(region)" class="mw-redirect" title="Gießen (region)">Gießen</a>):</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a>: <a href="/wiki/Freiburg_(region)" title="Freiburg (region)">Freiburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karlsruhe_(region)" title="Karlsruhe (region)">Karlsruhe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart_(region)" title="Stuttgart (region)">Stuttgart</a>, <a href="/wiki/T%C3%BCbingen_(region)" title="Tübingen (region)">Tübingen</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>: <a href="/wiki/Upper_Bavaria" title="Upper Bavaria">Upper Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lower_Bavaria" title="Lower Bavaria">Lower Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Upper_Palatinate" title="Upper Palatinate">Upper Palatinate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Upper_Franconia" title="Upper Franconia">Upper Franconia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Middle_Franconia" title="Middle Franconia">Middle Franconia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lower_Franconia" title="Lower Franconia">Lower Franconia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swabia_(Bavaria)" title="Swabia (Bavaria)">Swabia</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hesse" title="Hesse">Hesse</a>: <a href="/wiki/Darmstadt_(region)" title="Darmstadt (region)">Darmstadt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gie%C3%9Fen_(region)" class="mw-redirect" title="Gießen (region)">Gießen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kassel_(region)" title="Kassel (region)">Kassel</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>: <a href="/wiki/Arnsberg_(region)" title="Arnsberg (region)">Arnsberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cologne_(region)" title="Cologne (region)">Cologne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Detmold_(region)" title="Detmold (region)">Detmold</a>, <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf_(region)" title="Düsseldorf (region)">Düsseldorf</a>, <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnster_(region)" title="Münster (region)">Münster</a></li>
</ul>
<p>On 1 January 2000 <a href="/wiki/Rhineland-Palatinate" title="Rhineland-Palatinate">Rhineland-Palatinate</a> disbanded its three <i>Regierungsbezirke</i> <a href="/wiki/Koblenz_(region)" title="Koblenz (region)">Koblenz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rheinhessen-Pfalz" title="Rheinhessen-Pfalz">Rheinhessen-Pfalz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trier_(region)" title="Trier (region)">Trier</a> – the employees and assets of the three <i>Bezirksregierungen</i> were converted into three public authorities responsible for the whole state, each covering a part of the former responsibilities of the <i>Bezirksregierung</i>.</p>
<p>On 1 January 2004, <a href="/wiki/Saxony-Anhalt" title="Saxony-Anhalt">Saxony-Anhalt</a> disbanded its three <i>Regierungsbezirke</i>: <a href="/wiki/Dessau_(region)" title="Dessau (region)">Dessau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halle_(region)" title="Halle (region)">Halle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Magdeburg_(region)" title="Magdeburg (region)">Magdeburg</a>. The responsibilities are now covered by a <i>Landesverwaltungsamt</i> with three offices at the former seats of the <i>Bezirksregierungen</i>.</p>
<p>At the foundation of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a> in 1946 by the merger of the three former <a href="/wiki/Free_State_of_Brunswick" title="Free State of Brunswick">Free States of Brunswick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Free_State_of_Oldenburg" title="Free State of Oldenburg">Oldenburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Free_State_of_Schaumburg-Lippe" title="Free State of Schaumburg-Lippe">Schaumburg-Lippe</a> and the former <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Prussia" title="Provinces of Prussia">Prussian province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Hanover" title="Province of Hanover">Hanover</a> the former two states became <i>Verwaltungsbezirke</i> (roughly administrative regions of extended competence) within Lower Saxony besides the less autonomous Prussian-style <i>Regierungsbezirke</i> comprising the former Province of Hanover and the tiny Schaumburg-Lippe. These differences were levelled on 1 January 1978, when four territorially redeployed <i>Regierungsbezirke</i> replaced the two <i>Verwaltungsbezirke</i> and the old six <i>Regierungsbezirke</i>: Brunswick and Oldenburg as well as Aurich, <a href="/wiki/Hanover_(region)" title="Hanover (region)">Hanover</a> (remaining mostly the same), Hildesheim, Lüneburg (old), Osnabrück and <a href="/wiki/Stade_(region)" title="Stade (region)">Stade</a>. On 1 January 2005, Lower Saxony disbanded its remaining four <i>Regierungsbezirke</i>: <a href="/wiki/Braunschweig_(region)" title="Braunschweig (region)">Brunswick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hanover_(region)" title="Hanover (region)">Hanover</a>, <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCneburg_(region)" title="Lüneburg (region)">Lüneburg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Weser-Ems" title="Weser-Ems">Weser-Ems</a>.</p>
<p>On 1 August 2008, <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a> restructured its districts (<i>Landkreise</i>) and changed the name of its <i>Regierungsbezirke</i> to <i>Direktionsbezirke</i>. This was necessary because one of the new districts did not fit with the borders of the old <i>Regierungsbezirke</i> and some responsibilities are now covered by the districts. The <i>Direktionsbezirke</i> are still named <i>Chemnitz</i>, <i>Dresden</i> and <i>Leipzig</i>. As of 1 March 2012, the <i>Direktionsbezirke</i> were merged into one <i>Landesdirektion</i>.</p>
<p>Four of the <a href="/wiki/New_states_of_Germany" title="New states of Germany">new federal states</a> re-established in 1990, <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg" title="Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a>, Mecklenburg-Vorpommern and <a href="/wiki/Thuringia" title="Thuringia">Thuringia</a>, decided not to implement <i>Regierungsbezirke</i>. In <a href="/wiki/Bremen_(state)" title="Bremen (state)">Bremen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schleswig-Holstein" title="Schleswig-Holstein">Schleswig-Holstein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saarland" title="Saarland">Saarland</a> they never existed.</p>
