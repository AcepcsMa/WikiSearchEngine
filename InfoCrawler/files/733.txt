<p><b>Martina Schradi</b> (/ʃʁadi/ born 1972) is a German author, cartoonist and certified psychologist from <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> She is known for <i>Oh, I see?!,</i> a collection of <a href="/wiki/Comics" title="Comics">comics</a> depicting the daily lives and struggles of people in the <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT*I</a> community.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-4">[4]</a></sup> After successfully exhibiting her work, Schradi began travelling throughout Germany to conduct workshops and readings. These workshops are designed to inform people on how to use her comics to fight prejudice.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martina_Schradi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After completing her <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">Psychology</a> diploma in <a href="/wiki/Erlangen" title="Erlangen">Erlangen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, Schradi practiced as a psychologist for several years.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> In addition, she completed degrees in Communication Design and Media Author in Nuremberg and <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a>. Eventually, she returned to sketching comics, a childhood passion, spending several years self-publishing them. Some of her comics have been released as the book <i>Oh, I see?!</i> (<i>Ach, so ist das?!</i> in the original German title) with the publishing company Zwerchfell.</p>
<h3><span id="Oh,_I_see?!"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Oh.2C_I_see.3F.21">Oh, I see?!</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martina_Schradi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Oh, I see?!">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Schradi noted discussions in the province of <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Wuerttemberg</a> on the implementation of LGBT*I themes in the school curriculum as catalyst for <i>Oh, I see?!.</i><sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">[3]</a></sup> The comics are a collection of true stories about people ages 20–68 in the LGBT*I community. Different themes are found throughout the comics including coming out stories, anonymity at work and experiences on how peers at school view <a href="/wiki/Homosexuality" title="Homosexuality">homosexuality</a>. A book was first published in 2014, but many of the comics can also be found on the official website for <i>Oh, I see?!,</i> available in German, English, Spanish and Russian. A Chinese translation is in preparation<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Beyond being a collection of people's lives, it is an anti-discrimination project supported by the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Ministry_of_Family_Affairs,_Senior_Citizens,_Women_and_Youth" title="Federal Ministry of Family Affairs, Senior Citizens, Women and Youth">Federal Ministry of Family Affairs, Senior Citizens, Women and Youth</a> in a project titled <a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toleranz_f%C3%B6rdern_-_Kompetenz_st%C3%A4rken" class="extiw" title="de:Toleranz fördern - Kompetenz stärken">Toleranz fördern – Kompetenz stärken</a>(Promoting tolerance-strengthening competence), the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.nuernberg.de/internet/menschenrechte_e/">Human Rights Office of the City of Nuremberg</a>, and the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://hms-stiftung.de/">Hannchen-Mehrzweck-Stiftung</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> More details on this project are given in a chapter which Schradi co-authored in the book <i>Teaching Gender</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pedagogy_in_Germany">Pedagogy in Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martina_Schradi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pedagogy in Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After discussions in Baden-Wuerttemberg concerning the inclusion of LGBT*I themes in the school curriculum were met with backlash, Schradi began collecting stories from people and sketched them.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">[3]</a></sup> Studies demonstrating how many people are still too afraid to out themselves at work also inspired her to do her work. In addition to this, many teachers still do not react to <a href="/wiki/Homophobia" title="Homophobia">homophobic</a> behavior in the classroom. The success of the comics gave her opportunities to be involved in how LGBT*I themes can be incorporated in the classroom.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beyond her work as a cartoonist, Schradi has been published, alongside <a href="/w/index.php?title=Christine_Burmann&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Christine Burmann (page does not exist)">Christine Burmann</a>, in a chapter of <i>Teaching Gender?</i> titled <i>Ach, so ist das?! Ein Antidiskriminierungsprojekt zu LSBTI* auch fuer die Schule (Oh, I see?! An anti-discrimination project about LSBTI* even for school).</i><sup id="cite_ref-:5_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-8">[8]</a></sup> There they discuss how comics can offer insight into the life of the LGBT*I community on a textual and visual level. More specifically, how these comics can inform people at workshops as well as further educate teachers for their curriculum. The work's relevance is cited in the prevalence of homophobia in modern-day Germany. An online questionnaire showed that almost half of the participants had been discriminated against in the previous year, and a third were <a href="/wiki/Closeted" title="Closeted">closeted</a> at their school.</p>
<p>Until 2015, she was in charge of the blog <i>Infothek Lehre,</i> which is funded by the German ministry of education and research.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It is a platform dedicated to informing teachers, and professors about current publications in the field of teaching. Schradi herself conducted interviews with people in the German education system from different fields where their ideas, methodologies, and goals were discussed.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activism">Activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martina_Schradi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Schradi has made it her mission to expose people to the daily lives and trials of people in the LGBT*I community.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-4">[4]</a></sup> This mission has been realized after her work was successfully exhibited in Nuremberg. Since then, she has introduced her work throughout Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Finland, Tunisia, Italy and Ukraine. A funding by the ministry of family has helped the team around Christine Burmann and Martina Schradi develop methodologies showing how teachers can discuss LGBT*I themes in their classrooms. In 2015, she had the opportunity to introduce her work at notable events such as the Proud At Work (PAW) conference.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-6">[6]</a></sup> Ways in which comics, such as <i>Oh, I see?!</i>, may be used as teaching material are discussed in workshops. <i>Oh, I see?!</i> is already being used at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg" title="University of Erlangen-Nuremberg">Friedrich-Alexander University</a> in conventions and workshops for the further education of teachers.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Schradi also worked as federal manager for Lambda e.V. an organization that acts on behalf of LGBT*I youth in the political and public spectrum. It also offers youth an opportunity to discuss their feelings in a non-judgmental environment, where they can be themselves.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In Schradi's own words, "We have dedicated ourselves to support the youth on their path of self-discovery and in psychological crisis situations."</p>
