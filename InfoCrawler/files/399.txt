<p>The <b>Catholic League</b> (<a href="/wiki/Latin_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <span lang="la" xml:lang="la"><i>Liga Catholica</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Katholische Liga</i></span>) was a <a href="/wiki/Coalition" title="Coalition">coalition</a> of <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_states_in_the_Holy_Roman_Empire" title="List of states in the Holy Roman Empire">states</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> formed 10 July 1609. While initially formed as a <a href="/wiki/Confederation" title="Confederation">confederation</a> to act politically to negotiate issues vis-à-vis the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Union" title="Protestant Union">Protestant Union</a> (formed 1608), modelled on the more intransigent ultra-Catholic <a href="/wiki/French_Catholic_League" class="mw-redirect" title="French Catholic League">French Catholic League</a> (1576), it was subsequently concluded as a <a href="/wiki/Military_alliance" title="Military alliance">military alliance</a> "for the defence of the Catholic religion and peace within the Empire".</p>
<p>Notwithstanding the league's founding, as had the founding of the Protestant Union, it further exacerbated long standing tensions between the <a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Reformation" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant Reformation">reformers</a> and the adherents of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a> which thereafter began to get worse with ever more frequent episodes of <a href="/wiki/Civil_disobedience" title="Civil disobedience">civil disobedience</a>, <a href="/wiki/Political_repression" title="Political repression">repression</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Retaliation" class="mw-redirect" title="Retaliation">retaliation</a> that would eventually ignite into the first phase of the <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years' War">Thirty Years' War</a> roughly a decade later with the act of rebellion and calculated insult known as the <a href="/wiki/Second_Defenestration_of_Prague" class="mw-redirect" title="Second Defenestration of Prague">Second Defenestration of Prague</a> on 23 May 1618.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catholic_League_(German)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1555, the <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Augsburg" title="Peace of Augsburg">Peace of Augsburg</a> was signed, which confirmed the result of the <a href="/wiki/Diet_of_Speyer_(1526)" title="Diet of Speyer (1526)">Diet of Speyer (1526)</a> and ended the violence between the Catholics and the <a href="/wiki/Lutherans" class="mw-redirect" title="Lutherans">Lutherans</a> in the Holy Roman Empire.</p>
<p>It stated that:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/F%C3%BCrst" title="Fürst">Princes</a> of the Holy Roman Empire (numbering 225) could choose the religion (Catholicism or Lutheranism) for their realms according to their conscience (the principle of <i><a href="/wiki/Cuius_regio,_eius_religio" title="Cuius regio, eius religio">cuius regio, eius religio</a></i>).</li>
<li>Lutherans living in an <i>ecclesiastical state</i> (under the control of a Catholic <a href="/wiki/Prince-bishop" title="Prince-bishop">prince-bishop</a>) could remain Lutherans.</li>
<li>Lutherans could keep the territory that they had captured from the Catholic Church since the <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Passau" title="Peace of Passau">Peace of Passau</a> (1552).</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Ecclesiastical_province" title="Ecclesiastical province">ecclesiastical leaders</a> of the Catholic Church (bishops) that converted to Lutheranism had to give up their territory (the principle called <i>reservatum ecclesiasticum</i>).</li>
</ul>
<p>Those occupying a state that had officially chosen either Catholicism or Lutheranism could not practice the religion differing to that of the state.</p>
<p>Although the Peace created a temporary end to hostilities, the underlying bases of the religious conflict remained unsolved. Both parties interpreted it at their convenience, the Lutherans in particular considering it only a momentary agreement. Further, <a href="/wiki/Calvinism" title="Calvinism">Calvinism</a> spread quickly throughout the Holy Roman Empire, adding a third major Christian worldview to the region, but its position was not supported in any way by the Augsburg terms, since Catholicism and Lutheranism were the only permitted creeds.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_foundation_of_the_Catholic_League">The foundation of the Catholic League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catholic_League_(German)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The foundation of the Catholic League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Katholische_Liga_Carl-Theodor_von_Piloty.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Katholische_Liga_Carl-Theodor_von_Piloty.jpg/220px-Katholische_Liga_Carl-Theodor_von_Piloty.jpg" width="220" height="234" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Katholische_Liga_Carl-Theodor_von_Piloty.jpg/330px-Katholische_Liga_Carl-Theodor_von_Piloty.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Katholische_Liga_Carl-Theodor_von_Piloty.jpg/440px-Katholische_Liga_Carl-Theodor_von_Piloty.jpg 2x" data-file-width="451" data-file-height="480" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Katholische_Liga_Carl-Theodor_von_Piloty.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Painting by <a href="/wiki/Karl_von_Piloty" title="Karl von Piloty">Karl von Piloty</a> showing the foundation of the Catholic League</div>

<p>The best documented reason of the foundation of the Catholic League was an incident in the town of <a href="/wiki/Donauw%C3%B6rth" title="Donauwörth">Donauwörth</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Free_Imperial_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Free Imperial City">Free Imperial City</a> within the territory of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>. On 25 April 1606, the Lutheran majority of the town barred the Catholic residents of the town from holding an annual <a href="/wiki/Procession" title="Procession">Markus procession</a>, to show the rule of their confession over the town. The Catholics, led by five monks, wanted to pass through the town and on to the nearby village of Ausesheim, showing their flags and singing hymns. They were permitted to do so by the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Augsburg" title="Peace of Augsburg">Peace of Augsburg</a>. The city council would only allow them to re-enter town without flags and singing. The conflict ended in a brawl.</p>
<p>On protest of the <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Augsburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishop of Augsburg">bishop of Augsburg</a>, Catholic Emperor <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Rudolf II, Holy Roman Emperor">Rudolf II</a> of <a href="/wiki/Habsburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Habsburg">Habsburg</a> threatened an <a href="/wiki/Imperial_ban" title="Imperial ban">Imperial ban</a> in case of further violation of the rights of the Catholic citizens. Nevertheless, next year similar anti-Catholic incidents of civil disobedience took place, and the participants of the Markus procession were thrown out of town.</p>
<p>Emperor Rudolf then declared an Imperial ban on the town and ordered <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_I,_Duke_of_Bavaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Maximilian I, Duke of Bavaria">Maximilian I, Duke of Bavaria</a> to execute the ban. Facing his army, the town surrendered. According to Imperial law, the disciplinary measures should not have been executed by the Catholic duke of Bavaria, but by the Protestant <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_W%C3%BCrttemberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Duke of Württemberg">duke of Württemberg</a>, who, like Donauwörth, was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Swabian_Circle" class="mw-redirect" title="Swabian Circle">Swabian Imperial Circle</a>. Maximilian <i>de facto</i> absorbed the former Free Imperial City, which was a violation of Imperial law as well.</p>
<p>In the same year, the Catholic majority of the <a href="/wiki/Diet_(assembly)" title="Diet (assembly)">Reichstag</a> meeting in the <a href="/wiki/Diet_of_Augsburg" title="Diet of Augsburg">Diet of Augsburg</a> resolved that the renewal of the Peace of Augsburg of 1555 should be conditional on the restoration of all church land appropriated since 1552. Acting on these events, the Protestant princes formed a military alliance on 14 May 1608, the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Union" title="Protestant Union">Protestant Union</a>, whose leader was <a href="/wiki/Frederick_IV,_Elector_Palatine" title="Frederick IV, Elector Palatine">Frederick IV</a> of Wittelsbach, the <a href="/wiki/Elector_Palatine" class="mw-redirect" title="Elector Palatine">Elector Palatine</a>.</p>
<p>To create a union of Catholic states as a counterpart to this Protestant Union, early in 1608 Maximilian started negotiations with other Catholic princes.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On 5 July 1608, the spiritual electors manifested a tendency in favour of the confederacy suggested by Maximilian. Opinions were even expressed as to the size of the confederate military forces to be raised.</p>
<p>In July 1609, the representatives of the Prince-Bishops of <a href="/wiki/Prince-Bishopric_of_Augsburg" title="Prince-Bishopric of Augsburg">Augsburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bishopric_of_Constance" title="Bishopric of Constance">Constance</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bishopric_of_Passau" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishopric of Passau">Passau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bishopric_of_Regensburg" title="Bishopric of Regensburg">Regensburg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bishopric_of_W%C3%BCrzburg" title="Bishopric of Würzburg">Würzburg</a> assembled at <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Archbishopric_of_Salzburg" title="Archbishopric of Salzburg">Prince-Archbishop of Salzburg</a>, having shown disapproval, was not invited, and the Prince-Bishop of <a href="/wiki/Bishopric_of_Eichst%C3%A4dt" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishopric of Eichstädt">Eichstädt</a> hesitated. On 10 July 1609, the participating states concluded an alliance "for the defence of the Catholic religion and peace within the Empire." The most important regulation of the League was the prohibition of attacks on one another. Instead of fighting, conflicts had to be decided by the laws of the Empire or, if these failed to solve the conflict, by arbitration within the League. Should one member be attacked, it had to be helped with military or alternatively legal support. Duke Maximilian was to be the president, and the Prince-Bishops of Augsburg, Passau, and Würzburg his councillors. The League was to continue for nine years.</p>
<p>The Munich Diet failed to erect a substantial structure for the newly formed League. On 18 June 1609, the Electors of <a href="/wiki/Archbishopric_of_Mainz" class="mw-redirect" title="Archbishopric of Mainz">Mainz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electorate_of_Cologne" title="Electorate of Cologne">Cologne</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Electorate_of_Trier" title="Electorate of Trier">Trier</a> had proposed an army of 20,000 men. They had also considered making Maximilian president of the alliance, and on August 30 they announced their adhesion to the Munich agreement, provided that Maximilian accepted the Elector of Mainz, arch-<a href="/wiki/Chancellor" title="Chancellor">chancellor</a> of the Empire, as co-president.</p>
<p>To create a structure, several general meetings of the members were arranged. On 10 February 1610, the representatives of all the important Catholic states, except for Austria and Salzburg — and a great number of the smaller ones — met at Würzburg to decide the organization, funding and arming of the League. This was the real beginning of the Catholic League. The <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Pope</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Emperor</a> and the <a href="/wiki/King_of_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="King of Spain">King of Spain</a>, who had been informed by Maximilian, were all favorably disposed towards the undertaking.</p>
<p>The main problem of the League was the unreadiness of its members. In April 1610, the contributions of all its members were not yet paid; Maximilian threatened to resign. To prevent him from doing so, Spain, which had made the giving of a subsidy dependent on Austria's enrollment in the League, waived this condition, and the pope promised a further contribution.</p>
<p>The conduct of the Union in the Jülich dispute and the warlike operations of the Union army in <a href="/wiki/Alsace" title="Alsace">Alsace</a> seemed to make a battle between League and Union inevitable.</p>
<p>In the year 1613 at <a href="/wiki/Regensburg" title="Regensburg">Regensburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> joined the League. The assembly now appointed no less than three war-directors: Duke Maximilian, and Archdukes Albert and Maximilian of Austria. The object of the League was now declared "a Christian legal defense" The membership of Austria made the League part of the struggles between the emperor and his Protestant vassals in <a href="/wiki/Bohemia" title="Bohemia">Bohemia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lower_Austria" title="Lower Austria">Lower Austria</a>, that would lead to the beginning of the Thirty Years' War.</p>

<p>Duke Maximilian refused to accept the resolutions of Ratisbon and even resigned the post as president, when <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_III,_Archduke_of_Austria" title="Maximilian III, Archduke of Austria">Archduke Maximilian III</a> of Austria, the Prince Elector of Mainz and the Prince Elector of Trier, protested the inclusion of the <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Augsburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishop of Augsburg">Bishop of Augsburg</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Provost_of_Ellwangen" class="mw-redirect" title="Provost of Ellwangen">Provost of Ellwangen</a> in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bavarian_Directory&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bavarian Directory (page does not exist)">Bavarian Directory</a>. On 27 May 1617, with the Prince-Bishops of Bamberg, Eichstädt, Würzburg, and the Prince-Provost of Ellwangen, Bavaria formed a separate league for nine years.</p>
<p>At the end of 1618, the position of the Emperor in Bohemia as in Lower and <a href="/wiki/Upper_Austria" title="Upper Austria">Upper Austria</a> gradually became critical. Searching for help, the Emperor tried to restore the League. A meeting of several of the ecclesiastical Princes decided to reconstruct the League on its original basis. It would consist of two groups: the Rhenish district under the presidency of Mainz, and the Oberland district, presided by Bavaria; the treasury and the military command were to be considered separate. Maximilian could only lead the whole of the troops when he had to appear in the Rhenish district. On 31 May, the Oberland both groups were established and bound themselves to render mutual help for six years.</p>
<p>After the death of <a href="/wiki/Matthias,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Matthias, Holy Roman Emperor">Matthias, Holy Roman Emperor</a> and <a href="/wiki/King_of_Bohemia" class="mw-redirect" title="King of Bohemia">king of Bohemia</a>, the kingdom deprived his successor, <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Ferdinand II, Holy Roman Emperor">Ferdinand II</a> in 1618 of the Bohemian crown, and elected <a href="/wiki/Frederick_V,_Elector_Palatine" class="mw-redirect" title="Frederick V, Elector Palatine">Frederick V, Elector Palatine</a> as King, on August 26 and 27 August 1619. After the election as Emperor, Ferdinand conferred with the spiritual electors at Frankfurt, asking for the support of the League.</p>
<p>Now the formation of a confederate army began. With 7,000 men, Bavaria supplied the largest contribution to the army, whose strength was fixed at Würzburg in December 1619, as 21,000 infantry and 4000 cavalry. Commander in chief was <a href="/wiki/Johann_Tserclaes,_Count_of_Tilly" title="Johann Tserclaes, Count of Tilly">Johann Tserclaes, Count of Tilly</a>, a descendant of a Catholic <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Brabant" title="Duchy of Brabant">Brabantine</a> family.</p>
<p>Facing the superiority of the League army of 30,000 men confronting the Protestant Union's army of 10,000, on 3 July 1620, the Union agreed to cease all hostilities between both parties during the war in Austria and Bohemia.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_League_in_War">The League in War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catholic_League_(German)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The League in War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Battle_of_White_Mountain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Battle_of_White_Mountain.jpg/300px-Battle_of_White_Mountain.jpg" width="300" height="242" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Battle_of_White_Mountain.jpg/450px-Battle_of_White_Mountain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Battle_of_White_Mountain.jpg/600px-Battle_of_White_Mountain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="644" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Battle_of_White_Mountain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Battle_of_White_Mountain" title="Battle of White Mountain">Battle of White Mountain</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Without the risk of an attack the League could use all its military forces to support the emperor. The same month, the army was relocated to Upper Austria. Tilly won the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_White_Mountain" title="Battle of White Mountain">Battle of White Mountain</a> north of <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a> on 8 November 1620, in which half of the enemy forces were killed or captured, losing only 700 men. The Emperor regained control over Bohemia and the first stage of the League's activity during the Thirty Years' War ended.</p>
<p>After the end of the <a href="/wiki/Bohemian_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Bohemian War">Bohemian War</a>, the League's army fought in central Germany, but was defeated at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mingolsheim" title="Battle of Mingolsheim">Battle of Mingolsheim</a> on 27 April 1622, after which they joined with the Spanish to fight and win the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Wimpfen" title="Battle of Wimpfen">Battle of Wimpfen</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Margrave_of_Baden-Durlach" class="mw-redirect" title="Margrave of Baden-Durlach">Margrave of Baden-Durlach</a> on May 6. Following these victories, the army captured the city of <a href="/wiki/Heidelberg" title="Heidelberg">Heidelberg</a>, the capital of the leader of the Protestant Union, following an eleven-week siege on September 19. The Protestant prince <a href="/wiki/Christian_the_Younger_of_Brunswick" title="Christian the Younger of Brunswick">Christian, Duke of Brunswick-Lüneburg</a>, raised another army, but was defeated at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Stadtlohn" title="Battle of Stadtlohn">Battle of Stadtlohn</a> where 13,000 out of his army of 15,000 were lost. This victory virtually ended all Protestant resistance in Germany. This caused Denmark's king <a href="/wiki/Christian_IV_of_Denmark" title="Christian IV of Denmark">Christian IV</a> to enter the Thirty Years' War in 1625 to protect Protestantism and also in a bid to make himself the primary leader of Northern Europe.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Johann_Tserclaes_Tilly.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Johann_Tserclaes_Tilly.jpg/200px-Johann_Tserclaes_Tilly.jpg" width="200" height="268" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Johann_Tserclaes_Tilly.jpg/300px-Johann_Tserclaes_Tilly.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Johann_Tserclaes_Tilly.jpg/400px-Johann_Tserclaes_Tilly.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1520" data-file-height="2040" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Johann_Tserclaes_Tilly.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Johann_Tserclaes,_Count_of_Tilly" title="Johann Tserclaes, Count of Tilly">Johann Tserclaes, Count of Tilly</a>, commander in chief of the army of the Catholic League</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The league's army fought and defeated the Danish on 26–27 August 1626 at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lutter" title="Battle of Lutter">Battle of Lutter</a>, destroying more than half the fleeing Danish army.</p>
<p>Because this and other victories by Wallenstein, Denmark was forced to sue for peace at the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_L%C3%BCbeck" title="Treaty of Lübeck">Treaty of Lübeck</a>. Now, the Catholic League hit its peak. Almost the whole German territories were under their control. The danger of imperial hegemony, resulting from this success, made the Swedish King <a href="/wiki/Gustavus_Adolphus_of_Sweden" title="Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden">Gustavus Adolphus</a> become involved in the conflict in 1630.</p>
<p>While <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Stettin_(1630)" title="Treaty of Stettin (1630)">Gustavus Adolphus landed his army in Pomerania</a> and tried to make alliances with the leaders of Northern Germany, the League's army laid siege to the city of <a href="/wiki/Magdeburg" title="Magdeburg">Magdeburg</a> for two months from 20 March 1631, as the city had promised to support Sweden. On May 20, 40,000 successfully attacked Magdeburg. A massacre of the populace ensued in which 25,000 of the 30,000 inhabitants of the city perished while fires destroyed much of the city.</p>
<p>It is not clear whether the commander in chief of the League's forces, Count Tilly ordered the massacre. Magdeburg was a strategically vital city in the Elbe River region and was needed as a resupply center for the looming fight against the Swedes. Therefore, it would have been logical behavior, not to destroy, but to occupy the town with troops of the League.</p>
<p>In 1630, Ferdinand II dismissed his Generalissimus <a href="/wiki/Wallenstein" class="mw-redirect" title="Wallenstein">Wallenstein</a>. Now, the Catholic League was in control of all the Catholic armed forces.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_end_of_the_Catholic_League">The end of the Catholic League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catholic_League_(German)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The end of the Catholic League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Breitenfeld_(1631)" title="Battle of Breitenfeld (1631)">First Battle of Breitenfeld</a>, the Catholic League led by General Tilly was defeated by the Swedish forces. A year later (1632), they met again in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Rain" title="Battle of Rain">Battle of Rain</a>, and this time General Tilly was killed. The upper hand had now switched from the league to the union, led by Sweden, who were able to attack and capture or destroy the territories of the Catholic League. Even <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, was conquered. Thereafter, the German Catholic League did not play a major role in later events.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Prague_(1635)" title="Peace of Prague (1635)">Peace of Prague</a> of 30 May 1635, was a treaty between the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a>, Ferdinand II, and most of the Protestant states of the Empire. It effectively ended the civil war aspect of the Thirty Years' War. The <a href="/wiki/Edict_of_Restitution" title="Edict of Restitution">Edict of Restitution</a> of 1629 was effectively revoked, with the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Augsburg" title="Peace of Augsburg">Peace of Augsburg</a> of 1555 being reestablished as at 12 November 1627.</p>
<p>One of the most important regulations was that formal alliances between states of the Empire were prohibited. The armies of the various states were to be unified with those of the Emperor as an army for the Empire as a whole. The result of this clause was the end of the Catholic League, a now prohibited alliance between states of the Empire.</p>
<p>As well as ending the fighting between the various states, the treaty also ended religion as a source of national conflict; the principle of <i><a href="/wiki/Cuius_regio,_eius_religio" title="Cuius regio, eius religio">cuius regio, eius religio</a></i> was established for good within the Empire.</p>
