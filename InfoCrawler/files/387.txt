<p><b>Bavaria-Munich</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Bayern-München</i></span>) was a duchy which was part of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> from 1392 to 1505.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bavaria-Munich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the death of <a href="/wiki/Stephen_II,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Stephen II, Duke of Bavaria">Stephen II</a> in 1375, his sons <a href="/wiki/Stephen_III,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Stephen III, Duke of Bavaria">Stephen III</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frederick,_Duke_of_Bavaria-Landshut" class="mw-redirect" title="Frederick, Duke of Bavaria-Landshut">Frederick</a>, and <a href="/wiki/John_II,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="John II, Duke of Bavaria">John II</a> jointly ruled <a href="/wiki/Bavaria-Landshut" title="Bavaria-Landshut">Bavaria-Landshut</a>. After seventeen years, the brothers decided to formally divide their inheritance. John received Bavaria-Munich, Stephen received <a href="/wiki/Bavaria-Ingolstadt" title="Bavaria-Ingolstadt">Bavaria-Ingolstadt</a>, while Frederick kept what remained of Bavaria-Landshut. In 1429 portions of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria-Straubing" title="Bavaria-Straubing">Bavaria-Straubing</a> including the city of Straubing were united with Bavaria-Munich. The duchy existed for a little more than a hundred years before Bavaria was reunited under <a href="/wiki/Albert_IV,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Albert IV, Duke of Bavaria">Albert IV, Duke of Bavaria</a>.</p>
