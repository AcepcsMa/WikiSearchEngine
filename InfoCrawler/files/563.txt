<p><b>World music</b> (also called <b>global music</b> or <b>international music</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) is a musical category encompassing many different styles of music from around the globe, which includes many <a href="/wiki/Music_genre" title="Music genre">genres</a> including some forms of <a href="/wiki/Western_culture#Music" title="Western culture">Western music</a> represented by <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk music</a>, as well as selected forms of ethnic music, <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_music" title="Indigenous music">indigenous music</a>, neotraditional music, and music where more than one cultural tradition, such as ethnic music and Western <a href="/wiki/Popular_music" title="Popular music">popular music</a>, intermingle.</p>
<p>World music's inclusive nature and elasticity as a musical category may pose for some obstacles to a universal definition, but its ethic of interest in the culturally exotic is encapsulated in <i><a href="/wiki/FRoots" title="FRoots">fRoots</a></i> magazine's description of the genre as "local music from out there".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The term was popularized in the 1980s as a marketing category for non-Western traditional music.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">Globalization</a> has facilitated the expansion of world music's audiences and scope.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It has grown to include hybrid subgenres such as world fusion, global fusion, ethnic fusion,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Worldbeat" title="Worldbeat">worldbeat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nationalgeographic1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nationalgeographic1-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminology">Terminology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_music&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Delhi_2_Dublin_in_2012.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Delhi_2_Dublin_in_2012.jpg/260px-Delhi_2_Dublin_in_2012.jpg" width="260" height="131" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Delhi_2_Dublin_in_2012.jpg/390px-Delhi_2_Dublin_in_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Delhi_2_Dublin_in_2012.jpg/520px-Delhi_2_Dublin_in_2012.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5184" data-file-height="2612" /></a>

<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Delhi_2_Dublin_in_2012.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Delhi_2_Dublin" title="Delhi 2 Dublin">Delhi 2 Dublin</a> in 2012</div>

<p>The term has been credited to <a href="/wiki/Ethnomusicologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnomusicologist">ethnomusicologist</a> <a href="/wiki/Robert_E._Brown" title="Robert E. Brown">Robert E. Brown</a>, who coined it in the early 1960s at <a href="/wiki/Wesleyan_University" title="Wesleyan University">Wesleyan University</a> in Connecticut, where he developed undergraduate through the doctoral programs in the discipline. To enhance the process of learning, he invited more than a dozen visiting performers from Africa and Asia and began a world music concert series.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The term became current in the 1980s as a marketing/classificatory device in the media and the <a href="/wiki/Music_industry" title="Music industry">music industry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> There are several conflicting definitions for world music. One is that it consists of "all the music in the world", though such a broad definition renders the term virtually meaningless.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The term also is taken as a classification of music that combines <a href="/wiki/Western_world" title="Western world">Western</a> popular music styles with one of many <a href="/wiki/Music_genre" title="Music genre">genres</a> of non-Western music that are also described as <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk music</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnic music">ethnic music</a>. However, world music is not exclusively traditional folk music. It may include cutting edge <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop music</a> styles as well. Succinctly, it can be described as "local music from out there",<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> or "someone else's local music".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> It is a very nebulous term with an increasing number of genres that fall under the umbrella of world music to capture musical trends of combined ethnic style and texture, including Western elements (examples noted in this section).</p>
<p>World music may incorporate distinctive non-Western <a href="/wiki/Musical_scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Musical scale">scales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Musical_mode" class="mw-redirect" title="Musical mode">modes</a> and/or musical <a href="/wiki/Inflection_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Inflection (music)">inflections</a>, and often features distinctive traditional <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_instrument" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnic instrument">ethnic instruments</a>, such as the <a href="/wiki/Kora_(instrument)" title="Kora (instrument)">kora</a> (West African <a href="/wiki/Harp" title="Harp">harp</a>), the <a href="/wiki/Steel_drum" class="mw-redirect" title="Steel drum">steel drum</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sitar" title="Sitar">sitar</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Didgeridoo" title="Didgeridoo">didgeridoo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Music from around the world exerts wide cross-cultural influence as styles naturally influence one another, and in recent years world music has also been marketed as a successful genre in itself. Academic study of world music, as well as the musical genres and individual artists associated with it appear in such disciplines as <a href="/wiki/Anthropology" title="Anthropology">anthropology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Folkloristics" title="Folkloristics">folkloristics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Performance_studies" title="Performance studies">performance studies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ethnomusicology" title="Ethnomusicology">ethnomusicology</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Evolving_terminology">Evolving terminology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_music&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Evolving terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Anda_Union-1.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Anda_Union-1.JPG/180px-Anda_Union-1.JPG" width="180" height="259" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Anda_Union-1.JPG/270px-Anda_Union-1.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Anda_Union-1.JPG/360px-Anda_Union-1.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3008" data-file-height="4323" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Anda_Union-1.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Anda Union at a music festival</div>

<p>In the age of <a href="/wiki/Digital_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Digital music">digital music production</a> the increased availability of high-quality, ethnic music <a href="/wiki/Music_sampling" class="mw-redirect" title="Music sampling">samples</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sound_bites" class="mw-redirect" title="Sound bites">sound bites</a> and <a href="/wiki/Music_loops" class="mw-redirect" title="Music loops">loops</a> from every known region are commonly used in <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">commercial</a> <a href="/wiki/Music_production" class="mw-redirect" title="Music production">music production</a>, which has exposed a vast spectrum of indigenous music texture to developing, <a href="/wiki/Independent_music" title="Independent music">independent artists</a>. These influences proliferate quickly in a web-based music industry that is now percolating as a much larger, predominantly self-promoted menu, via an increasing number of <a href="/wiki/Independent_music" title="Independent music">indie-artist</a>-friendly, <a href="/wiki/Music_streaming" class="mw-redirect" title="Music streaming">streaming</a> <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> options, such as <a href="/wiki/Last.fm" title="Last.fm">Last.fm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhapsody_(online_music_service)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rhapsody (online music service)">Rhapsody</a>, <a href="/wiki/Live365" title="Live365">Live365</a>, Jango Artist Airplay and <a href="/wiki/ReverbNation.com" class="mw-redirect" title="ReverbNation.com">ReverbNation</a>.</p>
<p>An amalgamation of <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">roots music</a> in the global, <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Contemporary music">contemporary</a> listening palette has become apparent, which weakens the role major entertainment labels can play in the cultural perception of genre boundaries. As a result, definitions of the genre have become particularly varied, and determined by particularly wide-ranging and varied opinions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Similar terminology between distinctly different sub-categories under primary music genres, such as world, rock and pop can be as ambiguous and confusing to industry moguls as it is to consumers. This is especially true in the context of world music, where branches of ethnically influenced pop trends are as genre-defined by consumer perception as they are by the music industry forums that govern the basis for categorical distinction. Academic scholars tend to agree that, in today's world of consumer music reviews and <a href="/wiki/Blogging" class="mw-redirect" title="Blogging">blogging</a>, global music culture's public perception is what ultimately distils a prevailing basis for definition from genre ambiguity, regardless of how clearly a category has been outlined by corporate <a href="/wiki/Marketing" title="Marketing">marketing</a> forums and music <a href="/wiki/Journalism" title="Journalism">journalism</a>. The world music genre's gradual migration from a clear spectrum of <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">roots music</a> traditions to an extended list of hybrid subgenres is a good example of the motion genre boundaries can exhibit in a <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">globalizing</a> <a href="/wiki/Pop_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Pop culture">pop culture</a>.</p>
<p>The classic, original definition of world music was in part created to instill a perceived authenticity and distinction between indigenous music traditions and those that eventually become diluted by pop culture, and the <a href="/wiki/Debate" title="Debate">modern debate</a> over how possible it is to maintain that perception in the richly diverse genre of world music is ongoing.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a report on the 2014 <i>globalFEST</i> National Public Radio's Anastasia Tsioulcas said "Even within the "world music" community, nobody likes the term "world music." It smacks of all kinds of loaded issues, from cultural colonialism to questions about what's "authentic" and what isn't (and who might get to police such inquiries), and forces an incredible array of styles that don't have anything in common under the label of "exotic Other." What's more: I believe that in many people's imaginations, "world music" means a kind of fairly awful, gloppy, hippy-ish, worldbeat fusion. It's a problematic, horrible term that satisfies absolutely no one."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Forms">Forms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_music&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Forms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:232px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alan_Stivell_-_Jeudis_du_port_2013.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Alan_Stivell_-_Jeudis_du_port_2013.jpg/230px-Alan_Stivell_-_Jeudis_du_port_2013.jpg" width="230" height="153" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Alan_Stivell_-_Jeudis_du_port_2013.jpg/345px-Alan_Stivell_-_Jeudis_du_port_2013.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Alan_Stivell_-_Jeudis_du_port_2013.jpg/460px-Alan_Stivell_-_Jeudis_du_port_2013.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4608" data-file-height="3072" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alan_Stivell_-_Jeudis_du_port_2013.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Alan Stivell in concert at <a href="/wiki/Brest,_France" title="Brest, France">Brest</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a>), 2013</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Examples of popular forms of world music include the various forms of non-European classical music (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Koto_(musical_instrument)" class="mw-redirect" title="Koto (musical instrument)">koto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/Guzheng" title="Guzheng">guzheng</a> music, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> <a href="/wiki/Raga" title="Raga">raga</a> music, <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_people" title="Tibetan people">Tibetan</a> <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Tibet" title="Music of Tibet">chants</a>), <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Eastern_Europe" class="mw-redirect" title="Music of Eastern Europe">Eastern European folk music</a> (e.g. the village music of the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_State_Television_Female_Vocal_Choir" title="Bulgarian State Television Female Vocal Choir">The Mystery Of The Bulgarian Voices</a>), <a href="/wiki/Nordic_folk_music" title="Nordic folk music">Nordic folk music</a> and the many forms of folk and tribal music of the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Indonesian music">Indonesian music</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Breton_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Breton people">Breton</a> musician <a href="/wiki/Alan_Stivell" title="Alan Stivell">Alan Stivell</a> pioneered the connection between traditional folk music, modern <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock music</a> and world music with his 1972 album <i><a href="/wiki/Renaissance_of_the_Celtic_Harp" title="Renaissance of the Celtic Harp">Renaissance of the Celtic Harp</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> At this time, Stivell's contemporary, <a href="/wiki/Welsh_people" title="Welsh people">Welsh</a> singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Meic_Stevens" title="Meic Stevens">Meic Stevens</a> popularised <a href="/wiki/Welsh_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Welsh music">Welsh folk</a> music.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Neo-traditional <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh language</a> <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Wales" title="Music of Wales">music</a> featuring a fusion of modern instruments and traditional instruments such as the <a href="/wiki/Pibgorn_(instrument)" title="Pibgorn (instrument)">Pibgorn</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Triple_harp" title="Triple harp">Welsh harp</a> has been further developed by artists such as <a href="/wiki/Bob_Delyn_a%27r_Ebillion" title="Bob Delyn a'r Ebillion">Bob Delyn a'r Ebillion</a>. Lebanese musical pioneer <a href="/wiki/Lydia_Canaan" title="Lydia Canaan">Lydia Canaan</a> fused <a href="/wiki/Middle-Eastern" class="mw-redirect" title="Middle-Eastern">Middle-Eastern</a> <a href="/wiki/Quarter_note" title="Quarter note">quarter notes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Microtonal_music" title="Microtonal music">microtones</a> with <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">anglophone</a> folk, and is listed in the catalog of the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame_and_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and Museum">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and Museum</a>'s Library and Archives<sup id="cite_ref-rock_hall_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rock_hall-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Daily_Star_Rock_Hall_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Star_Rock_Hall-22">[22]</a></sup> as the first rock star of the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Daily_Star_Rock_Hall_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Star_Rock_Hall-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>More recently, other Welsh-language bands such as <a href="/wiki/Calan_(band)" title="Calan (band)">Calan</a> and <a href="/wiki/9_Bach" class="mw-redirect" title="9 Bach">9 Bach</a> have achieved international acclaim.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Experimental <a href="/wiki/Psychedelic_rock" title="Psychedelic rock">psychedelic rock</a> group <a href="/wiki/Super_Furry_Animals" title="Super Furry Animals">Super Furry Animals</a>'s Welsh-language album <i><a href="/wiki/Mwng" title="Mwng">Mwng</a></i> was released in May 2000 and re-released in May 2015, captures the spirit of World music as 'trying to hang on to something that's under serious threat from advanced capitalism'.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>The broad category of world music includes isolated forms of ethnic music from diverse geographical regions. These dissimilar strains of ethnic music are commonly categorized together by virtue of their indigenous roots. Over the 20th century, the invention of <a href="/wiki/Sound_recording" class="mw-redirect" title="Sound recording">sound recording</a>, low-cost international air travel and common access to global communication among artists and the general public has given rise to a related phenomenon called "<a href="/wiki/Crossover_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Crossover (music)">crossover</a>" music. Musicians from diverse cultures and locations could readily access recorded music from around the world, see and hear visiting musicians from other cultures and visit other countries to play their own music, creating a melting pot of stylistic influences. While communication technology allows greater access to obscure forms of music, the pressures of commercialization also present the risk of increasing musical homogeneity, the blurring of regional identities, and the gradual extinction of traditional local music-making practices.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hybrid_examples">Hybrid examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_music&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Hybrid examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vampire_Weekend_Red_Rocks_05.20.13.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Vampire_Weekend_Red_Rocks_05.20.13.jpg/200px-Vampire_Weekend_Red_Rocks_05.20.13.jpg" width="200" height="133" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Vampire_Weekend_Red_Rocks_05.20.13.jpg/300px-Vampire_Weekend_Red_Rocks_05.20.13.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Vampire_Weekend_Red_Rocks_05.20.13.jpg/400px-Vampire_Weekend_Red_Rocks_05.20.13.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3888" data-file-height="2592" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vampire_Weekend_Red_Rocks_05.20.13.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Vampire_Weekend" title="Vampire Weekend">Vampire Weekend</a> performing at <a href="/wiki/Red_Rocks_Amphitheatre" title="Red Rocks Amphitheatre">Red Rocks Amphitheatre</a> in 2013</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Since the music industry established this term, the fuller scope of what an average music consumer defines as "world" music in today's market has grown to include various blends of ethnic music tradition, style and interpretation,<sup id="cite_ref-nationalgeographic1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nationalgeographic1-7">[7]</a></sup> and derivative world music genres have been coined to represent these hybrids, such as World fusion, Global fusion, Ethnic fusion or <a href="/wiki/Worldbeat" title="Worldbeat">Worldbeat</a>. Good examples of hybrid, world fusion are the <a href="/wiki/Irish_folk_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish folk music">Irish</a> / <a href="/wiki/West_African_music" class="mw-redirect" title="West African music">West African</a> meld of <a href="/wiki/Afro_Celt_Sound_System" title="Afro Celt Sound System">Afro Celt Sound System</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> the pan-cultural sound of Aomusic<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> / <a href="/wiki/Finnish_folk_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Finnish folk music">Finnish folk</a> music of <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4rttin%C3%A4" title="Värttinä">Värttinä</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> each of which bear tinges of <a href="/wiki/Popular_music" title="Popular music">contemporary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">Western</a> influence—an increasingly noticeable element in the expansion genres of world music. World fusion / Worldbeat / Ethnic fusion / Global fusion can also blend specific indigenous sounds with more blatant elements of <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">Western pop</a>. Good examples are <a href="/wiki/Paul_Simon" title="Paul Simon">Paul Simon</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Graceland_(album)" title="Graceland (album)">Graceland</a></i>, on which South African <a href="/wiki/Mbaqanga" title="Mbaqanga">Mbaqanga</a> music is heard, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Gabriel" title="Peter Gabriel">Peter Gabriel</a>'s work with Pakistani <a href="/wiki/Sufi" class="mw-redirect" title="Sufi">Sufi</a> singer, <a href="/wiki/Nusrat_Fateh_Ali_Khan" title="Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan">Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Deep_Forest" title="Deep Forest">Deep Forest</a> project, in which vocal loops from <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a> are blended with Western, contemporary rhythmic textures and <a href="/wiki/Harmony" title="Harmony">harmony structure</a>, and the work of <a href="/wiki/Mango_(singer)" title="Mango (singer)">Mango</a>, who combined pop and rock music with world elements.</p>
<p>More recent bands who exhibit experimentation with World music Hybrid Forms include <a href="/wiki/Vampire_Weekend" title="Vampire Weekend">Vampire Weekend</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Very_Best" title="The Very Best">The Very Best</a>, Island Cassettes &amp; <a href="/wiki/Yeasayer" title="Yeasayer">Yeasayer</a>, many of which cite inspiration from the work of Paul Simon.</p>
<p>Depending on style and context, world music can sometimes share the <a href="/wiki/New-age_music" title="New-age music">new-age music</a> genre, which is a category that often includes <a href="/wiki/Ambient_music" title="Ambient music">Ambient music</a> and textural expressions from indigenous <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">roots</a> sources. Good examples are <a href="/wiki/Singing_bowl" title="Singing bowl">Tibetan bowls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tuvan_throat_singing" title="Tuvan throat singing">Tuvan throat singing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Gregorian music">Gregorian chant</a> or <a href="/wiki/Native_American_flute" title="Native American flute">Native American flute</a> music. World music blended with new-age music is a sound, loosely classified as the hybrid genre, ethnic fusion. Examples of ethnic fusion are <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Gunn" title="Nicholas Gunn">Nicholas Gunn</a>'s "Face-to-Face" from <i>Beyond Grand Canyon,</i> featuring authentic Native American flute combined with synthesizers, and "Four Worlds" from <i>The Music of the Grand Canyon</i>, featuring spoken word from Razor Saltboy of the <a href="/wiki/Navajo_Nation" title="Navajo Nation">Navajo Indian Nation</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_fusion">World fusion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_music&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: World fusion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The subgenre world fusion is often mistakenly assumed to refer exclusively to a blending of Western <a href="/wiki/Jazz_fusion" title="Jazz fusion">jazz fusion</a> elements with world music. Although such a hybrid expression falls easily into the world fusion category, the suffix "fusion" in the term world fusion should not be assumed to mean only jazz fusion. Western jazz combined with strong elements of world music is more accurately termed <i>world fusion jazz</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ethno-jazz" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethno-jazz">ethnic jazz</a></i> or <i>non-Western jazz</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> World fusion and global fusion are nearly synonymous with the genre term <i><a href="/wiki/Worldbeat" title="Worldbeat">worldbeat</a></i>, and though these are considered subgenres of popular music, they may also imply universal expressions of the more general term, <i>world music</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-nationalgeographic1_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nationalgeographic1-7">[7]</a></sup> Throughout the 1970s and 80s, <a href="/wiki/Jazz_fusion" title="Jazz fusion">fusion</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Jazz_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Jazz music">jazz music</a> genre implied a blending of jazz and <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock music</a>, which is where the misleading assumption is rooted.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popular_non-Western_genres">Popular non-Western genres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_music&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Popular non-Western genres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although it primarily describes traditional music, the world music category also includes popular music from non-Western urban communities (e.g. <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South African</a> <a href="/wiki/Township_music" title="Township music">"township" music</a>) and non-European music forms that have been influenced by other so-called third-world musics (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Afro-Cuban_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Afro-Cuban music">Afro-Cuban music</a>), although Western-style popular song sourced from non-English-speaking countries in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> (e.g. <a href="/wiki/French_pop_music" title="French pop music">French pop music</a>) would not generally be considered world music.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> is one of the great European capitals for world music.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> For many years, the city has attracted numerous musicians from former colonies in <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>. This scene is aided by the fact that there are many concerts and institutions that help promote the music.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algerian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Moroccan</a> music have an important presence in the French capital. Hundreds of thousands of Algerian and Moroccan immigrants have settled in Paris, bringing the sounds of <a href="/wiki/Amazigh" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazigh">Amazigh</a> (Berber), <a href="/wiki/Ra%C3%AF" title="Raï">raï</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gnawa_music" title="Gnawa music">Gnawa music</a>. Algerian raï also found a large French audience.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The West African community is also very large, integrated by people from <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ivory_Coast" title="Ivory Coast">Ivory Coast</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Guinea" title="Guinea">Guinea</a>. They have introduced <a href="/wiki/Manding_languages" title="Manding languages">manding</a> <a href="/wiki/Griot" title="Griot">jeli</a> music, <a href="/wiki/Mbalax" title="Mbalax">mbalax</a> and other styles.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="After_1987:_Women_and_beyond">After 1987: Women and beyond</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_music&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: After 1987: Women and beyond">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One origin of the term was the initiation of <a href="/wiki/F%C3%AAte_de_la_Musique" title="Fête de la Musique">World Music Day</a> (<a href="/wiki/F%C3%AAte_de_la_Musique" title="Fête de la Musique">Fête de la Musique</a>) in 1982 in France. World Music Day has been celebrated on 21 June every year since then. On 29 June 1987 a meeting of interested parties gathered to capitalize on the marketing of this genre. Arguably popular interest was sparked with the release in 1986 of <a href="/wiki/Paul_Simon" title="Paul Simon">Paul Simon</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Graceland_(album)" title="Graceland (album)">Graceland</a></i> album.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The concept behind the album was to express his own sensibilities using the sounds he had fallen in love with while listening to artists from Southern Africa, including <a href="/wiki/Ladysmith_Black_Mambazo" title="Ladysmith Black Mambazo">Ladysmith Black Mambazo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Savuka" title="Savuka">Savuka</a>. This project and the work of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Gabriel" title="Peter Gabriel">Peter Gabriel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Clegg_(musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Johnny Clegg (musician)">Johnny Clegg</a> among others had, to some degree, introduced non-Western music to a wider audience. They saw this as an opportunity. <a href="/wiki/Zila_Khan" title="Zila Khan">Zila Khan</a> is one of today's leading singers in World Music.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Before 1987, world music had a following but it was still difficult for interested parties to sell their music to the larger music stores.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Although specialist music stores had been important in developing the genre over many years, the record companies, broadcasters and journalists had been finding it difficult to build a following because the music, itself, seemed too scarce. However, they were aware that the <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> and classical markets had developed a crossover audience. They decided the best way forward would be a collective strategy to bring the music to a wider audience.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>At around this time, <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Co-operation" title="Cultural Co-operation">Cultural Co-operation</a> started the Music Village Festival. This regular series of free world music festivals continues to run. They also started a network of world music artists to help promote their work.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1987_meeting">1987 meeting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_music&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 1987 meeting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the outset of the 1987 meeting, the musician Roger Armstrong advised the reason why something had to be done:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>[He] felt that the main problem in selling our kind of material lay with the UK retail outlets and, specifically, the fact that they did not know how to rack it coherently. This discouraged [the retail stores] from stocking the material in any depth and made it more difficult for the record buyers to become acquainted with our catalogs.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The first concern of the meetings was to select the umbrella name that this music would be listed under. Suggestions included "world beat" and prefixing words such as "hot" or "tropical" to existing genre titles. "World music" won after a show of hands, but initially it was not meant to be the title for a whole new genre—but just something the record labels could place on record sleeves to distinguish them during the forthcoming campaign. Afterward, they agreed that despite the publicity campaign, this wasn't an exclusive club—and that for the good of all, any label that sold this type of music could use the name.</p>
<p>Another issue was the distribution methods at the time. Most main labels were unhappy with the lack of specialist knowledge in their sales force, which led to poor service. Many larger outlets were reluctant to carry the music, because they favored larger releases they could promote within the store. It was difficult to justify a large presentation expense with limited stock going into stores.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_music&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One of the marketing strategies used in the <a href="/wiki/Gramophone_record" title="Gramophone record">vinyl</a> market at the time was the use of browser cards, which would appear in the record racks. As part of the "world music" campaign, it was decided that these would be a two color affair designed to carry a special offer package; to aid the retailer a selection of labels would also be included, presumably for shelf or rack edging.</p>
<p>In an unprecedented move, all of the world music labels coordinated together and developed a compilation cassette for the cover of the music magazine <i><a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a></i>. The overall running time was 90 minutes, each package containing a mini-catalog showing the other releases on offer.</p>
<p>By the time of a second meeting it became clear that a successful campaign required its own dedicated press officer. The press officer would be able to juggle various deadlines and sell the music as a concept—not just to national stations, but also regional <a href="/wiki/DJ" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ">DJs</a> keen to expand their musical variety. DJs were a key resource as it was important to make "world music" important to people outside London—most regions after all had a similarly heritage to tap into. A cost-effective way of achieving all this would be a leafleting campaign.</p>
<p>The next step was to develop a world music chart, gathering together selling information from around fifty shops, so that it would finally be possible to see which were big sellers in the genre—so new listeners could see what was particularly popular. It was agreed that the <i>NME</i> could again be involved in printing the chart and also <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Week" title="Music Week">Music Week</a></i> and the London <a href="/wiki/Listings_magazine" title="Listings magazine">listings magazine</a> <i><a href="/wiki/City_Limits_(magazine)" title="City Limits (magazine)">City Limits</a></i>. It was also suggested that <a href="/wiki/Andy_Kershaw" title="Andy Kershaw">Andy Kershaw</a> might be persuaded to do a run down of this chart on his show regularly.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_to_immigration_and_multiculturalism">Relationship to immigration and multiculturalism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_music&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Relationship to immigration and multiculturalism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Canadian_cultures" title="Music of Canadian cultures">Music of Canadian cultures</a></div>
<p>In most wealthy industrialized countries, large amounts of immigration from other regions has been ongoing for many decades. This has introduced non-Western music to Western audiences not only as "exotic" imports, but also as local music played by fellow citizens. <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_music_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Caribbean music in the United Kingdom">Caribbean music in the United Kingdom</a> or <a href="/wiki/Chicano_rock" title="Chicano rock">Chicano rock</a> in the United States would be two obvious examples.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> But the process is ongoing and continues to produce new forms. In the 2010s several musicians from immigrant communities in the West rose to global popularity, such as Haitian-American <a href="/wiki/Wyclef_Jean" title="Wyclef Jean">Wyclef Jean</a>, Somali-Canadian <a href="/wiki/K%27naan" title="K'naan">K'naan</a>, Tamil-Briton <a href="/wiki/M.I.A._(artist)" class="mw-redirect" title="M.I.A. (artist)">M.I.A.</a>, or Lebanese-Colombian <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a>, often blending the music of their heritage with hip-hop or pop. Cuban-born singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Addys_Mercedes" title="Addys Mercedes">Addys Mercedes</a> started of her international career from Germany mixing traditional elements of Son with pop.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>The diverse nature of major Western cities has changed the pace of musical intermixing.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Once, an established Western artist might collaborate with an established African artist to produce an album or two. Now, new bands and new genres are built from the ground up by young performers. For example, the <a href="/wiki/Punjabi_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Punjabi music">Punjabi</a>-<a href="/wiki/Irish_folk_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish folk music">Irish</a> fusion band <a href="/wiki/Delhi_2_Dublin" title="Delhi 2 Dublin">Delhi 2 Dublin</a> is from neither India nor Ireland, but <a href="/wiki/Vancouver,_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="Vancouver, Canada">Vancouver, Canada</a>. <a href="/wiki/Country_for_Syria" title="Country for Syria">Country for Syria</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> based music collective, blends <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">American Country music</a> with the music of <a href="/wiki/Refugees_of_the_Syrian_Civil_War" title="Refugees of the Syrian Civil War">Syrian refugees</a> and local <a href="/wiki/Turkish_music_(style)" title="Turkish music (style)">Turkish music.</a><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Vancouver,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (July 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> musicians and composers also work collectively to create original compositions for various combinations of ethnic and western instruments. The JUNO nominated <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.orchidensemble.com">Orchid Ensemble</a> is a trio of two Chinese instruments and the marimba (plus other percussion instruments). It has been touring across North American since 1997. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.vi-co.org">The Vancouver Inter-Cultural Orchestra</a> stages musicians and instruments from China, Taiwan, India, Iran, Africa, Vietnam,.... with Canadian western orchestral players.</p>
<p>There is also Canada's most famous Traditional Indian Classical Musician, <a href="/wiki/Cassius_Khan" title="Cassius Khan">Cassius Khan</a> who is an award-winning internationally renowned as the world's only <a href="/wiki/Ghazal" title="Ghazal">Ghazal</a> singer who accompanies himself on the <a href="/wiki/Tabla" title="Tabla">Tabla</a> a two piece hand drum indigenous to India. Khan himself has not made his views unknown on his dislike to Robert E Brown's term "World Music" by publicly stating he found the term "Colonial and Oppressing" and he has been a hot issue in most underground meetings of the musical elites in Canada.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (July 2014)">original research?</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Music_Month">World Music Month</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_music&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: World Music Month">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The World Music Month was designated on October 1987.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It is a music festival held at the Town &amp; Country Club in London, and it was the start of the winter season for both <a href="/wiki/WOMAD" class="mw-redirect" title="WOMAD">WOMAD</a> and <i>Arts Worldwide</i>. The main press release stressed the issues inherent in the campaign.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague. (July 2014)">vague</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Since the early 1980s the enthusiasm for music from 'outside' Western pop culture has been steadily mounting.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> More and more international artists, many of whom are big stars in their own countries, are coming to England and North America on tour. They started off, like the <a href="/wiki/Bhundu_Boys" title="Bhundu Boys">Bhundu Boys</a>, playing small clubs and pubs, but now many acts are so popular that they are filling larger venues.</p>
<p>Examples of radio shows that feature world music include <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_of_Music_(radio_show)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="World of Music (radio show) (page does not exist)">World of Music</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Voice_of_America" title="Voice of America">Voice of America</a>, <i>Transpacific Sound Paradise</i> on <a href="/wiki/WFMU" title="WFMU">WFMU</a>, <i>The Planet</i> on Australia's <a href="/wiki/ABC_Radio_National" class="mw-redirect" title="ABC Radio National">ABC Radio National</a>, DJ Edu presenting <i>D.N.A: DestiNation Africa</i> on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1Xtra" title="BBC Radio 1Xtra">BBC Radio 1Xtra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adil_Ray" title="Adil Ray">Adil Ray</a> on the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Asian_Network" title="BBC Asian Network">BBC Asian Network</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andy_Kershaw" title="Andy Kershaw">Andy Kershaw</a>'s show on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_3" title="BBC Radio 3">BBC Radio 3</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Gillett" title="Charlie Gillett">Charlie Gillett</a>'s show<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> on the <a href="/wiki/BBC_World_Service" title="BBC World Service">BBC World Service</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s_radio_programs">2010s radio programs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_music&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2010s radio programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>World music radio programs today often play <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Africa" title="Music of Africa">African</a> hip hop or <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a> artists, <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Crossover (music)">crossover</a> <a href="/wiki/Bhangra_(music)" title="Bhangra (music)">Bhangra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latin_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin American">Latin American</a> jazz groups, etc. Common media for world music include <a href="/wiki/Public_radio" class="mw-redirect" title="Public radio">public radio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Webcasting" class="mw-redirect" title="Webcasting">webcasting</a>, the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Public_Radio" class="mw-redirect" title="National Public Radio">NPR</a>, and ABC (<a href="/wiki/Australian_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Australian Broadcasting Corporation">Australian Broadcasting Corporation</a>). The scope of what is considered world music has increased.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> By default, non-region-specific or multi-cultural world music projects are often listed under the generic category of world music.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticisms">Criticisms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_music&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Criticisms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The generally accepted geographic standard for what designates world music is withstanding criticism for its universal asymmetry, where nearly every classifiable region under the term is defined, relative to <a href="/wiki/Western_culture" title="Western culture">Western</a> music culture; this is primarily due to the fact that dominant <a href="/wiki/Corporate_structure" title="Corporate structure">corporate structures</a> for music <a href="/wiki/Record_distribution" class="mw-redirect" title="Record distribution">distribution</a> and <a href="/wiki/Promotion_(marketing)" title="Promotion (marketing)">promotion</a> originate in <span class="nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a></span> and <span class="nowrap"><a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a></span>, as do the forums for establishing industry genre categories. Therefore, in market context, "ethnic" music is synthetically defined by a radius that extends from a Western center.</p>
<p>Some musicians and curators of music have come to dislike the term "world music". To these critics, "world music" is a parochial, catch-all marketing term for non-Western music of all genres. In <span class="nowrap">October 1999</span>, <span class="nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Luaka_Bop" title="Luaka Bop">Luaka Bop</a></span> label founder and ex-<a href="/wiki/Talking_Heads" title="Talking Heads">Talking Heads</a> frontman <a href="/wiki/David_Byrne" title="David Byrne">David Byrne</a> wrote an "<span class="nowrap">I Hate World Music</span>" editorial in <i><span class="nowrap"><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></span></i> explaining his objections to the term. Byrne argued that the labelling and categorization of other cultures as "exotic" serves to attract an insincere consumption and deter other potential consumers.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Awards_for_world_music" title="Awards for world music">Awards for world music</a></div>
<p>The BBC Radio 3 Awards for World Music was an award given to world music artists between 2002 and 2008, sponsored by <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_3" title="BBC Radio 3">BBC Radio 3</a>. The award was thought up by <i><a href="/wiki/FRoots" title="FRoots">fRoots</a></i> magazine's editor <a href="/wiki/Ian_A._Anderson" title="Ian A. Anderson">Ian Anderson</a>, inspired by the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_2_Folk_Awards" title="BBC Radio 2 Folk Awards">BBC Radio 2 Folk Awards</a>. Award categories included: Africa, Asia/Pacific, Americas, Europe, Mid East and North Africa, Newcomer, Culture Crossing, Club Global, Album of the Year, and Audience Award. Initial lists of nominees in each category were selected annually by a panel of several thousand industry experts. Shortlisted nominees were voted on by a twelve-member jury, which selected the winners in every category except for the Audience Award category. These jury members were appointed and presided over by the BBC.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The annual awards ceremony was held at the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Proms" class="mw-redirect" title="BBC Proms">BBC Proms</a> and winners were given an award called a "Planet". In March 2009, the BBC made a decision to axe the BBC Radio 3 Awards for World Music.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>In response to the BBC's decision to end its awards program, the British world music magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Songlines_(magazine)" title="Songlines (magazine)">Songlines</a></i> launched the Songlines Music Awards in 2009 "to recognise outstanding talent in world music".<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/WOMEX_Awards" title="WOMEX Awards">WOMEX Awards</a> were introduced in 1999 to honor the high points of world music on an international level and to acknowledge musical excellence, social importance, commercial success, political impact and lifetime achievement.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Every October at the WOMEX event, the award figurine—an ancient mother goddess statue dating back about 6000 years to the Neolithic age—is presented in an award ceremony to a worthy member of the world music community.</p>

<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://matasora.com/">Matasora World Music Festival</a> held in Bandung, Java.</li>

<p><b>Iran</b></p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Fajr_International_Music_Festival" title="Fajr International Music Festival">Fajr International Music Festival</a> is <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>'s most prestigious Music Festival founded in 1986. The festival is affiliated with <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> and includes national and international competition sections. Since its establishment, many musicians from several countries like <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> participated in the event. The festival have enjoyed a strong presence of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a> countries as well.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Ireland</b></p>
<ul>
<li>A directory of Music Festivals in Ireland—<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.musicfestivalsireland.ie">Music Festivals Ireland—www.musicfestivalsireland.ie</a></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Italy</b></p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ariano_Folkfestival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ariano Folkfestival (page does not exist)">Ariano Folkfestival</a> is a five-day world music festival held every summer in <a href="/wiki/Ariano_Irpino" title="Ariano Irpino">Ariano Irpino</a>, a small town in southern Italy.</li>
<li>The World Music Festival Lo Sguardo di Ulisse was first held in 1997 in <a href="/wiki/Campania" title="Campania">Campania</a>, Italy.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Macedonia</b></p>
<ul>
<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.offest.com.mk">OFFest</a> is a five-day world music festival held every summer (started 2002) in <a href="/wiki/Skopje,_Macedonia" class="mw-redirect" title="Skopje, Macedonia">Skopje</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Malaysia</b></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rainforest_World_Music_Festival" title="Rainforest World Music Festival">Rainforest World Music Festival</a> is another world music festival held in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Mali</b></p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Festival_in_the_Desert" class="mw-redirect" title="Festival in the Desert">Festival in the Desert</a> takes place every year at <a href="/wiki/Essakane" title="Essakane">Essakane</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Timbuktu" title="Timbuktu">Timbuktu</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a> and has achieved international status in spite of the difficulties of reaching its location.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Morocco</b></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mawazine" title="Mawazine">Mawazine</a> is a festival of world music that takes place annually in <a href="/wiki/Rabat" title="Rabat">Rabat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, featuring Arab and international music icons.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p><b>New Zealand</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Festival in New Plymouth, New Zealand. Early March each year.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Poland</b></p>
<ul>
<li>The Cross-Culture Warsaw Festival, Poland. September each year.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></li>
<li>Brave Festival, <a href="/wiki/Wroc%C5%82aw" title="Wrocław">Wrocław</a>, Poland. July each year. <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.bravefestival.pl">[2]</a></li>
<li>Ethno Port, <a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84" title="Poznań">Poznań</a>, Poland. June each year. <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.ethnoport.pl/">[3]</a></li>
<li>Ethno Jazz Festival in Wrocław, Poland. Several events throughout the whole year. <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.ethnojazz.pl">[4]</a></li>
<li>Different Sounds (Inne brzmienia), Lublin, Poland. July each year.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></li>
<li>Francophonic Festival in <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>, Poland. March each year. <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.fpf.com.pl/">[5]</a></li>
<li>Nowa Tradycja (New Tradition), Warsaw, Poland. May each year.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></li>
<li>Siesta Festival, <a href="/wiki/Gda%C5%84sk" title="Gdańsk">Gdańsk</a>, Poland. First edition in April/May 2011. <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.siestafestival.pl/">[6]</a></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Portugal</b></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/FMM_Sines_%E2%80%93_Festival_M%C3%BAsicas_do_Mundo" class="mw-redirect" title="FMM Sines – Festival Músicas do Mundo">Festival Músicas do Mundo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sines,_Portugal" class="mw-redirect" title="Sines, Portugal">Sines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Set%C3%BAbal_District" title="Setúbal District">Setúbal District</a> is a world music festival first held in 1998.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Romania</b></p>
<ul>
<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.plai.ro">Plai Festival</a> in Timisoara</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Serbia</b></p>
<ul>
<li>The <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.serbiamusicfestival.com/index_en.html">Serbia World Music Festival</a> is a three-day world music festival held every summer in <a href="/wiki/Takovo" title="Takovo">Takovo</a>, a small village in central Serbia.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Spain</b> Spain's most important world music festivals are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Etnosur, in Alcalá la Real, Jaén (<a href="/wiki/Andaluc%C3%ADa" class="mw-redirect" title="Andalucía">Andalucía</a> region)</li>

<li>Festival Internacional de Música Popular Tradicional in <a href="/wiki/Vilanova_i_la_Geltr%C3%BA" title="Vilanova i la Geltrú">Vilanova i la Geltrú</a> / <a href="/wiki/Vilanova_International_World_Music_Festival" title="Vilanova International World Music Festival">Vilanova International World Music Festival</a> (<a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>)</li>
<li>La Mar de Músicas, in Cartagena (<a href="/wiki/Murcia" title="Murcia">Murcia</a> region)</li>

<li>Territorios, in Seville</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Sweden</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Urkult <a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%A4s%C3%A5ker,_%C3%85ngermanland&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Näsåker, Ångermanland (page does not exist)">Näsåker, Ångermanland</a> August each year. <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.urkult.se/">[7]</a></li>
<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.cecsweden.org/en/cec-sweden-green-world-yoga-festival">Green World Yoga &amp; Sacred Music Festival</a>, July this year at Skåne.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Turkey</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Konya Mystic Music Festival, held annually in <a href="/wiki/Konya" title="Konya">Konya</a> since 2004, in recent years in commemoration of <a href="/wiki/Rumi" title="Rumi">Rumi</a>'s birthday. The festival features traditional music from around the world with a mystical theme, religious function and/or sacred content.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></li>
<li>Fethiye World Music Festival. The Fethiye World Music Festival presents musicians from different countries of the world for concerts that will contribute to the city’s arts and culture.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Uganda</b></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Milege#Milege_World_Music_Festival" title="Milege">Milege World Music Festival</a> has become a big festival in Uganda inviting musicians and fans from all over Africa and the rest of the world to enjoy live music, games, sports and so on. The festival runs for three consecutive days every November at the Botanical Gardens, <a href="/wiki/Entebbe" title="Entebbe">Entebbe</a>, Uganda</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Ukraine</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Svirzh World Music Festival (<a href="/wiki/Lviv" title="Lviv">Lviv</a> region)</li>
</ul>
<p><b>United Kingdom</b></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Glastonbury_Festival" title="Glastonbury Festival">Glastonbury Festival</a> held at Worthy Farm near Glastonbury each year</li>
<li>Musicport World Music Festival held annually at The Spa Pavilion, Whitby, North Yorkshire.</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_Village_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Music Village Festival (page does not exist)">Music Village Festival</a> is Europe's longest running festival of world cultures. It is held every two years in London and has been running since 1983. It is organised by the <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Co-operation" title="Cultural Co-operation">Cultural Co-operation</a></li>
<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.drumcamp.co.uk">Drum Camp</a> established in 1996 is a unique world music festival. Each event takes you on a musical journey around the world, combining ancient rhythmic traditions with the most modern and cutting edge grooves and beats.</li>
</ul>
<p><b>United States</b></p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=California_World_Music_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="California World Music Festival (page does not exist)">California World Music Festival</a> is held each July at the <a href="/wiki/Nevada_County,_California" title="Nevada County, California">Nevada County</a> Fairgrounds.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/World_Sacred_Music_Festival" title="World Sacred Music Festival">World Sacred Music Festival</a> is held annually in Olympia, Washington State, sponsored by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Interfaith_Works&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Interfaith Works (page does not exist)">Interfaith Works</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/FloydFest" title="FloydFest">FloydFest</a> in <a href="/wiki/Floyd,_Virginia" title="Floyd, Virginia">Floyd, Virginia</a>, United States, has featured artists from a wide diversity of styles including <a href="/wiki/Ani_DiFranco" title="Ani DiFranco">Ani DiFranco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geno_Delafose" title="Geno Delafose">Geno Delafose</a> &amp; <a href="/w/index.php?title=French_Rockin%27_Boogie&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="French Rockin' Boogie (page does not exist)">French Rockin' Boogie</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Trumystic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Trumystic (page does not exist)">Trumystic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nickel_Creek" title="Nickel Creek">Nickel Creek</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Akoya_Afrobeat_Enemble&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Akoya Afrobeat Enemble (page does not exist)">Akoya Afrobeat Enemble</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Finger_Lakes_GrassRoots_Festival_of_Music_and_Dance" title="Finger Lakes GrassRoots Festival of Music and Dance">Finger Lakes GrassRoots Festival of Music and Dance</a> in <a href="/wiki/Trumansburg,_New_York" title="Trumansburg, New York">Trumansburg, New York</a>, United States, has featured artists from a variety of world and ethnic music genres including <a href="/wiki/Tinariwen" title="Tinariwen">Tinariwen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Mapfumo" title="Thomas Mapfumo">Thomas Mapfumo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Franti" title="Michael Franti">Michael Franti</a>, <a href="/wiki/D%27Gary" title="D'Gary">D'Gary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boubacar_Traore" class="mw-redirect" title="Boubacar Traore">Boubacar Traore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mamadou_Diabate" class="mw-redirect" title="Mamadou Diabate">Mamadou Diabate</a>, and many more from around the world.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Stern_Grove_Festival" title="Stern Grove Festival">Stern Grove Festival</a> is a San Francisco celebration of musical and cultural diversity. Examples: <a href="/wiki/Lucinda_Williams" title="Lucinda Williams">Lucinda Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Doe" title="John Doe">John Doe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ojos_de_Brujo" title="Ojos de Brujo">Ojos de Brujo</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=O-Maya&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="O-Maya (page does not exist)">O-Maya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ladysmith_Black_Mambazo" title="Ladysmith Black Mambazo">Ladysmith Black Mambazo</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Funk_Brothers" class="mw-redirect" title="Funk Brothers">Funk Brothers</a> and also <a href="/wiki/Symphony_orchestra" class="mw-redirect" title="Symphony orchestra">symphony orchestras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">operatic</a> stars.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Starwood_Festival" title="Starwood Festival">Starwood Festival</a> is a seven-day <a href="/wiki/Neopagan" class="mw-redirect" title="Neopagan">Neopagan</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Age" title="New Age">New Age</a>, multi-cultural and world music festival that has been held in July every year since 1981 at various locations in the United States. It has featured such world music acts as <a href="/wiki/Amampondo" title="Amampondo">Amampondo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Babatunde_Olatunji" title="Babatunde Olatunji">Babatunde Olatunji</a>, <a href="/wiki/Badal_Roy" title="Badal Roy">Badal Roy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sikiru_Adepoju" title="Sikiru Adepoju">Sikiru Adepoju</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Prodigals" title="The Prodigals">the Prodigals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yaya_Diallo" title="Yaya Diallo">Yaya Diallo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Merl_Saunders" title="Merl Saunders">Merl Saunders</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Rainforest_Band" title="Rainforest Band">Rainforest Band</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baka_Beyond" title="Baka Beyond">Baka Beyond</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Kent_(musician)" title="Stephen Kent (musician)">Stephen Kent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyro_Baptista" title="Cyro Baptista">Cyro Baptista</a>, <a href="/wiki/Airto_Moreira" title="Airto Moreira">Airto Moreira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Muruga_Booker" title="Muruga Booker">Muruga Booker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gaelic_Storm" title="Gaelic Storm">Gaelic Storm</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Halim_El-Dabh" title="Halim El-Dabh">Halim El-Dabh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
