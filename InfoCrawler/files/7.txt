				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Gerard Salton Award</b> is presented by the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a> (ACM) <a href="/wiki/Special_Interest_Group_on_Information_Retrieval" title="Special Interest Group on Information Retrieval">Special Interest Group on Information Retrieval</a> (SIGIR) every three years to an individual who has made "significant, sustained and continuing contributions to research in information retrieval". SIGIR also co-sponsors (with <a href="/w/index.php?title=SIGWEB&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="SIGWEB (page does not exist)">SIGWEB</a>) the <a href="/wiki/Vannevar_Bush_Award" title="Vannevar Bush Award">Vannevar Bush Award</a>, for the best paper at the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Conference_on_Digital_Libraries" title="Joint Conference on Digital Libraries">Joint Conference on Digital Libraries</a>.</p>

<b>For ...</b> <i>"More than twenty years of significant, sustained and continuing contributions to research in information retrieval. His contributions to the theoretical development and practical use of Bayesian inference networks and language modelling for retrieval, and to their evaluation through extensive experiment and application, are particularly important. The Center for Intelligent Information Retrieval which he founded illustrates the strong synergies between fundamental research and its application to a wide range of practical information management problems."</i></li>
<li>2006 - <a href="/wiki/C._J._van_Rijsbergen" title="C. J. van Rijsbergen">C. J. van Rijsbergen</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Glasgow" title="University of Glasgow">University of Glasgow</a>&#160;: "Quantum haystacks."</li>

<li>2012 - <a href="/wiki/Norbert_Fuhr" title="Norbert Fuhr">Norbert Fuhr</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Duisburg-Essen" title="University of Duisburg-Essen">University of Duisburg-Essen</a>: "Information Retrieval as Engineering Science."</li>
