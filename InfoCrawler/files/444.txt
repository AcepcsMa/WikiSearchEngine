<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Germany</a> had a system of <a href="/wiki/Military_district" title="Military district">military districts</a> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Wehrkreis</i></span>) to relieve field commanders of as much administrative work as possible and to provide a regular flow of trained recruits and supplies to the Field Army. The <a href="/wiki/Field_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Field Army">Field Army</a> (<i>Oberbefehlshaber des Heeres</i>) was separate from the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Home_Army_(Germany)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Home Army (Germany) (page does not exist)">Home Command</a> (<i>Heimatkriegsgebiet</i>). The responsibilities of training, conscription, supply, and equipment were entrusted to the Home Command.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In peacetime, the <i>Wehrkreis</i> was the home to the army corps of the same number and all subordinate units of that formation. The corps commander also commanded the <i>Wehrkreis</i>. Command of the <i>Wehrkreis</i> passed to the corps second-in-command at the outbreak of war.</p>
<p>Before the start of the war, there were also four Motorized Army Corps (in effect, staffs to control the training of Panzer and Light Panzer formations). These had no corresponding military districts, but were served (as regards conscription and supplies) by the districts in which Corps headquarters or subordinate formations had their Home Garrison Stations. These Corps were:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/XIV_Panzer_Corps" title="XIV Panzer Corps">XIV. Armeekorps (mot.)</a> ("XIV Army Corps (Motorized)")</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=XV_Motorised_Corps&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="XV Motorised Corps (page does not exist)"><i>XV. Armeekorps (mot.)</i></a> ("XV Army Corps (Motorized)")</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/XVI_Panzer_Corps" class="mw-redirect" title="XVI Panzer Corps"><i>XVI. Armeekorps (mot.)</i></a> ("XVI Army Corps (Motorized)")</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=%27%27XIX._Armeekorps%27%27_(Germany)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="''XIX. Armeekorps'' (Germany) (page does not exist)"><i>XIX. Armeekorps</i></a> ("XIX Army Corps")</li>
</ul>
<p>Each <i>Wehrkreis</i> controlled <i>Wehrbezirk Hauptquartier</i> ("District Army Headquarters") and <i>Wehrersatzbezirk Hauptquartier</i> ("Alternate District Army Headquarters"). (These <i>Bezirk</i> HQs corresponded to civil political districts falling within the area of the <i>Wehrkreis</i>.) These in turn controlled <i>Bereich Hauptsitze</i> ("Area headquarters"), which controlled <i>Unterregion Hauptsitze</i> ("Sub-region Headquarters").</p>
<p>At the start of the war, there were fifteen Districts in Germany. During the war, four were added, and some Districts had territory added to them from other countries conquered by Germany.</p>

<li>XVIII - <a href="/wiki/Salzburg" title="Salzburg">Salzburg</a> (<a href="/wiki/Styria" title="Styria">Styria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carinthia_(state)" class="mw-redirect" title="Carinthia (state)">Carinthia</a>, <a href="/wiki/State_of_Tyrol" class="mw-redirect" title="State of Tyrol">Tyrol</a>, and northern <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>)</li>
</ul>
<p><i>Wehrkreise</i> XX and XXI were established to control <a href="/wiki/Danzig" class="mw-redirect" title="Danzig">Danzig</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84" title="Poznań">Posen</a>: the areas which were part of the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a> before <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, were awarded to Poland in 1918, and were <a href="/wiki/Polish_areas_annexed_by_Nazi_Germany" title="Polish areas annexed by Nazi Germany">annexed by Germany</a> after the <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Poland" title="Invasion of Poland">conquest of Poland in 1939</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li>XX - <a href="/wiki/Danzig" class="mw-redirect" title="Danzig">Danzig</a> (Danzig Free State, <a href="/wiki/Polish_Corridor" title="Polish Corridor">Polish Corridor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neustadt_an_der_Dosse" class="mw-redirect" title="Neustadt an der Dosse">Neustadt an der Dosse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stargard_Szczeci%C5%84ski" class="mw-redirect" title="Stargard Szczeciński">Stargard in Pommern</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marienwerder" class="mw-redirect" title="Marienwerder">Marienwerder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Graudenz" class="mw-redirect" title="Graudenz">Graudenz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bydgoszcz" title="Bydgoszcz">Bromberg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Toru%C5%84" title="Toruń">Thorn</a>)</li>
<li>XXI - <a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84" title="Poznań">Posen</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84" title="Poznań">Posen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leszno" title="Leszno">Lissa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hohensalza" class="mw-redirect" title="Hohensalza">Hohensalza</a>, <a href="/wiki/W%C5%82oc%C5%82awek" title="Włocławek">Leslau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kalisz" title="Kalisz">Kalisch</a>, and <a href="/wiki/%C5%81%C3%B3d%C5%BA" title="Łódź">Litzmannstadt</a>)</li>
</ul>
<p>Two additional <i>Wehrkreise</i> were established after the conquest of Poland. <i>Wehrkreis</i> <a href="/wiki/General-Government" class="mw-redirect" title="General-Government">General-Government</a> controlled the remainder of Poland. <i>Wehrkreis Böhmen-Mähren</i> controlled the so-called <a href="/wiki/Protectorate_of_Bohemia-Moravia" class="mw-redirect" title="Protectorate of Bohemia-Moravia">Protectorate of Bohemia-Moravia</a>: those parts of the Czech lands not part of the <a href="/wiki/Sudetenland" title="Sudetenland">Sudetenland</a>.</p>
