<p>The <b>War of the Succession of Landshut</b> resulted from a dispute between the duchies of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria-Munich" title="Bavaria-Munich">Bavaria-Munich</a> (<i>Bayern-München</i> in German) and <a href="/wiki/Bavaria-Landshut" title="Bavaria-Landshut">Bavaria-Landshut</a> (<i>Bayern-Landshut</i>). An earlier agreement between the different <a href="/wiki/House_of_Wittelsbach" title="House of Wittelsbach">Wittelsbach</a> lines, the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Pavia_(1329)" title="Treaty of Pavia (1329)">Treaty of Pavia (1329)</a>, concerned the law of succession and stated that if one branch should become extinct in the male line then the other would inherit. This agreement disregarded imperial law, which stipulated that the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a> should inherit should a line fail.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/George,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="George, Duke of Bavaria">George, Duke of Bavaria-Landshut</a> and his wife <a href="/wiki/Hedwig_Jagiellon,_Duchess_of_Bavaria" title="Hedwig Jagiellon, Duchess of Bavaria">Hedwig Jagiellon</a> failed to produce a male heir, so George - in a breach of both, imperial law at the one hand, the house treaty at the other - named his daughter Elisabeth as his heir. Because of the agreement, Duke <a href="/wiki/Albert_IV,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Albert IV, Duke of Bavaria">Albert</a> of the Munich line did not accept this, leading to war in 1503. Over the course of this two-year war, many villages surrounding Landshut were reduced to ashes, such as <a href="/wiki/Ergolding" title="Ergolding">Ergolding</a>.</p>
<p>The war ended in 1505 with the death of Elisabeth and her husband <a href="/wiki/Ruprecht_of_the_Palatinate_(Bishop_of_Freising)" title="Ruprecht of the Palatinate (Bishop of Freising)">Ruprecht, Count Palatine of the Rhine</a>, and a decision through arbitration by <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Maximilian I, Holy Roman Emperor">Emperor Maximilian I</a> on 30 July 1505 at the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Diet_(Holy_Roman_Empire)" title="Imperial Diet (Holy Roman Empire)">Diet</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>. The two grandsons of George, <a href="/wiki/Otto_Henry,_Elector_Palatine" title="Otto Henry, Elector Palatine">Otto Henry</a> (<i>Otto-Heinrich</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Philip,_Duke_of_Palatinate-Neuburg" title="Philip, Duke of Palatinate-Neuburg">Philip</a>, retained <a href="/wiki/Palatinate-Neuburg" title="Palatinate-Neuburg">Palatinate-Neuburg</a> (<i>Junge Pfalz</i>), a fragmented region from the upper <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> over <a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconia</a> to the northern part of the <a href="/wiki/Upper_Palatinate" title="Upper Palatinate">Upper Palatinate</a>. <a href="/wiki/Neuburg_an_der_Donau" title="Neuburg an der Donau">Neuburg an der Donau</a> was chosen as the capital of the new state. Because the two heirs had not yet reached their majority, <a href="/wiki/Frederick_II,_Count_Palatine_of_the_Rhine" class="mw-redirect" title="Frederick II, Count Palatine of the Rhine">Frederick II, Count Palatine of the Rhine</a>, served as regent in a caretaker regime. The rest of the territory went to the Munich line of the House of Wittelsbach.</p>
<p>The emperor took the territory around <a href="/wiki/Kufstein" title="Kufstein">Kufstein</a> for himself as reward for his mediation; the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_City_of_Nuremberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Imperial City of Nuremberg">Imperial City of Nuremberg</a> gained important territories to the east of the city, including the authorities of <a href="/wiki/Lauf_an_der_Pegnitz" title="Lauf an der Pegnitz">Lauf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hersbruck" title="Hersbruck">Hersbruck</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Altdorf_bei_N%C3%BCrnberg" title="Altdorf bei Nürnberg">Altdorf</a>. As <a href="/wiki/Count_Palatine" class="mw-redirect" title="Count Palatine">Count Palatine</a>, Otto-Heinrich spent huge sums of money to build a palace at Neuburg an der Donau. Through inheritance, he later became <a href="/wiki/Elector_Palatine" class="mw-redirect" title="Elector Palatine">Elector Palatine</a>, where his additions <i>Ottheinrichsbau</i> to <a href="/wiki/Heidelberg_Castle" title="Heidelberg Castle">Heidelberg Castle</a> made him one of the most important builders of the German <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a>.</p>
