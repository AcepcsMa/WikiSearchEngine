<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Did_you_know...">Did you know...</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="26_September_2017">26 September 2017</span></h3>
<ul>
<li><i><b>01:30, 26 September 2017 (UTC)</b></i></li>
</ul>
<div class="dyk-img" style="float: right; margin-left: 0.5em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Titian_-_The_Tribute_Money_-_Google_Art_Project_(715452).jpg" class="image" title="Titian's &quot;The Tribute Money&quot; (1516)"><img alt="Titian's &quot;The Tribute Money&quot; (1516)" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Titian_-_The_Tribute_Money_-_Google_Art_Project_%28715452%29.jpg/100px-Titian_-_The_Tribute_Money_-_Google_Art_Project_%28715452%29.jpg" width="100" height="137" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Titian_-_The_Tribute_Money_-_Google_Art_Project_%28715452%29.jpg/150px-Titian_-_The_Tribute_Money_-_Google_Art_Project_%28715452%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Titian_-_The_Tribute_Money_-_Google_Art_Project_%28715452%29.jpg/200px-Titian_-_The_Tribute_Money_-_Google_Art_Project_%28715452%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3060" data-file-height="4188" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;"><i>The Tribute Money</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>... that <a href="/wiki/Titian" title="Titian">Titian</a>'s 1516 work <i><b><a href="/wiki/The_Tribute_Money_(Titian)" title="The Tribute Money (Titian)">The Tribute Money</a></b></i> <i>(pictured)</i> was painted to be a cupboard door?</li>
<li>... that the <b><a href="/wiki/Glover%27s_pika" title="Glover's pika">Glover's pika</a></b> was at different times treated as a subspecies of the <a href="/wiki/Turkestan_red_pika" title="Turkestan red pika">Turkestan red pika</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_red_pika" title="Chinese red pika">Chinese red pika</a>, but is now accepted as an independent species?</li>
<li>... that carbon-carrying minerals are known as <b><a href="/wiki/Organic_mineral" title="Organic mineral">organic minerals</a></b>, except for some that were considered inorganic before 1828?</li>
<li>... that <a href="/wiki/Dua_Lipa" title="Dua Lipa">Dua Lipa</a> said that her song "<b><a href="/wiki/New_Rules_(song)" title="New Rules (song)">New Rules</a></b>" talks about setting rules to keep "your distance from someone who's bad for you"?</li>
<li>... that while working on the <b><a href="/wiki/Representation_theory_of_the_Lorentz_group" title="Representation theory of the Lorentz group">representations of the Lorentz group</a></b>, an encounter with <a href="/wiki/Paul_Dirac" title="Paul Dirac">Dirac</a> convinced <a href="/wiki/Harish-Chandra" title="Harish-Chandra">Harish-Chandra</a> that he did not have "the mysterious sixth sense which one needs in order to succeed in physics"?</li>
<li>... that residents of <a href="/wiki/Uttan" title="Uttan">Uttan</a> aided <b><a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Mendonca" title="Gilbert Mendonca">Gilbert Mendonca</a></b><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s candidacy in the <a href="/wiki/Maharashtra_Legislative_Assembly_election,_2009" title="Maharashtra Legislative Assembly election, 2009">2009 Maharashtra Legislative Assembly election</a> by refusing to allow other candidates to campaign in their town?</li>
<li>... that in <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_One_Day_International_cricket" title="Women's One Day International cricket">Women's One Day International cricket</a>, <b><a href="/wiki/India_women%27s_national_cricket_team_record_by_opponent" title="India women's national cricket team record by opponent">India</a></b> has the fourth highest number of victories?</li>
<li>... that the 1896 novel <i>The Courage of Her Convictions</i> by <b><a href="/wiki/Caroline_Augusta_Huling" title="Caroline Augusta Huling">Caroline Augusta Huling</a></b> is the story of a woman who is <a href="/wiki/Artificial_insemination" title="Artificial insemination">artificially inseminated</a>?</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="25_September_2017">25 September 2017</span></h3>
<ul>
<li><i><b>12:00, 25 September 2017 (UTC)</b></i></li>
</ul>
<div class="dyk-img" style="float: right; margin-left: 0.5em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 120px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tom_Doerr%27s_Lambda.jpg" class="image" title="Tom Doerr's lambda"><img alt="Tom Doerr's lambda" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/ec/Tom_Doerr%27s_Lambda.jpg/120px-Tom_Doerr%27s_Lambda.jpg" width="120" height="86" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/ec/Tom_Doerr%27s_Lambda.jpg/180px-Tom_Doerr%27s_Lambda.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/ec/Tom_Doerr%27s_Lambda.jpg/240px-Tom_Doerr%27s_Lambda.jpg 2x" data-file-width="363" data-file-height="261" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">Tom Doerr's lambda</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>... that the <a href="/wiki/Lambda" title="Lambda">lambda</a> <i>(pictured)</i> designed by <b><a href="/wiki/Tom_Doerr" title="Tom Doerr">Tom Doerr</a></b> as a symbol for the <a href="/wiki/Gay_Activists_Alliance" title="Gay Activists Alliance">Gay Activists Alliance</a> was <a href="/wiki/Chrome_yellow" title="Chrome yellow">chrome yellow</a>, a reference to <a href="/wiki/Aldous_Huxley" title="Aldous Huxley">Aldous Huxley</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/Crome_Yellow" title="Crome Yellow">Crome Yellow</a></i>?</li>
<li>... that the <b><a href="/wiki/St._Collins_Lane" title="St. Collins Lane">St. Collins Lane</a></b> luxury shopping centre replaced a building considered one of <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>'s worst?</li>
<li>... that <a href="/wiki/Scotts_Bluff_National_Monument" title="Scotts Bluff National Monument">the bluffs</a> near where <b><a href="/wiki/Hiram_Scott" title="Hiram Scott">Hiram Scott</a></b> died in 1828 bear his name?</li>
<li>... that a <a href="/wiki/Canoeing" title="Canoeing">canoeist</a> bore the <b><a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics" title="São Tomé and Príncipe at the 2016 Summer Olympics">São Tomé and Príncipe flag</a></b> at the opening ceremony of the 2016 Summer Olympics, but at the closing ceremony it was carried by an unnamed volunteer?</li>
<li>... that <a href="/wiki/Maryam_Mirzakhani" title="Maryam Mirzakhani">Maryam Mirzakhani</a> won the <b><a href="/wiki/Ruth_Lyttle_Satter_Prize_in_Mathematics" title="Ruth Lyttle Satter Prize in Mathematics">Ruth Lyttle Satter Prize</a></b> for women in mathematics a year before she won the <a href="/wiki/Fields_Medal" title="Fields Medal">Fields Medal</a> for the same work?</li>
<li>... that the acorn worm <i><b><a href="/wiki/Saccoglossus_bromophenolosus" title="Saccoglossus bromophenolosus">Saccoglossus bromophenolosus</a></b></i> is named after a chemical found in its tissues?</li>
<li>... that as part of her journalism degree, sportscaster <b><a href="/wiki/Kelly_Crull" title="Kelly Crull">Kelly Crull</a></b> worked at <a href="/wiki/KOMU-TV" title="KOMU-TV">KOMU-TV</a>?</li>
<li>... that while many people think "<b><a href="/wiki/Trapped_(Jimmy_Cliff_song)" title="Trapped (Jimmy Cliff song)">Trapped</a></b>" is a <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a> song from the 1980s, it was written and recorded by <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Cliff" title="Jimmy Cliff">Jimmy Cliff</a> in 1972?</li>
</ul>
