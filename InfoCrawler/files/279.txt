<p><b>Burgomaster</b> (alternatively spelled <b>burgermeister</b>, literally <i>master of the <a href="/wiki/Citizenship" title="Citizenship">town</a></i>, <i>master of the <a href="/wiki/Borough" title="Borough">borough</a></i>, <i>master of the <a href="/wiki/Fortress" class="mw-redirect" title="Fortress">fortress</a></i>, or <i>master of the citizens</i>) is the English form of various terms in or derived from <a href="/wiki/Germanic_languages" title="Germanic languages">Germanic languages</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Chief_magistrate" title="Chief magistrate">chief magistrate</a> or chairman of the executive council, usually of a sub-national level of administration such as a city or a similar entity. The name in English was derived from the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Burgemeester" title="Burgemeester">burgemeester</a></i>. In some cases, Burgomaster was the title of the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> and <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Sovereign" class="mw-redirect" title="Sovereign">sovereign</a> (or partially or de facto sovereign) <a href="/wiki/City-state" title="City-state">city-state</a>, sometimes combined with other titles (as in <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Hamburg" title="Government of Hamburg">First Mayor and President of the Senate</a>). Contemporary titles are commonly translated into English as <i><a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Municipal_government">Municipal government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burgomaster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Municipal government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bartholom%C3%A4us_Bruyn_(I)_-_Burgomaster_Arnold_von_Brauweiler_-_WGA03660.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Bartholom%C3%A4us_Bruyn_%28I%29_-_Burgomaster_Arnold_von_Brauweiler_-_WGA03660.jpg/170px-Bartholom%C3%A4us_Bruyn_%28I%29_-_Burgomaster_Arnold_von_Brauweiler_-_WGA03660.jpg" width="170" height="245" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Bartholom%C3%A4us_Bruyn_%28I%29_-_Burgomaster_Arnold_von_Brauweiler_-_WGA03660.jpg/255px-Bartholom%C3%A4us_Bruyn_%28I%29_-_Burgomaster_Arnold_von_Brauweiler_-_WGA03660.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Bartholom%C3%A4us_Bruyn_%28I%29_-_Burgomaster_Arnold_von_Brauweiler_-_WGA03660.jpg/340px-Bartholom%C3%A4us_Bruyn_%28I%29_-_Burgomaster_Arnold_von_Brauweiler_-_WGA03660.jpg 2x" data-file-width="671" data-file-height="966" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bartholom%C3%A4us_Bruyn_(I)_-_Burgomaster_Arnold_von_Brauweiler_-_WGA03660.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Arnold von Brauweiler, a German burgomaster, known in German as <i>Bürgermeister</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>Bürgermeister (literally: 'master of the citizens'), in German: in Germany, Austria, and formerly in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. In Switzerland, the title was abolished mid-19th century; various current titles for roughly equivalent offices include <i><a href="/wiki/Gemeinde" title="Gemeinde">Gemeindepr</a>äsident</i>, <i>Stadtpräsident</i>, <i>Gemeindeamtmann</i>, and <i>Stadtamtmann</i>.
<ul>
<li><i>Oberbürgermeister</i> ('Supreme Burgomaster') is the most common version for a mayor in a big city in Germany (it is not in use in Austria). The <i>Ober-</i> (lit. <i>upper</i>) prefix is used in many ranking systems for the next level up including military designations. The mayors of cities, which simultaneously comprise one of Germany's 112 <a href="/wiki/Kreisfreie_Stadt" class="mw-redirect" title="Kreisfreie Stadt">urban districts</a> usually bear this title. <i>Urban districts</i> are comparable to <a href="/wiki/Independent_city" title="Independent city">independent cities</a> in the English-speaking world. However, also the mayors of some cities, which do not comprise an urban district, but often used to comprise one until the territorial reforms in the 1970s, bear the title <i>Oberbürgermeister</i>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Borgmester (<a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>)</li>
<li>Borgarstjóri (<a href="/wiki/Faroese_language" title="Faroese language">Faroese</a>)</li>
<li>Borgermester (<a href="/wiki/Bokm%C3%A5l" title="Bokmål">Norwegian (Bokmål)</a>)</li>
<li>Börgermester (<a href="/wiki/Low_German" title="Low German">Low German</a>)</li>
<li>Burgomaestre (Spanish)</li>
<li>Purkmistr (Czech)</li>
<li>Burgumaisu (<a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque</a>)</li>
<li>Borgomastro or Sindaco-Borgomastro (Italian): in few communes of <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Burgemeester" title="Burgemeester">Burgemeester</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: in <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> a party-political post, though formally nominated by the regional government and answerable to it, the federal state and even the province. Mayor. In the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> nominated by the municipal council but appointed by the crown. In theory above the parties, in practice a high-profile party-political post.</li>
<li>Bourgmestre (French) in Belgium, <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_the_Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" class="mw-redirect" title="Administrative divisions of the Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a></li>
<li>Bürgermeister (Estonian)</li>
<li>Burmistras (<a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_language" title="Lithuanian language">Lithuanian</a>), derived from German.</li>
<li>Buergermeeschter (<a href="/wiki/Luxembourgish_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Luxembourgish language">Luxembourgish</a>)</li>
<li>Polgármester (<a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>), derived from German.</li>
<li>Burmistrz (<a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>), a <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayoral</a> title, derived from German. The German form <i>Oberbürgermeister</i> ('Supreme Burgomaster') is often translated as <i>Nadburmistrz</i>. The German-derived terminology reflects the involvement of German settlers in the early history of many Polish towns.</li>
<li>Borgmästare, <i>kommunalborgmästare</i> (<a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a>); the title is not used in Sweden in present times, the closest equivalent being <i>kommunalråd</i> (often translated to English as <a href="/wiki/Municipal_commissioner" class="mw-redirect" title="Municipal commissioner">Municipal commissioner</a>) or <i>borgarråd</i> (only in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_Municipality" title="Stockholm Municipality">Stockholm City</a>).</li>
<li>Boargemaster (<a href="/wiki/West_Frisian_language" title="West Frisian language">West Frisian</a>)</li>
<li>Pormestari (<a href="/wiki/Finnish_language" title="Finnish language">Finnish</a>)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="City_state_government">City state government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burgomaster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: City state government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>In history (sometimes until the beginning of the 19th century) in many <a href="/wiki/Free_imperial_city" title="Free imperial city">free imperial cities</a> (such as <a href="/wiki/Bremen" title="Bremen">Bremen</a>, Hamburg, <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCbeck" title="Lübeck">Lübeck</a> etc.) the function of burgomaster was usually held simultaneously by three persons, serving as an executive college. One of the three being burgomaster in chief for a year (called in some cases in German: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>präsidierender Bürgermeister</i></span>; in <i>presiding burgomaster</i>), the second being the prior burgomaster in chief, the third being the upcoming one. <i>Präsidierender Bürgermeister</i> is now an obsolete formulation sometimes found in historic texts.</li>
<li>In an important city, especially in a <a href="/wiki/City_state" class="mw-redirect" title="City state">city state</a> (Stadtstaat), where one of the <i>Bürgermeister</i> has a rank equivalent to that of a <a href="/wiki/Minister-president" title="Minister-president">minister-president</a> (<a href="/wiki/Governor_(US)" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor (US)">governor</a>), there can be several posts called <i>Bürgermeister</i> in the city's executive college, justifying the use of a compound title for the actual highest magistrate (also rendered as <a href="/wiki/Lord_mayor" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord mayor">lord mayor</a>), such as:
<ul>
<li><i>Regierender Bürgermeister</i> (literally 'governing burgomaster', commonly translated as <a href="/wiki/Governing_Mayor_of_Berlin" title="Governing Mayor of Berlin">governing mayor</a>) in <a href="/wiki/West_Berlin" title="West Berlin">West Berlin</a> and reunited Berlin,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> while in Berlin the term <i>Bürgermeister</i> without attribute – English <i>Mayor</i> – refers to his deputies,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and while the heads of the 12 <a href="/wiki/Borough" title="Borough">boroughs</a> of Berlin are called <i>Bezirksbürgermeister</i>, English <i>borough mayor</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><i>Erster Bürgermeister</i> (literally <a href="/wiki/First_Mayor_of_the_city-state_of_Hamburg" class="mw-redirect" title="First Mayor of the city-state of Hamburg">First Burgomaster</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a></li>
<li><i>Bürgermeister und Präsident des Senats</i> ('Burgomaster and President of <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Bremen" title="Senate of Bremen">the Senate</a>') in <a href="/wiki/Bremen_(state)" title="Bremen (state)">Bremen</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Compound_title_at_supra-municipal_level">Compound title at supra-municipal level</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burgomaster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Compound title at supra-municipal level">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><i><b>Amtsbürgermeister</b></i> (German; roughly translated: 'District Burgomaster') can be used for the chief magistrate of a Swiss constitutive canton, as in <a href="/wiki/Aargau" title="Aargau">Aargau</a> 1815–1831 (next styled <i><a href="/wiki/Landamman" class="mw-redirect" title="Landamman">Landamman</a></i>)</li>
