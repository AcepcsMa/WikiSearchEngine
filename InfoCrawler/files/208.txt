<p>The Colonial expansion under the <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">crown of Castile</a> was initiated by the Spanish <i><a href="/wiki/Conquistador" title="Conquistador">conquistadores</a></i> and developed by its administrators and missionaries. The motivations for colonial expansion were trade and the spread of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic">Catholic</a> faith through <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">indigenous</a> conversions.</p>
<p>Beginning with the 1492 arrival of <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> and continuing for over three centuries, the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a> would expand across the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> Islands, half of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, most of <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a> and much of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> (including present day <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Southwestern_United_States" title="Southwestern United States">Southwestern</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="West Coast of the United States">Pacific Coastal</a> regions of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>). It is estimated that during the colonial period (1492–1832), a total of 1.86 million Spaniards settled in the Americas and a further 3.5 million immigrated during the post-colonial era (1850–1950).</p>
<p>In the early 19th century, the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_American_wars_of_independence" title="Spanish American wars of independence">Spanish American wars of independence</a> resulted in the emancipation of most Spanish colonies in the Americas, except for <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, which were finally given up in 1898, following the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish–American War">Spanish–American War</a>, together with <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> in the Pacific. Spain's loss of these last territories politically ended the Spanish rule in the Americas.</p>

<p>The Catholic Monarchs <a href="/wiki/Isabella_I_of_Castile" title="Isabella I of Castile">Isabella of Castile</a>, Queen of <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">Castile</a> and her husband <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_Aragon" title="Ferdinand II of Aragon">King Ferdinand</a>, King of <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon" title="Crown of Aragon">Aragon</a>, pursued a policy of joint rule of their kingdoms and created a single Spanish monarchy. Even though Castile and Aragon were ruled jointly by their respective monarchs, they remained separate kingdoms. The Catholic Monarchs gave official approval for the plans of Genoese mariner <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> for a voyage to reach India by sailing West. The funding came from the queen of Castile, so the profits from Spanish expedition flowed to Castile. In the extension of Spanish sovereignty to its overseas territories, authority for expeditions of discovery, conquest, and settlement resided in the monarchy.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="West_Indies">West Indies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: West Indies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Columbus made four voyages to the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a> as the monarchs granted Columbus the governorship of the new territories, and financed more of his trans-Atlantic journeys. He founded <a href="/wiki/La_Navidad" title="La Navidad">La Navidad</a> on the island later named <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a> (now divided into Haiti and the Dominican Republic), in what is present day <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a> on his first voyage. After its destruction by the indigenous <a href="/wiki/Taino" class="mw-redirect" title="Taino">Taino</a> people, the town of Isabella was begun in 1493, on his second voyage. In 1496 his brother, <a href="/wiki/Bartholomew_Columbus" title="Bartholomew Columbus">Bartholomew</a>, founded <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo</a>. By 1500, despite a high death rate, there were between 300 and 1000 Spanish settled in the area. The local <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Taíno">Taíno</a> people continued to resist, refusing to plant crops and abandoning their Spanish-occupied villages. The first mainland explorations were followed by a phase of inland expeditions and conquest. In 1500 the city of <a href="/wiki/Nueva_C%C3%A1diz" title="Nueva Cádiz">Nueva Cádiz</a> was founded on the island of <a href="/wiki/Cubagua" title="Cubagua">Cubagua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, and it was followed by the founding by <a href="/wiki/Alonso_de_Ojeda" title="Alonso de Ojeda">Alonso de Ojeda</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aragua_(state)" class="mw-redirect" title="Aragua (state)">Santa Cruz</a> in present-day <a href="/wiki/Guajira_Peninsula" title="Guajira Peninsula">Guajira peninsula</a>. <a href="/wiki/Cuman%C3%A1" title="Cumaná">Cumaná</a> in Venezuela was the first permanent settlement founded by <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europeans</a> in the mainland <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> in 1501 by <a href="/wiki/Franciscan_Order" class="mw-redirect" title="Franciscan Order">Franciscan</a> <a href="/wiki/Friar" title="Friar">friars</a>, but due to successful attacks by the indigenous people, it had to be refounded several times, until <a href="/wiki/Diego_Hern%C3%A1ndez_de_Serpa" title="Diego Hernández de Serpa">Diego Hernández de Serpa</a>'s foundation in 1569. The Spanish founded San Sebastian de Uraba in 1509 but abandoned it within the year. There is indirect evidence that the first permanent Spanish mainland settlement established in the Americas was <a href="/wiki/Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_la_Antigua_del_Dari%C3%A9n" title="Santa María la Antigua del Darién">Santa María la Antigua del Darién</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_Mexico" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish conquest of Mexico">Spanish conquest of Mexico</a></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For more details on this topic, see <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="Spanish conquest of Yucatán">Spanish conquest of Yucatán</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_Guatemala" title="Spanish conquest of Guatemala">Spanish conquest of Guatemala</a>.</div>

<p>The Spanish conquest of Mexico is generally understood to be the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_Mexico" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish conquest of Mexico">Spanish conquest of the Aztec Empire</a> (1519–21) which was the base for later conquests of other regions. Later conquests were protracted campaigns with less spectacular results than conquest of the Aztecs. The <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="Spanish conquest of Yucatán">Spanish conquest of Yucatán</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_Guatemala" title="Spanish conquest of Guatemala">Spanish conquest of Guatemala</a>, the war of Mexico's west, and the <a href="/wiki/Chichimeca_War" title="Chichimeca War">Chichimeca War</a> in northern Mexico expanded Spanish control over territory and indigenous populations.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> But not until the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_Peru" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish conquest of Peru">Spanish conquest of Peru</a> was the conquest of the Aztecs matched in scope by the victory over the Inca empire in 1532.</p>
<p>The Spanish conquest of the Aztec empire was led by <a href="/wiki/Hern%C3%A1n_Cort%C3%A9s" title="Hernán Cortés">Hernán Cortés</a>. The victory over the Aztecs was relatively quick, from 1519 to 1521, and aided by his <a href="/wiki/Tlaxcala_(Nahua_state)" title="Tlaxcala (Nahua state)">Tlaxcala</a> and other allies from indigenous city-states or <a href="/wiki/Altepetl" title="Altepetl">altepetl</a>. These polities allied against the <a href="/wiki/Aztec" title="Aztec">Aztec</a> empire, to which they paid tribute following conquest or threat of conquest, leaving the city-states' political hierarchy and social structure in place.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="Spanish conquest of Yucatán">Spanish conquest of Yucatán</a> was a much longer campaign, from 1551 to 1697, against the <a href="/wiki/Maya_peoples" title="Maya peoples">Maya peoples</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucatán Peninsula">Yucatán Peninsula</a> of present-day Mexico and northern <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. When <a href="/wiki/Hern%C3%A1n_Cort%C3%A9s" title="Hernán Cortés">Hernán Cortés</a> landed ashore at present day <a href="/wiki/Veracruz,_Veracruz" class="mw-redirect" title="Veracruz, Veracruz">Veracruz</a> and founded the Spanish city there on April 22, 1519, marks the beginning of 300 years of Spanish hegemony over the region. The assertion of royal control over the Kingdom of New Spain and the initial Spanish conquerors took over a decade, with importance of the region meriting the creation of the Viceroyalty of New Spain was established by Charles V in 1535 with the appointment of Don <a href="/wiki/Antonio_de_Mendoza" title="Antonio de Mendoza">Antonio de Mendoza</a> as the first viceroy.</p>
<p>Spain colonized and exerted control of <a href="/wiki/Alta_California" title="Alta California">Alta California</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_missions_in_California" title="Spanish missions in California">Spanish missions in California</a> until the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_secularization_act_of_1833" title="Mexican secularization act of 1833">Mexican secularization act of 1833</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Peru">Peru</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Peru">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In 1532 at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cajamarca" title="Battle of Cajamarca">Battle of Cajamarca</a> a group of Spaniards under <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Pizarro" title="Francisco Pizarro">Francisco Pizarro</a> and their <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas#South_America" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">indigenous Andean</a> <a href="/wiki/Indian_auxiliaries" title="Indian auxiliaries">Indian auxiliaries</a> native allies ambushed and captured the Emperor <a href="/wiki/Atahualpa" title="Atahualpa">Atahualpa</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Inca_Empire" title="Inca Empire">Inca Empire</a>. It was the first step in a long campaign that took decades of fighting to subdue the mightiest <a href="/wiki/Empire" title="Empire">empire</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>. In the following years Spain extended its rule over the Empire of the <a href="/wiki/Inca_civilization" class="mw-redirect" title="Inca civilization">Inca civilization</a>.</p>
<p>The Spanish took advantage of a recent civil war between the factions of the two brothers Emperor <a href="/wiki/Atahualpa" title="Atahualpa">Atahualpa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hu%C3%A1scar" title="Huáscar">Huáscar</a>, and the enmity of <a href="/wiki/Classification_of_indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas#South_America" title="Classification of indigenous peoples of the Americas">indigenous nations</a> the Incas had subjugated, such as the <a href="/wiki/Huancas" class="mw-redirect" title="Huancas">Huancas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chachapoyas_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Chachapoyas culture">Chachapoyas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ca%C3%B1ari" title="Cañari">Cañaris</a>. In the following years the <a href="/wiki/Conquistador" title="Conquistador">conquistadors</a> and indigenous allies extended control over Greater Andes Region. The <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_Per%C3%BA" class="mw-redirect" title="Viceroyalty of Perú">Viceroyalty of Perú</a> was established in 1542. The <a href="/wiki/Neo-Inca_State" title="Neo-Inca State">last Inca stronghold</a> was conquered by the Spanish in 1572.</p>
<h3><span id="Río_de_la_Plata_and_Paraguay"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="R.C3.ADo_de_la_Plata_and_Paraguay">Río de la Plata and Paraguay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Río de la Plata and Paraguay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>European explorers arrived in <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata" title="Río de la Plata">Río de la Plata</a> in 1516. Their first Spanish settlement in this zone was the <a href="/wiki/Sancti_Spiritu_(Argentina)" title="Sancti Spiritu (Argentina)">Fort of Sancti Spiritu</a> established in 1527 next to the <a href="/wiki/Paran%C3%A1_River" title="Paraná River">Paraná River</a>. <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>, a permanent colony, was established in 1536 and in 1537 <a href="/wiki/Asunci%C3%B3n" title="Asunción">Asunción</a> was established in the area that is now <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a>. Buenos Aires suffered attacks by the indigenous peoples that forced the settlers away, and in 1541 the site was abandoned. A second (and permanent) settlement was established in 1580 by <a href="/wiki/Juan_de_Garay" title="Juan de Garay">Juan de Garay</a>, who arrived by sailing down the <a href="/wiki/Paran%C3%A1_River" title="Paraná River">Paraná River</a> from <a href="/wiki/Asunci%C3%B3n" title="Asunción">Asunción</a> (now the capital of <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a>). He dubbed the settlement "Santísima Trinidad" and its port became "Puerto de Santa María de los Buenos Aires." The city came to be the head of the <a href="/wiki/Governorate_of_the_R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata" title="Governorate of the Río de la Plata">Governorate of the Río de la Plata</a> and in 1776 elevated to be the capital of the new <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_the_R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata" title="Viceroyalty of the Río de la Plata">Viceroyalty of the Río de la Plata</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Granada">New Granada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: New Granada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_the_Muisca" title="Spanish conquest of the Muisca">Spanish conquest of the Muisca</a></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_the_Chibchan_Nations" title="Spanish conquest of the Chibchan Nations">Spanish conquest of the Chibchan Nations</a></div>
<p>Between 1537 and 1543, six<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Spanish expeditions entered highland Colombia, conquered the <a href="/wiki/Muisca_Confederation" title="Muisca Confederation">Muisca Confederation</a>, and set up the <a href="/wiki/New_Kingdom_of_Granada" title="New Kingdom of Granada">New Kingdom of Granada</a> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Nuevo Reino de Granada</i></span>). <a href="/wiki/Gonzalo_Jim%C3%A9nez_de_Quesada" title="Gonzalo Jiménez de Quesada">Gonzalo Jiménez de Quesada</a> was the leading conquistador with <a href="/wiki/Hern%C3%A1n_P%C3%A9rez_de_Quesada" title="Hernán Pérez de Quesada">his brother Hernán</a> second in command.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It was governed by the president of the <a href="/wiki/Audiencia_of_Bogot%C3%A1" class="mw-redirect" title="Audiencia of Bogotá">Audiencia of Bogotá</a>, and comprised an area corresponding mainly to modern-day <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a> and parts of <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>. The <a href="/wiki/List_of_conquistadors_in_Colombia" title="List of conquistadors in Colombia">conquistadors</a> originally organized it as a <a href="/wiki/Captain_general" title="Captain general">captaincy general</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_Peru" title="Viceroyalty of Peru">Viceroyalty of Peru</a>. The crown established the <i><a href="/wiki/Audiencia_Real" class="mw-redirect" title="Audiencia Real">audiencia</a></i> in 1549. Ultimately, the kingdom became part of the <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_New_Granada" title="Viceroyalty of New Granada">Viceroyalty of New Granada</a> first in 1717 and permanently in 1739. After several attempts to set up independent states in the 1810s, the kingdom and the viceroyalty ceased to exist altogether in 1819 with the establishment of <a href="/wiki/Gran_Colombia" title="Gran Colombia">Gran Colombia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governing">Governing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Governing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_New_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Viceroyalty of New Spain">Viceroyalty of New Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_Peru" title="Viceroyalty of Peru">Viceroyalty of Peru</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Intendant#The_Spanish_Monarchy" title="Intendant">Intendant §&#160;The Spanish Monarchy</a></div>

<p>Spain's administration of its colonies in the Americas was divided into the <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_New_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Viceroyalty of New Spain">Viceroyalty of New Spain</a> 1535 (capital, <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9xico_City" class="mw-redirect" title="México City">México City</a>), and the <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_Peru" title="Viceroyalty of Peru">Viceroyalty of Peru</a> 1542 (capital, <a href="/wiki/Lima" title="Lima">Lima</a>). In the 18th century the additional <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_New_Granada" title="Viceroyalty of New Granada">Viceroyalty of New Granada</a> 1717 (capital, Bogotá), and <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_Rio_de_la_Plata" class="mw-redirect" title="Viceroyalty of Rio de la Plata">Viceroyalty of Rio de la Plata</a> 1776 (capital, Buenos Aires) were established from portions of the Viceroyalty of Peru.</p>
<p>This evolved from the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_the_Indies" title="Council of the Indies">Council of the Indies</a> and Viceroyalties into an <a href="/wiki/Intendant" title="Intendant">Intendant</a> system, raise more revenue and promote greater efficiency.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="19th_century">19th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 19th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Spanish_American_wars_of_independence" title="Spanish American wars of independence">Spanish American wars of independence</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Spanish_America_XVIII_Century_(Most_Expansion).png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Spanish_America_XVIII_Century_%28Most_Expansion%29.png/220px-Spanish_America_XVIII_Century_%28Most_Expansion%29.png" width="220" height="300" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Spanish_America_XVIII_Century_%28Most_Expansion%29.png/330px-Spanish_America_XVIII_Century_%28Most_Expansion%29.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Spanish_America_XVIII_Century_%28Most_Expansion%29.png/440px-Spanish_America_XVIII_Century_%28Most_Expansion%29.png 2x" data-file-width="749" data-file-height="1022" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Spanish_America_XVIII_Century_(Most_Expansion).png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Spanish colonialization in the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>During the Napoleonic <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_War" title="Peninsular War">Peninsular War</a> in Europe between France and Spain, assemblies called <i><a href="/wiki/Junta_(Peninsular_War)" title="Junta (Peninsular War)">juntas</a></i> were established to rule in the name of <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_VII_of_Spain" title="Ferdinand VII of Spain">Ferdinand VII of Spain</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Libertadores" title="Libertadores">Libertadores</a> (Spanish and Portuguese for "Liberators") were the principal leaders of the Spanish American wars of independence. They were predominantly <i><a href="/wiki/Criollo_people" title="Criollo people">criollos</a></i> (Americas-born people of European ancestry, mostly Spanish or Portuguese), <a href="/wiki/Bourgeois" class="mw-redirect" title="Bourgeois">bourgeois</a> and influenced by <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberalism</a> and in some cases with military training in the <a href="/wiki/Homeland" title="Homeland">mother country</a>.</p>
<p>In 1809 the first declarations of independence from Spanish rule occurred in the <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_New_Granada" title="Viceroyalty of New Granada">Viceroyalty of New Granada</a>. The first two were in present-day <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> at <a href="/wiki/Sucre" title="Sucre">Sucre</a> (May 25), and <a href="/wiki/La_Paz" title="La Paz">La Paz</a> (July 16); and the third in present-day <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> at <a href="/wiki/Quito" title="Quito">Quito</a> (August 10). In 1810 Mexico declared independence, with the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_War_of_Independence" title="Mexican War of Independence">Mexican War of Independence</a> following for over a decade. In 1821 <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Treaty of Córdoba">Treaty of Córdoba</a> established Mexican independence from Spain and concluded the War. The <a href="/wiki/Plan_of_Iguala" title="Plan of Iguala">Plan of Iguala</a> was part of the peace treaty to establish a constitutional foundation for an independent Mexico.</p>
<p>These began a movement for <a href="/wiki/Spanish_American_wars_of_independence" title="Spanish American wars of independence">colonial independence</a> that spread to Spain's other colonies in the Americas. The ideas from the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a> influenced the efforts. All of the colonies, except Cuba and Puerto Rico, attained independence by the 1820s. The <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a> offered support, wanting to end the Spanish monopoly on trade with its colonies in the Americas.</p>
<p>In 1898, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> achieved victory in the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish–American War">Spanish–American War</a> with Spain, ending the Spanish colonial era. Spanish possession and rule of its remaining colonies in the Americas ended in that year with its sovereignty transferred to the United States. The United States took occupation of <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. Puerto Rico continues to be a possession of the United States, now officially continues as a <a href="/wiki/Self-governing" class="mw-redirect" title="Self-governing">self-governing</a> <a href="/wiki/Unincorporated_territory" class="mw-redirect" title="Unincorporated territory">unincorporated territory</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographic_impact">Demographic impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Demographic impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It has been estimated that over 1.86 million Spaniards emigrated to Latin America in the period between 1492 and 1824, with millions more continuing to immigrate following independence.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Hispaniola, the indigenous Taíno pre-contact population before the arrival of Columbus of several hundred thousand had declined to sixty thousand by 1509. Although population estimates vary, Dominican friar <a href="/wiki/Bartolom%C3%A9_de_las_Casas" title="Bartolomé de las Casas">Bartolomé de las Casas</a>, the "Defender of the Indians" estimated there were 6 million (6,000,000) <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Taíno people">Taíno</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arawak_peoples" class="mw-redirect" title="Arawak peoples">Arawak</a> in the Caribbean at the time of Columbus's arrival in 1492.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The population of the Native Amerindian population in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> declined by an estimated 90% (reduced to 1–2.5 million people) by the early 17th century. In <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> the indigenous Amerindian pre-contact population of around 6.5 million declined to 1 million by the early 17th century.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The overwhelming cause of decline in both Mexico and Peru was <a href="/wiki/Infectious_diseases" class="mw-redirect" title="Infectious diseases">infectious diseases</a>, although the brutality of the <a href="/wiki/Encomienda" title="Encomienda">Encomienda</a> also played a significant part in the population decline.</p>
<p>Of the history of the indigenous population of <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sherburne_F._Cook" title="Sherburne F. Cook">Sherburne F. Cook</a> (1896–1974) was the most painstakingly careful researcher. From decades of research he made estimates for the pre-contact population and the history of demographic decline during the Spanish and post-Spanish periods. According to Cook, the indigenous Californian population at first contact, in 1769, was about 310,000 and had dropped to 25,000 by 1910. The vast majority of the decline happened after the Spanish period, during the <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexican</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a> periods of Californian history (1821–1910), with the most dramatic collapse (200,000 to 25,000) occurring in the US period (1846–1910).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
