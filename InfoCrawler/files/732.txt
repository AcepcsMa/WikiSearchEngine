<p><b>Lorenz Ritter</b> (24 November 1832 – 3 September 1921) was a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> painter and etcher.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenz_Ritter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was born at <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, and a pupil of <a href="/wiki/Carl_Alexander_Heideloff" title="Carl Alexander Heideloff">Heideloff</a>. He painted chiefly in <a href="/wiki/Watercolor" class="mw-redirect" title="Watercolor">watercolors</a> and etched numerous architectural views of Nuremberg and some subjects from <a href="/wiki/North_Italy" class="mw-redirect" title="North Italy">North Italy</a>. He died in Nuremberg.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenz_Ritter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His older brother, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Ritter_(painter)" title="Paul Ritter (painter)">Paul Ritter</a>, was also a painter.</p>
