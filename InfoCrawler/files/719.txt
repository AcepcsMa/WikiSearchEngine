<p><b>Siegfried Jerusalem</b> (born 17 April 1940) is a German <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">operatic</a> <a href="/wiki/Tenor" title="Tenor">tenor</a>. Closely identified with the <a href="/wiki/Tenor#Heldentenor" title="Tenor">heldentenor</a> roles of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Wagner" title="Richard Wagner">Wagner</a>, he has performed Siegfried, Siegmund, Lohengrin, Parsifal, and Tristan to wide acclaim. Since the 1990s, he has focused on <a href="/wiki/Lied" title="Lied">lieder</a>, particularly those by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Strauss" title="Richard Strauss">Strauss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gustav_Mahler" title="Gustav Mahler">Mahler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Schumann" title="Robert Schumann">Schumann</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginnings">Beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siegfried_Jerusalem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Siegfried Jerusalem was born at <a href="/wiki/Oberhausen" title="Oberhausen">Oberhausen</a>, and studied piano, violin and bassoon at the <a href="/wiki/Folkwang_University_of_the_Arts" title="Folkwang University of the Arts">Folkwangschule</a> in <a href="/wiki/Essen" title="Essen">Essen</a>. He began his career as bassoonist, being engaged in <a href="/wiki/Hof,_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Hof, Germany">Hof</a> in 1961 and for the next ten years in <a href="/wiki/Reutlingen" title="Reutlingen">Reutlingen</a>: from 1971–77, he was attached to the <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart_Radio_Symphony_Orchestra" title="Stuttgart Radio Symphony Orchestra">Radio Symphony Orchestra of the South-German Radio in Stuttgart</a>. In 1971, he also began taking voice lessons with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hertha_Kalcher&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hertha Kalcher (page does not exist)">Hertha Kalcher</a> of Stuttgart.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1975, when due to perform in the orchestra of a television production of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Gypsy_Baron" title="The Gypsy Baron">Der Zigeunerbaron</a></i>, he stood in for the tenor <a href="/wiki/Franco_Bonisolli" title="Franco Bonisolli">Franco Bonisolli</a> at the last minute.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operatic_singer">Operatic singer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siegfried_Jerusalem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operatic singer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thenceforth Jerusalem made singing his career. His first major role was <a href="/wiki/Lohengrin_(opera)" title="Lohengrin (opera)">Lohengrin</a> in 1976, a role in which he soon appeared at Darmstadt, Aachen, Hamburg, and at the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Z%C3%BCrcher_Festspiele&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zürcher Festspiele (page does not exist)">Zurich Festival</a>, and later in Munich and the New York Met. He followed this with Max in <i><a href="/wiki/Der_Freisch%C3%BCtz" title="Der Freischütz">Der Freischütz</a></i> at Stuttgart. His Bayreuth career opened in 1977, where by 1981 he had performed Froh (<i><a href="/wiki/Das_Rheingold" title="Das Rheingold">Das Rheingold</a></i>), the Young Sailor (<i><a href="/wiki/Tristan_und_Isolde" title="Tristan und Isolde">Tristan und Isolde</a></i>) and then Lohengrin, <i><a href="/wiki/Parsifal" title="Parsifal">Parsifal</a></i> and Walter von Stolzing (<i><a href="/wiki/Die_Meistersinger_von_N%C3%BCrnberg" title="Die Meistersinger von Nürnberg">Die Meistersinger von Nürnberg</a></i>).</p>
<p>His Parsifal was also heard in Vienna at that time, and his Siegmund (<i><a href="/wiki/Die_Walk%C3%BCre" title="Die Walküre">Die Walküre</a></i>) (with <a href="/wiki/Jessye_Norman" title="Jessye Norman">Jessye Norman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Theo_Adam" title="Theo Adam">Theo Adam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yvonne_Minton" title="Yvonne Minton">Yvonne Minton</a>) in Dresden (1981). In addition to stage appearances he gave various concert performances which, like the Dresden <i>Walküre</i> or the Rome <i>Lohengrin</i> were broadcast internationally. From 1978, he became a regular member of the German Opera in Berlin, and had permanent contract with the companies in Stuttgart, Zürich, Munich, and Vienna as well as making international guest appearances.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>His <a href="/wiki/Bayreuth_Festival" title="Bayreuth Festival">Bayreuth</a> career continued with Siegfried in 1988 (<i><a href="/wiki/Siegfried_(opera)" title="Siegfried (opera)">Siegfried</a></i>) and 1989 (<i><a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6tterd%C3%A4mmerung" title="Götterdämmerung">Götterdämmerung</a></i>). In 1990, he appeared at the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Opera" title="Metropolitan Opera">Metropolitan Opera</a> with <a href="/wiki/James_Levine" title="James Levine">James Levine</a>, singing both Siegfrieds, as well as the difficult role of Loge. The production was televised, and is available on DVD. In 1992, his Bayreuth-Siegfried was made as a DVD again. On this performance, Jerusalem was singing with <a href="/wiki/Graham_Clark_(tenor)" title="Graham Clark (tenor)">Graham Clark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anne_Evans" title="Anne Evans">Anne Evans</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Tomlinson_(bass)" title="John Tomlinson (bass)">John Tomlinson</a>, and 1993 he debuted as Tristan at the Bayreuth Festival, and took the role to most of the major houses in Europe.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siegfried_Jerusalem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 9 October 1997, he was presented with the German <a href="/wiki/Federal_Cross_of_Merit" class="mw-redirect" title="Federal Cross of Merit">Federal Cross of Merit</a>. His last performance at Bayreuth was in 1999 as Tristan. He currently teaches at the College of Music in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recordings">Recordings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siegfried_Jerusalem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Recordings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jerusalem performs in a series of audio and video recordings of Wagner operas conducted by <a href="/wiki/James_Levine" title="James Levine">James Levine</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Opera" title="Metropolitan Opera">Met</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Barenboim" title="Daniel Barenboim">Daniel Barenboim</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Boulez" title="Pierre Boulez">Pierre Boulez</a> at Bayreuth, and others made in the 1980s and 1990s.</p>
<p>Jerusalem has also made an attempt at some crossover music in two albums <i>The Glory of Love</i> (1996) and <i>Avalon</i> (2002).</p>
