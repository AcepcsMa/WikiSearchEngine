<p><b>Nuremberg Airport</b> (<a href="/wiki/International_Air_Transport_Association_airport_code" class="mw-redirect" title="International Air Transport Association airport code">IATA</a>: <b>NUE</b>,&#160;<a href="/wiki/International_Civil_Aviation_Organization_airport_code" class="mw-redirect" title="International Civil Aviation Organization airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>EDDN</b>), German: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i><a href="/wiki/Albrecht_D%C3%BCrer" title="Albrecht DÃ¼rer">Albrecht DÃ¼rer</a> Flughafen NÃ¼rnberg</i></span>, is the <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconian</a> <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_area" title="Metropolitan area">metropolitan area</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> and the second-busiest airport in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a> after <a href="/wiki/Munich_Airport" title="Munich Airport">Munich Airport</a>. With about 3.3 million passengers handled in 2013, it is <a href="/wiki/List_of_airports_in_Germany" title="List of airports in Germany">Germany's 10th biggest airport</a>. It is located approximately 5&#160;km north of Nuremberg's city centre and offers flights within Germany as well as to European metropolitan and leisure destinations, especially along the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-summer13_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-summer13-4">[4]</a></sup> The airport handled 3.5 million passengers in 2016.</p>

<p>Nuremberg Airport was the first airport constructed in Germany after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. It was inaugurated on 6 April 1955.</p>
<p>In 1960, the number of passengers at Nuremberg Airport reached 100,000 for the first time. In 1961 the runway was extended from 1,900 to 2,300 metres (7,500&#160;ft), and in 1968 the runway was extended to its present length of 2,700 metres (8,900&#160;ft), allowing jumbo jets to use it. On 12 July 1970, a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747" title="Boeing 747">Boeing 747</a> landed at the airport for the first time and attracted 20,000 visitors.</p>
<p>The apron was enlarged in 1977 and in 1981 a new passenger terminal with an observation deck and a restaurant replaced the previous building. In December 1986, the one million passenger mark was passed for the first time.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_from_the_1990s">Development from the 1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development from the 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1997/98, <a href="/wiki/Air_Berlin" title="Air Berlin">Air Berlin</a> established a winter hub at the airport, making it the airline's second most important tourist interchange airport, after <a href="/wiki/Palma_de_Mallorca" class="mw-redirect" title="Palma de Mallorca">Palma de Mallorca</a>.</p>
<p>The new control tower commenced operations in 1999 and the metro station was opened. In 2002, departure hall 2 was extended and a year later the cargo centre CCN2 with 6,500&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (70,000&#160;sq&#160;ft) of storage space and 4,600&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (50,000&#160;sq&#160;ft) of office space was inaugurated after one and a half years' construction.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2005 Nuremberg Airport celebrated its 50th anniversary with 45,000 visitors. The new transfer control terminal with a floor space of 8,500&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (91,000&#160;sq&#160;ft) and a new main gate (<i>Tor 1</i>) were completed a year later in 2006. In addition, a fully automatic luggage sorting system was put into operation.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Nuremberg Airport has been voted "Best German Airport" by readers of the Business Traveller magazine consecutively since 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2013 Air Berlin permanently shut down its winter seasonal hub in Nuremberg which had been maintained for several years.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2014 the airport was named after <a href="/wiki/Albrecht_D%C3%BCrer" title="Albrecht DÃ¼rer">Albrecht DÃ¼rer</a>, who was born in Nuremberg.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2016, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> announced to open a base at Nuremberg Airport consisting of two aircraft while four additional routes will be inaugurated.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In the same month, <a href="/wiki/Air_Berlin" title="Air Berlin">Air Berlin</a> announced to close its maintenance facilities at the airport due to cost cutting and restructuring measures.<sup id="cite_ref-abtechnikshutdown_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abtechnikshutdown-10">[10]</a></sup> Shortly after, <a href="/wiki/Germania_(airline)" title="Germania (airline)">Germania</a> announced to open a new base at Nuremberg Airport consisting of one aircraft which will serve several new leisure routes.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Runway">Runway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Runway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Runway" title="Runway">runway</a> 10/28 is 2,700 by 45&#160;m (8,858 by 148&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-AIP_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AIP-3">[3]</a></sup> Takeoff and landing of all current aircraft, including widebody aircraft (e.g. Boeing 747) or cargo planes (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Antonov_An-124_Ruslan" title="Antonov An-124 Ruslan">Antonov An-124 Ruslan</a>) are possible. However, Nuremberg Airport is not licensed for the <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A380" title="Airbus A380">Airbus A380</a>.</p>
<p>Starting in July 2009, the runway was refurbished gradually in several phases. The surfaces of the runway and taxiways were renovated using the latest technology. A new flare-path, drainage channels and a new electric ring surrounding the entire runway were added. In 2010, the runway was shortened to 2,300&#160;m (7,500&#160;ft) temporarily to allow construction to continue. In 2011, work on the centerpiece of the runway began. The work was completed in 2015.</p>
<p>The apron is 246,845&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (2,657,020&#160;sq&#160;ft) in space and provides parking positions for 37 planes.</p>

<p>The passenger terminal consists of two departure halls and one arrival hall which are all linked landside and airside. The check-in area features 40 desks.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The extension of departure hall 2 was inaugurated on 30 April 1992 and was originally dimensioned for 2.8 million passengers per year. Now there is room for 5 million passengers per year. Daylight dominates the transparent construction made of steel and glass drafted by Nuremberg architects Grabow and Hoffmann. The construction phase took three years and cost about 100 million <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Mark" title="Deutsche Mark">Deutsche Mark</a>. The extension of the apron was included in the building costs as well as three modern air bridges. Today, there are five finger docks available.</p>
<p>On 25 January 2007 the newest addition, the Transfer-Control-Terminal (TCT) was opened. It not only serves as a capacity extension but it also allows for new legislation concerning security measures: since EU Regulation 2320/2002 airports have to make sure that non-EU passengers are controlled before continuing their trip to countries of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> and don't get mixed up with passengers who have already been checked.</p>

<p>In 1987, <i>Cargo Center Nuremberg</i> (CCN) was put into operation.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> When the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> ended and after the collapse of the Soviet Union, Nuremberg won back its central location in Europe. As a consequence Nuremberg Airport and <a href="/wiki/Cargo_airline" title="Cargo airline">air freight</a> quickly gained in importance in the 1990s.</p>
<p>Nuremberg is also the economic and service metropolis of Northern Bavaria with approximately 150,000 companies and enterprises taking advantage of the locality of Nuremberg as a traffic junction of highways and railroads. The region's export share of 42% is remarkably high and above German average. In addition, several headquarters of internationally operating companies are located in the region, for example <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Bosch_GmbH" title="Robert Bosch GmbH">Bosch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puma_SE" class="mw-redirect" title="Puma SE">Puma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Faber-Castell" title="Faber-Castell">Faber-Castell</a>.</p>
<p>Due to the positive trend, <i>Cargo Center II</i> (CCN II) was built in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Today, almost 13,317&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (143,340&#160;sq&#160;ft) storage space and 7,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (75,000&#160;sq&#160;ft) of office space is available at Nuremberg Airport. 107,123 tons of cargo were handled in 2010.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Control_tower">Control tower</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Control tower">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Flugsicherung" title="Deutsche Flugsicherung">Deutsche Flugsicherung</a> (DFS), which is in charge of air traffic control in Germany, moved into the 48-metre-high (157&#160;ft) tower in November 1998. The control tower at Nuremberg Airport was designed by architect <a href="/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Behnisch" class="mw-redirect" title="GÃ¼nther Behnisch">GÃ¼nther Behnisch</a> and has become the architectural landmark of the airport with its dynamic silhouette. It was built because the original control tower was only 18 meters high. The project cost approximately 30 million Deutsche Mark.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parking">Parking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Parking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are about 8,000 car parking spaces at Nuremberg Airport. Apart from three car parks, there are various parking lots in close vicinity to the terminals. The newest facility is car park P3 with seven levels and 2,200 parking spaces. There are different tariffs to choose from, for example "BusinessParken" (business parking) or "UrlauberParken" (holiday parking).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Nuremberg Airport also offers <a href="/wiki/Valet_parking" title="Valet parking">valet parking</a> with additional services, like refueling, car wash, maintenance or safekeeping of valuables. All parking facilities are no more 5 minutes' walking distance from the terminals. There are short-term parking spots directly on the airport forecourt in front of the terminals.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air_rescue">Air rescue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Air rescue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nuremberg Airport is also a center for <i>Deutsche Rettungsflugwacht e.V</i> (DRF) and <i>HDM Flugservice</i> <a href="/wiki/Air_ambulance" class="mw-redirect" title="Air ambulance">air rescue services</a> which operate a rescue helicopter and an intensive care helicopter, respectively. Furthermore, several <a href="/wiki/ADAC" title="ADAC">ADAC</a> air ambulances and <i>Flight Ambulance International</i> (FAI)<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> are based in Nuremberg.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ground_transportation">Ground transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Ground transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Metro">Metro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Metro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:U-Bahnhof_Flughafen_U_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/U-Bahnhof_Flughafen_U_2.jpg/220px-U-Bahnhof_Flughafen_U_2.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/U-Bahnhof_Flughafen_U_2.jpg/330px-U-Bahnhof_Flughafen_U_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/U-Bahnhof_Flughafen_U_2.jpg/440px-U-Bahnhof_Flughafen_U_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1296" data-file-height="972" /></a>

The airport's <a href="/wiki/Flughafen_(Nuremberg_U-Bahn)" title="Flughafen (Nuremberg U-Bahn)">U-Bahn station</a></div>

<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_U-Bahn" title="Nuremberg U-Bahn">U-Bahn</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">Metro</a>) line <a href="/wiki/U2_(Nuremberg_U-Bahn)" title="U2 (Nuremberg U-Bahn)">U2</a> serves the airport at the <a href="/wiki/Flughafen_(Nuremberg_U-Bahn)" title="Flughafen (Nuremberg U-Bahn)">Flughafen</a> station. Trains connect the airport with the centre of the city every 10 to 15 minutes. The ride to the <i>Hauptbahnhof</i> (Central Railway Station) and the nearby <i>Altstadt</i> (historic old town) only takes 12 minutes.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car">Car</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Car">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nuremberg Airport is located 5&#160;km (3.1&#160;mi) north<sup id="cite_ref-AIP_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AIP-3">[3]</a></sup> of the city centre. It is accessible via nearby <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_3" title="Bundesautobahn 3">Motorway A 3</a>, which connects to <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_9" title="Bundesautobahn 9">Motorway A 9</a> as well as Motorways <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_73" title="Bundesautobahn 73">A 73</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_6" title="Bundesautobahn 6">A 6</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bus number 32 connects the airport with bus and tram stop <i>Thon</i>. Since December 2015 new line 33 was installed, allowing passengers from Nurembergs west-neighbouring city <a href="/wiki/F%C3%BCrth" title="FÃ¼rth">FÃ¼rth</a> getting to the airport quicker without taking a detour via <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_Central_Station" title="Nuremberg Central Station">Nuremberg Central Station</a>. Since the extension of Tram Line 4 from Thon to am Wegfeld, Bus line 30 which formerly terminated in Thon has been rerouted to the airport, thus offering a direct connection to downtown <a href="/wiki/Erlangen" title="Erlangen">Erlangen</a> from the airport for the first time.</p>
<p>Because of the airport's close-in location and its direct connections to local streets, it is also possible to walk or ride a bicycle from nearby neighborhoods right up to the terminal.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environment">Environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to developing strategies to reduce noise pollution the department also implements regular measurements of <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">air pollutants</a> and soil analyses. In 2003, a biomonitoring campaign with honey bees was launched at the airport.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>The water collected on the 70 ha of sealed or covered areas is being filtered and analyzed before it gets fed into receiving water courses, to prevent pollution due to oils or fuels. If the analyzed <a href="/wiki/Total_organic_carbon" title="Total organic carbon">TOC</a> value is above the threshold level, the water is discharged into the sewerage. Over the years, surface and aircraft <a href="/wiki/Deicing" class="mw-redirect" title="Deicing">de-icing fluids</a> have been replaced by substances with higher biodegradability.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_plans">Expansion plans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Expansion plans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Airport_Business_Center">Airport Business Center</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Airport Business Center">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, it was decided that a new hotel with conference rooms and offices will be built at the airport roundabout. ConTech GmbH and the architect's office Christ, both from Nuremberg, will realize the project with investor ZBI.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In 2011 the plans were put on hold until the motorway connection is completed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Motorway_access">Motorway access</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Motorway access">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Direct access to <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_3" title="Bundesautobahn 3">motorway A3</a> has been planned for several years. A direct route to the airport with a tunnel under the runway to reduce traffic through city district Ziegelstein is favored and spatial planning has already been completed. However, further planning has been delayed as <a href="/wiki/List_of_environmental_organizations" title="List of environmental organizations">environmental organization</a> <i>Bund Naturschutz</i> and alliance <i>Nein zur Flughafen-Nordanbindung!</i> are vehemently against the plans.</p>
