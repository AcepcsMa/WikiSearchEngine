			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">European Route of Industrial Heritage</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">

				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>European Route of Industrial Heritage</b> (<b>ERIH</b>) is a network (<a href="/wiki/Theme_routes" class="mw-redirect" title="Theme routes">theme route</a>) of the most important <a href="/wiki/Industrial_heritage" title="Industrial heritage">industrial heritage</a> sites in Europe. This is a tourism industry information initiative to present a network of industrial heritage sites across Europe. The aim of the project is to create interest for the common European Heritage of the Industrialisation and its remains. ERIH also wants to promote regions, towns and sites showing the industrial history and market them as visitor attractions in the leisure and tourism industry.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Anchor_points">Anchor points</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Route_of_Industrial_Heritage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Anchor points">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The – virtual – main route is built by the so-called Anchor Points. These are Industrial Heritage sites which are the historically most important and most attractive for visitors. The route leads through 13 countries thus far (in 2014): United Kingdom, the Netherlands, Belgium, Luxembourg, Germany, France, Spain, Italy, Czech Republic, Poland, Sweden, Norway and Denmark.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_Routes">Regional Routes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Route_of_Industrial_Heritage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Regional Routes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Regional Routes (like the <a href="/wiki/The_Industrial_Heritage_Trail" class="mw-redirect" title="The Industrial Heritage Trail">Route der Industriekultur</a> in the Ruhr) cover regions as where industrial history has left its mark. Currently (2017) there are seventeen:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>Austria</dt>
<dd>Styrian Iron Trail</dd>
<dt>Germany</dt>
<dd>Northwest</dd>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Ruhr" title="Ruhr">Ruhrgebiet</a></dd>
<dd>Industrial Valleys</dd>
<dd>Euregio Maas-Rhine</dd>
<dd>Saxony-Anhalt</dd>
<dd>Lusatia</dd>
<dd>Rhine-Main</dd>
<dd>Saar Lor Lux</dd>
<dt>Netherlands</dt>
<dd>HollandRoute</dd>
<dd>Euregio Maas-Rhine</dd>
<dt>Poland</dt>
<dd>Silesia</dd>
<dt>Spain</dt>
<dd>Catalonia</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>United Kingdom</dt>
<dd>Southwest Yorkshire<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></dd>
<dd>South Wales<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></dd>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="European_Theme_Routes">European Theme Routes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Route_of_Industrial_Heritage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: European Theme Routes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thirteen European Theme Routes show the diversity of industrial landscapes all over Europe and the common roots of industrial history:<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Appication of Power</li>
<li>Housing and Architecture</li>
<li>Industry and War</li>
<li>Iron and Steel</li>
<li>Industrial Landscapes</li>
<li>Mining</li>
<li>Paper</li>
<li>Production &amp; Manufacturing</li>
<li>Salt</li>
<li>Service and Leisure Industry</li>
<li>Textiles</li>
<li>Transport &amp; Communication</li>
<li>Water</li>
</ul>
