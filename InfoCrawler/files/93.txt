<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:green;"><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Geography_Portal"><big>The Geography Portal</big></span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #FABD23; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">

<center><a href="/wiki/Human_geography" title="Human geography">Human geography</a></center>
<p><a href="/wiki/File:Snow-cholera-map-1.jpg" class="image" title="Original map by John Snow showing the clusters of cholera cases in the London epidemic of 1854"><img alt="Original map by John Snow showing the clusters of cholera cases in the London epidemic of 1854" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Snow-cholera-map-1.jpg/200px-Snow-cholera-map-1.jpg" width="200" height="187" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Snow-cholera-map-1.jpg/300px-Snow-cholera-map-1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Snow-cholera-map-1.jpg/400px-Snow-cholera-map-1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3045" data-file-height="2840" /></a><br /></p>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">Geography</a></b> is the <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a> that studies the lands, the features, the inhabitants, and the phenomena of the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>. A literal translation would be "to describe or write about the Earth". The first person to use the word "geography" was <a href="/wiki/Eratosthenes" title="Eratosthenes">Eratosthenes</a> (276–194 BC). Four historical traditions in geographical research are the <a href="/wiki/Spatial_analysis" title="Spatial analysis">spatial analysis</a> of the natural and the <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> phenomena (geography as the study of distribution), the <a href="/wiki/Area_studies" title="Area studies">area studies</a> (places and regions), the study of the human-land relationship, and research in the <a href="/wiki/Earth_science" title="Earth science">Earth sciences</a>. Modern geography is an all-encompassing discipline that foremost seeks to understand the Earth and all of its human and natural complexities—not merely where objects are, but how they have changed and come to be. Geography has been called "the world discipline" and "the bridge between the human and the <a href="/wiki/Physical_science" class="mw-redirect" title="Physical science">physical science</a>". Geography is divided into two main branches: <a href="/wiki/Human_geography" title="Human geography">human geography</a> and <a href="/wiki/Physical_geography" title="Physical geography">physical geography</a>.</p>

<div style="border: 1px solid #FABD23; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:left;margin-right:1em"><a href="/wiki/File:Pulteney_Bridge,_daytime,_from_weir.jpg" class="image" title="Bath, Somerset"><img alt="Bath, Somerset" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Pulteney_Bridge%2C_daytime%2C_from_weir.jpg/120px-Pulteney_Bridge%2C_daytime%2C_from_weir.jpg" width="120" height="88" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Pulteney_Bridge%2C_daytime%2C_from_weir.jpg/180px-Pulteney_Bridge%2C_daytime%2C_from_weir.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Pulteney_Bridge%2C_daytime%2C_from_weir.jpg/240px-Pulteney_Bridge%2C_daytime%2C_from_weir.jpg 2x" data-file-width="815" data-file-height="598" /></a></div>
<b><a href="/wiki/Bath,_Somerset" title="Bath, Somerset">Bath</a></b> is a <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> in south-west <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, most famous for its baths fed by three <a href="/wiki/Hot_spring" title="Hot spring">hot springs</a>. The city was first recorded as a <a href="/wiki/Roman_Britain" title="Roman Britain">Roman</a> <a href="/wiki/Spa" title="Spa">spa</a>, though tradition suggests an earlier foundation. The waters from its spring were considered to be a cure for many afflictions. From <a href="/wiki/Elizabethan" class="mw-redirect" title="Elizabethan">Elizabethan</a> to <a href="/wiki/Georgian_era" title="Georgian era">Georgian</a> times it was a resort city for the wealthy. As a result of its popularity during the latter period, the city contains many fine examples of <a href="/wiki/Georgian_architecture" title="Georgian architecture">Georgian architecture</a>, particularly <a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Crescent" class="mw-redirect" title="The Royal Crescent">The Royal Crescent</a>. The city has a population of over 90,000 and is a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a>.

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:green;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Featured_biography">Featured biography</span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #FABD23; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.2em"><a href="/wiki/File:Lord_Stanley_by_Hugh_Cecil.jpg" class="image" title="Albert Stanley, 1st Baron Ashfield"><img alt="Albert Stanley, 1st Baron Ashfield" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/54/Lord_Stanley_by_Hugh_Cecil.jpg/120px-Lord_Stanley_by_Hugh_Cecil.jpg" width="120" height="162" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/54/Lord_Stanley_by_Hugh_Cecil.jpg/180px-Lord_Stanley_by_Hugh_Cecil.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/54/Lord_Stanley_by_Hugh_Cecil.jpg/240px-Lord_Stanley_by_Hugh_Cecil.jpg 2x" data-file-width="332" data-file-height="448" /></a></div>
<b><a href="/wiki/Albert_Stanley,_1st_Baron_Ashfield" title="Albert Stanley, 1st Baron Ashfield">Albert Stanley, 1st Baron Ashfield</a></b> was managing director, then chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Underground_Electric_Railways_Company_of_London" title="Underground Electric Railways Company of London">Underground Electric Railways Company of London</a> (UERL) from 1910 to 1933 and chairman of the <a href="/wiki/London_Passenger_Transport_Board" title="London Passenger Transport Board">London Passenger Transport Board</a> (LPTB) from 1933 to 1947. At a young age, he held senior positions in the developing tramway systems of Detroit and New Jersey. In 1907, his management skills led to his recruitment by the UERL, which was struggling through a financial crisis. He quickly integrated the company's management and used advertising and public relations to improve profits. As managing director of the UERL from 1910, he led the take-over of competing underground railway companies and bus and tram operations to form an integrated transport operation known as the Combine. He was <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Ashton-under-Lyne_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Ashton-under-Lyne (UK Parliament constituency)">Ashton-under-Lyne</a> from <span class="nowrap">December 1916</span> to <span class="nowrap">January 1920</span> and was <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Trade_and_Industry" class="mw-redirect" title="Secretary of State for Trade and Industry">President of the Board of Trade</a> between <span class="nowrap">December 1916</span> and <span class="nowrap">May 1919</span>. He returned to the UERL and then chaired it and its successor the LPTB during the organisation's greatest period of expansion between the two World Wars, making it a world-respected organisation considered an exemplar of the best form of public administration.

<p>A view from space of an <a href="/wiki/Explosive_eruption" title="Explosive eruption">explosive eruption</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sarychev_Peak" title="Sarychev Peak">Sarychev Peak</a> on <span class="nowrap">June 12</span>, 2009. The <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a> is the central peak of <b><a href="/wiki/Matua_(island)" title="Matua (island)">Matua</a></b>, an uninhabited island in the <a href="/wiki/Kuril_Islands" title="Kuril Islands">Kuril archipelago</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Okhotsk" title="Sea of Okhotsk">Sea of Okhotsk</a> east of Russia and north of Japan. During World War&#160;II, the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army" title="Imperial Japanese Army">Imperial Japanese Army</a> had an airfield on the island, which was taken over by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Border_Troops" title="Soviet Border Troops">Soviet Border Troops</a> after the war and abandoned in 1999.</p>
