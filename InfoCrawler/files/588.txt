<p>The <i><b>Katharinenkirche</b></i> (St. Catherine's Church) in <b><a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a></b>, <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, was an important <a href="/wiki/Mediaeval" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediaeval">mediaeval</a> church, destroyed during the Second World War and preserved as a ruin.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katharinenkirche,_Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mendel_II_047_v.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Mendel_II_047_v.jpg/220px-Mendel_II_047_v.jpg" width="220" height="319" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Mendel_II_047_v.jpg/330px-Mendel_II_047_v.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Mendel_II_047_v.jpg/440px-Mendel_II_047_v.jpg 2x" data-file-width="686" data-file-height="994" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mendel_II_047_v.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Illustration from 1587</div>

<p>St. Catherine's was the church of a former <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Order" title="Dominican Order">Dominican</a> convent, in the <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Bamberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Diocese of Bamberg">Diocese of Bamberg</a>, famous for its <a href="/wiki/Medieval" class="mw-redirect" title="Medieval">Medieval</a> Library. It was founded in 1295 by Konrad von Neumarkt and his wife Adelheid, <a href="/wiki/Patrician_(post-Roman_Europe)" title="Patrician (post-Roman Europe)">patricians</a> of the Pfinzig family. In the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> it had an important medieval library. After the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Reformation" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant Reformation">Reformation</a>, it became a <a href="/wiki/Lutheran" class="mw-redirect" title="Lutheran">Lutheran</a> church. The convent was closed in 1596 after the last inhabitant died.</p>
<p>The church was associated with the <a href="/wiki/Meistersinger" title="Meistersinger">Meistersingers</a> who met there from 1620 to 1778, and is featured in the opening scene of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Wagner" title="Richard Wagner">Richard Wagner</a>'s opera <i><a href="/wiki/Die_Meistersinger_von_N%C3%BCrnberg" title="Die Meistersinger von NÃ¼rnberg">Die Meistersinger von NÃ¼rnberg</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELee2014_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELee2014-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
