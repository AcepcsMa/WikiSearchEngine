			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Henry Ferrers, 4th Baron Ferrers of Groby</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Henry Ferrers, 4th Baron Ferrers of Groby</b> (1356–1388) was a fourteenth century English <a href="/wiki/English_peerage" class="mw-redirect" title="English peerage">nobleman</a>. He was a professional soldier, taking part in a number of campaigns during the reign of <a href="/wiki/Richard_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Richard II">Richard II</a>, served on several <a href="/wiki/Royal_commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal commission">royal commissions</a>, was a <a href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_peace" title="Justice of the peace">justice of the peace</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Member_of_parliament" title="Member of parliament">member of parliament</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_and_early_life">Youth and early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henry_Ferrers,_4th_Baron_Ferrers_of_Groby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Youth and early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:K-075-Coat_of_Arms-FERRERS-William_de_Ferrers_(%22Guillemes_de_Ferieres%22).png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/K-075-Coat_of_Arms-FERRERS-William_de_Ferrers_%28%22Guillemes_de_Ferieres%22%29.png/220px-K-075-Coat_of_Arms-FERRERS-William_de_Ferrers_%28%22Guillemes_de_Ferieres%22%29.png" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/K-075-Coat_of_Arms-FERRERS-William_de_Ferrers_%28%22Guillemes_de_Ferieres%22%29.png/330px-K-075-Coat_of_Arms-FERRERS-William_de_Ferrers_%28%22Guillemes_de_Ferieres%22%29.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/K-075-Coat_of_Arms-FERRERS-William_de_Ferrers_%28%22Guillemes_de_Ferieres%22%29.png/440px-K-075-Coat_of_Arms-FERRERS-William_de_Ferrers_%28%22Guillemes_de_Ferieres%22%29.png 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="1200" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:K-075-Coat_of_Arms-FERRERS-William_de_Ferrers_(%22Guillemes_de_Ferieres%22).png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Ferrers of Groby family <a href="/wiki/Armorial" class="mw-redirect" title="Armorial">armorial</a>, <i>Gules, 7 mascules Or.</i></div>

<p>He was the only son of <a href="/wiki/William_Ferrers,_3rd_Baron_Ferrers_of_Groby" title="William Ferrers, 3rd Baron Ferrers of Groby">William, Lord Ferrers</a> by his father's first marriage to Margaret Uford, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Robert_d%27Ufford,_1st_Earl_of_Suffolk" title="Robert d'Ufford, 1st Earl of Suffolk">Robert d'Ufford, Earl of Suffolk</a> and Margaret Norwich.<sup id="cite_ref-oxforddnb.com_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oxforddnb.com-1">[1]</a></sup> He was born in <a href="/wiki/Tilty_Abbey" title="Tilty Abbey">Tilty Abbey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a> on 6 February 1356, and baptised in nearby <a href="/wiki/Stebbing" title="Stebbing">Stebbing</a><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1916_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1916-2">[2]</a></sup> Whist still a <a href="/wiki/Minor_(law)" title="Minor (law)">minor</a>, in the words of the family's most recent biographer, he "fell prey to the fraudulent schemes of his father's feoffees," who attempted to dispossess Henry of certain Essex and <a href="/wiki/Warwickshire" title="Warwickshire">Warwickshire</a> <a href="/wiki/Estate_(law)" title="Estate (law)">estates</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-oxforddnb.com_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oxforddnb.com-1">[1]</a></sup> Two of his father's <a href="/wiki/Feoffee" title="Feoffee">feoffees</a>, a clerk called Edmund de Stebbing and one Robert de Bradenham attempted to use a <a href="/wiki/Forged" class="mw-redirect" title="Forged">forged</a> <a href="/wiki/Legal_release" title="Legal release">release</a>, which would allow them to take the manors into their own hands.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1916_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1916-2">[2]</a></sup> At some point, but certainly before 27 June 1371 he married Joan, possibly the daughter of Sir Thomas de Hoo of <a href="/wiki/Luton_Hoo" title="Luton Hoo">Luton Hoo</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-3">[3]</a></sup> they had one son, his heir <a href="/wiki/William_Ferrers,_5th_Baron_Ferrers_of_Groby" title="William Ferrers, 5th Baron Ferrers of Groby">William</a>, who had been born on 25 April, 1372 in Hoo, <a href="/wiki/Bedfordshire" title="Bedfordshire">Bedfordshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-oxforddnb.com_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oxforddnb.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henry_Ferrers,_4th_Baron_Ferrers_of_Groby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1377, he paid <a href="/wiki/Homage_(feudal)" title="Homage (feudal)">homage</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fealty" title="Fealty">fealty</a> to <a href="/wiki/Edward_III_of_England" title="Edward III of England">King Edward III</a> for his <a href="/wiki/Property" title="Property">patrimony</a> and those lands held in <a href="/wiki/Dower" title="Dower">dower</a> by his father's second wife Margaret, his <a href="/wiki/Stepmother" title="Stepmother">stepmother</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1916_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1916-2">[2]</a></sup> During the following decade, Ferrers was regularly appointed to royal commission within <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire" title="Leicestershire">Leicestershire</a>, including those of <a href="/wiki/Commission_of_Array" class="mw-redirect" title="Commission of Array">Array</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oyer_and_Terminer" class="mw-redirect" title="Oyer and Terminer">Oyer and Terminer</a>, and as a <a href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_Peace" class="mw-redirect" title="Justice of the Peace">Justice of the Peace</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-oxforddnb.com_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oxforddnb.com-1">[1]</a></sup> He was also summoned to <a href="/wiki/English_parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="English parliament">parliament</a> as <i>Henrico de Ferrariis de Groby</i> from the August 1377 parliament to that of December 1387.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ferrers was essentially a professional soldier, taking part in five campaigns during the reign of Richard II alone.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">[4]</a></sup> He performed much royal service in the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Years%27_War" title="Hundred Years' War">Hundred Years' War</a>, being part of the <a href="/wiki/Thomas_of_Woodstock,_1st_Duke_of_Gloucester" title="Thomas of Woodstock, 1st Duke of Gloucester">earl of Buckingham</a>'s contingent in 1377 (in which he was a <a href="/wiki/Captain" title="Captain">captain</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">[4]</a></sup> and that of the <a href="/wiki/John_of_Gaunt" title="John of Gaunt">duke of Lancaster</a> in 1378. Ferrers fought again under Buckingham in the 1380-1 <a href="/wiki/Britanny" class="mw-redirect" title="Britanny">Britanny</a> <a href="/wiki/Chevauch%C3%A9e" title="Chevauchée">chevauchée</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-3">[3]</a></sup> He took part in <a href="/wiki/Richard_II_of_England" title="Richard II of England">Richard II</a>'s <a href="/w/index.php?title=Invasion_of_Scotland_(1385)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Invasion of Scotland (1385) (page does not exist)">invasion of Scotland</a> of August 1385<sup id="cite_ref-oxforddnb.com_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oxforddnb.com-1">[1]</a></sup> where he was with Richard in the main <a href="/wiki/Battle" title="Battle">battle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-3">[3]</a></sup> Two years later the king, with <a href="/wiki/Anne_of_Bohemia" title="Anne of Bohemia">Queen Anne</a>, stayed with Henry Ferrers at <a href="/wiki/Groby" title="Groby">Groby</a> whilst on a <a href="/wiki/Royal_progress" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal progress">Royal progress</a> around <a href="/wiki/The_Midlands" title="The Midlands">The Midlands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1382, he and two others were found by <a href="/wiki/Inquisition_post_mortem" title="Inquisition post mortem">Inquisition <i>post mortem</i></a> to be the heirs of William Ufford, Earl of Suffolk, by rights of their wives, Suffolk's sisters.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">[4]</a></sup> He died 3 February 1388 aged 31;<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> his wife Joan survived him,<sup id="cite_ref-oxforddnb.com_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oxforddnb.com-1">[1]</a></sup> dying in 1394.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
