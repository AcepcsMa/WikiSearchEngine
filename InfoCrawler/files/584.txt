			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Schöner Brunnen</h1>

<p><b>Schöner Brunnen</b> (en:beautiful fountain) is a 14th-century fountain located on <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>'s main market next to the town hall and is considered one of the main attractions of the city's Historical Mile. The fountain is approximately 19 meters high and has the shape of a Gothic <a href="/wiki/Spire" title="Spire">spire</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sch%C3%B6ner_Brunnen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The fountain was built by <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Beheim" title="Heinrich Beheim">Heinrich Beheim</a> from 1385 to 1396.<sup id="cite_ref-esp-nirn_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-esp-nirn-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sch%C3%B6ner_Brunnen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 40 colorful figures that adorn the fountain represent the world view of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>. They are <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a>, the seven <a href="/wiki/Liberal_arts" class="mw-redirect" title="Liberal arts">liberal arts</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Four_Evangelists" title="Four Evangelists">four Evangelists</a>, the four <a href="/wiki/Church_Fathers" title="Church Fathers">Church Fathers</a>, the seven <a href="/wiki/Prince-elector" title="Prince-elector">Prince-electors</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Nine_Worthies" title="Nine Worthies">Nine Worthies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moses" title="Moses">Moses</a> and seven <a href="/wiki/Prophet" title="Prophet">Prophets</a> (<a href="/wiki/Hosea" title="Hosea">Hosea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_(biblical_figure)" title="Daniel (biblical figure)">Daniel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremiah" title="Jeremiah">Jeremiah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ezekiel" title="Ezekiel">Ezekiel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amos_(prophet)" title="Amos (prophet)">Amos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isaiah" title="Isaiah">Isaiah</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joel_(prophet)" title="Joel (prophet)">Joel</a>).</p>
<p>The two brass rings embedded in the fence surrounding the fountain on opposite sides are said to bring good luck to those who spin them.</p>
