<p>The <i><b>Journal of the ACM</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer-reviewed</a> <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">scientific journal</a> covering <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> in general, especially theoretical aspects. It is an official journal of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Its current <a href="/wiki/Editor-in-chief" title="Editor-in-chief">editor-in-chief</a> is <a href="/wiki/%C3%89va_Tardos" title="Éva Tardos">Éva Tardos</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cornell_University" title="Cornell University">Cornell University</a>).</p>
<p>The journal was established in 1954<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and "computer scientists universally hold the <i>Journal of the ACM (JACM)</i> in high esteem".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
