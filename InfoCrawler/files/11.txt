			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Doctoral advisor</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>A <b>doctoral advisor</b> (also <b>dissertation director</b> or <b>dissertation advisor</b>) is a member of a <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> <a href="/wiki/Faculty_(academic_staff)" title="Faculty (academic staff)">faculty</a> whose role is to guide <a href="/wiki/Graduate_student" class="mw-redirect" title="Graduate student">graduate students</a> who are candidates for a <a href="/wiki/Doctorate" title="Doctorate">doctorate</a>, helping them select coursework, as well as shaping, refining and directing the students' choice of sub-<a href="/wiki/Academic_discipline" class="mw-redirect" title="Academic discipline">discipline</a> in which they will be examined or on which they will write a <a href="/wiki/Dissertation" class="mw-redirect" title="Dissertation">dissertation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Students generally choose advisors based on their areas of interest within their discipline, their desire to work closely with particular graduate faculty, and the willingness and availability of those faculty to work with them.</p>
<p>In some countries, the student's advisor serves as the chair of the doctoral examination or dissertation committees. In some cases, though, the person who serves those roles may be different from the faculty member who has most closely advised the student. For instance, in the <a href="/wiki/Education_in_the_Netherlands#Tertiary_education" title="Education in the Netherlands">Dutch academic system</a>, only full professors (<i>hoogleraren</i>) may chair doctoral examinations, so students who have been advised by lower-ranked faculty members will have a full professor as their official advisor (or <i>promotor</i>) and their actual advisor as <i>co-promotor</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In other countries, such as Spain, the doctoral advisor has the role of a mentor, but is not allowed to form part of the examination committee. This is a body of 5 experts independently selected by the rectorate among 10 candidates proposed by the university's department.</p>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Academic_genealogy" title="Academic genealogy">academic genealogy</a> may be traced based on student's doctoral advisors, going up and down the lines of academic "descent" in a manner analogous to a traditional <a href="/wiki/Genealogy" title="Genealogy">genealogy</a>.</p>
