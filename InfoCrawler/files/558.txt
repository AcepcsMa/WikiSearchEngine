<p><b>Marcus Bosch</b> (born 28 October 1969) is a German conductor. He was <a href="/wiki/Generalmusikdirektor" class="mw-redirect" title="Generalmusikdirektor">Generalmusikdirektor</a> (GMD) in Aachen, and has held the position at the <a href="/wiki/Staatstheater_N%C3%BCrnberg" title="Staatstheater Nürnberg">Staatstheater Nürnberg</a> from 2011. He is the artistic director of an opera festival in his home town. Conducting internationally, he has been appointed professor at the <a href="/wiki/Musikhochschule_M%C3%BCnchen" class="mw-redirect" title="Musikhochschule München">Musikhochschule München</a> in 2016. He was acclaimed for his recording of <a href="/wiki/Anton_Bruckner" title="Anton Bruckner">Bruckner</a>'s complete symphonies, among others.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Bosch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Heidenheim_an_der_Brenz" title="Heidenheim an der Brenz">Heidenheim an der Brenz</a>, Bosch studied in <a href="/wiki/Heidelberg" title="Heidelberg">Heidelberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mannheim" title="Mannheim">Mannheim</a>. He took the Kapellmeister career, working in <a href="/wiki/Osnabr%C3%BCck" title="Osnabrück">Osnabrück</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Osnabr.C3.BCck_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Osnabr.C3.BCck-1">[1]</a></sup> at theatres in <a href="/wiki/Wiesbaden" title="Wiesbaden">Wiesbaden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saarbr%C3%BCcken" title="Saarbrücken">Saarbrücken</a>, and with the Staatsorchester <a href="/wiki/Halle_(Saale)" title="Halle (Saale)">Halle</a>, to learn a large repertory in opera and concert.<sup id="cite_ref-Heidenheim_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heidenheim-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He conducted as a guest orchestras such as <a href="/wiki/Orchestre_National_de_Belgique" class="mw-redirect" title="Orchestre National de Belgique">Orchestre National de Belgique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orchestre_Philharmonique_du_Luxembourg" class="mw-redirect" title="Orchestre Philharmonique du Luxembourg">Orchestre Philharmonique du Luxembourg</a>, Orchestra Nazionale della RAI Torino, <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorfer_Symphoniker" class="mw-redirect" title="Düsseldorfer Symphoniker">Düsseldorfer Symphoniker</a>, <a href="/wiki/MDR_Sinfonieorchester" class="mw-redirect" title="MDR Sinfonieorchester">MDR Sinfonieorchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchner_Philharmoniker" class="mw-redirect" title="Münchner Philharmoniker">Münchner Philharmoniker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Staatskapelle_Dresden" title="Staatskapelle Dresden">Staatskapelle Dresden</a>. He conducted opera at the <i>GöteborgsOperan</i> and the <a href="/wiki/Komische_Oper_Berlin" title="Komische Oper Berlin">Komische Oper Berlin</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-N.C3.BCrnberg_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-N.C3.BCrnberg-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vocapella_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vocapella-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bosch is a regular guest at the <a href="/wiki/Hamburgische_Staatsoper" class="mw-redirect" title="Hamburgische Staatsoper">Hamburgische Staatsoper</a>, where he first conducted Beethoven's <i><a href="/wiki/Fidelio" title="Fidelio">Fidelio</a></i> in 2005, later Mozart's <i><a href="/wiki/Die_Entf%C3%BChrung_aus_dem_Serail" title="Die Entführung aus dem Serail">Die Entführung aus dem Serail</a></i>, Weber's <i><a href="/wiki/Der_Freisch%C3%BCtz" title="Der Freischütz">Der Freischütz</a></i>, Verdi's <i><a href="/wiki/Falstaff_(opera)" title="Falstaff (opera)">Falstaff</a></i> and York Höller's <i><a href="/wiki/Der_Meister_und_Margarita" title="Der Meister und Margarita">Der Meister und Margarita</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-N.C3.BCrnberg_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-N.C3.BCrnberg-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was <a href="/wiki/Generalmusikdirektor" class="mw-redirect" title="Generalmusikdirektor">Generalmusikdirektor</a> in <a href="/wiki/Aachen" title="Aachen">Aachen</a> from 2002,<sup id="cite_ref-Heidenheim_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heidenheim-2">[2]</a></sup> where he recorded works by Mahler, Mozart, Verdi, Brahms and Wagner with the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sinfonieorchester_Aachen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sinfonieorchester Aachen (page does not exist)">Sinfonieorchester Aachen</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinfonieorchester_Aachen" class="extiw" title="de:Sinfonieorchester Aachen">de</a>)</span>. He recorded Bruckner's complete symphonies,<sup id="cite_ref-N.C3.BCrnberg_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-N.C3.BCrnberg-3">[3]</a></sup> and received acclaim especially for his readings of the <a href="/wiki/Symphony_No._3_(Bruckner)" title="Symphony No. 3 (Bruckner)">Third</a> (in the first version of 1873), the <a href="/wiki/Symphony_No._7_(Bruckner)" title="Symphony No. 7 (Bruckner)">Seventh</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Symphony_No._8_(Bruckner)" title="Symphony No. 8 (Bruckner)">Eighth</a>. From 2010, he has directed the opera festival <a href="/w/index.php?title=Opernfestspiele_Heidenheim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Opernfestspiele Heidenheim (page does not exist)">Opernfestspiele Heidenheim</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opernfestspiele_Heidenheim" class="extiw" title="de:Opernfestspiele Heidenheim">de</a>)</span> in his hometown.<sup id="cite_ref-Heidenheim_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heidenheim-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the 2011/12 season, Bosch succeeded <a href="/wiki/Christof_Prick" class="mw-redirect" title="Christof Prick">Christof Prick</a> as GMD at the <a href="/wiki/Staatstheater_N%C3%BCrnberg" title="Staatstheater Nürnberg">Staatstheater Nürnberg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-N.C3.BCrnberg_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-N.C3.BCrnberg-3">[3]</a></sup> His rendition of Wagner's <i>Tristan und Isolde</i>, with Vincent Wolfsteiner and <a href="/wiki/Lioba_Braun" title="Lioba Braun">Lioba Braun</a> in the title roles, was broadcast live on radio and to 50 cinemas in Germany and Austria.<sup id="cite_ref-Koch_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Koch-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MUC_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MUC-6">[6]</a></sup> A reviewer noted a sound world with clear contours ("Konturscharfe Klangräume"), serving the dramatic action.<sup id="cite_ref-Koch_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Koch-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Bosch was appointed professor of conducting at the <a href="/wiki/Musikhochschule_M%C3%BCnchen" class="mw-redirect" title="Musikhochschule München">Musikhochschule München</a>. He will leave the GMD position in Nürnberg after the 2017/18 season to focus on teaching in Munich, where he will lead the university orchestra.<sup id="cite_ref-Suk_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suk-7">[7]</a></sup> Also in 2016 Bosch founded the Capella Aquileia whose players are from the Heidenheim Opera Orchestra. The orchestra's first recording, a Coviello Classics CD of Schumann's Second and Fourth Symphonies and "Genoveva" Overture, was nominated for an International Classical Music Award ("Symphonic" Category) in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Heidenheim_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heidenheim-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
