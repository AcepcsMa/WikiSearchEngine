			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Realschule</i></h1>

<p><i><b>Realschule</b></i> (<small>German:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ʁeˈaːlʃuːlə]</a></span>) is a type of <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein" title="Liechtenstein">Liechtenstein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>. It has also existed in <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> (<i>realna gimnazija</i>), <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> (<i>realskole</i>), <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> (<i>realskola</i>), <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> (<i>reáliskola</i>), <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a> (<i>realka</i>) and in the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> (реальное училище).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Realschule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Realschule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/Johann_Joachim_Becher" title="Johann Joachim Becher">Johann Joachim Becher</a>, education also had a mandate to support the replacement of <a href="/wiki/The_Holy_Roman_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="The Holy Roman Empire">the Holy Roman Empire</a> with a more <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republican</a> <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth" title="Commonwealth">commonwealth</a>. His school would have had the task of creating education and teaching an organized state structure. His ideal was the artisan, the educated scholar, the universal scientist.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Situation_of_the_school">Situation of the school</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Realschule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Situation of the school">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the German <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a> system, <i>Realschule</i> is ranked between <a href="/wiki/Hauptschule" title="Hauptschule">Hauptschule</a> (lowest) and <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(school)" title="Gymnasium (school)">Gymnasium</a> (highest). After completing the <i>Realschule</i>, good students are allowed to attend a professional Gymnasium or a general-education Gymnasium. They can also attend a <i>Berufsschule</i> or do an <a href="/wiki/Apprenticeship" title="Apprenticeship">apprenticeship</a>.</p>
<p>In most states of Germany, students start the <i>Realschule</i> at the age of ten or eleven and typically finish school at the age of 16–17. In some states, <i>Realschulen</i> have recently been replaced by <i><a href="/wiki/Oberschule" class="mw-redirect" title="Oberschule">Oberschulen</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Sekundarschule" class="mw-redirect" title="Sekundarschule">Sekundarschulen</a></i>. In 2006 1.32 million German students attended a <i>Realschule</i>.</p>
<p>At <i>Realschule</i>, a student gets an extended education and learns at least one foreign language, usually <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. In the state of <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a>, after the sixth grade, the student has to choose among <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Home_economics" title="Home economics">home economics</a>, and a second foreign language, usually <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>. The new subject becomes the student's fifth main subject, after German, maths, science and English; and it is also possible to learn other foreign languages in free workshops. Other subjects are <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Social_sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Social sciences">social sciences</a>, <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economics</a>, <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">history</a>, religious education, and physical education. After the 8th grade a student has to choose between <a href="/wiki/Arts" class="mw-redirect" title="Arts">arts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a>.</p>
<p>High school diplomas obtained in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> or the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> are usually acknowledged as a <i><a href="/wiki/Mittlere_Reife" title="Mittlere Reife">Mittlere Reife</a></i> (graduation from a <i>Realschule</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In some cases however students may apply for certain subjects at a university. All students holding an American high school diploma may apply for the <i><a href="/wiki/Studienkolleg" title="Studienkolleg">Studienkolleg</a></i>, and after successfully graduating from this they may attend a German university.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Those holding a high school diploma can choose from a wider range of possible major subjects at a German university if they did well on the <a href="/wiki/SAT" title="SAT">SAT</a> or <a href="/wiki/ACT_(test)" title="ACT (test)">ACT</a>. Those who scored higher than 1300 on the SAT or higher than 28 on the ACT may apply for any subject at a German university.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Gymnasia_and_Realgymnasia" class="mw-redirect" title="Gymnasia and Realgymnasia">Gymnasia and <i>Realgymnasia</i></a> are the classical higher or secondary schools of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Abolition_of_the_Realschule_in_Berlin_and_Hamburg">Abolition of the <i>Realschule</i> in Berlin and Hamburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Realschule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Abolition of the Realschule in Berlin and Hamburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Starting in 2010/2011, <i>Realschulen</i> were formally abolished in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> and merged with <i><a href="/wiki/Hauptschule" title="Hauptschule">Hauptschulen</a></i> and the old <i><a href="/wiki/Gesamtschule" class="mw-redirect" title="Gesamtschule">Gesamtschulen</a></i> to form a new type of comprehensive school, called <i>Stadtteilschule</i> in Hamburg and <i>Sekundarschule</i> in Berlin.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Performance_of_students_attending_the_Realschule">Performance of students attending the <i>Realschule</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Realschule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Performance of students attending the Realschule">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the PISA examination the students attending a <i>Realschule</i> were outperformed by those attending a Gymnasium. However, they did better than those attending a <i>Hauptschule</i> or a Gesamtschule school.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism:_class-ism">Criticism: class-ism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Realschule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Criticism: class-ism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The German tripartite system of education has been widely criticized for separating children along class lines at a very early age. For instance, in some German states, a decision is made in the sixth or even the fourth grade about whether a child is to continue in the Gymnasium, the <i>Realschule</i>, or the <i>Hauptschule</i>. Only the Gymnasium is a <a href="/wiki/University-preparatory_school" title="University-preparatory school">university-preparatory school</a>, so critics argue essentially a decision is made as early as the fourth grade about whether a child will be allowed to attend college.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The system is considered so onerous outside Germany that the <a href="/wiki/OECD" title="OECD">OECD</a> even sent a special envoy<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> to Germany to condemn current German practice.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Specifically, the Brazilian expert found that German schools basically separate children according to <a href="/wiki/Social_class" title="Social class">social class</a>, with children of academics and professionals more often being sent on to a Gymnasium, and working-class children being sent to a <i>Realschule</i> or a <i>Hauptschule</i>.</p>
<p>According to critics, the system is widely considered within Germany<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> to be socially useful in the sense that the upper class is able to reserve the best schools for itself without having to resort to private schools. Finally, no democratic society outside the German-speaking world has tripartite school systems that separate children largely according to background; indeed, this finding was the main complaint about Germany in the recent PISA study.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Proponents of the tripartite system consider the arguments brought forward by the critics to be invalid. They point to the fact that not only Gymnasium, but also comprehensive schools and schools serving mature students such as the <i><a href="/wiki/Kolleg" title="Kolleg">Kolleg</a></i> or the <i><a href="/wiki/Berufsoberschule" title="Berufsoberschule">Berufsoberschule</a></i> offer the possibility for the <i>Abitur</i>. Also it is possible to attend college without holding the <i>Abitur</i>. They also hold the opinion that state-funded <i>Realschulen</i> and Gymnasiums offered many working-class children the possibility to move up the social ladder. Also, proponents of the tripartite system fear the abolishing Gymnasia and <i>Realschulen</i> will lead to the growth of a private school sector.</p>
<p>Proponents of the <i>Realschule</i> also hold the opinion that it aids students' personality development.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> According to a study those attending a <i>Realschule</i> became more altruistic and more likely to care for others over time.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_.22Erweiterte_Realschule.22_and_.22Realschule_Plus.22">The "Erweiterte Realschule" and "Realschule Plus"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Realschule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The &quot;Erweiterte Realschule&quot; and &quot;Realschule Plus&quot;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The "Erweiterte Realschule" (expanded <i>Realschule</i>) is a school that exists in the German State of the <a href="/wiki/Saarland" title="Saarland">Saarland</a>. It is not to be confused with the <i>Realschule</i>. While the <i>Realschule</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Selective_school" title="Selective school">selective school</a>, the <i>Erweiterte Realschule</i> is a school that does not select its students on the basis of academic achievement or aptitude. It streams students by perceived ability after 7th grade. The "<i>Realschule Plus</i>" is a non-selective school that exists in the State of <a href="/wiki/Rhineland-Palatinate" title="Rhineland-Palatinate">Rhineland-Palatinate</a>. It offers general education classes (that resemble normal <i>Realschule</i> classes) as well as remedial classes. The <i>Erweiterte Realschule</i> and the <i>Realschule Plus</i> are not the school of first choice for many students and often are attended by students who have been turned down by or have been expelled from other schools.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Norway">Norway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Realschule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Norway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <i>realskole</i> existed in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> between 1935 and 1970. It replaced the former <i>middelskole</i>, and was the level between <i>folkeskole</i> (primary school) and <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(school)" title="Gymnasium (school)">gymnasium</a>. The majority historically left school after <i>folkeskole</i>, and the <i>realskole</i> was meant as preparation for the <i>gymnasium</i>, itself a preparation for university studies. Only a small minority attended <i>gymnasium</i> in those times.</p>
