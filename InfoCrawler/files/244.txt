<p><b>Jan Nagel</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Dutch" title="Help:IPA/Dutch">[jɑn ˈnaːɣəl]</a></span>; c. 1560–1602) was a Dutch Renaissance painter.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Nagel_(painter)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jan_Nagel_(attr)_Juno.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Jan_Nagel_%28attr%29_Juno.jpg/170px-Jan_Nagel_%28attr%29_Juno.jpg" width="170" height="234" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Jan_Nagel_%28attr%29_Juno.jpg/255px-Jan_Nagel_%28attr%29_Juno.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Jan_Nagel_%28attr%29_Juno.jpg/340px-Jan_Nagel_%28attr%29_Juno.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2192" data-file-height="3017" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jan_Nagel_(attr)_Juno.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
File:Jan Nagel (attr) Juno.jpg</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Nagel was born in <a href="/wiki/Haarlem" title="Haarlem">Haarlem</a>. Houbraken mentions him only as the teacher of <a href="/wiki/Claes_Jacobsz_van_der_Heck" title="Claes Jacobsz van der Heck">Claes Jacobsz van der Heck</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Houbraken_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Houbraken-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Karel van Mander, who also mentions him on the last page of his <a href="/wiki/Schilder-boeck" title="Schilder-boeck">Schilder-boeck</a> as the teacher of Claes Jacobsz van der Heck, he was a follower of the Antwerp painter <a href="/wiki/Cornelis_Molenaer" title="Cornelis Molenaer">Cornelis Molenaer</a>, as good as he was at landscape painting, but better at portraits, from either Haarlem or Alkmaar, who died at the Hague in 1602. He spelled his name Ian Nagel or Naghel.<sup id="cite_ref-Mander_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mander-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the RKD his daughter Neeltge married the painter Sijbert Cornelisz Moninckx, father of <a href="/wiki/Pieter_Moninckx" title="Pieter Moninckx">Pieter Moninckx</a>, and he became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Confrerie_Pictura" title="Confrerie Pictura">Guild of Saint Luke</a> in the Hague in 1600, where he later died.<sup id="cite_ref-RKD_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RKD-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the archives of the Haarlem <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_rhetoric" title="Chamber of rhetoric">chamber of rhetoric</a> called <a href="/wiki/Trouw_moet_Blycken" class="mw-redirect" title="Trouw moet Blycken">Trouw moet Blycken</a>, he was a member ("camerist") who painted their oldest blazoen from 1593.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He died in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a>.</p>
