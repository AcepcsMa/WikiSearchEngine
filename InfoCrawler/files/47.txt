<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" style="margin:0.5em; background:#cef2e0; font-family:inherit; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3bfb1; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span id="From_today's_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today.27s_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:0.1em 0.6em;">
<div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 102px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dave_Gallaher_(cropped).jpg" class="image" title="Dave Gallaher in 1905"><img alt="Dave Gallaher in 1905" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Dave_Gallaher_%28cropped%29.jpg/100px-Dave_Gallaher_%28cropped%29.jpg" width="100" height="125" class="thumbborder" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Dave_Gallaher_%28cropped%29.jpg/150px-Dave_Gallaher_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Dave_Gallaher_%28cropped%29.jpg/200px-Dave_Gallaher_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="1500" /></a></div>
</div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Dave_Gallaher" title="Dave Gallaher">Dave Gallaher</a></b> (30 October 1873 – 4 October 1917) was a New Zealand <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> footballer and the captain of the 1905–06 <a href="/wiki/The_Original_All_Blacks" title="The Original All Blacks">Original All Blacks</a>. They were the first <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_national_rugby_union_team" title="New Zealand national rugby union team">representative New Zealand team</a> to tour the British Isles, winning 34 out of 35 matches on their world tour. With his vice-captain <a href="/wiki/Billy_Stead" title="Billy Stead">Billy Stead</a>, Gallaher co-wrote the classic rugby text <i>The Complete Rugby Footballer</i>. Retiring as a player after the tour, he took up coaching, and was a <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_rugby_union_terms#Selector" title="Glossary of rugby union terms">selector</a> for both <a href="/wiki/Auckland_rugby_union_team" title="Auckland rugby union team">Auckland</a> and New Zealand for most of the following decade. The Originals helped to cement rugby as New Zealand's national sport, but Gallaher's role as <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Ellison#Wing-forward" title="Thomas Ellison">wing-forward</a> contributed to decades of strain between the rugby authorities of New Zealand and the <a href="/wiki/Home_Nations" title="Home Nations">Home Nations</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/World_Rugby" title="World Rugby">International Rugby Football Board</a> effectively outlawed the position in 1931. During the <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">First World War</a>, Gallaher was killed at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Passchendaele" title="Battle of Passchendaele">Battle of Passchendaele</a> in Belgium. He has been inducted into the <a href="/wiki/World_Rugby_Hall_of_Fame" title="World Rugby Hall of Fame">World Rugby Hall of Fame</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Sports_Hall_of_Fame" title="New Zealand Sports Hall of Fame">New Zealand Sports Hall of Fame</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Gallaher_Shield" title="Gallaher Shield">Gallaher Shield</a> is awarded annually to the winner of Auckland's club championship, and the <a href="/wiki/Dave_Gallaher_Trophy" title="Dave Gallaher Trophy">Dave Gallaher Trophy</a> is contested between the national teams of <a href="/wiki/France_national_rugby_union_team" title="France national rugby union team">France</a> and New Zealand. (<a href="/wiki/Dave_Gallaher" title="Dave Gallaher"><b>Full&#160;article...</b></a>)</p>

<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/List_of_counties_in_West_Virginia" title="List of counties in West Virginia">U.S. state of West Virginia has 55 counties</a></b>. Fifty of them existed at the time of the <a href="/wiki/Wheeling_Convention" title="Wheeling Convention">Wheeling Convention</a> in 1861, before which <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginia</a> was part of the Commonwealth of <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>. The remaining five (<a href="/wiki/Grant_County,_West_Virginia" title="Grant County, West Virginia">Grant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mineral_County,_West_Virginia" title="Mineral County, West Virginia">Mineral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_County,_West_Virginia" title="Lincoln County, West Virginia">Lincoln</a>, <a href="/wiki/Summers_County,_West_Virginia" title="Summers County, West Virginia">Summers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mingo_County,_West_Virginia" title="Mingo County, West Virginia">Mingo</a>) were formed within the state after its admission to the United States on June 20, 1863. The <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_West_Virginia" title="Constitution of West Virginia">West Virginia Constitution</a> was ratified in 1872, replacing the state constitution created in 1863 when West Virginia became a state. Article 9, Section 8, of the West Virginia Constitution permits the creation of additional <a href="/wiki/County_(United_States)" title="County (United States)">counties</a> if a majority of citizens in the proposed new county vote for its creation and the new county has a minimum area of 400 square miles (1,036&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) and a population of at least 6,000. Creation of a new county is prohibited if it would bring another county below these thresholds. Randolph County is the largest by area at 1,040 square miles (2,694&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), and <a href="/wiki/Hancock_County,_West_Virginia" title="Hancock County, West Virginia">Hancock County</a> is the smallest at 83 square miles (215&#160;km<sup>2</sup>). (<b><a href="/wiki/List_of_counties_in_West_Virginia" title="List of counties in West Virginia">Full&#160;list...</a></b>)</p>
