			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Der Stürmer</i></h1>

<p><i><b>Der Stürmer</b></i> (<small>pronounced</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[deːɐ̯ ˈʃtʏʁmɐ]</a></span>, lit. "The Stormer/Attacker/Striker") was a weekly German <a href="/wiki/Tabloid_(newspaper_format)" title="Tabloid (newspaper format)">tabloid-format</a> <a href="/wiki/Newspaper" title="Newspaper">newspaper</a> published by <a href="/wiki/Julius_Streicher" title="Julius Streicher">Julius Streicher</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Gauleiter" title="Gauleiter">Gauleiter</a> of <a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconia</a>, from 1923 to the end of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, with brief suspensions in publication due to legal difficulties. It was a significant part of <a href="/wiki/Nazi_propaganda" class="mw-redirect" title="Nazi propaganda">Nazi propaganda</a> and was vehemently <a href="/wiki/Antisemitism" title="Antisemitism">anti-Semitic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-conscience228_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conscience228-1">[1]</a></sup> The paper was not an official publication of the Nazi party, but was published privately by Streicher.<sup id="cite_ref-bayern_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bayern-2">[2]</a></sup> For this reason, the paper did not display the Nazi party swastika in its logo.<sup id="cite_ref-bayern_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bayern-2">[2]</a></sup> The paper was a very lucrative business for Streicher, and made him a multi-millionaire.<sup id="cite_ref-bayern_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bayern-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Unlike the <i><a href="/wiki/V%C3%B6lkischer_Beobachter" title="Völkischer Beobachter">Völkischer Beobachter</a></i> (<i>The <a href="/wiki/V%C3%B6lkisch" class="mw-redirect" title="Völkisch">Völkisch</a> Observer</i>), the official party paper which gave itself an outwardly serious appearance, <i>Der Stürmer</i> often ran material such as <a href="/wiki/Caricature" title="Caricature">caricatures</a> of Jews and accusations of <a href="/wiki/Blood_libel" title="Blood libel">blood libel</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-conscience228_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conscience228-1">[1]</a></sup> as well as sexually explicit, <a href="/wiki/Anticlericalism" class="mw-redirect" title="Anticlericalism">anti-Catholic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anti-communism" title="Anti-communism">anti-Communist</a>, and anti-<a href="/wiki/Monarchism" title="Monarchism">monarchist</a> propaganda.</p>
<p>The newspaper originated at <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> during <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>'s attempt to establish power and control. The first copy of <i>Der Stürmer</i> was published on 20 April 1923.<sup id="cite_ref-one_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-one-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>Der Stürmer</i>’s circulation grew over time, distributing to a large percentage of the German population as well as Argentina, Brazil, Canada and the United States. The newspaper reached a peak circulation of 486 000 in 1937.<sup id="cite_ref-bayern_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bayern-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>As early as 1933, Streicher was calling for the extermination of the Jews in <i>Der Stürmer</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> During the war, Streicher regularly authorized articles demanding the annihilation and extermination of the <a href="/wiki/Jewish_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish race">Jewish race</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-one_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-one-3">[3]</a></sup> After the war, he was convicted of <a href="/wiki/Crimes_against_humanity" title="Crimes against humanity">crimes against humanity</a> and executed.<sup id="cite_ref-Streicher_judgement_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Streicher_judgement-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Racist_caricatures">Racist caricatures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Der_St%C3%BCrmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Racist caricatures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Der Stürmer</i> was best known for its antisemitic caricatures, which depicted Jews as ugly characters with exaggerated facial features and misshapen bodies. In his propaganda work, Streicher furthered Medieval stereoptypes, e.g., that Jews killed children, sacrificed them and drank their blood<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. The large majority of these drawings were the work of <a href="/wiki/Philipp_Rupprecht" title="Philipp Rupprecht">Philipp Rupprecht</a>, known as <i>Fips</i>, who was one of the best-known anti-Semitic cartoonists of the Third Reich. Through the adaptation and amalgamation of almost every existing anti-Semitic stereotype, myth and tradition, Rupprecht's virulent attacks aimed predominantly at the dehumanization and demonization of Jews.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the bottom of the title page there was always the motto <i>"Die Juden sind unser Unglück!"</i> ("The Jews are our misfortune!"), coined by <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_von_Treitschke" title="Heinrich von Treitschke">Heinrich von Treitschke</a> in the 1880s.<sup id="cite_ref-HHBS_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HHBS-7">[7]</a></sup> In the nameplate was the motto <i>"Deutsches Wochenblatt zum Kampfe um die Wahrheit"</i> ("German Weekly Newspaper in the Fight for Truth").<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>Most of its readers were young people and people from the lowest strata of German society. Copies of <i>Der Stürmer</i> were displayed in prominent cases throughout the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Reich</a>; as well as advertising the publication, these cases also allowed its articles to reach those readers who either did not have time to buy and read a daily newspaper in depth, or could not afford the expense. In 1927, it sold about 27,000 copies every week; by 1935, its circulation had increased to around 480,000.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nazi_attitudes_towards_the_paper">Nazi attitudes towards the paper</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Der_St%C3%BCrmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nazi attitudes towards the paper">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since the late 1920s, Streicher's vulgar and inconsiderate style was increasingly a cause of embarrassment for the Nazi party.<sup id="cite_ref-bayern_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bayern-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1936 the sale of the <i>Der Stürmer</i> in Berlin was restricted during the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Olympic_Games" class="mw-redirect" title="Berlin Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bayern_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bayern-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Goebbels" title="Joseph Goebbels">Joseph Goebbels</a> tried to ban the newspaper in 1938.<sup id="cite_ref-bayern_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bayern-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hermann_G%C3%B6ring" title="Hermann Göring">Hermann Göring</a> forbade <i>Der Stürmer</i> in all of his departments, and <a href="/wiki/Baldur_von_Schirach" title="Baldur von Schirach">Baldur von Schirach</a> banned it as a means of education in the <a href="/wiki/Hitler_Youth" title="Hitler Youth">Hitler Youth</a> hostels and other education facilities by a <i>"Reichsbefehl"</i> ("Reich command").<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Göring harboured a particularly intense hatred of the paper, especially after it published a libellous article alleging that his daughter <a href="/wiki/Edda_G%C3%B6ring" title="Edda Göring">Edda</a> had been conceived through <a href="/wiki/Artificial_insemination" title="Artificial insemination">artificial insemination</a>. It was only through Hitler's intervention that Streicher was spared any punishment.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, other senior Nazi officials, including <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Himmler" title="Heinrich Himmler">Heinrich Himmler</a> (head of the SS), <a href="/wiki/Robert_Ley" title="Robert Ley">Robert Ley</a> (leader of the <a href="/wiki/German_Labour_Front" title="German Labour Front">German Labour Front</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Max_Amann" title="Max Amann">Max Amann</a> (proprietor of the <i>Zentral Verlag</i> (Central Press), comprising 80% of the German press in 1942), endorsed the publication, and their statements were often published in the paper. <a href="/wiki/Albert_Forster" title="Albert Forster">Albert Forster</a>, <i>Gauleiter</i> of <a href="/wiki/Danzig" class="mw-redirect" title="Danzig">Danzig</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Gda%C5%84sk" title="Gdańsk">Gdańsk</a>), wrote in 1937:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>With pleasure I say that the <i>Stürmer,</i> more than any other daily or weekly newspaper, has made clear to the people in simple ways the danger of Jewry. Without Julius Streicher and his <i>Stürmer,</i> the importance of a solution to the Jewish question would not be seen to be as critical as it actually is by many citizens. It is therefore to be hoped that those who want to learn [the] unvarnished truth about the Jewish question will read the <i>Stürmer.</i><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Hitler considered Streicher's "primitive methods" to be effective in influencing "the man in the street." <sup id="cite_ref-one_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-one-3">[3]</a></sup> Although Streicher and his paper were increasingly isolated in the Nazi party, Hitler continued to support Streicher and was an avid reader of <i>Der Stürmer</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-bayern_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bayern-2">[2]</a></sup> In December 1941 he stated: "Streicher is reproached for his <i>Stürmer</i>. The truth is the opposite of what people say: he idealised the Jew. The Jew is baser, fiercer, more diabolical than Streicher depicted him."<sup id="cite_ref-tabletalk154331332_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tabletalk154331332-11">[11]</a></sup> In February 1942, he praised the newspaper: "One must never forget the services rendered by the <i>Stürmer</i> ... Now that Jews are known for what they are, nobody any longer thinks that Streicher libelled them."<sup id="cite_ref-tabletalk154331332_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tabletalk154331332-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hermann_Rauschning" title="Hermann Rauschning">Hermann Rauschning</a>, who claimed to be Hitler's 'confidant', said in the mid-1930s:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Anti-Semitism ... was beyond question the most important weapon in [Hitler's] propagandist arsenal, and almost everywhere it was of deadly efficiency. That was why he had allowed Streicher, for example, a free hand. The man's stuff, too, was amusing, and very cleverly done. Wherever, he wondered, did Streicher get his constant supply of new material? He, Hitler, was simply on thorns to see each new issue of the <i>Stürmer.</i> It was the one periodical that he always read with pleasure, from the first page to the last.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>During the war, the paper's circulation dropped because of paper shortages, as well as Streicher's exile from Nuremberg for corruption. More ominously, because of <a href="/wiki/The_Holocaust" title="The Holocaust">the Holocaust</a>, the people it targeted had begun to disappear from everyday life, which diminished the paper's relevance.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Hitler, however, insisted that Streicher receive sufficient support to continue publishing <i>Der Stürmer</i>.</p>
<p>After the war, Streicher was tried at the <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_trials" title="Nuremberg trials">Nuremberg trials</a>. His publishing and speaking activities were a major part of the evidence presented against him. In essence, the prosecutors took the line that Streicher's role in <a href="/wiki/Julius_Streicher#Trial_and_execution" title="Julius Streicher">inciting Germans to exterminate Jews</a> made him an <a href="/wiki/Accessory_(legal_term)" title="Accessory (legal term)">accessory</a> to murder, and thus as culpable as those who actually carried out the killing. Prosecutors also introduced evidence that Streicher continued his incendiary articles and speeches when he was well aware that Jews were being slaughtered. Streicher was found guilty of <a href="/wiki/Crimes_against_humanity" title="Crimes against humanity">crimes against humanity</a> and hanged.<sup id="cite_ref-Streicher_judgement_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Streicher_judgement-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Antisemitic_content">Antisemitic content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Der_St%C3%BCrmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Antisemitic content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Stuermer1934.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/fa/Stuermer1934.gif/220px-Stuermer1934.gif" width="220" height="299" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/f/fa/Stuermer1934.gif 1.5x" data-file-width="270" data-file-height="367" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Stuermer1934.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
1934 <i>Stürmer</i> issue: "Storm above Judah" - criticizing institutional churches as "Judaized" organizations. Caption: <i>I called the Jews a cursed people, but you have made out of them the Elect Nation.</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>According to the American writer <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Showalter" title="Dennis Showalter">Dennis Showalter</a>, "a major challenge of political anti-Semitism involves overcoming the images of the 'Jew next door' — the living, breathing acquaintance or associate whose simple existence appears to deny the validity of that negative stereotype."<sup id="cite_ref-two_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-two-13">[13]</a></sup> <i>Der Stürmer'</i>s lurid content appealed to a large spectrum of readers who were lower class and less-sophisticated.<sup id="cite_ref-one_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-one-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>Der Stürmer</i> was known for its use of simple themes that took little thought.<sup id="cite_ref-two_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-two-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Der Stürmer</i> often gave descriptions of how to identify Jewish people. The paper often included racist political cartoons, including anti-Semitic caricatures. Besides the graphic depictions, articles often focused on imaginary fears, exaggerations and perceived behavioral differences between Jews and other German citizens.<sup id="cite_ref-two_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-two-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sexual_crimes">Sexual crimes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Der_St%C3%BCrmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sexual crimes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Deutsches_Historisches_Museum_Der_St%C3%BCrmerplakat.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Deutsches_Historisches_Museum_Der_St%C3%BCrmerplakat.jpg/180px-Deutsches_Historisches_Museum_Der_St%C3%BCrmerplakat.jpg" width="180" height="375" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Deutsches_Historisches_Museum_Der_St%C3%BCrmerplakat.jpg/270px-Deutsches_Historisches_Museum_Der_St%C3%BCrmerplakat.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Deutsches_Historisches_Museum_Der_St%C3%BCrmerplakat.jpg/360px-Deutsches_Historisches_Museum_Der_St%C3%BCrmerplakat.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1128" data-file-height="2352" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Deutsches_Historisches_Museum_Der_St%C3%BCrmerplakat.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Poster showing <i><a href="/wiki/Rassenschande" title="Rassenschande">Rassenschande</a></i> ("racial shame") ca. 1935.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Stories of <i><a href="/wiki/Rassenschande" title="Rassenschande">Rassenschande</a></i>, i.e. the Jewish men and German women having sex, were staples of <i>Der Stürmer</i><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> but many were creations of Streicher's imagination, derived from little fact, or random occurrences. Streicher described Jews as sex offenders who were<sup id="cite_ref-two_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-two-13">[13]</a></sup> "violators of the innocent", "perpetrators of bizarre sex crimes", and "ritual murderers" who performed in religious ceremonies using blood of other humans, usually <a href="/wiki/Christians" class="mw-redirect" title="Christians">Christians</a>. Streicher also frequently reported attempts of child molestation by Jews. <i>Der Stürmer</i> never lacked details about sex, names, and crimes in order to keep readers aroused and entertained. These accusations, articles and crimes printed in <i>Der Stürmer</i> were often inaccurate and rarely investigated by staff members.</p>
<p>In the newspaper's opinion, if a German girl became pregnant by a Jew, the Jew would deny paternity, offer to pay for an abortion, fail to pay child support, or simply leave for the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-two_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-two-13">[13]</a></sup> Within <i>Der Stürmer</i> it was not uncommon to hear reports of German women killing their children because they did not want to bring a "Jewish bastard into the world".<sup id="cite_ref-two_13-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-two-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>"For Julius Streicher the Jews' hatred for <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> was concealed only for one reason: Business."<sup id="cite_ref-two_13-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-two-13">[13]</a></sup> Jewish businessmen were often portrayed as doing almost anything to obtain financial wealth which included, in his words, "become a usurer, a traitor, a murderer".<sup id="cite_ref-two_13-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-two-13">[13]</a></sup> In the summer of 1931, Streicher focused much of the paper's attention on a Jewish owned butchery. One <a href="/wiki/Philanthropic" class="mw-redirect" title="Philanthropic">philanthropic</a> merchant operated a soup kitchen; <i>Der Stürmer</i> ran articles accusing the business of poisoning the food served. <i>Der Stürmer</i> criticized and twisted every single price increase and decrease in Jewish shops, as well as their charitable donations as a further form of financial greed. This attack on Jewish benevolence received the most public criticism out of all of <i>Der Stürmer'</i>s antisemitic propaganda. Its "Letter Box" encouraged the reporting of Jewish illegal acts; the unofficial style helped prevent suspicion of propaganda, and lent it an air of authenticity.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
