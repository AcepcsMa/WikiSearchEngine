			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Imperial castle</h1>

The <a href="/wiki/Imperial_castle_of_M%C3%BCnzenberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Imperial castle of Münzenberg">imperial castle of Münzenberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hesse" title="Hesse">Hesse</a>, Germany</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>An <b>imperial castle</b> or <i><b>Reichsburg</b></i> was a castle built by order of the Holy Roman Emperor, whose management was entrusted to <i><a href="/wiki/Ministerialis" title="Ministerialis">Reichsministeriales</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Burgmann" title="Burgmann">Burgmannen</a></i>. It is not possible to identify a clear distinction between imperial castles and the fortified <a href="/wiki/Kaiserpfalz" title="Kaiserpfalz">imperial palaces</a> or <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigspfalz" class="mw-redirect" title="Königspfalz"><i>Pfalzen</i></a>, because many imperial castles were used by German kings for temporary stays. A large number of imperial castles were built in regions such as <a href="/wiki/Swabia" title="Swabia">Swabia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Palatinate_(region)" title="Palatinate (region)">Palatinate</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Alsace" title="Alsace">Alsace</a>, where there were a high density of imperial estates (<i>Reichsgüter</i>) during the <a href="/wiki/Hohenstaufen_era" class="mw-redirect" title="Hohenstaufen era">Hohenstaufen era</a>.</p>
