<p><b>August Franz Frank</b> (5 April 1898 – 21 March 1984) was a <a href="/wiki/Nazi" class="mw-redirect" title="Nazi">Nazi</a> <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a> official of the <a href="/wiki/SS-Wirtschafts-Verwaltungshauptamt" class="mw-redirect" title="SS-Wirtschafts-Verwaltungshauptamt">SS Main Economic Administration Office</a> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>SS-Wirtschafts-Verwaltungshauptamt</i></span>), generally known by its initials WVHA. The WVHA was, among other things, responsible for the administration of the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_concentration_camps" title="Nazi concentration camps">Nazi concentration camps</a>. After the war, the higher WVHA officials, including Frank, were placed on trial for, and convicted of, <a href="/wiki/War_crime" title="War crime">war crimes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crimes_against_humanity" title="Crimes against humanity">crimes against humanity</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_Nazi_career">Early Nazi career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=August_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early Nazi career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frank joined the <a href="/wiki/Schutzstaffel" title="Schutzstaffel">SS</a> as a private on 1 May 1932, and the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Party" title="Nazi Party">Nazi Party</a> on 1 January 1933. From 1933 to 1935, Frank worked at minor administrative duties in connection with a number of small industries at the <a href="/wiki/Dachau_concentration_camp" title="Dachau concentration camp">Dachau concentration camp</a> manned by inmate labor, most of which were concerned with concentration camp maintenance.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rise_in_Nazi_hierarchy">Rise in Nazi hierarchy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=August_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rise in Nazi hierarchy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1935, at the request of <a href="/wiki/Oswald_Pohl" title="Oswald Pohl">Oswald Pohl</a>, Frank became SS Administrative Officer of the Special Purpose Troops (<i><a href="/wiki/SS-Verf%C3%BCgungstruppe" title="SS-Verfügungstruppe">SS-Verfügungstruppe</a></i>) and of the concentration camp guards, the SS Death's Head units (<i><a href="/wiki/SS-Totenkopfverb%C3%A4nde" title="SS-Totenkopfverbände">SS-Totenkopfverbände</a></i>), although the presence of a bureaucratic rival somewhat limited his authority in the second capacity. In February 1940, Frank became chief supply officer of the <a href="/wiki/Waffen_SS" class="mw-redirect" title="Waffen SS">Waffen SS</a> and Death's Head units under Pohl.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concentration_camp_administrator">Concentration camp administrator</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=August_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Concentration camp administrator">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When the WVHA was organized in 1942, he became Pohl's deputy chief of WVHA and chief of Department (<i>Amtsgruppe</i>) A, the administrative division of WVHA. He served in this capacity until 1 September 1943, when he was permitted to resign to become administrative chief of the <a href="/wiki/Ordnungspolizei" title="Ordnungspolizei">Order Police</a>. <a href="/wiki/Amtsgruppe_A" class="mw-redirect" title="Amtsgruppe A">Amtsgruppe A</a> was the administrative branch of WVHA. It comprised five offices (<i>Ämter</i>), as follows:</p>
<ul>
<li>Amt A-1: budgets.</li>
<li>Amt A-2: finance and payroll.</li>
<li>Amt A-3: legal matters.</li>
<li>Amt A-4: auditing office.</li>
<li>Amt A-5: personnel.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Involvement_in_the_Holocaust">Involvement in the Holocaust</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=August_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Involvement in the Holocaust">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>A measure of the detailed planning that Frank and other Nazis put into the carrying out of the Holocaust and the deprivation of the property of the murdered Jews can be gauged from <a href="/wiki/August_Frank_memorandum" title="August Frank memorandum">a memorandum prepared by Frank on 26 September 1942</a>. For example, Frank gave instructions on dealing with the underwear of the murder victims. This memorandum, when it came to light after the war, played a key role in refuting Frank's claims that he had no knowledge that Jews were being murdered en masse in the extermination camps of <a href="/wiki/Operation_Reinhard" title="Operation Reinhard">Operation Reinhard</a>. The memorandum is also notable as an example of the use of the Nazi euphemism "evacuation" of the Jews, which meant their systematic murder.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_965-967_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_965-967-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Crimes_against_humanity">Crimes against humanity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=August_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Crimes against humanity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After World War II, Frank was placed on <a href="/wiki/Pohl_trial" title="Pohl trial">trial</a> on accusations that during his work at WVHA he had committed <a href="/wiki/Crimes_against_humanity" title="Crimes against humanity">crimes against humanity</a>. The court concerned itself with Frank's conduct between 1 September 1939, and 1 September 1943. Frank's defense was that he thought the only people in concentration camps were solely composed of German nationals who were either habitual criminals or genuine threats to the Nazi regime. This argument was rejected by the court, which found that Frank knew of and actively participated in the Nazi slave labor program.</p>

<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">It cannot be imagined that he believed that all the inmates of the 20 concentration camps and the 165 labor camps scattered throughout the entire continent of Europe were German nationals, composed of habitual criminals, anti-Nazi and asocial persons, and others whom the Reich for security purposes thought best to imprison.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>The court held that the following evidence, among other things, showed Frank's knowledge of the international slave labor program that the Nazi concentration camps had become:</p>
<ul>
<li>A letter from <a href="/wiki/Oswald_Pohl" title="Oswald Pohl">Oswald Pohl</a>, dated 26 June 1942, to all Amtsgruppen chiefs (which would have included Frank), stating that the head of every branch office which was provided with prisoners or prisoners of war for work was responsible for the prevention of escape, robbery, and sabotage;</li>
<li>A letter signed by <a href="/wiki/Oswald_Pohl" title="Oswald Pohl">Oswald Pohl</a>, but actually dictated by Frank, to <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Himmler" title="Heinrich Himmler">Heinrich Himmler</a>, which discussed the commanders of many of the concentration camps and their qualifications and recommendations for reassignments, detachments, and promotions.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_Frank_memorandum" title="August Frank memorandum">Frank's memorandum of 26 September 1942</a> to SS administrators at Lublin and Auschwitz directing that the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_badge" title="Yellow badge">Jewish Star</a> be removed from the garments of deceased inmates.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>Frank appears to have planned to personally profit from concentration camp labor. He was an incorporating partner with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Georg_Loerner&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Georg Loerner (page does not exist)">Georg Loerner</a>, another WVHA official, in a leather and textile enterprise at Dachau.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup> Based on this and other evidence, the court rejected Frank's testimony as incredible:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">it must be concluded, therefore, that Frank knew that the slave labor was being supplied by the concentration camps on a tremendous scale. It must also be conclusively presumed that Frank knew that slavery constituted a crime against humanity.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Involvement_in_genocide">Involvement in genocide</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=August_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Involvement in genocide">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frank's lawyer claimed Frank "did not work for the political aims of National Socialism." This position was rejected by the court:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">It is futile to claim that the program of extermination of the Jews, or the ravaging of the Eastern countries, or the program of enforced slave labor, or the devastation of conquered territory, stemmed from National Socialist policy but not from the Reich. The SS, in which Frank attained the high rank of Obergruppenfuehrer, was a National Socialist agency, and anyone who worked, as Frank did, for 8 years in the higher councils of that agency cannot successfully claim that he was separated from its political activities and purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>In particular, Frank at his trial claimed he only became aware of the Jewish extermination program after hearing Himmler's <a href="/wiki/Posen_speeches" title="Posen speeches">Posen speech</a> on 4 October 1943, a month after he had left the WVHA. By this time, the Nazis had nearly completed the mass killings of the Jews of Poland and nearby areas of Eastern Europe in what has become known as <a href="/wiki/Operation_Reinhard" title="Operation Reinhard">Action Reinhard</a>, also known as Operation Reinhard and the Reinhard action. Frank handled the huge amount of personal property that was either robbed from the Jews while they were alive or stolen from their bodies (there were 2,000 car loads of textiles, for instance<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup>). In his 26 September 1942 memorandum, Frank had chosen to designate this property as "Jewish concealed and stolen goods."<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup> The court rejected Frank's claim that he couldn't have known of the source of these goods:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">But even if we were to give Frank's contention full faith and credit (which we do not), we come to the inescapable conclusion that if he was not a confederate in murder he certainly was in larceny. By what process of law or reason did the Reich become entitled to one hundred million Reichsmarks' worth of personal property owned by persons whom they had enslaved and who died, even from natural causes, in their servitude? Robbing the dead, even without the added offense of killing, is and always has been a crime. And when it is organized, planned, and carried out on a hundred-million-mark scale, it becomes an aggravated crime, and anyone who takes part in it is a criminal.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>Frank claimed that he didn't know and had no reason to know that the people from whom the property had come from had been murdered en masse; he testified that he thought all the property accumulating from Operation Reinhard had come from Jews who had died naturally in concentration camps. The court rejected this contention, relying again on the categories of property Frank had dealt with in his 26 September 1942 memorandum&#160;:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">It is difficult to imagine a convoy of Jews from the East, packed so tightly into freight cars that many died, carrying with them for their comfort and convenience such items as electric razors, feather beds, umbrellas, thermos jugs, and baby carriages. It is equally incredible that they would be able to keep such articles in the concentration camps until they died of natural causes. It is fair to assume that the prisoners who froze to death or who died from exhaustion and exposure were not equipped with feather beds, quilts, and woolen blankets. Nor can it be believed that before being herded off to Auschwitz or Lublin they were given an opportunity to gather up their collections of old coins and stamps with which to amuse themselves during their idle time.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquitted_of_mass_murder">Acquitted of mass murder</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=August_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Acquitted of mass murder">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although the court had ruled that Frank was criminally answerable for the slave labor program and the looting of Jewish property, he escaped criminal liability for the murders themselves, as the court viewed him as generally being only involved after the people had already been murdered.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span id="Member_of_criminal_organization_(Nazi_SS)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Member_of_criminal_organization_.28Nazi_SS.29">Member of criminal organization (Nazi SS)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=August_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Member of criminal organization (Nazi SS)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frank was found guilty also of being a member of a criminal organization, that is, the <a href="/wiki/Schutzstaffel" title="Schutzstaffel">Nazi SS</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_992-997_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_992-997-1">[1]</a></sup> Frank's highest rank in the SS was Lieutenant General (<i><a href="/wiki/Obergruppenf%C3%BChrer" title="Obergruppenführer">Obergruppenführer</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_200_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_200-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sentence_and_commutation">Sentence and commutation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=August_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sentence and commutation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 3 November 1947 Frank was sentenced to life in prison by the tribunal with the following words:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">AUGUST FRANK, this Tribunal has adjudged you guilty under counts two, three, and four of the indictment filed in this case. For the crimes of which you have thus been convicted, this Tribunal sentences you to imprisonment for the remainder of your natural life, at such place of confinement as shall be determined by competent authority.<sup id="cite_ref-Pohl_1062-1063_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pohl_1062-1063-4">[4]</a></sup></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>In 1951 Frank's sentence was commuted to 15 years.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He died in March 1984.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
