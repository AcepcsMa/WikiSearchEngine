			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">St. Martha, Nuremberg</h1>

<p><b>St. Martha</b> is a medieval church of the former free imperial city of <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Martha,_mother_of_Symeon_Stylites_the_Younger" class="mw-redirect" title="Martha, mother of Symeon Stylites the Younger">Saint Martha</a>. Since 1800 it is a <a href="/wiki/Continental_Reformed_church" title="Continental Reformed church">Reformed church</a>, the community of which forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Evangelical_Reformed_Church_in_Bavaria_and_Northwestern_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Evangelical Reformed Church in Bavaria and Northwestern Germany">Evangelical Reformed Church (regional church)</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St._Martha,_Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Conrad and John of Waldstomer donated the church and the associated hospital in 1363. The church was consecrated on 24 March 1385. From 1390 to 1430, wealthy Nuremberg families donated a series of stained-glass windows to the chancel of the church.</p>
<p>As a result of the <a href="/wiki/Reformation" title="Reformation">Reformation</a> the church closed in 1526. It was then used as a theatre by the Nuremberg Meistersingers. In 1614, plays were forbidden in churches, and by 1627 it was restored to use as a church.</p>
<p>The church was only lightly damaged during the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, when a bomb hit the roof and chancel arch and by 1946 was back in use. On 5 June 2014, the church was greatly damaged in a fire,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and is being rebuilt.</p>
<p>Fortunately, the medieval church windows had been removed to storage for renovation work, and should be re-inserted when the church is restored.</p>
