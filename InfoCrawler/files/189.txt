<p><i><b>The Handmaid's Tale</b></i> is an American television series created by <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Miller_(producer)" title="Bruce Miller (producer)">Bruce Miller</a> based on the 1985 <a href="/wiki/The_Handmaid%27s_Tale" title="The Handmaid's Tale">novel of the same name</a> by Canadian writer <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Atwood" title="Margaret Atwood">Margaret Atwood</a>. It was ordered by streaming service <a href="/wiki/Hulu" title="Hulu">Hulu</a> with a straight-to-series order of 10 episodes, with the production beginning in late 2016.</p>
<p>The first three episodes of the series premiered on April 26, 2017, with the subsequent seven episodes added on a weekly basis every Wednesday. In May 2017, it was renewed for a second season to premiere in 2018. The series garnered extremely positive reviews and won eight <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Primetime Emmy Awards">Primetime Emmy Awards</a> from thirteen nominations, including <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Drama_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Drama Series">Outstanding Drama Series</a> in 2017. It is the first series on a streaming platform to win an "Outstanding Series" Emmy.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Handmaid%27s_Tale_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the near future, <a href="/wiki/Total_fertility_rate" title="Total fertility rate">fertility rates</a> collapse as a result of <a href="/wiki/Sexually_transmitted_diseases" class="mw-redirect" title="Sexually transmitted diseases">sexually transmitted diseases</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environmental_pollution" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental pollution">environmental pollution</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2017Douhat_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017Douhat-2">[2]</a></sup> With this chaos in place, the <a href="/wiki/Totalitarian" class="mw-redirect" title="Totalitarian">totalitarian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian_Reconstructionism" title="Christian Reconstructionism">Christian theonomic</a> government of "<a href="/wiki/Gilead" title="Gilead">Gilead</a>" <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">establishes rule</a> in the former United States in the aftermath of a <a href="/wiki/Second_American_Civil_War" title="Second American Civil War">civil war</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Douthat2017_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Douthat2017-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Williams2017_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Williams2017-5">[5]</a></sup> Society is organized by power-hungry leaders along a new, militarized, hierarchical regime of fanaticism and newly created social classes, in which women are brutally subjugated, and by law are not allowed to work, own property, handle money, or read.<sup id="cite_ref-Williams2017_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Williams2017-5">[5]</a></sup> Worldwide infertility has resulted in the conscription of the few remaining fertile women in Gilead, called <a href="/wiki/Handmaiden" title="Handmaiden">handmaids</a>, according to an "extremist interpretation" of a <a href="/wiki/Christian_Bible" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian Bible">Biblical</a> account.<sup id="cite_ref-Williams2017_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Williams2017-5">[5]</a></sup> They are assigned to the homes of the ruling elite, where they must submit to ritualized rape with their male masters in order to become pregnant and bear children for those men and their wives.</p>
<p>June Osborne, renamed Offred (<a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_Moss" title="Elisabeth Moss">Elisabeth Moss</a>), is the Handmaid assigned to the home of Gileadan Commander Fred Waterford (<a href="/wiki/Joseph_Fiennes" title="Joseph Fiennes">Joseph Fiennes</a>) and his wife Serena Joy (<a href="/wiki/Yvonne_Strahovski" title="Yvonne Strahovski">Yvonne Strahovski</a>). She is subject to the strictest rules and constant scrutiny; an improper word or deed on her part can lead to brutal punishment. Offred, who is named after her male master like all Handmaids, can remember the "time before", when she was married with a daughter and had her own name and identity, but all she can safely do now is follow the rules of Gilead in the hope that she can someday live free and be reunited with her daughter. The Waterfords, key players in the rise of Gilead, have their own conflicts with the realities of the society they have helped create.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main">Main</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Handmaid%27s_Tale_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Main">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_Moss" title="Elisabeth Moss">Elisabeth Moss</a> as June Osborne/Offred, a Handmaid to Commander Fred Waterford and his wife, Serena Joy, mother of Hannah and wife of Luke.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Joseph_Fiennes" title="Joseph Fiennes">Joseph Fiennes</a> as Commander Fred Waterford, a high-ranking government official and Offred's master, who secretly meets with her to form a connection in the hopes she doesn't kill herself like her predecessor. Both he and his wife played an instrumental role in Gilead's founding.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Yvonne_Strahovski" title="Yvonne Strahovski">Yvonne Strahovski</a> as Serena Joy Waterford, Fred's wife and a former conservative cultural activist who appears to have accepted her new limited role in a society she helped create. Capable of great cruelty, she desperately wants to become a mother.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alexis_Bledel" title="Alexis Bledel">Alexis Bledel</a> as Emily/Ofglen (later Ofsteven), Offred's Handmaid shopping partner. She is considered a "gender traitor" (a term used in Gilead in reference to homosexuality) and knows of a resistance movement called Mayday.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Madeline_Brewer" title="Madeline Brewer">Madeline Brewer</a> as Janine/Ofwarren (later Ofdaniel), a Handmaid with a fragile psyche who has had her right eye removed as punishment for misbehavior. She is convinced her Commander truly loves her and that he wants to run away with her and start a family.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ann_Dowd" title="Ann Dowd">Ann Dowd</a> as Aunt Lydia, a brutal instructor and guardian of the Handmaids. However, she seems to develop a fondness for Janine/Ofwarren.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/O._T._Fagbenle" title="O. T. Fagbenle">O. T. Fagbenle</a> as Luke Bankole, June's husband from before Gilead and father to their daughter Hannah. As June is his second wife, their union is considered invalid in the new society and Hannah to be illegitimate.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Max_Minghella" title="Max Minghella">Max Minghella</a> as Nick Blaine, Commander Waterford's driver and a former drifter from Michigan who develops feelings for Offred.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Samira_Wiley" title="Samira Wiley">Samira Wiley</a> as Moira, June's best friend since college. With June's help, she is able to escape her life as a handmaid but ultimately goes to work at one of Gilead's many unofficial brothels/sex clubs as a "Jezebel" named Ruby. She appears to have given up the will to escape Gilead altogether until June gives her the strength to escape again.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Amanda_Brugel" title="Amanda Brugel">Amanda Brugel</a> as Rita, a Martha, or housekeeper. Her son was killed in the Gilead takeover. (recurring season 1, starring season 2)<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In June 2016, <a href="/wiki/Reed_Morano" title="Reed Morano">Reed Morano</a> was announced as director of the series.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Samira_Wiley" title="Samira Wiley">Samira Wiley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Max_Minghella" title="Max Minghella">Max Minghella</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ann_Dowd" title="Ann Dowd">Ann Dowd</a> joined the cast in July 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Fiennes" title="Joseph Fiennes">Joseph Fiennes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madeline_Brewer" title="Madeline Brewer">Madeline Brewer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yvonne_Strahovski" title="Yvonne Strahovski">Yvonne Strahovski</a> were cast in August 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> followed by <a href="/wiki/O._T._Fagbenle" title="O. T. Fagbenle">O. T. Fagbenle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Brugel" title="Amanda Brugel">Amanda Brugel</a> in September 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In October 2016, <a href="/wiki/Ever_Carradine" title="Ever Carradine">Ever Carradine</a> joined the cast,<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Bledel" title="Alexis Bledel">Alexis Bledel</a> was added in January 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Filming on the series took place in Toronto, Mississauga, Hamilton, and <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Ontario" title="Cambridge, Ontario">Cambridge</a>, Ontario, from September 2016 to February 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> The first full trailer of the TV series was released by Hulu on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> on March 23, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The series premiered on April 26, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-premiere_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-premiere-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 3, 2017, <i>The Handmaid's Tale</i> was renewed for a second season to premiere in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Moss told the news media that the subsequent episodes will cover further developments in the story, filling in some of the unanswered questions and continuing the narrative already "finished" in the book.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The second season will consist of 13 episodes and will begin filming in fall 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Alexis Bledel will return as a series regular. Showrunner Bruce Miller stated that he envisioned ten seasons of the show, stating "Well, you know, honestly, when I started, I tried to game out in my head what would ten seasons be like? If you hit a home run, you want energy to go around the bases, you want enough story to keep going, if you can hook the audience to care about these people enough that they're actually crying at the finale."<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcast">Broadcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Handmaid%27s_Tale_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Broadcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first three episodes of the series premiered on April 26, 2017, with the subsequent seven episodes added on a weekly basis.<sup id="cite_ref-premiere_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-premiere-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> In Canada, the series is broadcast by <a href="/wiki/Bravo_(Canada)" title="Bravo (Canada)">Bravo</a>, beginning with the first two episodes premiering on April 30.<sup id="cite_ref-torstar-hmtbravo_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-torstar-hmtbravo-40">[40]</a></sup> In Scandinavia, the series is available on <a href="/wiki/HBO_Nordic" class="mw-redirect" title="HBO Nordic">HBO Nordic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> In the UK and Ireland, the series premiered on May 28, 2017, and airs every Sunday on <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>In New Zealand, the series was released on the subscription video on demand service <a href="/wiki/Lightbox_(New_Zealand)" title="Lightbox (New Zealand)">Lightbox</a> on June 8, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> In Australia, the series premiered on the TV channel <a href="/wiki/SBS_(Australian_TV_channel)" title="SBS (Australian TV channel)">SBS</a>'s video streaming service <a href="/wiki/SBS_(Australian_TV_channel)#SBS_On_Demand" title="SBS (Australian TV channel)">SBS on Demand</a> on July 6, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Handmaid%27s_Tale_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Handmaid's Tale</i> has received high acclaim from television critics. On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, it has a score of 92 out of 100 based on 40 reviews, indicating "universal acclaim".<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> The season has a 96% approval rating on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> with an average score of 8.63 out of 10 based on 92 reviews. The site's critical consensus is, "Haunting and vivid, <i>The Handmaid's Tale</i> is an endlessly engrossing adaptation of Margaret Atwood's dystopian novel that's anchored by a terrific central performance from Elisabeth Moss."<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Daniel Fienberg of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> called it "probably the spring's best new show and certainly its most important".<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Jen Chaney of <i><a href="/wiki/Vulture_(website)" class="mw-redirect" title="Vulture (website)">Vulture</a></i> gave it a highly positive review, and wrote that it is "A faithful adaptation of the book that also brings new layers to Atwood's totalitarian, sexist world of forced surrogate motherhood" and that "this series is meticulously paced, brutal, visually stunning, and so suspenseful from moment to moment that only at the end of each hour will you feel fully at liberty to exhale".<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>There was much debate on whether parallels could be drawn between the series (and by extension, the book it is based on) and American society following <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>'s and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Pence" title="Mike Pence">Mike Pence</a>'s election as <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_the_United_States" title="Vice President of the United States">Vice President of the United States</a>, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
