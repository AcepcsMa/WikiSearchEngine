<p><b>Max-Morlock-Stadion</b> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˌmaksˈmɔʁlɔkˌʃtaːdi̯ɔn]</a></span> is a <a href="/wiki/Stadium" title="Stadium">stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, Germany, which was opened in 1928. It is located next to <a href="/wiki/Zeppelinfeld" class="mw-redirect" title="Zeppelinfeld">Zeppelinfeld</a>. It also neighbors the <a href="/wiki/Arena_N%C3%BCrnberger_Versicherung" title="Arena Nürnberger Versicherung">Nuremberg Arena</a>.</p>
<p>Since 1966, it has been home stadium to the German <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> club <a href="/wiki/1._FC_N%C3%BCrnberg" title="1. FC Nürnberg">1. FC Nürnberg</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/1972_Summer_Olympics" title="1972 Summer Olympics">1972 Summer Olympics</a>, it hosted six <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1972_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 1972 Summer Olympics">football</a> matches. In 1967, it hosted the <a href="/wiki/1967_European_Cup_Winners%27_Cup_Final" title="1967 European Cup Winners' Cup Final">European Cup Winners' Cup final</a> between <a href="/wiki/Rangers_F.C." title="Rangers F.C.">Rangers</a> and <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>. Bayern won 1–0.</p>
<p>Originally it was known as the <b>Städtisches Stadion</b> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈʃtɛtɪʃəs ˌʃtaːdi̯ɔn]</a></span> (English: Municipal Stadium) until 1945, when it was renamed <b>Victory Stadium</b>. In 1961, it returned to its original name until 1991, when it received the name <b>Frankenstadion</b> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈfʁaŋkn̩ˌʃtaːdi̯ɔn]</a></span>. On 14 March 2006, the stadium was renamed <b>easyCredit-Stadion</b> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ʔiːziˈkʁɛdɪtˌʃtaːdi̯ɔn]</a></span> for a period of five years, after a sponsorship deal with the German bank <a href="/wiki/DZ_Bank" title="DZ Bank">DZ Bank</a>. Many fans of the 1. FC Nuremberg, led by the "Ultras Nuremberg" introduced on 1 April 2006, held demonstration against the name and symbolically renamed the stadium with its current name, in honour of one of the best players in the club's history, <a href="/wiki/Max_Morlock" title="Max Morlock">Max Morlock</a>. On 14 February 2013, the stadium was renamed <b>Grundig Stadion</b> (<small>pronounced</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈɡʁʊndɪç ˌʃtaːdi̯ɔn]</a></span> or <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈɡʁʊndɪɡ-]</a></span>), after a sponsorship deal with <a href="/wiki/Grundig" title="Grundig">Grundig</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In July 2016, the stadium's name changed back to <b>Stadion Nürnberg</b> after the city of Nuremberg could not find a new sponsor.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> From 1 July 2017, the stadium's name officially became Max-Morlock-Stadion.</p>
<p>The stadium hosted five games of the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a>, including the famous match between <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a>, consequently known as the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Nuremberg_(2006_FIFA_World_Cup)" title="Battle of Nuremberg (2006 FIFA World Cup)">Battle of Nuremberg</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Max-Morlock-Stadion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The available facilities at the stadium include two changing rooms for players, changing rooms for coaches, referees. Also physician and treatment rooms are available. A 300 m² press area, an area for press conferences, and three TV studios make the stadium a truly modern one. 1200 m² makes up the VIP area with room for 800 guests. To compensate for the large amount of seats there are 15,000 parking spaces with 205 for VIPs.</p>
<p>The stadium also has track and field facilities that follow international regulation. A full sprinkler system, that feeds the grass with rain water. The pitch is also heated, and lit with a floodlight system. There are two 60 m² video walls that provide video to the fans. There is also a full power back up system, powered by diesel generators.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Max-Morlock-Stadion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beginning in 1933, the <a href="/wiki/National_Socialist_German_Workers_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="National Socialist German Workers Party">National Socialists</a> began to use the stadium as a marching area for the <a href="/wiki/Hitler_Youth" title="Hitler Youth">Hitler Youth</a>. The fourth <i>Deutsche Kampfspiele</i>, one of the biggest events organized by the <a href="/wiki/NSRL" class="mw-redirect" title="NSRL">Nazi Sports Body</a>, took place in this stadium from 23–29 July 1934.</p>
<p>Following 1963, the stadium was reconditioned multiple times, so that it could meet the requirements for football in the Bundesliga.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Renovations">Renovations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Max-Morlock-Stadion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Renovations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Max-Morlock-Stadion has been renovated two times, once in 1988 to 1991, and then again renovation began again in 2002, to be ready in time for the 2006 World Cup. The 2002 renovation cost €56.2 million; the cost is split between the city of Nürnberg, the State of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a> and the building society, which managed the stadium. The stadium has had its capacity increased to 48,548. this modernisation was designed and realized by HPP Architects. This was achieved by extending the southwest and northwest grandstand. The playing field was lowered by 1.30 metres in order to provide all seats an unrestricted view of the field. The <a href="/wiki/Max_Morlock" title="Max Morlock">Max Morlock</a> place was developed as a place for fans to meet and enjoy something to eat; the total area of this place is 1,000 metres. In the summer of 2012, the capacity was increased to 50,000.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concerts">Concerts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Max-Morlock-Stadion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Concerts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/P!nk" class="mw-redirect" title="P!nk">P!nk</a> performed at the stadium on July 15, 2010 during <a href="/wiki/The_Funhouse_Summer_Carnival" class="mw-redirect" title="The Funhouse Summer Carnival">The Funhouse Summer Carnival</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Max-Morlock-Stadion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The stadium and the adjacent <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_Arena" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuremberg Arena">Nuremberg Arena</a> are well serviced by <a href="/wiki/Public_transportation" class="mw-redirect" title="Public transportation">public transportation</a> to facilitate transport of fans from and to the various sports and musical events taking place there:</p>
<ul>
<li>Bus stop <i><a href="/wiki/Max_Morlock" title="Max Morlock">Max-Morlock-Platz</a></i>, right in front of the stadium. Serviced by Bus line 55</li>
<li><i>Frankenstadion</i> station, about 400 Meters (1300&#160;ft) or a 5-minute walk from the stadium. Serviced by <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_S-Bahn" title="Nuremberg S-Bahn">S-Bahn</a> line S2</li>
<li><i>Dutzendteich</i> station and <a href="/wiki/Tram" title="Tram">tramway</a> stop, about 1300&#160;m (4000&#160;ft) or a 10-minute walk from the stadium. Services by Bus lines 55 and 65, Tramway line 6 and S-Bahn line S2</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Messe_(Nuremberg_U-Bahn)" title="Messe (Nuremberg U-Bahn)">Messe subway station</a>, about 1800&#160;m (5500&#160;ft) or a 15-minute walk from the Stadium. Serviced by <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_U-Bahn" title="Nuremberg U-Bahn">U-Bahn (Subway)</a> lines U1 and U11</i></li>
</ul>
<p>During mass sports and entertainment events, such as <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> games or the annual <a href="/wiki/Rock_im_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Rock im Park">Rock im Park</a> festival, additional S-Bahn trains running between main station and Frankenstadion station are being put into service. Before the 2006 <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a>, Frankenstadion station had the length of its existing platform doubled and an additional platform built for that purpose.</p>
