<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:white;"><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Biography_Portal"><big>The Biography Portal</big></span></h2>

<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">

<div style="float:right;margin-left:1em">
<center><a href="/wiki/Lives_of_the_Most_Excellent_Painters,_Sculptors,_and_Architects" title="Lives of the Most Excellent Painters, Sculptors, and Architects">Lives of the Artists</a></center>
<p><a href="/wiki/File:Vite.jpg" class="image" title="Lives of the Most Excellent Painters, Sculptors, and Architects"><img alt="Lives of the Most Excellent Painters, Sculptors, and Architects" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Vite.jpg/150px-Vite.jpg" width="150" height="213" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Vite.jpg/225px-Vite.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Vite.jpg/300px-Vite.jpg 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="568" /></a><br /></p>

<p>A <b><a href="/wiki/Biography" title="Biography">biography</a></b> is a detailed description or account of someone's life. More than a list of basic facts (education, work, relationships, and death), a biography also portrays a subject's experience of these events. Unlike a profile or <a href="/wiki/Curriculum_vitae" title="Curriculum vitae">curriculum vitae</a> (<a href="/wiki/Resume" class="mw-redirect" title="Resume">résumé</a>), a biography presents a subject's life story, highlighting various aspects of his or her life, including intimate details of experience, and may include an analysis of a subject's personality.</p>
<p>Biographical works are usually non-fiction, but fiction can also be used to portray a person's life. One in-depth form of biographical coverage is called legacy writing. Biographical works in diverse media—from literature to film—form the <a href="/wiki/Genre" title="Genre">genre</a> known as biography.</p>
<p>An <b>authorized biography</b> is written with the permission, cooperation, and, at times, participation of a subject or a subject's heirs.</p>
<p>An <b><a href="/wiki/Autobiography" title="Autobiography">autobiography</a></b> is about a life of a subject, written by that subject or sometimes with a collaborator.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:white;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Featured_biography"><i>Featured biography</i></span></h2>

<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:left;margin-right:1em"><a href="/wiki/File:Shen_Kua.JPG" class="image" title="Modern artist's impression of Shen Kuo."><img alt="Modern artist's impression of Shen Kuo." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Shen_Kua.JPG/120px-Shen_Kua.JPG" width="120" height="108" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Shen_Kua.JPG/180px-Shen_Kua.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Shen_Kua.JPG/240px-Shen_Kua.JPG 2x" data-file-width="848" data-file-height="764" /></a></div>
<b>Shen Kuo</b> or <b>Shen Kua</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">沈括</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Shěn Kuò</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Wade%E2%80%93Giles" title="Wade–Giles">Wade–Giles</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-wadegile" xml:lang="zh-Latn-wadegile">Shen K'uo</span></i>) (1031–1095), <a href="/wiki/Chinese_style_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Chinese style name">style name</a> <b>Cunzhong</b> and <a href="/wiki/Chinese_style_name#H.C3.A0o" class="mw-redirect" title="Chinese style name">pseudonym</a> <b>Mengqi Weng</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Polymath" title="Polymath">polymathic</a> <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/History_of_science_and_technology_in_China" title="History of science and technology in China">scientist</a> and statesman of the <a href="/wiki/Song_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Song Dynasty">Song Dynasty</a> (960–1279). Excelling in many fields of study and statecraft, he was a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_mathematics" title="Chinese mathematics">mathematician</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_astronomy" title="Chinese astronomy">astronomer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meteorologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Meteorologist">meteorologist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geologist" title="Geologist">geologist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zoologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Zoologist">zoologist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Botanist" class="mw-redirect" title="Botanist">botanist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_medicine" title="Traditional Chinese medicine">pharmacologist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agronomist" class="mw-redirect" title="Agronomist">agronomist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Archaeologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Archaeologist">archaeologist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethnographer" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnographer">ethnographer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cartographer" class="mw-redirect" title="Cartographer">cartographer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Encyclopedist" class="mw-redirect" title="Encyclopedist">encyclopedist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Military_history_of_China_(pre-1911)" class="mw-redirect" title="Military history of China (pre-1911)">general</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foreign_relations_of_Imperial_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Foreign relations of Imperial China">diplomat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hydraulics" title="Hydraulics">hydraulic engineer</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Chinese_inventions" title="List of Chinese inventions">inventor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Academies_(Shuyuan)" title="Academies (Shuyuan)">academy chancellor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finance_minister" title="Finance minister">finance minister</a>, governmental state inspector, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_poetry" title="Chinese poetry">poet</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Music_of_China" title="Music of China">musician</a>. He was the head official for the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_astronomy" title="Chinese astronomy">Bureau of Astronomy</a> in the Song court, as well as an Assistant Minister of Imperial Hospitality. At court his political allegiance was to the Reformist faction known as the <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Song_Dynasty#Partisans_and_factions.2C_reformers_and_conservatives" class="mw-redirect" title="History of the Song Dynasty">New Policies Group</a>, headed by <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Chancellor of China">Chancellor</a> <a href="/wiki/Wang_Anshi" title="Wang Anshi">Wang Anshi</a> (1021–1086). <b>(<a href="/wiki/Shen_Kuo" title="Shen Kuo">Read more...</a>)</b>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/Beppe_Grillo" title="Beppe Grillo">Giuseppe Piero "Beppe" Grillo</a></b> (born 21 July 1948) is an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedian" title="Comedian">comedian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">blogger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Political_activism" class="mw-redirect" title="Political activism">political activist</a>. Along with <a href="/wiki/Gianroberto_Casaleggio" title="Gianroberto Casaleggio">Gianroberto Casaleggio</a>, Grillo co-founded the <a href="/wiki/Five_Star_Movement" title="Five Star Movement">Five Star Movement</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Populist" class="mw-redirect" title="Populist">populist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eurosceptic" class="mw-redirect" title="Eurosceptic">Eurosceptic</a> political party, in 2009.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:white;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Did_you_know">Did you know</span></h2>

<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.5em;">
<p><a href="/wiki/File:Prince_Antasari_Portrait_from_2009_2000_rupiah_bill.jpg" class="image" title="Prince Antasari on 2009 series 2000 rupiah bill"><img alt="Prince Antasari on 2009 series 2000 rupiah bill" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Prince_Antasari_Portrait_from_2009_2000_rupiah_bill.jpg/96px-Prince_Antasari_Portrait_from_2009_2000_rupiah_bill.jpg" width="96" height="120" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Prince_Antasari_Portrait_from_2009_2000_rupiah_bill.jpg/144px-Prince_Antasari_Portrait_from_2009_2000_rupiah_bill.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Prince_Antasari_Portrait_from_2009_2000_rupiah_bill.jpg/192px-Prince_Antasari_Portrait_from_2009_2000_rupiah_bill.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1012" data-file-height="1266" /></a></p>
</div>
<ul>
<li>... that <b><a href="/wiki/Prince_Antasari" title="Prince Antasari">Prince Antasari</a></b> <i>(pictured)</i>, who led a war against <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch colonists</a> for over three years, was ultimately defeated by smallpox?</li>
<li>... that <b><a href="/wiki/Tonya_Butler" title="Tonya Butler">Tonya Butler</a></b>, while playing for the <a href="/wiki/University_of_West_Alabama" title="University of West Alabama">University of West Alabama</a> under head coach <b><a href="/wiki/Randy_Pippin" title="Randy Pippin">Randy Pippin</a></b>, became the first female to score a <a href="/wiki/Field_goal_(American_and_Canadian_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Field goal (American and Canadian football)">field goal</a> in a <a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association" title="National Collegiate Athletic Association">NCAA</a> <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">college football</a> game?</li>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:white;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Quote_of_the_week"><i>Quote of the week</i></span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="width:100%;text-align:center;">
<p>"When are you people going to learn? It's not about who's right or wrong. No denomination's nailed it yet, and they never will because they're all too self-righteous to realize that it doesn't matter what you have faith in, just that you have faith. Your hearts are in the right place, but your brains need to wake up. I have issues with anyone who treats faith as a burden instead of a blessing. You people don't celebrate your faith; you mourn it."</p>
<p>— <i><a href="/wiki/Kevin_Smith" title="Kevin Smith">Kevin Smith</a></i></p>
