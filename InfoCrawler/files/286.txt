<p><b>Schwabach</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈʃvaːbax]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Town#Germany" title="Town">town</a> of about 40,000 inhabitants near <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> in the centre of the region of <a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconia</a> in the North of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>. The city is an autonomous administrative district (<i>kreisfreie Stadt</i>). Schwabach is also the name of a river, which runs through the city prior joining the <a href="/wiki/Rednitz" title="Rednitz">Rednitz</a>.</p>
<p>Schwabach is famous for its crafts made of <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a>, particularly <a href="/wiki/Metal_leaf" title="Metal leaf">gold foil</a>. In 2004, Schwabach celebrated this tradition with an anniversary festival, marking "500 years gold foil in Schwabach".</p>
<p>Around 1500, a local typesetter developed the "<a href="/wiki/Schwabacher" title="Schwabacher">Schwabacher</a>" font. This font was used for printing the first Bible in German, which had been worked out by <a href="/wiki/Martin_Luther" title="Martin Luther">Martin Luther</a>.</p>
<p>Schwabach is also the birthplace of composer <a href="/wiki/Adolf_von_Henselt" title="Adolf von Henselt">Adolf von Henselt</a>, the botanist <a href="/wiki/Johann_Gottfried_Zinn" title="Johann Gottfried Zinn">Johann Gottfried Zinn</a>, the biologist <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Baumeister" title="Ralf Baumeister">Ralf Baumeister</a> and one of the developers of <a href="/wiki/MP3" title="MP3">MP3</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bernhard_Grill" title="Bernhard Grill">Bernhard Grill</a>. It was often visited by <a href="/wiki/Albrecht_D%C3%BCrer" title="Albrecht Dürer">Albrecht Dürer</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schwabach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name derives from the old <a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconian</a> name <i>Suapaha</i> (later <i>Suabaha</i>, then <i>Villa Suabach</i>) which translates as "Schwaben-Bach" in modern <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>, which means "<a href="/wiki/Swabia" title="Swabia">Swabian</a> <a href="/wiki/Stream" title="Stream">stream</a>", the first part of the name was given by the Franconians who came to the area about a millennium after the <a href="/wiki/Hallstatt_culture" title="Hallstatt culture">Hallstatt culture</a> to the people living on the banks of that stream, which were perceived as "<a href="/wiki/Swabia" title="Swabia">Swabians</a>" by them, while the second part of the name is a reference to the stream which flows through town.</p>

<ul>
<li>750 BC–AD 500: Archaeological evidence of settlement.</li>
<li>600–700: Name Schwabach first used to refer to the settlement and the river.</li>
<li>1346: City wall built.</li>
<li>1371: Municipal law established.</li>
<li>1469: Town church built (still in use).</li>
<li>1500: (ca.) Schwabacher font invented.</li>
<li>1528: City hall built (still in use).</li>
<li>1633: First needle factory established in Schwabach.</li>
<li>1723: Schwabach river flooded to highest point ever. Markings of the flood are still visible.</li>
<li>1768: The landmark <i>Old <a href="/wiki/Tilia" title="Tilia">Linden Tree</a></i> planted (still there).</li>
<li>1792: Schwabach became part of <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a>.</li>
<li>1797: <a href="/wiki/Goethe" class="mw-redirect" title="Goethe">Goethe</a> stayed overnight in Schwabach.</li>
<li>1806: Schwabach became part of Bavaria.</li>
<li>1849: Railway station built.</li>
<li>1941: Schwabach bombed in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a></li>
<li>1945: American military base established in Schwabach.</li>
<li>1953: City coat-of-arms introduced.</li>
<li>1972: Schwabach became an autonomous administrative district.</li>
<li>1975: Partner-city relationship with <a href="/wiki/Les_Sables_d%27Olonne" class="mw-redirect" title="Les Sables d'Olonne">Les Sables d'Olonne</a> established.</li>
<li>1980: Schwabach receives the European Union prize for cultural heritage.</li>
<li>1992: US Army to be withdrawn from Schwabach and surrounding areas.</li>
<li>2004: 500-year anniversary celebration of the gold foil industry.</li>
</ul>
