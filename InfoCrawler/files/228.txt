<p><b>Mary Russell, Duchess of Bedford</b> <span class="noexcerpt" style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Dame_Commander_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire">DBE</a> <a href="/wiki/Royal_Red_Cross" title="Royal Red Cross">ARRC</a> <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Linnean_Society_of_London" class="mw-redirect" title="Fellow of the Linnean Society of London">FLS</a></span> (26 September 1865 – ca. 22 March 1937) was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Aviator" class="mw-redirect" title="Aviator">aviator</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ornithologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Ornithologist">ornithologist</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_and_personal_life">Early and personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Russell,_Duchess_of_Bedford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early and personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born as <b>Mary Du Caurroy Tribe</b> at <a href="/wiki/Stockbridge,_Hampshire" title="Stockbridge, Hampshire">Stockbridge, Hampshire</a>, she was the daughter of Walter Harry Tribe, Anglican Archdeacon of <a href="/wiki/Lahore" title="Lahore">Lahore</a>. On 31 January 1888, she married <a href="/wiki/Herbrand_Russell,_11th_Duke_of_Bedford" title="Herbrand Russell, 11th Duke of Bedford">Lord Herbrand Russell</a> at <a href="/wiki/Barrackpore" title="Barrackpore">Barrackpore</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. When Lord Herbrand inherited his childless brother's titles in 1893, she was styled as the Duchess of Bedford.<sup id="cite_ref-Mary01_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mary01-2">[2]</a></sup> Her only child, <a href="/wiki/Hastings_Russell,_12th_Duke_of_Bedford" title="Hastings Russell, 12th Duke of Bedford">Hastings</a>, was born on 21 December 1888.<sup id="cite_ref-Mary01_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mary01-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_and_activism">Work and activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Russell,_Duchess_of_Bedford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Work and activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A major area of organisation and work for the Duchess was in founding four hospitals in <a href="/wiki/Woburn,_Bedfordshire" title="Woburn, Bedfordshire">Woburn</a> and in the grounds of <a href="/wiki/Woburn_Abbey" title="Woburn Abbey">Woburn Abbey</a>. The principal establishment was the Abbey Hospital that she financed and built in 1914, and where she worked as a nurse and radiographer through to the 1930s.<sup id="cite_ref-buxton_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buxton-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Duchess was a <a href="/wiki/Ornithology" title="Ornithology">collector and watcher of birds</a>, and took an interest in bird migration. Between 1909 and 1914 she spent much time on <a href="/wiki/Fair_Isle" title="Fair Isle">Fair Isle</a>, often in the company of <a href="/wiki/William_Eagle_Clarke" title="William Eagle Clarke">William Eagle Clarke</a>. Her journal, <i>A Bird-watcher's Diary</i>, was privately published in 1938 after her death.</p>
<p>She was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Tax_Resistance_League" title="Women's Tax Resistance League">Women's Tax Resistance League</a>, a group associated with the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Social_and_Political_Union" title="Women's Social and Political Union">Women’s Social and Political Union</a> that used <a href="/wiki/Tax_resistance" title="Tax resistance">tax resistance</a> to protest the disenfranchisement of women during the British <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_suffrage" title="Women's suffrage">women's suffrage</a> movement.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aviation">Aviation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Russell,_Duchess_of_Bedford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aviation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:MaryRussellBedford.jpg" class="image"><img alt="MaryRussellBedford.jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/MaryRussellBedford.jpg/220px-MaryRussellBedford.jpg" width="220" height="279" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/MaryRussellBedford.jpg/330px-MaryRussellBedford.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5e/MaryRussellBedford.jpg 2x" data-file-width="347" data-file-height="440" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:MaryRussellBedford.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Late in life at age 63, the Duchess became interested in aviation, that she claimed gave her some relief from her constant <a href="/wiki/Tinnitus" title="Tinnitus">tinnitus</a>, although she eventually became totally deaf. On 2 August 1929, she departed on a record-breaking flight of 10,000 miles from <a href="/wiki/Lympne_Airport" title="Lympne Airport">Lympne Airport</a> to <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">Karachi</a> (then in India) and return to <a href="/wiki/Croydon_Airport" title="Croydon Airport">Croydon Airport</a> in eight days. She was accompanied in her single-engined <a href="/wiki/Fokker_F.VII" title="Fokker F.VII">Fokker F.VII</a> (G-EBTS, Princess Xenia, which she renamed "The Spider" for its tenacity) by her personal pilot Captain <a href="/wiki/C._D._Barnard" title="C. D. Barnard">C. D. Barnard</a> and mechanic Robert (Bob) Little.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On 8 April 1930 she made her first solo flight, in her <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_DH.60_Moth" title="De Havilland DH.60 Moth">DH.60G Moth</a> (G-AAAO).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 10 April 1930 she embarked on a record-breaking flight from Lympne Airport to <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a>, in "The Spider", flying 9,000 miles in 91 hours and twenty minutes over 10 days, again with Barnard and Little.<sup id="cite_ref-Mary01_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mary01-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1934 and again in 1935, with co-pilot F/Lt R. C. Preston in <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Puss_Moth" title="De Havilland Puss Moth">de Havilland Puss Moth</a> G-ABOC, the Duchess made extensive flights from England to the Western Sahara and Northern Nigeria.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Russell,_Duchess_of_Bedford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 1918 the duchess was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Red_Cross" title="Royal Red Cross">Royal Red Cross</a> in the second Associate grade, for her services to wartime nursing at Woburn Auxiliary Hospital.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She was appointed a <a href="/wiki/Dame_Commander_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire">Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire</a> in 1928.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> She was also Dame of Grace of the <a href="/wiki/Venerable_Order_of_Saint_John" class="mw-redirect" title="Venerable Order of Saint John">Order of Saint John</a> (DGStJ)<sup id="cite_ref-Mary01_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mary01-2">[2]</a></sup> and a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Linnean_Society" class="mw-redirect" title="Linnean Society">Linnean Society</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Imperial College">Imperial College</a> (FLS).<sup id="cite_ref-Mary01_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mary01-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Russell,_Duchess_of_Bedford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The duchess died aged 71, in March 1937, three months before <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Earhart" title="Amelia Earhart">Amelia Earhart</a>'s death, after leaving Woburn Abbey in a <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_DH.60_Moth" title="De Havilland DH.60 Moth">DH.60GIII Moth Major</a> (G-ACUR), that crashed into the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> off <a href="/wiki/Great_Yarmouth" title="Great Yarmouth">Great Yarmouth</a>; her body was never recovered.<sup id="cite_ref-buxton_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buxton-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-jackson_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jackson-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
