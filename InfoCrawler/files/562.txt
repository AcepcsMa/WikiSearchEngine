<p>The <b>Bardentreffen</b> (German for <a href="/wiki/Bard" title="Bard">bard</a>'s meeting) is an annual open-air music festival in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, Germany. The first Bardentreffen took place on the 400th anniversary of the death of <a href="/wiki/Meistersinger" title="Meistersinger">meistersinger</a> <a href="/wiki/Hans_Sachs" title="Hans Sachs">Hans Sachs</a> in 1976.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Setup">Setup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bardentreffen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Setup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The venues are spread over the whole historical city and the event takes place on the first weekend of the school summer holidays in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>. Starting with a more political approach including singer songwriter groups of the political left, the Bardentreffen grew into a more multicultural approach and has established itself as an important venue for professional and amateur bands of world music.</p>
<p>There are no entry tickets, the city of Nuremberg and various sponsors take care of band fees and organization. Charly Fischer was head of the organization of the Bardentreffen for nearly 30 years and went into retreat 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Besides "official" sponsored gigs, a part of the city is open for amateurs. <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style">Style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bardentreffen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The background of the group is traditional folk, "Trends" and "experimental", however any band taking part has to mix own vocals and instruments, instrumental only and mere cover bands are not allowed.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Venues comprise among others the Hauptmarkt, Lorenzer Platz, Sebalder Platz, the Schütt island and St. Katharina. Famous contributors include among other <a href="/wiki/Klaus_Lage" title="Klaus Lage">Klaus Lage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Konstantin_Wecker" title="Konstantin Wecker">Konstantin Wecker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Gillespie_(English_singer)" title="Mark Gillespie (English singer)">Mark Gillespie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Stivell" title="Alan Stivell">Alan Stivell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiddler%27s_Green" title="Fiddler's Green">Fiddler's Green</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Cat_Empire" title="The Cat Empire">The Cat Empire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Thalheim" title="Barbara Thalheim">Barbara Thalheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kvitretten" title="Kvitretten">Kvitretten</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joan_Armatrading" title="Joan Armatrading">Joan Armatrading</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leningrad_Cowboys" title="Leningrad Cowboys">Leningrad Cowboys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donnie_Munro" title="Donnie Munro">Donnie Munro</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vadim_Kurilev&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vadim Kurilev (page does not exist)">Vadim Kurilev</a>, <a href="/wiki/St._Vincent_(musician)" title="St. Vincent (musician)">Anne Clark</a> and <a href="/wiki/LaBrassBanda" title="LaBrassBanda">LaBrassBanda</a>. It is an official policy to provide a genuine mix of newcomers and established bands.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Special_motto">Special motto</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bardentreffen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Special motto">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2014, the festival used the motto "<a href="/wiki/War_and_peace" class="mw-redirect" title="War and peace">war and peace</a>" referring to the centenary of the beginning of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ff_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ff-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><a href="/wiki/Pippo_Pollina" title="Pippo Pollina">Pippo Pollina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aziza_Brahim" title="Aziza Brahim">Aziza Brahim</a> were among the most famous acts on the festival. The 2014 festival has been deemed the largest <a href="/wiki/World_music" title="World music">world music</a> festival in Germany, starring 368 artists from 31 nations.<sup id="cite_ref-ff_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ff-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
