<p><b>Augsburg</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈʔaʊ̯ksbʊʁk]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata">&#160;(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Augsburg.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3b/Augsburg.ogg" class="internal" title="Augsburg.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</small>; <a href="/wiki/Bavarian_language" title="Bavarian language">Bavarian</a>: <span lang="bar" xml:lang="bar"><i>Augschburg</i></span>) is a city in <a href="/wiki/Swabia_(Bavaria)" title="Swabia (Bavaria)">Swabia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, Germany. It was a <a href="/wiki/Free_Imperial_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Free Imperial City">Free Imperial City</a> for over 500 years, and is notable for the <a href="/wiki/Augsburg_Confession" title="Augsburg Confession">Augsburg Confession</a>.</p>
<p>It is a <a href="/wiki/College_town" title="College town">university town</a> and home of the <a href="/wiki/Regierungsbezirk" title="Regierungsbezirk">Regierungsbezirk</a> <a href="/wiki/Swabia_(administrative_region)" class="mw-redirect" title="Swabia (administrative region)">Schwaben</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Swabia_(administrative_region)" class="mw-redirect" title="Swabia (administrative region)">Bezirk Schwaben</a>. Augsburg is an <a href="/wiki/Urban_districts_of_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Urban districts of Germany">urban district</a> and home to the institutions of the <a href="/wiki/Augsburg_(district)" title="Augsburg (district)">Landkreis Augsburg</a>. It is the third-largest city in Bavaria (after <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>) with a population of 286,000 citizens.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> After <a href="/wiki/Neuss" title="Neuss">Neuss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trier" title="Trier">Trier</a>, Augsburg is Germany's third oldest city, being founded by the Romans as <a href="/wiki/Augsburg#Early_history" title="Augsburg">Augusta Vindelicorum</a>, named after the <a href="/wiki/Roman_emperor" title="Roman emperor">Roman emperor</a> <a href="/wiki/Augustus" title="Augustus">Augustus</a>.</p>
<p>Augsburg is the only German city with its own legal holiday, the <i><a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Augsburger_Hohes_Friedensfest" class="extiw" title="de:Augsburger Hohes Friedensfest">Augsburger Hohes Friedensfest</a></i>, celebrated on August 8 of every year. This gives Augsburg more legal holidays than any other region or city in Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Augsburg was the home of two <a href="/wiki/Patrician_(post-Roman_Europe)" title="Patrician (post-Roman Europe)">patrician</a> families that rose to great prominence internationally, replacing the <a href="/wiki/Medici" class="mw-redirect" title="Medici">Medicis</a> as Europe's leading bankers, the <a href="/wiki/Fugger" title="Fugger">Fugger</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Welser" title="Welser">Welser</a> families.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Augsburg lies at the convergence of the <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alpine</a> rivers <a href="/wiki/Lech_(river)" title="Lech (river)">Lech</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wertach_(river)" title="Wertach (river)">Wertach</a> and on the <a href="/wiki/Singold" title="Singold">Singold</a>. The oldest part of the city and the southern quarters are on the northern foothills of a high terrace, which emerged between the steep rim of the hills of <a href="/wiki/Friedberg_(Bayern)" class="mw-redirect" title="Friedberg (Bayern)">Friedberg</a> in the east and the high hills of the west. In the south extends the Lechfeld, an <a href="/wiki/Outwash_plain" title="Outwash plain">Outwash plain</a> of the post <a href="/wiki/Ice_age" title="Ice age">ice age</a> between the rivers <a href="/wiki/Lech_(river)" title="Lech (river)">Lech</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wertach" title="Wertach">Wertach</a>, where rare primeval landscapes were preserved. The Augsburg city forest and the Lech valley heaths today rank among the most species-rich middle European habitats.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On Augsburg borders the <a href="/wiki/Nature_park" title="Nature park">nature park</a> <a href="/wiki/Augsburg_Western_Woods_Nature_Park" title="Augsburg Western Woods Nature Park">Augsburg Western Woods</a> - a large forestland. The city itself is also heavily greened. As a result, in 1997 Augsburg was the first German city to win the Europe-wide contest <a href="/wiki/Entente_Florale" title="Entente Florale">Entente Florale</a> for Europe's greenest and most livable city.</p>

<p>Augsburg is surrounded by the counties <a href="/wiki/Augsburg_(district)" title="Augsburg (district)">Landkreis Augsburg</a> in the west and <a href="/wiki/Aichach-Friedberg" title="Aichach-Friedberg">Aichach-Friedberg</a> in the east.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Reilly_184.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Reilly_184.jpg/170px-Reilly_184.jpg" width="170" height="287" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Reilly_184.jpg/255px-Reilly_184.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Reilly_184.jpg/340px-Reilly_184.jpg 2x" data-file-width="752" data-file-height="1270" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Reilly_184.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Territories of the Prince-Bishopric and of the Imperial City of Augsburg</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The city was founded in 15&#160;BC by <a href="/wiki/Nero_Claudius_Drusus" title="Nero Claudius Drusus">Drusus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tiberius" title="Tiberius">Tiberius</a> as <i><b>Augusta Vindelicorum</b></i> (<small>Latin pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Latin" title="Help:IPA/Latin">[awˈɡʊsta wɪndɛlɪˈkoːrʊ̃]</a></span> <small><a href="/wiki/Traditional_English_pronunciation_of_Latin" title="Traditional English pronunciation of Latin">English pronunciation of Latin</a>:</small> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/aʊ/: 'ou' in 'mouth'">aʊ</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ɡ/: 'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="/uː/: 'oo' in 'goose'">uː</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'v' in 'vie'">v</span><span title="/ɪ/: 'i' in 'kit'">ɪ</span><span title="'n' in 'nigh'">n</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ɛ/: 'e' in 'dress'">ɛ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ɪ/: 'i' in 'kit'">ɪ</span><span title="/ˌ/: secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/oʊ/: 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span></span>/</a></span></span><sup id="cite_ref-Collins3_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Collins3-4">[4]</a></sup>), on the orders of their stepfather <a href="/wiki/Emperor" title="Emperor">Emperor</a> <a href="/wiki/Augustus" title="Augustus">Augustus</a>. The name means "Augusta of the <a href="/wiki/Vindelicia" class="mw-redirect" title="Vindelicia">Vindelici</a>". This garrison camp soon became the capital of the Roman province of <a href="/wiki/Raetia" title="Raetia">Raetia</a>.</p>
<p>Early development was due to a 400-year affiliation with the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>, especially because of its excellent military, economic and geographic position at the convergence of the <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alpine</a> rivers <a href="/wiki/Lech_(river)" title="Lech (river)">Lech</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wertach_(river)" title="Wertach (river)">Wertach</a>, and with direct access to most important Alpine passes. Thus, Augsburg was the intersection of many important European east-west and north-south connections, which later evolved as major trade routes of the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Around 120&#160;AD Augsburg became the capital of the Roman province <a href="/wiki/Raetia" title="Raetia">Raetia</a>. Augsburg was sacked by the <a href="/wiki/Hun" class="mw-redirect" title="Hun">Huns</a> in the 5th century AD, by <a href="/wiki/Charlemagne" title="Charlemagne">Charlemagne</a> in the 8th century, and by <a href="/wiki/Welf_I,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Welf I, Duke of Bavaria">Welf of Bavaria</a> in the 11th century, but arose each time to greater prosperity.</p>

<p>Augsburg was granted the status of a <a href="/wiki/Free_Imperial_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Free Imperial City">Free Imperial City</a> on March 9, 1276 and from then until 1803, it was independent of its former overlord, the <a href="/wiki/Prince-Bishopric" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince-Bishopric">Prince-Bishop of Augsburg</a>. Frictions between the city-state and the prince-bishops were to remain frequent however, particularly after Augsburg became Protestant and curtailed the rights and freedoms of Catholics.</p>
<p>With a strategic location as intersection of trade routes to Italy, the Free Imperial City became a major trading center. Augsburg produced large quantities of woven goods, cloth and textiles. Augsburg became the base of two banking families that rose to great prominence, the <a href="/wiki/Fugger" title="Fugger">Fuggers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Welser" title="Welser">Welsers</a>. The Fugger family donated the <a href="/wiki/Fuggerei" title="Fuggerei">Fuggerei</a> part of the city devoted to housing for needy citizens in 1516, which remains in use today.</p>

<p>In 1530, the <a href="/wiki/Augsburg_Confession" title="Augsburg Confession">Augsburg Confession</a> was presented to the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Diet_of_Augsburg" title="Diet of Augsburg">Diet of Augsburg</a>. Following the <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Augsburg" title="Peace of Augsburg">Peace of Augsburg</a> in 1555, after which the rights of religious minorities in imperial cities were to be legally protected, a mixed Catholic–Protestant city council presided over a majority Protestant population; <i>see <a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parit%C3%A4tische_Reichsstadt" class="extiw" title="de:Paritätische Reichsstadt">Paritätische Reichsstadt</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span id="Thirty_Years'_War"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Thirty_Years.27_War">Thirty Years' War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Thirty Years' War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Religious peace in the city was largely maintained despite increasing Confessional tensions until the <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years' War">Thirty Years' War</a> (1618–1648). In 1629, Holy Roman Emperor <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Ferdinand II, Holy Roman Emperor">Ferdinand II</a> issued the <a href="/wiki/Edict_of_Restitution" title="Edict of Restitution">Edict of Restitution</a>, which restored the legal situation of 1552 and again curtailed the rights of the Protestant citizens. The inequality of the <a href="/wiki/Edict_of_Restitution" title="Edict of Restitution">Edict of Restitution</a> was rescinded when in April 1632, the Swedish army under <a href="/wiki/Gustavus_Adolphus_of_Sweden" title="Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden">Gustavus Adolphus</a> captured Augsburg without resistance.</p>
<p>In 1634, the Swedish army was routed at nearby <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_N%C3%B6rdlingen_(1634)" title="Battle of Nördlingen (1634)">Nördlingen</a>. By October 1634, Catholic troops had surrounded Augsburg. The Swedish garrison refused to surrender and a siege ensued through the winter of 1634/35 and thousands died from hunger and disease. According to J. N. Hays, "In the period of the Swedish occupation and the Imperial siege the population of the city was reduced from about 70,000 to about 16,000, with <a href="/wiki/Typhus" title="Typhus">typhus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_Death" title="Black Death">plague</a> playing major roles."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Nine_Years'_War"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Nine_Years.27_War">Nine Years' War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Nine Years' War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1686, <a href="/wiki/Leopold_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Leopold I, Holy Roman Emperor">Emperor Leopold I</a> formed the <i>League of Augsburg</i>, termed by the English as the "Grand Alliance" after England joined in 1689: a <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> <a href="/wiki/Coalition" title="Coalition">coalition</a>, consisting (at various times) of <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg" title="Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a>, England, the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Electorate_of_the_Palatinate" class="mw-redirect" title="Electorate of the Palatinate">Palatinate</a> of the Rhine, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Savoy" title="Savoy">Savoy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">United Provinces</a>. It was formed to defend the <a href="/wiki/Electorate_of_the_Palatinate" class="mw-redirect" title="Electorate of the Palatinate">Palatinate</a> from France. This organization fought against France in the <a href="/wiki/Nine_Years_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Nine Years War">Nine Years War</a>.</p>
<p>Augsburg's peak boom years occurred during the 15th and 16th centuries thanks to the bank and metal businesses of the merchant families Fugger and Welser, who held a local near total monopoly on their respective industries. Augsburg's wealth attracted artists seeking patrons and rapidly became a creative centre for famous painters, sculptors and musicians notably birthplace of&#160;: the <a href="/wiki/Hans_Holbein_the_Younger" title="Hans Holbein the Younger">Holbein</a> painter family. In later centuries the city was the birthplace of the composer <a href="/wiki/Leopold_Mozart" title="Leopold Mozart">Leopold Mozart</a><sup id="cite_ref-allmusic_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-7">[7]</a></sup> and the playwright <a href="/wiki/Berthold_Brecht" class="mw-redirect" title="Berthold Brecht">Berthold Brecht</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rococo" title="Rococo">Rococo</a> became so prevalent that it became known as “Augsburg style” throughout Germany.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="End_of_Free_Imperial_City_status_and_Industrial_Revolution_revival">End of Free Imperial City status and Industrial Revolution revival</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: End of Free Imperial City status and Industrial Revolution revival">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In 1806, when the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> was dissolved, Augsburg lost its independence and was annexed to the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bavaria" title="Kingdom of Bavaria">Kingdom of Bavaria</a>. In 1817, the city became an administrative capital of the <i>Oberdonaukreis</i>, then administrative capital in 1837 for the district <a href="/wiki/Swabia_(administrative_region)" class="mw-redirect" title="Swabia (administrative region)">Swabia and Neuburg</a>.</p>
<p>During the end of the 19th century, <a href="/wiki/Augsburg_textile_and_industry_museum" title="Augsburg textile and industry museum">Augsburg's textile industry</a> again rose to prominence followed by the connected machine manufacturing industry.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Military">Military</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Military">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Augsburg was historically a militarily important city due to its strategic location. During the German re-armament before <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">the Second World War</a>, the Wehrmacht enlarged Augsburg's one original Kaserne (barracks) to three: Somme Kaserne (housing Wehrmacht Artillerie-Regiment 27); Arras Kaserne (housing Wehrmacht Infanterie Regiment 27) and Panzerjäger Kaserne (housing Panzerabwehr-Abteilung 27 (later Panzerjäger-Abteilung 27)). Wehrmacht Panzerjäger-Abteilung 27 was later moved to <a href="/wiki/F%C3%BCssen" title="Füssen">Füssen</a>.</p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, one subcamp of the <a href="/wiki/Dachau_concentration_camp" title="Dachau concentration camp">Dachau concentration camp</a> was located outside Augsburg, supplying approximately 1,300 forced labourers to local military-related industry, most especially the <a href="/wiki/Messerschmitt" title="Messerschmitt">Messerschmitt</a> AG military aircraft firm headquartered in Augsburg.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1941, <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Hess" title="Rudolf Hess">Rudolf Hess</a> without <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>'s permission secretly took off from a local airport and flew to <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> to meet the <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Douglas-Hamilton,_14th_Duke_of_Hamilton" title="Douglas Douglas-Hamilton, 14th Duke of Hamilton">Duke of Hamilton</a>, and crashed in <a href="/wiki/Eaglesham" title="Eaglesham">Eaglesham</a> in an attempt to mediate the end of the European front of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and join sides for the upcoming Russian Campaign.</p>
<p>The Reichswehr Infanterie Regiment 19 was stationed in Augsburg and became the base unit for the Wehrmacht Infanterie Regiment 40, a subsection of the Wehrmacht Infanterie Division 27 (which later became the Wehrmacht Panzerdivision 17). Elements of Wehrmacht II Battalion of Gebirgs-Jäger-Regiment 99 (especially Wehrmacht Panzerjäger Kompanie 14) was composed of parts of the Wehrmacht Infanterie Division 27. The Infanterie Regiment 40 remained in Augsburg until the end of the war, finally surrendering to the United States when in 28 April 1945, the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Army" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Army">U.S. Army</a> occupied the <a href="/wiki/Bombing_of_Augsburg_in_World_War_II" title="Bombing of Augsburg in World War II">heavily bombed and damaged city</a>.</p>
<p>Following the war, the three Kaserne would change hands confusingly between the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> and Germans, finally ending up in US hands for the duration of the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>. The former Wehrmacht Kaserne became the three main US barracks in Augsburg: Reese, Sheridan and FLAK. US Base FLAK had been an anti-aircraft barracks since 1936 and US Base Sheridan "united" the former infantry barracks with a smaller Kaserne for former <a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Luftwaffe</a> communications units.</p>
<p>The American military presence in the city started with the U.S. 5th Infantry Division stationed at FLAK Kaserne from 1945 to 1955, then by <a href="/wiki/U.S._11th_Airborne_Division" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. 11th Airborne Division">11th Airborne Division</a>, followed by the <a href="/wiki/24th_Infantry_Division_(United_States)" title="24th Infantry Division (United States)">24th Infantry Division</a>, U.S. Army <a href="/wiki/VII_Corps_(United_States)" title="VII Corps (United States)">VII Corps</a> artillery, <a href="/wiki/701st_Military_Intelligence_Brigade" class="mw-redirect" title="701st Military Intelligence Brigade">USASA Field Station Augsburg</a> and finally the <a href="/wiki/66th_Military_Intelligence_Brigade_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="66th Military Intelligence Brigade (United States)">66th Military Intelligence Brigade</a>, which returned the former Kaserne to German hands in 1998. Originally the Heeresverpflegungshauptamt Südbayern and an Officers' caisson existed on or near the location of Reese-Kaserne, but was demolished by the occupying Americans.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Municipality">Municipality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Municipality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_Augsburg" title="List of mayors of Augsburg">List of mayors of Augsburg</a></div>
<p>From 1266 until 1548, the terms <i>Stadtpfleger</i> (head of town council) and <i><a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">Mayor</a></i> were used interchangeably, or occasionally, simultaneously. In 1548 the title was finally fixed to <i>Stadtpfleger</i>, who officiated for several years and was then awarded the title for life (though no longer governing), thus resulting confusingly, in records of two or more simultaneous <i>Stadtpfleger</i>.</p>
<p>After the transfer to <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a> in 1806, Augsburg was ruled by a <a href="/wiki/Magistrate" title="Magistrate">Magistrate</a> with two mayors, supported by an additional council of "Community Commissioners": the <i>Gemeindebevollmächtige</i>.</p>
<p>As of 1907, the Mayor was entitled <a href="/wiki/Lord_Mayor" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Mayor">Oberbürgermeister</a>, as Augsburg had reached a population of 100,000, as per the Bavarian <a href="/wiki/Gemeindeordnung" title="Gemeindeordnung">Gemeindeordnung</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Members_of_the_Bundestag">Members of the Bundestag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Members of the Bundestag">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Augsburg is located in the <i>Wahlkreis 253 Augsburg-Stadt</i> constituency, which includes <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigsbrunn" title="Königsbrunn">Königsbrunn</a> and the District of Augsburg (<a href="/wiki/Augsburg_(district)" title="Augsburg (district)">Landkreis Augsburg</a>).</p>
<p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volker_Ullrich_(Politiker)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Volker Ullrich (Politiker) (page does not exist)">Volker Ullrich</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Christlich-Soziale_Union_in_Bayern" class="mw-redirect" title="Christlich-Soziale Union in Bayern">CSU</a> was directly elected to the <a href="/wiki/Bundestag" title="Bundestag">Bundestag</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bundestagswahl_2013" class="mw-redirect" title="Bundestagswahl 2013">18th German Bundestag</a>.</p>
<p>Indirectly elected to the Bundestag to adhere to the <a href="/wiki/Party-list_proportional_representation" title="Party-list proportional representation">Landesliste</a> were <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ulrike_Bahr&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ulrike Bahr (page does not exist)">Ulrike Bahr</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Sozialdemokratische_Partei_Deutschlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Sozialdemokratische Partei Deutschlands">SPD</a> and <a href="/wiki/Claudia_Roth" title="Claudia Roth">Claudia Roth</a> for <a href="/wiki/B%C3%BCndnis_90/Die_Gr%C3%BCnen" class="mw-redirect" title="Bündnis 90/Die Grünen">Bündnis 90/Die Grünen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roads">Roads</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Roads">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The main road link is <a href="/wiki/Autobahn" title="Autobahn">autobahn</a> <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_8" title="Bundesautobahn 8">A 8</a> between <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_transport">Public transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Public transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Public transport is very well catered for. It is controlled by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburger_Verkehrsverbund&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Augsburger Verkehrsverbund (page does not exist)">Augsburger Verkehrsverbund</a> (Augsburg transport union, AVV) extended over central Swabia. There are seven rail <a href="/wiki/Regionalbahn" title="Regionalbahn">Regionalbahn</a> lines, five <a href="/wiki/Tram" title="Tram">tram</a> lines, 27 city bus lines and six night bus lines, as well as, several taxi companies.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Trams_in_Augsburg" title="Trams in Augsburg">Augsburg tramway network</a> is now 35.5&#160;km-long after the opening of new lines to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Augsburg" title="University of Augsburg">university</a> in 1996, the northern city boundary in 2001 and to the Klinikum Augsburg (Augsburg hospital) in 2002. Tram line 6, which runs 5.2&#160;km from Friedberg West to Hauptbahnhof (Central Station), opened in December 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail_services">Rail services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Rail services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>Augsburg has seven stations, the <a href="/wiki/Augsburg_Central_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Augsburg Central Station">Central Station</a> (<i>Hauptbahnhof</i>), <a href="/wiki/Augsburg-Hochzoll_station" title="Augsburg-Hochzoll station">Hochzoll</a>, <a href="/wiki/Augsburg-Oberhausen_station" title="Augsburg-Oberhausen station">Oberhausen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Augsburg_Haunstetterstra%C3%9Fe_station" title="Augsburg Haunstetterstraße station">Haunstetterstraße</a>, <a href="/wiki/Augsburg_Morellstra%C3%9Fe_station" title="Augsburg Morellstraße station">Morellstraße</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg_Messe_station&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Augsburg Messe station (page does not exist)">Messe</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg-Inningen_station&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Augsburg-Inningen station (page does not exist)">Inningen</a>. The Central Station, built from 1843 to 1846, is Germany’s oldest main station in a large city still providing services in the original building. It is currently being modernized and an underground tram station is built underneath it. <a href="/wiki/Augsburg_Hauptbahnhof" title="Augsburg Hauptbahnhof">Hauptbahnhof</a> is on the <a href="/wiki/Munich%E2%80%93Augsburg_railway" title="Munich–Augsburg railway">Munich–Augsburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ulm%E2%80%93Augsburg_railway" title="Ulm–Augsburg railway">Ulm–Augsburg lines</a> and is connected by <a href="/wiki/InterCityExpress" class="mw-redirect" title="InterCityExpress">ICE</a> and <a href="/wiki/InterCity" title="InterCity">IC</a> services to <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dortmund" title="Dortmund">Dortmund</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a>. As of December 2007, the French <a href="/wiki/TGV" title="TGV">TGV</a> connected Augsburg with a direct High Speed Connection to <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. In addition <a href="/wiki/EuroCity" title="EuroCity">EC</a> and night train services connect to <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> and connections will be substantially improved by the creation of the planned <a href="/wiki/Magistrale_for_Europe" class="mw-redirect" title="Magistrale for Europe">Magistrale for Europe</a>.</p>
<p>The AVV operates seven <a href="/wiki/Regionalbahn" title="Regionalbahn">Regionalbahn</a> lines from the main station to:</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Mammendorf" title="Mammendorf">Mammendorf</a></li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air_transport">Air transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Air transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Until 2005 Augsburg was served by nearby <a href="/wiki/Augsburg_Airport" title="Augsburg Airport">Augsburg Airport</a> (AGB). In that year all air passenger transport was relocated to <a href="/wiki/Munich_Airport" title="Munich Airport">Munich Airport</a>. Since then, the airport is used almost entirely for <a href="/wiki/General_aviation" title="General aviation">general aviation</a> and business airplanes.<sup id="cite_ref-flughafen-augsburg_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flughafen-augsburg-17">[17]</a></sup> Passenger flights resumed in 2016 with a once-per-week flight to Salzburg.</p>

<p>Augsburg is a vibrant industrial city. Many global market leaders namely <a href="/wiki/MAN_SE" title="MAN SE">MAN</a>, <a href="/wiki/EADS" class="mw-redirect" title="EADS">EADS</a> or <a href="/wiki/KUKA" title="KUKA">KUKA</a> produce high technology products like printing systems, large diesel engines, <a href="/wiki/Industrial_robots" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial robots">industrial robots</a> or components for the <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A380" title="Airbus A380">Airbus A380</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ariane_(rocket_family)" title="Ariane (rocket family)">Ariane</a> carrier rocket. After Munich, Augsburg is considered the high-tech centre for Information and Communication in Bavaria and takes advantage of its lower operating costs, yet close proximity to <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> and potential customers.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/FC_Augsburg" title="FC Augsburg">FC Augsburg</a> is a football team based in Augsburg and plays in the <a href="/wiki/WWK_ARENA" class="mw-redirect" title="WWK ARENA">WWK ARENA</a>. FC Augsburg was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Fu%C3%9Fball-Bundesliga" class="mw-redirect" title="Fußball-Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> in 2011. The new stadium (opened in July 2009) also hosted games of the <a href="/wiki/2011_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="2011 FIFA Women's World Cup">2011 FIFA Women's World Cup</a>.</p>
<p>The city is home to a <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Eishockey_Liga" title="Deutsche Eishockey Liga">DEL</a> (first-division) ice hockey team, the <a href="/wiki/Augsburger_Panther" title="Augsburger Panther">Augsburger Panther</a>. The original club, AEV, was formed in 1878, the oldest German ice sport club and regularly draws around 4000 spectators, quite reasonable for German ice hockey. Home games are played at the <a href="/wiki/Curt_Frenzel_Stadion" class="mw-redirect" title="Curt Frenzel Stadion">Curt Frenzel Stadion</a>: a recently rebuilt (2012–2013) indoor rink and modern stadium. Also Augsburg is home to one of the most traditional German Baseball clubs, the Augsburg Gators.</p>
<p>For the <a href="/wiki/1972_Summer_Olympics" title="1972 Summer Olympics">1972 Olympic Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Lech_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Lech River">Lech River</a> dam protective diversionary canal for river ice was converted into the world's first <a href="/wiki/Artificial_whitewater" title="Artificial whitewater">artificial whitewater</a> slalom course: the <a href="/wiki/Eiskanal" class="mw-redirect" title="Eiskanal">Eiskanal</a> and remains a world-class venue for whitewater competition and served as prototype for two dozen similar foreign courses.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_city_nicknames">Local city nicknames</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Local city nicknames">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While commonly called <i>Fuggerstadt</i> (Fuggers' city) due to the <a href="/wiki/Fuggers" class="mw-redirect" title="Fuggers">Fuggers</a> residing there, within Swabia it is also often referred to as <i>Datschiburg</i>: which originated sometime in the 19th century refers to Augsburg's favorite sweet: the <i>Datschi</i> made from fruit, preferably prunes, and thin cake dough.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The <i>Datschiburger Kickers</i> charity football team (founded in 1965) reflects this in its choice of team name.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Among younger people, the city is commonly called "Aux" for short.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
