<p>The <b>Siege of Nuremberg</b> or <b>Siege of NÃ¼rnberg</b> was a battle campaign that took place in 1632 about the Imperial City of <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War" title="Thirty Years' War">Thirty Years' War</a>.</p>
<p>In 1632, rather than face the numerically superior <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Imperial</a> army under the command of <a href="/wiki/Albrecht_von_Wallenstein" title="Albrecht von Wallenstein">Albrecht von Wallenstein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gustavus_Adolphus_of_Sweden" title="Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden">Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden</a> ordered a tactical retreat into the city of <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>. Wallenstein's army immediately started to <a href="/wiki/Investment_(military)" title="Investment (military)">invest</a> Nuremberg and laid <a href="/wiki/Siege" title="Siege">siege</a> to the city, waiting for hunger and <a href="/wiki/Epidemic" title="Epidemic">epidemics</a> to cripple the Swedish force.</p>
<p>It proved difficult for the besiegers to maintain the siege because the city was large and needed a large force to man the <a href="/wiki/Circumvallation" class="mw-redirect" title="Circumvallation">circumvallation</a>. In Wallenstein's camp, there were 120,000 soldiers, 50,000 horses, 15,000 women, and 15,000 servants. Foraging to supply such a large static besieging force proved to be extremely difficult.</p>
<p>Four <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxon</a> regiments and troops from the Rhine numbering around 50,000 troops with 6,000 cannon reinforced the 70,000 regular troops and 30,000 Nuremberg militia already deployed by Gustavus Adolphus.</p>
<p>With poor sanitation and inadequate supplies, both sides suffered from hunger, <a href="/wiki/Typhus" title="Typhus">typhus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scurvy" title="Scurvy">scurvy</a>. To try to break the deadlock, the forces under the command of Gustavus Adolphus attacked the entrenchments of the imperial army's circumvallation in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Alte_Veste" title="Battle of the Alte Veste">Battle of the Alte Veste</a> but failed to break through. Eventually, the siege ended after eleven weeks when the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Empire" title="Swedish Empire">Swedes</a> and their allies withdrew. Through a combination of disease, hunger and battle fatalities, about 10,000 inhabitants of Nuremberg and 20,000 Swedish and allied forces died. The Imperial army suffered about 20,000 dead. <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> was saved for the short term, but the engagement was not decisive.</p>
