<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:#00008B;"><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Technology_Portal"><big>The Technology Portal</big></span></h2>

<div style="border: 1px solid #0088F9; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #F7FAFF;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:right;margin-left:1em">
<center><a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">Astronaut</a></center>
<p><a href="/wiki/File:Astronaut-EVA.jpg" class="image" title="Astronaut"><img alt="Astronaut" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Astronaut-EVA.jpg/175px-Astronaut-EVA.jpg" width="175" height="175" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Astronaut-EVA.jpg/263px-Astronaut-EVA.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Astronaut-EVA.jpg/350px-Astronaut-EVA.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="3072" /></a><br /></p>

<p><br />
<b><a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">Technology</a></b> is the practical use of science, including the making, modification or improvement, applied activity or behavior, use and knowledge of <a href="/wiki/Tool" title="Tool">tools</a>, <a href="/wiki/Machine" title="Machine">machines</a>, techniques, <a href="/wiki/Craft" title="Craft">crafts</a>, <a href="/wiki/System" title="System">systems</a>, methods of organization, or environmental modifications or arrangement in order to solve a problem, improve a preexisting solution to a problem, achieve a goal or perform a specific function. It can also refer to the collection of such tools, machinery, modifications, environmental arrangement and procedures. Technologies significantly affect human as well as other animal species' ability to control and adapt to their natural environments. The word <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/technology" class="extiw" title="wikt:technology">technology</a></i> comes from <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> <i><span lang="gre" xml:lang="gre"><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%84%CE%B5%CF%87%CE%BD%CE%BF%CE%BB%CE%BF%CE%B3%CE%AF%CE%B1" class="extiw" title="wikt:τεχνολογία">τεχνολογία</a> (technología)</span></i>; from <i><span lang="" xml:lang=""><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%84%CE%AD%CF%87%CE%BD%CE%B7" class="extiw" title="wikt:τέχνη">τέχνη</a> (téchnē)</span></i>, meaning 'art, skill, craft', and <i><span lang="" xml:lang=""><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%BB%CE%BF%CE%B3%CE%AF%CE%B1" class="extiw" title="wikt:λογία">-λογία</a> (<a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/logia" class="extiw" title="wikt:logia">-logía</a>)</span></i>, meaning 'study of-'. The term can be applied either generally or to many specific areas, examples of which include <i>construction technology</i>, <i>medical technology</i> and <i>information technology</i>.</p>
<p>The human species' use of technology began with the conversion of natural resources into simple tools. The <a href="/wiki/Prehistory" title="Prehistory">prehistorical</a> discovery of the ability to control <a href="/wiki/Fire" title="Fire">fire</a> increased the available sources of food and the invention of the <a href="/wiki/Wheel" title="Wheel">wheel</a> helped humans in travelling in and controlling their environment. Recent technological developments, including the <a href="/wiki/Printing_press" title="Printing press">printing press</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Telephone" title="Telephone">telephone</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>, have lessened physical barriers to <a href="/wiki/Communication" title="Communication">communication</a> and allowed humans to interact freely on a global scale. However, not all technology has been used for peaceful purposes; the development of <a href="/wiki/Weapon" title="Weapon">weapons</a> of ever-increasing destructive power has progressed throughout history, from <a href="/wiki/Club_(weapon)" title="Club (weapon)">clubs</a> to <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapon" title="Nuclear weapon">nuclear weapons</a>.</p>
<p>Technology has affected <a href="/wiki/Society" title="Society">society</a> and its surroundings in a number of ways. In many societies, technology has helped develop more advanced <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economies</a> (including today's <a href="/wiki/Economic_globalization" title="Economic globalization">global economy</a>) and has allowed the rise of a <a href="/wiki/Leisure" title="Leisure">leisure</a> <a href="/wiki/Social_class" title="Social class">class</a>. Many technological processes produce unwanted by-products, known as <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a>, and deplete natural resources, to the detriment of the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> and its <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environment</a>. Various implementations of technology influence the <a href="/wiki/Value_(personal_and_cultural)" class="mw-redirect" title="Value (personal and cultural)">values</a> of a society and new technology often raises new ethical questions. Examples include the rise of the notion of <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">efficiency</a> in terms of human productivity, a term originally applied only to machines, and the challenge of traditional norms.</p>
<p>Philosophical debates have arisen over the present and future use of technology in society, with disagreements over whether technology improves the <a href="/wiki/Human_condition" title="Human condition">human condition</a> or worsens it. <a href="/wiki/Neo-Luddism" title="Neo-Luddism">Neo-Luddism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anarcho-primitivism" title="Anarcho-primitivism">anarcho-primitivism</a>, and similar movements criticise the pervasiveness of technology in the modern world, opining that it harms the environment and alienates people; proponents of ideologies such as <a href="/wiki/Transhumanism" title="Transhumanism">transhumanism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Techno-progressivism" title="Techno-progressivism">techno-progressivism</a> view continued technological progress as beneficial to society and the human condition. Indeed, until recently, it was believed that the development of technology was restricted only to human beings, but recent scientific studies indicate that other <a href="/wiki/Primates" class="mw-redirect" title="Primates">primates</a> and certain <a href="/wiki/Dolphin" title="Dolphin">dolphin</a> communities have developed simple tools and learned to pass their knowledge to other generations.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:#00008B;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_article">Selected article</span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #0088F9; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #F7FAFF;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:left;margin-right:1em"><a href="/wiki/File:Trinity_shot_color.jpg" class="image" title="Manhattan Project"><img alt="Manhattan Project" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Trinity_shot_color.jpg/104px-Trinity_shot_color.jpg" width="104" height="120" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Trinity_shot_color.jpg/156px-Trinity_shot_color.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Trinity_shot_color.jpg/209px-Trinity_shot_color.jpg 2x" data-file-width="987" data-file-height="1135" /></a></div>
The <b><a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Project" title="Manhattan Project">Manhattan Project</a></b> was a research and development program by the United States with the United Kingdom and Canada that produced the first <a href="/wiki/Atomic_bomb" class="mw-redirect" title="Atomic bomb">atomic bomb</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. From 1942 to 1946, the project was under the direction of <a href="/wiki/Major_General_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Major General (United States)">Major General</a> <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Groves" title="Leslie Groves">Leslie Groves</a> of the <a href="/wiki/US_Army_Corps_of_Engineers" class="mw-redirect" title="US Army Corps of Engineers">US Army Corps of Engineers</a>. The Army component of the project was designated the <b>Manhattan District</b>; "Manhattan" gradually superseded the official codename, "<b>Development of Substitute Materials</b>", for the entire project. Along the way, the Manhattan Project absorbed its earlier British counterpart, <a href="/wiki/Tube_Alloys" title="Tube Alloys">Tube Alloys</a>. Reactors were constructed at <a href="/wiki/Hanford_Site" title="Hanford Site">Hanford, Washington</a>, in which <b>uranium</b> was irradiated and <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_transmutation" title="Nuclear transmutation">transmuted</a> into plutonium. The plutonium was then chemically separated from the uranium. The gun-type design proved impractical to use with plutonium so a more complex implosion-type weapon was developed in a concerted design and construction effort at the project's weapons research and design laboratory in <a href="/wiki/Los_Alamos_National_Laboratory" title="Los Alamos National Laboratory">Los Alamos, New Mexico</a>. The first nuclear device ever detonated was an implosion-type bomb at the <a href="/wiki/Trinity_test" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity test">Trinity test</a>, conducted at New Mexico's <a href="/wiki/White_Sands_Missile_Range" title="White Sands Missile Range">Alamogordo Bombing and Gunnery Range</a> on 16 July 1945. <a href="/wiki/Little_Boy" title="Little Boy">Little Boy</a>, a gun-type weapon, and the implosion-type <a href="/wiki/Fat_Man" title="Fat Man">Fat Man</a> were used in the <a href="/wiki/Atomic_bombings_of_Hiroshima_and_Nagasaki" title="Atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki">atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki</a>, respectively.

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:#00008B;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Featured_biography">Featured biography</span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #0088F9; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #F7FAFF;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.2em"><a href="/wiki/File:I.M._Pei.JPG" class="image" title="I. M. Pei"><img alt="I. M. Pei" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/I.M._Pei.JPG/120px-I.M._Pei.JPG" width="120" height="136" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/I.M._Pei.JPG/180px-I.M._Pei.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/I.M._Pei.JPG/240px-I.M._Pei.JPG 2x" data-file-width="324" data-file-height="366" /></a></div>
<b><a href="/wiki/I._M._Pei" title="I. M. Pei">I. M. Pei</a></b> (born 1917) is a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Chinese American">Chinese American</a> architect, often called a master of <a href="/wiki/Modern_architecture" title="Modern architecture">modern architecture</a>. Born in <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a>, in 1935 he moved to the United States. While enrolled at <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a>, he became unhappy with the school's focus on <a href="/wiki/Beaux-Arts_architecture" title="Beaux-Arts architecture">Beaux-Arts architecture</a>, and spent his free time researching the emerging architects, especially <a href="/wiki/Le_Corbusier" title="Le Corbusier">Le Corbusier</a>. After graduating, he joined the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design" title="Harvard Graduate School of Design">Harvard Graduate School of Design</a> and formed a friendship with the <a href="/wiki/Bauhaus" title="Bauhaus">Bauhaus</a> architects <a href="/wiki/Walter_Gropius" title="Walter Gropius">Walter Gropius</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Breuer" title="Marcel Breuer">Marcel Breuer</a>. Pei spent ten years working with New York real estate magnate <a href="/wiki/William_Zeckendorf" title="William Zeckendorf">William Zeckendorf</a> before establishing his own independent design firm that eventually became <a href="/wiki/Pei_Cobb_Freed_%26_Partners" title="Pei Cobb Freed &amp; Partners">Pei Cobb Freed &amp;&#160;Partners</a>. Among the early projects on which Pei took the lead were the <a href="/wiki/L%27Enfant_Plaza_Hotel" title="L'Enfant Plaza Hotel">L'Enfant Plaza Hotel</a> in Washington, DC, and the <a href="/wiki/Green_Building_(MIT)" title="Green Building (MIT)">Green Building</a> at MIT. His first major recognition came with the <a href="/wiki/National_Center_for_Atmospheric_Research" title="National Center for Atmospheric Research">National Center for Atmospheric Research</a> in Colorado; his new stature led to his selection as chief architect for the <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Library" class="mw-redirect" title="John F. Kennedy Library">John F. Kennedy Library</a> in Massachusetts. He went on to design <a href="/wiki/Dallas_City_Hall" title="Dallas City Hall">Dallas City Hall</a> and the East Building of the <a href="/wiki/National_Gallery_of_Art" title="National Gallery of Art">National Gallery of Art</a>. In the early 1980s, Pei was the focus of controversy when he designed <a href="/wiki/Louvre_Pyramid" title="Louvre Pyramid">a glass-and-steel pyramid</a> for the <i><a href="/wiki/Louvre" title="Louvre">Louvre</a></i> museum in Paris. Pei has won a wide variety of prizes and awards in the field of architecture, including the 1983 <a href="/wiki/Pritzker_Prize" class="mw-redirect" title="Pritzker Prize">Pritzker Prize</a>, sometimes called the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prize</a> of architecture.
