<p><b>Johann Christoph Volkamer</b> (June 7, 1644 – August 26, 1720) was a <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a> merchant, manufacturer and <a href="/wiki/Botanist" class="mw-redirect" title="Botanist">botanist</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Johann_Christoph_Volkamer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Johann Christoph Volkamer (also: <i>Volcamer</i>, <i>Volckamer</i>, <i>Volkammer</i>, <i>Volcameris</i>) was the son of the physician <a href="/w/index.php?title=Johann_George_Volkamer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Johann George Volkamer (page does not exist)">Johann George Volkamer</a>. He was occupied by hobby with the botany and maintained a garden in the today's <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nurembergian</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gostenhof&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gostenhof (page does not exist)">Gostenhof</a> district. He published 1708-1714 a two volume work over <a href="/wiki/Citrus" title="Citrus">citrus</a> under the title "<i>Nurenbergische Hesperides, the thorough description of the noble <a href="/wiki/Citron" title="Citron">Citron</a>, used in within and neighbouring area, praised <a href="/wiki/Lemon" title="Lemon">Lemon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Orange_(fruit)" title="Orange (fruit)">Orange</a> fruits, like such, be received and carried away, velvet of a detailed dicription of most sorts, which some were brought to Nuremberg actually grown, others by different strange places there...</i> "</p>
<p>In Volkamers time one saw the "<i>Goldenen of apples in the citrus fruits <a href="/wiki/Hesperides" title="Hesperides">Hesperides</a></i>" the <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">Greek mythology</a>. Starting from late 17th Century developed a collecting passion for instance at the aristocracy, with which each tried to exceed the other one with the rarest and monsterious fruits.</p>
<p>With this work the term became "<i>Hesperides</i>" to the symbol for the flowering Nurembergian garden culture, those for instance lasted from 1650 on, up to the end 18th Century.</p>
<p>In Volkamers book he illustrated also Nurembergian landscapes, city opinions and above all gardens of the city, become in beside the Citrus plants and their fruits illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Engraving" title="Engraving">engraving</a>.</p>
<p>He is said to be influenced from the work of <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Baptista_Ferrari" title="Giovanni Baptista Ferrari">Giovanni Baptista Ferrari</a>.</p>
