<p><b>Friedrich-Alexander University Erlangen-Nürnberg</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg</i></span>, <b>FAU</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Public_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Public University">public</a> research university in the cities of <a href="/wiki/Erlangen" title="Erlangen">Erlangen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. The name <b>Friedrich-Alexander</b> comes from the university's first founder <a href="/wiki/Frederick,_Margrave_of_Brandenburg-Bayreuth" title="Frederick, Margrave of Brandenburg-Bayreuth">Friedrich, Margrave of Brandenburg-Bayreuth</a>, and its benefactor <a href="/wiki/Christian_Frederick_Charles_Alexander,_Margrave_of_Brandenburg-Ansbach" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian Frederick Charles Alexander, Margrave of Brandenburg-Ansbach">Christian Frederick Charles Alexander, Margrave of Brandenburg-Ansbach</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fau_his_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fau_his-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>FAU is the second largest state university in the state <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>. It has 5 faculties, 23 departments/schools, 30 clinical departments, 19 autonomous departments, 656 professors, 3,404 members of academic staff and roughly 13,000 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In winter semester 2014/15 around 39,085 students (including 3,556 foreign students) enrolled in the university in 239 fields of study, with about 2/3 studying at the Erlangen campus and the remaining 1/3 at the Nuremberg campus. These statistics put FAU in the list of top 10 largest universities in Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-fau-data_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fau-data-8">[8]</a></sup>In 2013, 5251 students graduated from the university and 663 doctorates and 50 post-doctoral theses were registered. Moreover, FAU received 171 million Euro (2013) external funding in the same year, making it one of the strongest third-party funded universities in Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-fau-data_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fau-data-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006 and 2007, as part of the national excellence initiative, FAU was chosen by the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Forschungsgemeinschaft" title="Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft">German Research Foundation</a> as one of the winners in the <a href="/wiki/German_Universities_Excellence_Initiative" title="German Universities Excellence Initiative">German Universities Excellence Initiative</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> FAU is also a member of DFG (<a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Forschungsgemeinschaft" title="Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft">Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Top_Industrial_Managers_for_Europe" title="Top Industrial Managers for Europe">Top Industrial Managers for Europe</a><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> network.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Academic_Ranking_of_World_Universities" title="Academic Ranking of World Universities">Academic Ranking of World Universities</a> for year 2014, FAU ranked second among German universities in Engineering/Technology and Computer Sciences group for all four ranking parameters <i>TOP</i>, <i>FUN</i>, <i>HiCi</i> and <i>PUB</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-fau-ranking3_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fau-ranking3-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fau-ranking2_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fau-ranking2-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fau-ranking1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fau-ranking1-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-shanghairanking.com_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shanghairanking.com-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Friedrich_von_Brandenburg-Bayreuth.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Friedrich_von_Brandenburg-Bayreuth.jpg/220px-Friedrich_von_Brandenburg-Bayreuth.jpg" width="220" height="270" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Friedrich_von_Brandenburg-Bayreuth.jpg/330px-Friedrich_von_Brandenburg-Bayreuth.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/70/Friedrich_von_Brandenburg-Bayreuth.jpg 2x" data-file-width="412" data-file-height="506" /></a>

<p>The university was founded in 1742, in <a href="/wiki/Bayreuth" title="Bayreuth">Bayreuth</a> by <a href="/wiki/Frederick,_Margrave_of_Brandenburg-Bayreuth" title="Frederick, Margrave of Brandenburg-Bayreuth">Frederick, Margrave of Brandenburg-Bayreuth</a>, and moved to Erlangen in 1743. <a href="/wiki/Christian_Frederick_Charles_Alexander,_Margrave_of_Brandenburg-Ansbach" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian Frederick Charles Alexander, Margrave of Brandenburg-Ansbach">Christian Frederick Charles Alexander, Margrave of Brandenburg-Ansbach</a> (one of the two namesakes of the institution) provided significant support to the early university. From the beginning, the university was a <a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> institution, but over time it slowly secularized. During the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi era</a>, the university was one of the first that had a majority of <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Party" title="Nazi Party">Nazi supporters</a> in the student council. In 1961, the business college in Nuremberg was merged with the university in Erlangen, so now the combined institution has a physical presence in the two cities. An engineering school was inaugurated in 1966. In 1972, the school of education (<a href="/wiki/Normal_school" title="Normal school">normal school</a>) in Nuremberg became part of the university.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline">Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Timeline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Below is a short timeline of FAU from its inception to its present form:</p>
<ul>
<li>1700–1704: The Schloss of the Margraves at Erlangen is built.</li>
<li>1743: Friedrich, Margrave of Brandenburg-Bayreuth, issues an edict whereby the university recently founded in Bayreuth is transferred to Erlangen. It has the four faculties of Protestant Theology, Jurisprudence, Medicine and Philosophy.</li>
<li>1769: The University at Erlangen is given the new name of <i>Friedrich-Alexander-Universität</i> in honour of Alexander, Margrave of Ansbach and Bayreuth.</li>
<li>1818: The library of the University of Altdorf, dissolved in 1809, is moved to Erlangen.</li>
<li>1824: The first hospital is built.</li>
<li>1825: The university moves into the Schloss.</li>
<li>1927: Science is taken out of the Faculty of Arts thus creating the new Faculty of Science.</li>
<li>1961: The FAU acquires a further faculty through merger with the Nuremberg College of Economics and Social Sciences (founded in 1919). The university's name is now <i>Friedrich-Alexander Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg</i>.</li>
<li>1966: The Faculty of Engineering is established. (FAU is thus the first of the traditional universities of the old federal republic to incorporate engineering as an independent faculty.)</li>
<li>1972: The Teacher Training College in Nuremberg is incorporated into the Faculty of Education.</li>
<li>1993: The FAU celebrates its 250th anniversary.</li>
<li>1994: The Free State of Bavaria purchases for the university 4.4 hectares of land in Erlangen previously owned by the US military. The area is now called <i>Röthelheim Campus</i>.</li>
<li>1995: The WiSo Faculty (Business Administration, Economics &amp; Social Sciences) celebrates its 75th anniversary.</li>
<li>2000: The Bavaria-California Technology Centre opens its headquarters at the University of Erlangen-Nürnberg.</li>
<li>2000: Inauguration of the Research Centre in Clinical Molecular Biology in Erlangen.</li>
<li>2001: Opening of the <i>Röthelheim Campus</i> on the site of the old artillery barracks.</li>
<li>2004: Inauguration of the new building at the WiSo Faculty of Business Administration, Economics &amp; Social Sciences in Nuremberg.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campuses">Campuses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Campuses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="FAU_Busan_campus">FAU Busan campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: FAU Busan campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>FAU is the first German university to establish a branch campus in <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Korea">Republic of Korea</a>. In November 2009, its campus project received approval from the Korean Ministry of Education, Science and Technology. The FAU Busan Branch Campus offers a Graduate School with a master's degree program in Chemical and Bioengineering and a research center.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, the university announced its intention of working toward making the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Free_Economic_Zone" title="Korean Free Economic Zone">Busan-Jinhae Free Economic Zone</a> an educational hub. To this end, FAU Busan works internationally with various companies and universities.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<p>The University Library Erlangen-Nürnberg is the library system of the Friedrich Alexander University and is a regional library for the region of Middle Franconia. As an academic universal library, it offers its users a wide range of specialist literature from all faculties and a variety of services. With approximately 5.4 million volumes, it is Bavaria's largest library outside the state capital Munich. Large parts of the media stock are also accessible in interregional lending. The University Library is a member of the Bibliotheksverbund Bayern (Bavarian library union).</p>

<p>In February 2007, the senate of the university decided upon a restructuring into five faculties.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Since October 2007, the FAU consists of:<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Faculty of <a href="/wiki/Humanities" title="Humanities">Humanities</a>, <a href="/wiki/Social_science" title="Social science">Social Sciences</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Theology" title="Theology">Theology</a></li>
<li>Faculty of <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">Business</a>, <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">Economics</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">Law</a></li>
<li>Faculty of <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">Medicine</a></li>
<li>Faculty of <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">Sciences</a></li>
<li>Faculty of <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">Engineering</a></li>
</ul>
<p>The following faculties were part of the university (sorted in the order in which they were founded):</p>
<ul>
<li>Theological faculty</li>
<li>Law faculty</li>
<li>Medical faculty</li>
<li>Philosophical faculty I (<a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a>, <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">history</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Social_sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Social sciences">social sciences</a>)</li>

<li>Science faculty II (<a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemistry</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceutics" title="Pharmaceutics">pharmaceutics</a>)</li>
<li>Science faculty III (<a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geology</a>/<a href="/wiki/Mineralogy" title="Mineralogy">mineralogy</a>/<a href="/wiki/Paleontology" title="Paleontology">paleontology</a>)</li>
<li><a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wirtschafts-_und_Sozialwissenschaftliche_Fakult%C3%A4t_der_Friedrich-Alexander-Universit%C3%A4t_Erlangen-N%C3%BCrnberg" class="extiw" title="de:Wirtschafts- und Sozialwissenschaftliche Fakultät der Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg">Business- and social sciences faculty (1961) in Nuremberg</a></li>

<li>Pedagogical faculty (1972) in Nuremberg</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Faculty_of_Engineering">Faculty of Engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Faculty of Engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Inception">Inception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Inception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 1962, after lengthy debate, the <a href="/wiki/Landtag_of_Bavaria" title="Landtag of Bavaria">Bavarian parliament</a> decided to establish a Faculty of Engineering in Erlangen. Then, the University of Erlangen thus won out against the city of Nuremberg, which, for decades, had been demanding the establishment of a college of engineering in Nuremberg. Since the expansive areas of building land required for this project were not available in the center of Erlangen, the foundations for a new university campus were laid in the south east of the town in 1964. The formal establishment of the Faculty of Engineering, then the seventh faculty at the University, took place in 1966. What was unique at the time was that the various engineering departments were subsumed, as a faculty, into the main university rather than constituting an independent university.<sup id="cite_ref-fau_his_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fau_his-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Present_status">Present status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Present status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Faculty of Engineering at FAU is a young educational and research institution. Since its foundation in 1966. The Faculty has five departments:</p>
<ul>
<li>Electrical, Electronic and Communication Engineering</li>
<li>Chemical and Biological Engineering</li>
<li>Materials Science and Engineering (This Department was ranked at 10th best in the world according to the Quantitative Ranking of Engineering Disciplines (QRED).)<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></li>
<li>Mechanical Engineering</li>
<li>Computer Science</li>
</ul>
<p>The Faculty has close connections both with other natural sciences and with traditional subjects at the university. The Faculty of Engineering currently concentrates on the following research fields:</p>
<ul>
<li>New Materials and Processes</li>
<li>Life Science Engineering and Medicine Technology</li>
<li>Energy Technology and Mobility</li>
<li>Modeling and Simulation</li>
<li>Optics and Optical Technologies</li>
<li>Information- and Communication Technologies</li>
<li>Micro-/Nano-electronics</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Excellence_initiative">Excellence initiative</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Excellence initiative">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The Excellence Initiative by the German federal and state governments to promote science and research at German universities aims to promote cutting-edge research and to strengthen the higher education and research in Germany to improve its international competitiveness and to make top performers in academia and science visible. As part of this initiative, FAU was awarded the contract for the Erlangen Graduate School in Advanced Optical Technologies (SAOT), which received 1.9 million euros of annual funding for the next five years. The Cluster of Excellence 'Engineering of Advanced Materials and Processes' (EAM) was also established at FAU as part of the initiative and has been approved in the second round. EAM is funded with 40 million euros.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span id="Cluster_of_Excellence_'Engineering_of_Advanced_Materials"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Cluster_of_Excellence_.27Engineering_of_Advanced_Materials">Cluster of Excellence 'Engineering of Advanced Materials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Cluster of Excellence 'Engineering of Advanced Materials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The Cluster of Excellence 'Engineering of Advanced Materials – Hierarchical Structure Formation for Functional Devices' (EAM) is the only interdisciplinary research collaboration of its type in Germany to focus on the investigation of functional materials and their processing at all length scales. The main research focus is on the fundamental and applied aspects of designing and creating novel high-performance materials. It is part of the Excellence Initiative of the <a href="/wiki/German_Research_Foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="German Research Foundation">German Research Foundation</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Erlangen_Graduate_School_in_Advanced_Optical_Technologies">Erlangen Graduate School in Advanced Optical Technologies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Erlangen Graduate School in Advanced Optical Technologies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The Erlangen Graduate School in Advanced Optical Technologies (SAOT) was founded in 2006. SAOT’s scientific focus lies on optics and optical technology, two fields which are considered key technologies of the 21st century. SAOT is currently funded with seven million euros.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alexander_von_Humboldt_Professorships">Alexander von Humboldt Professorships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Alexander von Humboldt Professorships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, the newly announced professor of physics and co-director of the <a href="/wiki/Max_Planck_Institute_for_the_Science_of_Light" title="Max Planck Institute for the Science of Light">Max Planck Institute for the Science of Light</a>, Prof. Vahid Sandoghdar was awarded an <a href="/wiki/Alexander_von_Humboldt_Foundation" title="Alexander von Humboldt Foundation">Alexander von Humboldt Professorship</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Germany’s highest-endowed international research award, endowed with €3.5 million. In the year 2011, the second in a row, FAU communications engineer and researcher Prof. Dr.-Ing Robert Schober (born 1971) was awarded an <a href="/wiki/Alexander_von_Humboldt_Foundation" title="Alexander von Humboldt Foundation">Alexander von Humboldt Professorship</a>, entailed with €3.5 million,<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> for an algorithm developed by him which is found in many modern phones today. In 2013, Prof. Oskar Painter received an Alexander von Humboldt Professorship as well. Prof. Painter is another new co-director of the <a href="/wiki/Max_Planck_Institute_for_the_Science_of_Light" title="Max Planck Institute for the Science of Light">Max Planck Institute for the Science of Light</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="German_Excellence_Initiative">German Excellence Initiative</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Erlangen-Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: German Excellence Initiative">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The University of Erlangen-Nürnberg was successful within the <a href="/wiki/German_Universities_Excellence_Initiative" title="German Universities Excellence Initiative">German Universities Excellence Initiative</a> in competing for a "cluster of excellence" and a graduate school.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> The Cluster of Excellence 'Engineering of Advanced Materials' (EAM)" focuses on interdisciplinary developing new materials, joining engineering and natural sciences. The Erlangen Graduate School in Advanced Optical Technologies emphasizes a strong focus in <a href="/wiki/Optics" title="Optics">optical</a> and <a href="/wiki/Photonics" title="Photonics">photonics</a> technology in the natural sciences, in engineering and the medical sciences and aims for a concise doctoral education. It is supplemented with a Master's degree program in the same topics.</p>
<p>After an in-depth evaluation, both programs were extended for the third phase of the German Excellence Initiative in 2012 until 2017. They contribute significantly to the research funding of the University, including five new research buildings, permanent new technical facilities and research and teaching staff. They also aim to increase the international perception of the contributing fields of research in Erlangen.</p>
