<p>The <b>Association for Computing Machinery</b> (<b>ACM</b>) is an international <a href="/wiki/Learned_society" title="Learned society">learned society</a> for <a href="/wiki/Computing" title="Computing">computing</a>. It was founded in 1947 and is the world's largest<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> scientific and educational <a href="/wiki/Computing" title="Computing">computing</a> society. It is a not-for-profit professional membership group.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Its membership is more than 100,000 as of 2011. Its headquarters are in New York City.</p>
<p>The ACM is an <a href="/wiki/Umbrella_organization" title="Umbrella organization">umbrella organization</a> for academic and scholarly interests in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>. Its motto is "Advancing Computing as a Science &amp; Profession".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Computing_Machinery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>ACM is organized into over 171 local chapters and 37 <a href="/wiki/Special_Interest_Group" title="Special Interest Group">Special Interest Groups</a> (SIGs), through which it conducts most of its activities. Additionally, there are over 500 college and university chapters. The first student chapter was founded in 1961 at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Louisiana_at_Lafayette" title="University of Louisiana at Lafayette">University of Louisiana at Lafayette</a>.</p>
<p>Many of the SIGs, such as <a href="/wiki/ACM_SIGGRAPH" title="ACM SIGGRAPH">SIGGRAPH</a>, <a href="/wiki/SIGPLAN" title="SIGPLAN">SIGPLAN</a>, <a href="/wiki/SIGCSE" title="SIGCSE">SIGCSE</a> and <a href="/wiki/SIGCOMM" title="SIGCOMM">SIGCOMM</a>, sponsor regular conferences, which have become famous as the dominant venue for presenting innovations in certain fields. The groups also publish a large number of specialized journals, magazines, and newsletters.</p>
<p>ACM also sponsors other computer science related events such as the worldwide <a href="/wiki/ACM_International_Collegiate_Programming_Contest" title="ACM International Collegiate Programming Contest">ACM International Collegiate Programming Contest</a> (ICPC), and has sponsored some other events such as the chess match between <a href="/wiki/Garry_Kasparov" title="Garry Kasparov">Garry Kasparov</a> and the <a href="/wiki/IBM_Deep_Blue" class="mw-redirect" title="IBM Deep Blue">IBM Deep Blue</a> computer.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Computing_Machinery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Computing_Machinery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main category: <a href="/wiki/Category:Association_for_Computing_Machinery_publications" title="Category:Association for Computing Machinery publications">Association for Computing Machinery publications</a></div>
<p>ACM publishes over 50 journals<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> including the prestigious<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_of_the_ACM" title="Journal of the ACM">Journal of the ACM</a></i>, and two general magazines for computer professionals, <i><a href="/wiki/Communications_of_the_ACM" title="Communications of the ACM">Communications of the ACM</a></i> (also known as <i>Communications</i> or <i>CACM</i>) and <i><a href="/wiki/ACM_Queue" title="ACM Queue">Queue</a></i>. Other publications of the ACM include:</p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/XRDS_(magazine)" title="XRDS (magazine)">ACM XRDS</a></i>, formerly "Crossroads", was redesigned in 2010 and is the most popular student computing magazine in the US.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/ACM_Interactions_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="ACM Interactions (magazine)">ACM Interactions</a></i>, an interdisciplinary HCI publication focused on the connections between experiences, people and technology, and the third largest ACM publication.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/ACM_Computing_Surveys" title="ACM Computing Surveys">ACM Computing Surveys</a></i> (CSUR)</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/ACM_Computers_in_Entertainment" title="ACM Computers in Entertainment">ACM Computers in Entertainment</a></i> (CIE)</li>
<li><i>ACM Special Interest Group: Computers and Society</i> (SIGCAS) <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>A number of journals, specific to subfields of computer science, titled <i>ACM Transactions</i>. Some of the more notable transactions include:
<ul>
<li><i>ACM Transactions on Computer Systems</i> (TOCS)</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/IEEE/ACM_Transactions_on_Computational_Biology_and_Bioinformatics" title="IEEE/ACM Transactions on Computational Biology and Bioinformatics">IEEE/ACM Transactions on Computational Biology and Bioinformatics</a></i> (TCBB)</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/ACM_Transactions_on_Computational_Logic" title="ACM Transactions on Computational Logic">ACM Transactions on Computational Logic</a></i> (TOCL)</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/ACM_Transactions_on_Computer-Human_Interaction" title="ACM Transactions on Computer-Human Interaction">ACM Transactions on Computer-Human Interaction</a></i> (TOCHI)</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/ACM_Transactions_on_Database_Systems" title="ACM Transactions on Database Systems">ACM Transactions on Database Systems</a></i> (TODS)</li>

</li>
</ul>
<p>Although <i>Communications</i> no longer publishes primary research, and is not considered a prestigious venue, many of the great debates and results in computing history have been published in its pages.</p>
<p>ACM has made almost all of its publications available to paid <a href="/wiki/Subscriber" class="mw-redirect" title="Subscriber">subscribers</a> online at its <a href="#Digital_Library">Digital Library</a> and also has a <a href="/wiki/ACM_Guide_to_Computing_Literature" title="ACM Guide to Computing Literature">Guide to Computing Literature</a>. Individual members additionally have access to <a href="/wiki/Safari_Books_Online" title="Safari Books Online">Safari Books Online</a> and Books24x7. ACM also offers insurance, online courses, and other services to its members.</p>
<p>In 1997, ACM Press published <i>Wizards and Their Wonders: Portraits in Computing</i> (<a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Book_Number" title="International Standard Book Number">ISBN</a>&#160;<a href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0897919602" title="Special:BookSources/0897919602">0897919602</a>), written by Christopher Morgan, with new photographs by <a href="/wiki/Louis_Fabian_Bachrach,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Louis Fabian Bachrach, Jr.">Louis Fabian Bachrach</a>. The book is a collection of historic and current portrait photographs of figures from the computer industry.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Portal_and_Digital_Library">Portal and Digital Library<span id="Portal"></span><span id="Digital_Library"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Computing_Machinery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Portal and Digital Library">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>ACM Portal</b> is an online service of the ACM.<sup id="cite_ref-portal_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-portal-7">[7]</a></sup> Its core are two main sections: <b>ACM Digital Library</b> and the <b><a href="/wiki/ACM_Guide_to_Computing_Literature" title="ACM Guide to Computing Literature">ACM Guide to Computing Literature</a></b>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The ACM Digital Library is the full-text collection of all articles published by the ACM in its articles, magazines and conference proceedings. The Guide is a bibliography in computing with over one million entries.<sup id="cite_ref-portal_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-portal-7">[7]</a></sup> The ACM Digital Library contains a comprehensive archive starting in the 1950s of the organization's journals, magazines, newsletters and conference proceedings. Online services include a forum called Ubiquity and Tech News digest. There is an extensive underlying bibliographic database containing key works of all genres from all major publishers of computing literature. This secondary database is a rich discovery service known as The ACM Guide to Computing Literature.</p>
<p>ACM adopted a hybrid <a href="/wiki/Open_access" title="Open access">Open Access</a> (OA) publishing model in 2013. Authors who do not choose to pay the OA fee must grant ACM publishing rights by either a copyright transfer agreement or a publishing license agreement.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>ACM was a "green" publisher before the term was invented. Authors may post documents on their own websites and in their institutional repositories with a link back to the ACM Digital Library's permanently maintained Version of Record.</p>
<p>All metadata in the Digital Library is open to the world, including <a href="/wiki/Abstracts" class="mw-redirect" title="Abstracts">abstracts</a>, linked references and citing works, citation and usage statistics, as well as all functionality and services. Other than the free articles, the full-texts are accessed by subscription.</p>
<p>There is also a mounting challenge to the ACM's publication practices coming from the <a href="/wiki/Open_access_(publishing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Open access (publishing)">open access</a> movement. Some authors see a centralized peer–review process as less relevant and publish on their home pages or on unreviewed sites like <a href="/wiki/ArXiv" title="ArXiv">arXiv</a>. Other organizations have sprung up which do their peer review entirely free and online, such as <a href="/wiki/Journal_of_Artificial_Intelligence_Research" title="Journal of Artificial Intelligence Research">Journal of Artificial Intelligence Research (JAIR)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Journal_of_Machine_Learning_Research" title="Journal of Machine Learning Research">Journal of Machine Learning Research (JMLR)</a> and the Journal of Research and Practice in Information Technology.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Membership_grades">Membership grades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Computing_Machinery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Membership grades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to student and regular members, ACM has several advanced membership grades to recognize those with multiple years of membership and "demonstrated performance that sets them apart from their peers".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fellows">Fellows</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Computing_Machinery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fellows">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/ACM_Fellow" title="ACM Fellow">ACM Fellow</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Fellows_of_the_Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="List of Fellows of the Association for Computing Machinery">List of Fellows of the Association for Computing Machinery</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/ACM_Fellow" title="ACM Fellow">ACM Fellows</a> Program was established by Council of the Association for Computing Machinery in 1993 "to recognize and honor outstanding ACM members for their achievements in computer science and information technology and for their significant contributions to the mission of the ACM." There are presently<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (December 2016)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> about 958 Fellows<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> out of about 75,000 professional members.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Distinguished_Members">Distinguished Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Computing_Machinery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Distinguished Members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006 ACM began recognizing two additional membership grades, one which was called Distinguished Members. Distinguished Members (Distinguished Engineers, Distinguished Scientists, and Distinguished Educators) have at least 15 years of professional experience and 5 years of continuous ACM membership and "have made a significant impact on the computing field". Note that in 2006 when the Distinguished Members first came out, one of the three levels was called "Distinguished Member" and was changed about two years later to "Distinguished Educator". Those who already had the Distinguished Member title had their titles changed to one of the other three titles.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior_Members">Senior Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Computing_Machinery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Senior Members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Also in 2006, ACM began recognizing Senior Members. Senior Members have ten or more years of professional experience and 5 years of continuous ACM membership.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chapters">Chapters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Computing_Machinery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Chapters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ACM has three kinds of chapters: <a href="/wiki/Special_Interest_Group" title="Special Interest Group">Special Interest Groups</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Professional Chapters, and Student Chapters.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of 2011, ACM has professional &amp; SIG Chapters in 56 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of 2014, there exist ACM student chapters in 41 different countries.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conferences">Conferences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Computing_Machinery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Conferences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main category: <a href="/wiki/Category:Association_for_Computing_Machinery_conferences" title="Category:Association for Computing Machinery conferences">Association for Computing Machinery conferences</a></div>
<p>ACM and its Special Interest Groups (SIGs) sponsors numerous conferences with 170 hosted worldwide in 2017. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.acm.org/conferences">ACM Conferences page</a> has an up-to-date complete list while a partial list is shown below. Most of the SIGs also have an annual conference. ACM conferences are often very popular publishing venues and are therefore very competitive. For example, the 2007 <a href="/wiki/SIGGRAPH" title="SIGGRAPH">SIGGRAPH</a> conference attracted about 30000 visitors, and CIKM only accepted 15% of the long papers that were submitted in 2005.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership">Leadership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Computing_Machinery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Leadership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote selfreference">See also: <a href="/wiki/Category:Presidents_of_the_Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Category:Presidents of the Association for Computing Machinery">Category:Presidents of the Association for Computing Machinery</a>.</div>
<p>The President of ACM for 2016–2018 is <a href="/wiki/Vicki_L._Hanson" title="Vicki L. Hanson">Vicki L. Hanson</a>, Distinguished Professor in the Department of Information Sciences and Technologies at the <a href="/wiki/Rochester_Institute_of_Technology" title="Rochester Institute of Technology">Rochester Institute of Technology</a> and Professor and Chair of Inclusive Technologies at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Dundee" title="University of Dundee">University of Dundee</a>, UK. She is successor of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_L._Wolf" title="Alexander L. Wolf">Alexander L. Wolf</a> (2014–2016), Dean of the <a href="/wiki/Jack_Baskin_School_of_Engineering" title="Jack Baskin School of Engineering">Jack Baskin School of Engineering</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Santa_Cruz" title="University of California, Santa Cruz">University of California, Santa Cruz</a>; <a href="/wiki/Vint_Cerf" title="Vint Cerf">Vint Cerf</a> (2012–2014), an American computer scientist who is recognized as one of "the fathers of the Internet"; Alain Chesnais (2010–2012), a French citizen living in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto, Ontario</a>, Canada, where he runs his company named Visual Transitions; and <a href="/wiki/Wendy_Hall" title="Wendy Hall">Dame Wendy Hall</a> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southampton" title="University of Southampton">University of Southampton</a>, UK (2008–2010).<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>ACM is led by a Council consisting of the President, Vice-President, Treasurer, Past President, SIG Governing Board Chair, Publications Board Chair, three representatives of the SIG Governing Board, and seven Members–At–Large. This institution is often referred to simply as "Council" in <i>Communications of the ACM</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="ACM_Council_on_Women_in_Computing">ACM Council on Women in Computing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Computing_Machinery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: ACM Council on Women in Computing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/ACM-W" title="ACM-W">ACM-W</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> the ACM council on <a href="/wiki/Women_in_computing" title="Women in computing">women in computing</a>, supports, celebrates, and advocates internationally for the full engagement of women in computing. ACM–W's main programs are regional celebrations of women in computing, ACM-W chapters, and scholarships for women CS students to attend research conferences. In India and Europe these activities are overseen by ACM-W India and ACM-W Europe respectively. ACM-W collaborates with organizations such as the <a href="/wiki/Anita_Borg_Institute" class="mw-redirect" title="Anita Borg Institute">Anita Borg Institute</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Center_for_Women_%26_Information_Technology" title="National Center for Women &amp; Information Technology">National Center for Women &amp; Information Technology (NCWIT)</a>, and <a href="/wiki/CRA-W:_Committee_on_the_Status_of_Women_in_Computing_Research" title="CRA-W: Committee on the Status of Women in Computing Research">Committee on the Status of Women in Computing Research (CRA-W)</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cooperation">Cooperation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Computing_Machinery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Cooperation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ACM's primary partner has been the <a href="/wiki/IEEE_Computer_Society" title="IEEE Computer Society">IEEE Computer Society</a> (IEEE-CS), which is the largest subgroup of the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Electrical_and_Electronics_Engineers" title="Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers">Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers</a> (IEEE). The IEEE focuses more on hardware and standardization issues than theoretical <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>, but there is considerable overlap with ACM's agenda. They have many joint activities including conferences, publications and awards.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> ACM and its SIGs co-sponsor about 20 conferences each year with IEEE-CS and other parts of IEEE.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Eckert-Mauchly_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Eckert-Mauchly Award">Eckert-Mauchly Award</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ken_Kennedy_Award" title="Ken Kennedy Award">Ken Kennedy Award</a>, both major awards in computer science, are given jointly by ACM and the IEEE-CS.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> They occasionally cooperate on projects like developing computing curricula.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>ACM has also jointly sponsored on events with other professional organizations like the <a href="/wiki/Society_for_Industrial_and_Applied_Mathematics" title="Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics">Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics</a> (SIAM).<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
