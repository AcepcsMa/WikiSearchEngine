<p><b>Robert Kurz</b> (24 December 1943 – 18 July 2012) was a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Marxist" class="mw-redirect" title="Marxist">Marxist</a> philosopher, social critic, journalist and editor of the journal <i>Exit!</i>. He was one of Germany's most prominent theorists of <a href="/wiki/Value_criticism" title="Value criticism">value criticism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> His works have yet to be translated into, and published in, <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_work">Life and work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Kurz_(philosopher)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Robert Kurz was born on 24 December 1943 in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> to a German working-class family. Kurz studied <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a>, <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">history</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paedagogy" class="mw-redirect" title="Paedagogy">paedagogy</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> of <a href="/wiki/Erlangen" title="Erlangen">Erlangen</a> without taking a degree. He was a member of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kommunistischer_Arbeiterbund_Deutschland&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kommunistischer Arbeiterbund Deutschland (page does not exist)">Kommunistischer Arbeiterbund Deutschland</a>, KABD (Communist Workers League of Germany) during the 1970s.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Kurz was a co-founder of the magazine <i>Marxistische Kritik</i> (<i>Marxist Critique</i>) in 1986.</p>
