<p><i><b>Communications of the ACM</b></i> is the monthly <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">journal</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a> (ACM). It was established in 1957, with <a href="/wiki/Saul_Rosen" title="Saul Rosen">Saul Rosen</a> its first managing editor. It is sent to all ACM members.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Articles are intended for readers with backgrounds in all areas of <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Information_systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Information systems">information systems</a>. The focus is on the practical implications of advances in information technology and associated management issues; ACM also publishes a variety of more theoretical journals.</p>
<p>The magazine straddles the boundary of a <a href="/wiki/List_of_science_magazines" title="List of science magazines">science magazine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trade_magazine" title="Trade magazine">trade magazine</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">scientific journal</a>. While the content is subject to <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer review</a>, the articles published are often summaries of research that may also be published elsewhere. Material published must be accessible and relevant to a broad readership.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1960 onward, <i>CACM</i> also published algorithms, expressed in <a href="/wiki/ALGOL" title="ALGOL">ALGOL</a>. The collection of algorithms later became known as the Collected Algorithms of the ACM.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
