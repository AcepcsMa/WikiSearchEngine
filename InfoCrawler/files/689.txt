<p><b>Klausen</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈklaʊ̯sn̩]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <span lang="it"><i>Chiusa</i></span> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Italian" title="Help:IPA/Italian">[ˈkjuːza]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Ladin_language" title="Ladin language">Ladin</a>: <i>Tluses</i> or <i>Tlüses</i>) is a <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> (municipality) in <a href="/wiki/South_Tyrol" title="South Tyrol">South Tyrol</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, located about 20 kilometres (12&#160;mi) northeast of the city of <a href="/wiki/Bolzano" title="Bolzano">Bolzano</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Klausen,_South_Tyrol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 30 November 2010, it had a population of 5,144 and an area of 51.4 square kilometres (19.8&#160;sq&#160;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-istat_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-istat-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Klausen borders the following municipalities: <a href="/wiki/Feldthurns" title="Feldthurns">Feldthurns</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lajen" title="Lajen">Lajen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarntal" title="Sarntal">Sarntal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vahrn" title="Vahrn">Vahrn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Villanders" title="Villanders">Villanders</a> and <a href="/wiki/Villn%C3%B6%C3%9F" title="Villnöß">Villnöß</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Frazioni">Frazioni</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Klausen,_South_Tyrol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Frazioni">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The municipality of Klausen contains the <i><a href="/wiki/Frazione" title="Frazione">frazioni</a></i> (subdivisions, mainly villages and hamlets) Gufidaun (Gudon), Latzfons (Lazfons), and Verdings (Verdignes).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Klausen,_South_Tyrol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat-of-arms">Coat-of-arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Klausen,_South_Tyrol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Coat-of-arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The emblem represents an <a href="/wiki/Argent" title="Argent">argent</a> dexter key on <a href="/wiki/Gules" title="Gules">gules</a>. The emblem was used as a seal from 1448, known from 1397 and granted in 1540 by Cardinal <i>Bernhard von Cles</i> Bishop of Brixen.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Linguistic_distribution">Linguistic distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Klausen,_South_Tyrol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Linguistic distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
