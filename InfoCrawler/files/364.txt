			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Rintfleisch massacres</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rintfleisch-Pogrom&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Rintfleisch-Pogrom">Rintfleisch-Pogrom</a>)</span></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Rintfleisch</b> or <b>Rindfleisch</b> movement was a series of massacres against <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Germany" title="History of the Jews in Germany">Jews</a> in the year 1298.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The event, in later terminology a <a href="/wiki/Pogrom" title="Pogrom">pogrom</a>, was the first large-scale persecutions in Germany since the <a href="/wiki/First_Crusade" title="First Crusade">First Crusade</a>.</p>
<p>It was set in the <a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconian</a> region during the civil strife between the elected <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Romans" title="King of the Romans">King of the Romans</a>, Count <a href="/wiki/Adolf,_King_of_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Adolf, King of Germany">Adolf of Nassau</a>, and his <a href="/wiki/House_of_Habsburg" title="House of Habsburg">Habsburg</a> rival Duke <a href="/wiki/Albert_I_of_Germany" title="Albert I of Germany">Albert of Austria</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Imperial</a> authority, traditionally concerned with the protection of the Jews, had temporarily collapsed. Already in 1287 the death of <a href="/wiki/Werner_of_Oberwesel" title="Werner of Oberwesel">Werner of Oberwesel</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Rhineland" title="Rhineland">Rhineland</a> had been blamed on Jews and about 500 were killed in revenge, followed by a series of <a href="/wiki/Blood_libel" title="Blood libel">blood libels</a>. When finally King Adolf was deposed and killed in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_G%C3%B6llheim" title="Battle of Göllheim">Battle of Göllheim</a> on 2 July 1298, the Franconian nobility gathered at Albert's <a href="/wiki/Prince-elector" title="Prince-elector">election</a> in <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a>.</p>
<p>When at the same time the Jews in the <a href="/wiki/Hohenlohe" title="Hohenlohe">Hohenlohe</a> town of <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B6ttingen" title="Röttingen">Röttingen</a> were accused of having obtained and <a href="/wiki/Host_desecration" title="Host desecration">desecrated a consecrated host</a>, one "Lord Rindtfleisch", who the sources refer to either as an impoverished knight or—more probably—a butcher (the term <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Rindfleisch" class="extiw" title="wikt:Rindfleisch">Rindfleisch</a></i> means "beef" in modern <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> spelling), gathered a mob around him and <a href="/wiki/Burned_at_the_stake" class="mw-redirect" title="Burned at the stake">burned</a> the Röttingen Jews on Sunday, April 20. Rintfleisch declared to have received a mandate from <a href="/wiki/Heaven" title="Heaven">heaven</a> to avenge the sacrilege and exterminate the Jews. The <a href="/wiki/Colmar" title="Colmar">Colmar</a> Dominican Rudolph refers to him in Latin as a <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/carnifex" class="extiw" title="wikt:carnifex">carnifex</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> i.e. butcher or executioner, but it is not clear if Rudolph meant his original profession, or his behaviour as a slaughterer of the Jews.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> According to contemporary sources the Lord of Röttingen, Kraft von Hohenlohe, was encumbered with debts to Jewish lenders.</p>
<p>After this, he and his mob went from town to town and killed all Jews that fell under their control, destroying the Jewish communities at <a href="/wiki/Rothenburg_ob_der_Tauber" title="Rothenburg ob der Tauber">Rothenburg ob der Tauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/W%C3%BCrzburg" title="Würzburg">Würzburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bamberg" title="Bamberg">Bamberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dinkelsb%C3%BChl" title="Dinkelsbühl">Dinkelsbühl</a>, <a href="/wiki/N%C3%B6rdlingen" title="Nördlingen">Nördlingen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Forchheim" title="Forchheim">Forchheim</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Free_Imperial_City_of_Nuremberg" title="Free Imperial City of Nuremberg">Free Imperial City of Nuremberg</a>, the Jews sought refuge in the <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_Castle" title="Nuremberg Castle">fortress</a> and were assisted by the Christian citizens, but Rintfleisch overcame the defenders and butchered the Jews on 1 August. The <i>Nürnberger <a href="/wiki/Memorbuch" title="Memorbuch">Memorbuch</a></i> contains the names of thousands of murdered Jews in numerous cities, among them <a href="/wiki/Mordechai_ben_Hillel" title="Mordechai ben Hillel">Mordechai ben Hillel</a>, a pupil of <a href="/wiki/Meir_of_Rothenburg" title="Meir of Rothenburg">Meir of Rothenburg</a>, with his wife and children. The communities at <a href="/wiki/Regensburg" title="Regensburg">Regensburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Augsburg" title="Augsburg">Augsburg</a> alone escaped the mass killing, as they were protected by the cities' magistrates. Spreading from Franconia to <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Bavaria" title="Duchy of Bavaria">Bavaria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Austria" title="Duchy of Austria">Austria</a>, the persecutors destroyed 146 communities, about 20,000 Jews were killed.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Austrian_Chronicle_of_95_Seigneurs" class="mw-redirect" title="Austrian Chronicle of 95 Seigneurs">Austrian Chronicle of 95 Seigneurs</a></i> about 100 years later alleged that King Albert I finally had Rintfleisch arrested and hanged. The cities in which Jews had been killed were required to pay fines to the king.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scholarship">Scholarship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rintfleisch_massacres&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Scholarship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In modern German texts the term <i>die Rintfleischbewegung</i> ("the Rintfleisch movement")<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> is less common than <i>das Rintfleischpogrom</i> ("the Rintfleisch pogrom")<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> though the Russian word <i><a href="/wiki/Pogrom" title="Pogrom">pogrom</a></i> did not exist in Germany at the time.</p>
