<p><b>Frederick</b> (<a href="/wiki/Middle_High_German" title="Middle High German">Middle High German</a>: <i>Friderich</i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><i>,</i> <a href="/wiki/German_Standard_German" title="German Standard German">Standard German</a>: <i>Friedrich</i>; 21 September 1371 – 20 September 1440) was the last <a href="/wiki/Burgrave_of_Nuremberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Burgrave of Nuremberg">Burgrave of Nuremberg</a> from 1397 to 1427 (as <b>Frederick VI</b>), Margrave of <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Ansbach" title="Principality of Ansbach">Brandenburg-Ansbach</a> from 1398, Margrave of <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Bayreuth" title="Principality of Bayreuth">Brandenburg-Kulmbach</a> from 1420, and <a href="/wiki/List_of_rulers_of_Brandenburg" title="List of rulers of Brandenburg">Elector of Brandenburg</a> (as <b>Frederick I</b>) from 1415 until his death. He became the first member of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Hohenzollern" title="House of Hohenzollern">House of Hohenzollern</a> to rule the <a href="/wiki/Margraviate_of_Brandenburg" title="Margraviate of Brandenburg">Margraviate of Brandenburg</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frederick_I,_Elector_of_Brandenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frederick was born in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, the second-born son of Burgrave <a href="/wiki/Frederick_V,_Burgrave_of_Nuremberg" title="Frederick V, Burgrave of Nuremberg">Frederick V</a> (1333–1398) and the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Wettin" title="House of Wettin">Wettin</a> princess <a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_of_Meissen" title="Elisabeth of Meissen">Elisabeth of Meissen</a>. He entered early into the service of his brother-in-law, the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Habsburg" title="House of Habsburg">Habsburg</a> duke <a href="/wiki/Albert_III,_Duke_of_Austria" title="Albert III, Duke of Austria">Albert III of Austria</a>. After Albert's death in 1395, he fought on the side of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Luxembourg" title="House of Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a> king <a href="/wiki/Sigismund,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Sigismund, Holy Roman Emperor">Sigismund of Hungary</a> against invading <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Wars_in_Europe" class="mw-redirect" title="Ottoman Wars in Europe">Ottoman</a> forces. He and his elder brother <a href="/wiki/John_III,_Burgrave_of_Nuremberg" title="John III, Burgrave of Nuremberg">John</a>, husband of Sigismund's sister <a href="/wiki/Margaret_of_Bohemia,_Burgravine_of_Nuremberg" title="Margaret of Bohemia, Burgravine of Nuremberg">Margaret of Bohemia</a>, fought in the 1396 <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Nicopolis" title="Battle of Nicopolis">Battle of Nicopolis</a> where they suffered a disastrous defeat.</p>
<p>After Frederick returned to Nuremberg, he divided the <a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconian</a> inheritance from his father with his brother John, who received the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Bayreuth" title="Principality of Bayreuth">Principality of Bayreuth</a>, while Frederick kept the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Ansbach" title="Principality of Ansbach">Principality of Ansbach</a>; the brothers ruled jointly in the <a href="/wiki/Burgraviate_of_Nuremberg" title="Burgraviate of Nuremberg">Burgraviate of Nuremberg</a>. At first Frederick tried to mediate in the imperial confusion between Sigismund's elder half-brother, the German king <a href="/wiki/Wenceslaus_of_Bohemia" class="mw-redirect" title="Wenceslaus of Bohemia">Wenceslaus</a>, and the party of Elector <a href="/wiki/Rupert,_King_of_Germany" title="Rupert, King of Germany">Rupert III of the Palatinate</a>, but from September 1399 he fought on the side of Rupert, husband of his sister <a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_of_Nuremberg" title="Elisabeth of Nuremberg">Elizabeth</a>, nonetheless. Wenceslaus was eventually deposed and Rupert elected <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Romans" title="King of the Romans">King of the Romans</a> on 21 August 1400.</p>
<p>Frederick resumed his rule of Ansbach in 1409 and after heavy feuding, entered into the service of King Sigismund. In 1410, the death of <a href="/wiki/Rupert,_King_of_the_Germans" class="mw-redirect" title="Rupert, King of the Germans">Rupert, King of the Germans</a>, left the throne of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> vacant. Sigismund enlisted Frederick's help in obtaining the throne. At the time, <a href="/wiki/Jobst_of_Moravia" title="Jobst of Moravia">Jobst of Moravia</a> ruled Brandenburg and thus was one of the <a href="/wiki/Prince-electors" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince-electors">prince-electors</a> who had the right to vote for the new king. However, Sigismund disputed Jobst's claim to Brandenburg and his right to vote in the imperial election. Sigismund claimed these rights for himself and designated Frederick to represent him as elector of Brandenburg in the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_election" title="Imperial election">imperial election</a> of 20 September 1410. While Sigismund won this initial vote, Jobst of Moravia won the support of a majority of electors in an election in October 1410 and himself claimed the imperial throne. Jobst's death under suspicious circumstances in January 1411 cleared the way for Sigismund's recovery of Brandenburg and his undisputed election as king of the empire later that year. In gratitude for Frederick's services, King Sigismund made him <i>Oberster Hauptmann</i> and <i>Verwalter der Marken</i> (1411). With an iron hand Frederick fought against the rebellious nobility of the <a href="/wiki/Marches" class="mw-redirect" title="Marches">March</a> of Brandenburg (in particular, the <a href="/wiki/Quitzow" class="mw-redirect" title="Quitzow">Quitzow</a> family) and, in the end, restored security. Frederick also became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Parakeet_Society" title="Parakeet Society">Parakeet Society</a> and of the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Constance" class="mw-redirect" title="League of Constance">League of Constance</a>.</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Constance" title="Council of Constance">Council of Constance</a> (30 April 1415) Sigismund granted Frederick the titles of <a href="/wiki/Margrave" title="Margrave">Margrave</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince-elector" title="Prince-elector">Prince-elector</a> of Brandenburg. On 21 October 1415 the Brandenburg states meeting in a <i><a href="/wiki/Landtag" title="Landtag">Landtag</a></i> asked him to rule in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>. The king awarded him the formal enfeoffment of the margravate on 18 April 1417. As Frederick did not agree with the forcible action of Sigismund against the <a href="/wiki/Hussites" title="Hussites">Hussites</a>, relations between them cooled.</p>
<p>Constant feuding with the nobility of Brandenburg led Frederick to withdraw to his castle at <a href="/wiki/Cadolzburg" title="Cadolzburg">Cadolzburg</a> in 1425 and transfer the regency of the margravate to his son <a href="/wiki/John,_Margrave_of_Brandenburg-Kulmbach" title="John, Margrave of Brandenburg-Kulmbach">John</a> in 1426 (Frederick, however, remained elector). After 1427 he organized the imperial war against the Hussites and subsequently provided substantial assistance in the mediation of the <a href="/wiki/Hussites#The_Council_of_Basel_and_Compacta_of_Prague" title="Hussites">Compacta of Prague</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Basel" class="mw-redirect" title="Council of Basel">Council of Basel</a> (30 November 1433).</p>
<p>Upon his death in 1440, Frederick was succeeded as elector by his second-eldest son, <a href="/wiki/Frederick_II,_Elector_of_Brandenburg" title="Frederick II, Elector of Brandenburg">Frederick II</a>.</p>

<p>He married <a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_of_Bavaria-Landshut" class="mw-redirect" title="Elisabeth of Bavaria-Landshut">Elisabeth of Bavaria-Landshut</a> (1383–13 November 1442, Ansbach), daughter of Duke <a href="/wiki/Frederick,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Frederick, Duke of Bavaria">Frederick of Bavaria-Landshut</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maddalena_Visconti" title="Maddalena Visconti">Maddalena Visconti</a>. Their children were:</p>
<ol>
<li><a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_of_Brandenburg,_Duchess_of_Brzeg-Legnica_and_Cieszyn" title="Elisabeth of Brandenburg, Duchess of Brzeg-Legnica and Cieszyn">Elisabeth</a> (1403–31 October 1449, <a href="/wiki/Legnica" title="Legnica">Liegnitz</a>), married:
