			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Antilles</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p>The <b>Antilles</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/æ/: 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'n' in 'nigh'">n</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ɪ/: 'i' in 'kit'">ɪ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">iː</span><span title="'z' in 'zoom'">z</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <i>Antilles</i> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">[ɑ̃.tij]</a></span> in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>; <i>Antillas</i> in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>; <i>Antillen</i> in <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> and <i>Antilhas</i> in <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>) is an <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a> bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> to the south and west, the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> to the northwest, and the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> to the north and east.</p>
<p>The Antillean islands are divided into two smaller groupings: the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">Greater Antilles</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>. The Greater Antilles includes the larger islands of <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a> (subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a>. The Lesser Antilles contains the northerly <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">Leeward Islands</a>, the southeasterly <a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands" title="Windward Islands">Windward Islands</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Antilles" title="Leeward Antilles">Leeward Antilles</a> just north of <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Lucayan_Archipelago" title="Lucayan Archipelago">Lucayan Archipelago</a> (consisting of <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">the Bahamas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands" title="Turks and Caicos Islands">Turks and Caicos Islands</a>), though part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a>, are generally not included among the Antillean islands.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">Geographically</a>, the Antillean islands are generally considered a subregion of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. Culturally speaking, Cuba, the Dominican Republic, and Puerto Rico – and sometimes the whole of the Antilles – are included in <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, although some sources avoid this socio-economic oversimplification by using the phrase "Latin America and the Caribbean" instead (see <a href="/wiki/Latin_America#Etymology_and_definitions" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>, "In Contemporary Usage"). In terms of <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geology</a>, the Greater Antilles are made up of <a href="/wiki/Continental_crust" title="Continental crust">continental rock</a>, as distinct from the Lesser Antilles, which are mostly young <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Coral_island" title="Coral island">coral islands</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antilles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Carte_antilles_1843.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Carte_antilles_1843.jpg/220px-Carte_antilles_1843.jpg" width="220" height="158" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Carte_antilles_1843.jpg/330px-Carte_antilles_1843.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Carte_antilles_1843.jpg/440px-Carte_antilles_1843.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2850" data-file-height="2043" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Carte_antilles_1843.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map of Antilles / Caribbean in 1843.</div>
</div>

<p>The word <i>Antilles</i> originated in the period before the European conquest of the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Antilia" class="mw-redirect" title="Antilia">Antilia</a></i> being one of those mysterious lands which figured on the <a href="/wiki/Medieval" class="mw-redirect" title="Medieval">medieval</a> <a href="/wiki/Chart" title="Chart">charts</a>, sometimes as an <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a>, sometimes as continuous land of greater or lesser extent, its location fluctuating in mid-ocean between the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the 1492 arrival of <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a>'s expedition in what was later called the West Indies, the European powers realized that the dispersed lands constituted an extensive archipelago inhabiting the Caribbean Sea and the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The Antilles were called multiple names before their current name became the norm. Early Spanish visitors called them the <i>Windward Islands</i>. They were also called the <i>Forward Islands</i> by 18th-century British.<sup id="cite_ref-Kitchin1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kitchin1-3">[3]</a></sup> Thereafter, the term <i>Antilles</i> was commonly assigned to the formation, and "Sea of the Antilles" became a common alternative name for the Caribbean Sea in various European languages.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antilles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Antilles were described in 1778 by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Kitchin" title="Thomas Kitchin">Thomas Kitchin</a> as once being called the <i>Caribbee Isles</i> in homage to the Carib people who were the islands' first inhabitants.</p>
