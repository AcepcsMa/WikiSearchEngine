<p>The <b>Basketball Bundesliga (BBL)</b> (<a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English language</a>: <i>Federal Basketball League</i>), for sponsorship reasons named <b>easyCredit BBL</b>, is the <a href="/wiki/German_basketball_league_system" title="German basketball league system">highest level</a> <a href="/wiki/Sports_league" title="Sports league">league</a> of <a href="/wiki/Professional_sports" title="Professional sports">professional</a> <a href="/wiki/Sports_club" title="Sports club">club</a> <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. The league comprises 18 teams. A BBL season is split into a league stage and a <a href="/wiki/Playoff" class="mw-redirect" title="Playoff">playoff</a> stage. At the end of the league stage, the top eight teams qualify for the playoff stage, and the teams positioned in the 17th and 18th places are <a href="/wiki/Relegation" class="mw-redirect" title="Relegation">relegated</a> to a lower tier league. The playoffs are played in a "<a href="/wiki/Playoff_format#Best-of-five_playoff" title="Playoff format">Best of five</a>" format. The winning team of the final round are crowned the <a href="/wiki/German_champions_(basketball)" class="mw-redirect" title="German champions (basketball)">German Champions</a> of that season.</p>
<p>In addition to the league competition, all BBL teams compete for the <a href="/wiki/German_Basketball_Cup" title="German Basketball Cup">German Cup</a> (BBL-Cup).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Teams playing <a href="/wiki/2._Basketball_Bundesliga" title="2. Basketball Bundesliga">in the second league</a> (<a href="/wiki/ProA" title="ProA">ProA</a> or <a href="/wiki/ProB" title="ProB">ProB</a>), or in a lower level <a href="/wiki/Regionalliga#Basketball" title="Regionalliga">Regionalliga</a>, are also eligible to participate in the German Cup.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> There are always 3 knock-out rounds that are played for the BBL-Cup. If more teams from the leagues below the BBL level apply for participation, then available places, and additional qualification rounds are added for them. The <a href="/wiki/Final_four" title="Final four">final four</a> remaining teams determine the rankings for <a href="/wiki/Bronze_medal" title="Bronze medal">bronze</a>, <a href="/wiki/Silver_medal" title="Silver medal">silver</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold</a> medals, in knock-out matches that are termed the BBL-TOP4. The gold winning team is the German Cup winner.</p>
<p>The Basketball Bundesliga is run by the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_Bundesliga_GmbH" title="Basketball Bundesliga GmbH">Basketball Bundesliga GmbH</a>. 74% of BBL GmbH is owned by the AG BBL e.V. (which is composed of the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_Bundesliga#Teams" title="Basketball Bundesliga">clubs</a>), and 26% by the <a href="/wiki/German_Basketball_Federation" title="German Basketball Federation">German Basketball Federation</a> (DBB).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basketball_Bundesliga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Germany, a national domestic <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> championship was first organised in 1939, and it was won by <a href="/w/index.php?title=LSV_Spandau&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="LSV Spandau (page does not exist)">LSV Spandau</a>. By 1944, almost all basketball activity in the country was forced to an end, due to the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>. In 1947, <a href="/w/index.php?title=MTSV_Schwabing_M%C3%BCnchen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="MTSV Schwabing München (page does not exist)">MTSV Schwabing München</a> became the first champion of post-war divided Germany.</p>
<p>The creation of a split <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West-German</a> federal-league, consisting of one northern division and one southern division, each comprising 10 teams, was decided on by the <a href="/wiki/German_Basketball_Federation" title="German Basketball Federation">German Basketball Federation</a> (DBB) in 1964. On October 1, 1966, the first season of the so-called Basketball Bundesliga started. Starting with the 1971–72 season, the size of each division was reduced to 8 teams.</p>
<p>With the 1975–76 season, the league structure was changed into a ten team first league (1. Basketball Bundesliga), and a 20 team second league (<a href="/wiki/2._Basketball_Bundesliga" title="2. Basketball Bundesliga">2. Basketball Bundesliga</a>). Only the second league was split into a northern and a southern division, of 10 teams each. In 1985, the top league was enlarged to a size of 12 teams, and two years later, each division of the second league was also enlarged to 12 teams.</p>
<p>In 1988, the championship mode "<a href="/wiki/Playoff_format#Best-of-five_playoff" title="Playoff format">Best of five</a>" was applied for the first time. Starting with the 1995–96 season, the first league consisted of 14 teams. The Basketball Bundesliga <a href="/wiki/GmbH" class="mw-redirect" title="GmbH">GmbH</a> (BBL) was founded in October 1996.</p>
<p>The federal leagues received their own administration within the framework of the German Basketball Federation in 1997. Since then, the second league divisions have been administered by the "AG 2. Bundesliga", while the BBL has been responsible for the first league. Two years later, a contract was signed between the BBL and the German Basketball Federation, in which the federation transferred its marketing/events rights to the BBL, for a 10-year duration, and in return, the BBL agreed to pay an annual "amateur support fee" of <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Mark" title="Deutsche Mark">DM</a> 600,000 (<a href="/wiki/%E2%82%AC" class="mw-redirect" title="€">€</a> 306,775).</p>
<p>Starting with the 2003–04 season, the top league was increased to 16 teams, and in 2006–07, it was further increased to its present size of 18 teams. For the following 2007–08 season, the structure of the second league was reshaped from its northern/southern divisions, into a <a href="/wiki/ProA" title="ProA">ProA</a> division, and a <a href="/wiki/ProB" title="ProB">ProB</a> division. These divisions remained under the administration by "AG 2. Bundesliga".</p>
<p>Between 1994 and 2001, the highest level German basketball league was called "<a href="/wiki/Veltins" title="Veltins">Veltins</a> Basketball Bundesliga", and from 2001, until 2003, the league was known as "<a href="/wiki/S.Oliver" title="S.Oliver">s.Oliver</a> Basketball Bundesliga". <a href="/wiki/Bayer_Giants_Leverkusen" title="Bayer Giants Leverkusen">Bayer Giants Leverkusen</a> hold the league titles won record, being the winner of 14 <a href="/wiki/List_of_German_basketball_champions" title="List of German basketball champions">German Basketball Championships</a>. However, since 1997, <a href="/wiki/ALBA_Berlin" class="mw-redirect" title="ALBA Berlin">ALBA Berlin</a> has dominated the league, winning their 8th title in 2008. Twenty-one teams have won the championship, since its inception.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sponsorship">Sponsorship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basketball_Bundesliga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sponsorship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2016, the BBL joined forces with <a href="/wiki/Tipbet" title="Tipbet">Tipbet</a>, a Malta-based betting company.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> This made Tipbet the Official Betting and Premium Sponsor of the league; the sponsorship agreement results in advertising activities throughout the arenas and online, and runs until 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arena_rules">Arena rules</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basketball_Bundesliga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Arena rules">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Currently, all Bundesliga clubs must play in arenas that seat at least 3,000 people.</p>
