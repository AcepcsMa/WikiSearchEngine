<p><b><a href="/wiki/September_1" title="September 1">September 1</a></b></p>
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.5em;" id="mp-otd-img">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 100px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Martha_last_passenger_pigeon_1914.jpg" class="image" title="Martha, the last passenger pigeon"><img alt="Martha, the last passenger pigeon" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Martha_last_passenger_pigeon_1914.jpg/100px-Martha_last_passenger_pigeon_1914.jpg" width="100" height="139" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Martha_last_passenger_pigeon_1914.jpg/150px-Martha_last_passenger_pigeon_1914.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Martha_last_passenger_pigeon_1914.jpg/200px-Martha_last_passenger_pigeon_1914.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1587" data-file-height="2206" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">Martha, the last passenger pigeon</div>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1774" title="1774">1774</a> – <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Gage" title="Thomas Gage">Thomas Gage</a>, royal governor of the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Massachusetts_Bay" title="Province of Massachusetts Bay">Province of Massachusetts Bay</a>, ordered soldiers to remove gunpowder from a <a href="/wiki/Gunpowder_magazine" title="Gunpowder magazine">magazine</a>, causing <a href="/wiki/Patriot_(American_Revolution)" title="Patriot (American Revolution)">Patriots</a> to <b><a href="/wiki/Powder_Alarm" title="Powder Alarm">prepare for war</a></b>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1804" title="1804">1804</a> – German astronomer <a href="/wiki/Karl_Ludwig_Harding" title="Karl Ludwig Harding">Karl Ludwig Harding</a> discovered one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Asteroid_belt" title="Asteroid belt">main belt asteroids</a>, naming it <b><a href="/wiki/3_Juno" title="3 Juno">Juno</a></b> after <a href="/wiki/Juno_(mythology)" title="Juno (mythology)">the Roman goddess</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1914" title="1914">1914</a> – The <b><a href="/wiki/Passenger_pigeon" title="Passenger pigeon">passenger pigeon</a></b>, which once numbered in the billions, became <a href="/wiki/Extinction" title="Extinction">extinct</a> when <a href="/wiki/Martha_(passenger_pigeon)" title="Martha (passenger pigeon)">the last individual</a> <i>(pictured)</i> died in captivity.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1969" title="1969">1969</a> – <b><a href="/wiki/Muammar_Gaddafi" title="Muammar Gaddafi">Muammar Gaddafi</a></b> led <a href="/wiki/1969_Libyan_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="1969 Libyan coup d'état">a coup d'état</a> to overthrow <a href="/wiki/Idris_I_of_Libya" class="mw-redirect" title="Idris I of Libya">Idris&#160;I of Libya</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1983" title="1983">1983</a> – A Soviet <a href="/wiki/Interceptor_aircraft" title="Interceptor aircraft">jet interceptor</a> shot down the civilian <b><a href="/wiki/Korean_Air_Lines_Flight_007" title="Korean Air Lines Flight 007">Korean Air Lines Flight 007</a></b> near <a href="/wiki/Sakhalin" title="Sakhalin">Sakhalin Island</a> in the North Pacific, killing all 246 passengers and 23 crew on board.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1792" title="1792">1792</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>: Due to an overwhelming fear that foreign armies would attack <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> and prisoners would revolt, thousands of people were <b><a href="/wiki/September_Massacres" title="September Massacres">summarily executed</a></b>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1864" title="1864">1864</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Union_Army" title="Union Army">Union forces</a> entered <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta, Georgia</a>, a day after the <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_Army" title="Confederate States Army">Confederate defenders</a> fled the city, bringing the <b><a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Campaign" title="Atlanta Campaign">Atlanta Campaign</a></b> to a close.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1946" title="1946">1946</a> – The <b><a href="/wiki/Interim_Government_of_India" title="Interim Government of India">interim government of India</a></b>, headed by <a href="/wiki/Jawaharlal_Nehru" title="Jawaharlal Nehru">Jawaharlal Nehru</a> <i>(pictured)</i>, was formed to assist the transition of India from <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British rule</a> to independence.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1957" title="1957">1957</a> – President <a href="/wiki/Ngo_Dinh_Diem" title="Ngo Dinh Diem">Ngô Đình Diệm</a> of <a href="/wiki/South_Vietnam" title="South Vietnam">South Vietnam</a> <b><a href="/wiki/Ngo_Dinh_Diem_presidential_visit_to_Australia" title="Ngo Dinh Diem presidential visit to Australia">became</a></b> the first foreign <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> to make a state visit to Australia.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/1159" title="1159">1159</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Alexander_III" title="Pope Alexander III">Pope Alexander III</a> was chosen as the successor of <a href="/wiki/Pope_Adrian_IV" title="Pope Adrian IV">Pope Adrian IV</a> in <b><a href="/wiki/Papal_election,_1159" title="Papal election, 1159">a disputed election</a></b>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1778" title="1778">1778</a> – <a href="/wiki/Anglo-French_War_1779" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo-French War 1779">Anglo-French War</a>: France <b><a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Dominica_(1778)" title="Invasion of Dominica (1778)">invaded</a></b> the island of <a href="/wiki/Dominica" title="Dominica">Dominica</a> and captured its British fort before the latter even knew that France had <a href="/wiki/Franco-American_alliance" title="Franco-American alliance">allied with the United States</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1936" title="1936">1936</a> – The last <b><a href="/wiki/Thylacine" title="Thylacine">thylacine</a></b> died in captivity in <a href="/wiki/Hobart_Zoo" title="Hobart Zoo">Hobart Zoo</a> in Australia.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1940" title="1940">1940</a> – <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>: The <i><a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Luftwaffe</a></i> changed their strategy in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Britain" title="Battle of Britain">Battle of Britain</a> and began bombing London and other British cities and towns <b><a href="/wiki/The_Blitz" title="The Blitz">for more than 50 consecutive nights</a></b> <i>(<a href="/wiki/Heinkel_He_111" title="Heinkel He 111">Heinkel bomber</a> pictured)</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2010" title="2010">2010</a> – A Chinese <a href="/wiki/Fishing_trawler" title="Fishing trawler">fishing trawler</a>, operating in <a href="/wiki/Senkaku_Islands_dispute" title="Senkaku Islands dispute">disputed waters</a>, <b><a href="/wiki/2010_Senkaku_boat_collision_incident" title="2010 Senkaku boat collision incident">collided</a></b> with <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Coast_Guard" class="mw-redirect" title="Japanese Coast Guard">Japanese Coast Guard</a> patrol boats near the <a href="/wiki/Senkaku_Islands" title="Senkaku Islands">Senkaku Islands</a>, sparking a major diplomatic dispute between the two countries.</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/1963" title="1963">1963</a> – A bomb planted by members of the <a href="/wiki/Ku_Klux_Klan" title="Ku Klux Klan">Ku Klux Klan</a> <b><a href="/wiki/16th_Street_Baptist_Church_bombing" title="16th Street Baptist Church bombing">exploded</a></b> in the <a href="/wiki/16th_Street_Baptist_Church" title="16th Street Baptist Church">16th Street Baptist Church</a>, an <a href="/wiki/African_American" class="mw-redirect" title="African American">African American</a> church in <a href="/wiki/Birmingham,_Alabama" title="Birmingham, Alabama">Birmingham, Alabama</a>, killing four children and injuring at least 22 others.</li>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/September_28" title="September 28">September 28</a></b>:</p>
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.5em;" id="mp-otd-img">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 90px;"><a href="/wiki/File:William1.jpg" class="image" title="William the Conqueror"><img alt="William the Conqueror" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/William1.jpg/90px-William1.jpg" width="90" height="149" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/William1.jpg/135px-William1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/William1.jpg/180px-William1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="229" data-file-height="380" /></a>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1066" title="1066">1066</a> – <a href="/wiki/William_the_Conqueror" title="William the Conqueror">William the Conqueror</a> <i>(pictured)</i> and his fleet of around 600 ships landed at <a href="/wiki/Pevensey" title="Pevensey">Pevensey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sussex" title="Sussex">Sussex</a>, beginning the <b><a href="/wiki/Norman_conquest_of_England" title="Norman conquest of England">Norman conquest of England</a></b>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1821" title="1821">1821</a> – The <b><a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_Independence_of_the_Mexican_Empire" title="Declaration of Independence of the Mexican Empire">Declaration of Independence of the Mexican Empire</a></b> from Spain was drafted in the <a href="/wiki/National_Palace_(Mexico)" title="National Palace (Mexico)">National Palace</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1901" title="1901">1901</a> – <a href="/wiki/Philippine%E2%80%93American_War" title="Philippine–American War">Philippine–American War</a>: Filipino guerrillas killed more than forty American soldiers in <b><a href="/wiki/Balangiga_massacre" title="Balangiga massacre">a surprise attack</a></b> in the town of <a href="/wiki/Balangiga,_Eastern_Samar" title="Balangiga, Eastern Samar">Balangiga</a> on <a href="/wiki/Samar_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Samar Island">Samar Island</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1941" title="1941">1941</a> – The short-lived <b><a href="/wiki/Drama_uprising" title="Drama uprising">Drama uprising</a></b> against the <a href="/wiki/Axis_occupation_of_Greece" title="Axis occupation of Greece">Bulgarian occupation in northern Greece</a> began.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2009" title="2009">2009</a> – <b><a href="/wiki/2009_Guinea_protest" title="2009 Guinea protest">A protest</a></b> held by 50,000 people in <a href="/wiki/Conakry" title="Conakry">Conakry</a>, Guinea, was forcefully disrupted by the military junta, resulting in at least 157 deaths and over 1,200 injuries.</li>
</ul>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/September_29" title="September 29">September 29</a></b>: <b><a href="/wiki/Michaelmas" title="Michaelmas">Michaelmas</a></b>; <b><a href="/wiki/Tasu%27a" title="Tasu'a">Day of Tasu'a</a></b> (Islam, 2017)</p>
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.5em;" id="mp-otd-img">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 120px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Goethe_after_Kraus.jpg" class="image" title="Goethe c. 1775"><img alt="Goethe c. 1775" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Goethe_after_Kraus.jpg/120px-Goethe_after_Kraus.jpg" width="120" height="150" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Goethe_after_Kraus.jpg/180px-Goethe_after_Kraus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Goethe_after_Kraus.jpg/240px-Goethe_after_Kraus.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="2000" /></a>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1774" title="1774">1774</a> – The publication of <i><b><a href="/wiki/The_Sorrows_of_Young_Werther" title="The Sorrows of Young Werther">The Sorrows of Young Werther</a></b></i> raised the 24-year-old <a href="/wiki/Johann_Wolfgang_von_Goethe" title="Johann Wolfgang von Goethe">Johann Wolfgang von Goethe</a> <i>(pictured)</i> to international fame.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1923" title="1923">1923</a> – The <b><a href="/wiki/British_Mandate_for_Palestine_(legal_instrument)" title="British Mandate for Palestine (legal instrument)">British Mandate for Palestine</a></b> came into effect, officially creating the protectorates of <a href="/wiki/Mandatory_Palestine" title="Mandatory Palestine">Palestine</a> under British administration and <a href="/wiki/Emirate_of_Transjordan" title="Emirate of Transjordan">Transjordan</a> as a separate <a href="/wiki/Emirate" title="Emirate">emirate</a> under <a href="/wiki/Abdullah_I_of_Jordan" title="Abdullah I of Jordan"><span class="nowrap">Abdullah I</span></a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1938" title="1938">1938</a> – At a conference in <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">Benito Mussolini</a>, British Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain" title="Neville Chamberlain">Neville Chamberlain</a>, and French Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/%C3%89douard_Daladier" title="Édouard Daladier">Édouard Daladier</a> <b><a href="/wiki/Munich_Agreement" title="Munich Agreement">reached a settlement</a></b>, signing it at about <span class="nowrap">1:30 am</span> the next day, stipulating that <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a> must cede the <a href="/wiki/Sudetenland" title="Sudetenland">Sudetenland</a> to Germany.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1963" title="1963">1963</a> – The <b><a href="/wiki/University_of_East_Anglia" title="University of East Anglia">University of East Anglia</a></b> was founded in <a href="/wiki/Norwich" title="Norwich">Norwich</a>, England, after talk of establishing such a university in the city began as early as the 19th century.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2006" title="2006">2006</a> – <b><a href="/wiki/Gol_Transportes_A%C3%A9reos_Flight_1907" title="Gol Transportes Aéreos Flight 1907">Gol Transportes Aéreos Flight 1907</a></b> collided in mid-air with an <a href="/wiki/Embraer_Legacy_600" title="Embraer Legacy 600">Embraer Legacy</a> business jet near <a href="/wiki/Peixoto_de_Azevedo" title="Peixoto de Azevedo">Peixoto de Azevedo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mato_Grosso" title="Mato Grosso">Mato Grosso</a>, Brazil, killing 154 people, and triggering a <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%932007_Brazilian_aviation_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="2006–2007 Brazilian aviation crisis">Brazilian aviation crisis</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Horatio_Nelson,_1st_Viscount_Nelson" title="Horatio Nelson, 1st Viscount Nelson">Horatio Nelson, 1st Viscount Nelson</a></b> (b.&#160;1758)&#160;<b>·</b>  <b><a href="/wiki/W._H._Auden" title="W. H. Auden">W. H. Auden</a></b> (d.&#160;1973)</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/1882" title="1882">1882</a> – The <b><a href="/wiki/Vulcan_Street_Plant" title="Vulcan Street Plant">Vulcan Street Plant</a></b>, the first <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectricity" title="Hydroelectricity">hydroelectric</a> central station to serve a system of private and commercial customers in North America, went on line in <a href="/wiki/Appleton,_Wisconsin" title="Appleton, Wisconsin">Appleton, Wisconsin</a>, US.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980</a> – <a href="/wiki/Xerox" title="Xerox">Xerox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Intel_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Intel Corporation">Intel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Digital_Equipment_Corporation" title="Digital Equipment Corporation">Digital Equipment Corporation</a> published the first <b><a href="/wiki/Ethernet" title="Ethernet">Ethernet</a></b> specifications <i>(<a href="/wiki/Modular_connector#8P8C" title="Modular connector">8P8C connector</a> pictured)</i>, currently the most widespread wired <a href="/wiki/Local_area_network" title="Local area network">local area network (LAN)</a> technology.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2009" title="2009">2009</a> – A 7.6 <a href="/wiki/Moment_magnitude_scale" title="Moment magnitude scale">M<sub>W</sub></a> earthquake <b><a href="/wiki/2009_Sumatra_earthquakes" title="2009 Sumatra earthquakes">struck</a></b> off the southern coast of <a href="/wiki/Sumatra" title="Sumatra">Sumatra</a>, Indonesia, killing 1,115 people and impacting an estimated 1.25&#160;million people.</li>
