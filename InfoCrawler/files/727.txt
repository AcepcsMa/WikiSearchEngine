<p><b>Peter Owen</b> (24 February 1927 – 31 May 2016) was a British publisher, the founder of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Owen_Publishers" title="Peter Owen Publishers">Peter Owen Publishers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-thetimes1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thetimes1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-thebookseller1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thebookseller1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was born <b>Peter Offenstadt</b> in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> in 1927, with <a href="/wiki/Rickets" title="Rickets">rickets</a>, the only child of a German Jewish couple.<sup id="cite_ref-thetimes1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thetimes1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-thebookseller1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thebookseller1-3">[3]</a></sup> His mother was Winifred Offenstadt.<sup id="cite_ref-thetimes1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thetimes1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was sent to live with his grandmother in England at the age of five, later to be joined by his parents, who were reluctant to leave Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was awarded the OBE for services to literature in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-thebookseller_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thebookseller-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Owen's funeral instructions included, "no religious crap of any kind".<sup id="cite_ref-thetimes1_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thetimes1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
