<p>A <b><a href="/wiki/Biography" title="Biography">biography</a></b> is a detailed description or account of someone's life. More than a list of basic facts (education, work, relationships, and death), a biography also portrays a subject's experience of these events. Unlike a profile or <a href="/wiki/Curriculum_vitae" title="Curriculum vitae">curriculum vitae</a> (<a href="/wiki/Resume" class="mw-redirect" title="Resume">résumé</a>), a biography presents a subject's life story, highlighting various aspects of his or her life, including intimate details of experience, and may include an analysis of a subject's personality.</p>
<p>Biographical works are usually non-fiction, but fiction can also be used to portray a person's life. One in-depth form of biographical coverage is called legacy writing. Biographical works in diverse media—from literature to film—form the <a href="/wiki/Genre" title="Genre">genre</a> known as biography.</p>
<p>An <b>authorized biography</b> is written with the permission, cooperation, and, at times, participation of a subject or a subject's heirs.</p>
<p>An <b><a href="/wiki/Autobiography" title="Autobiography">autobiography</a></b> is about a life of a subject, written by that subject or sometimes with a collaborator.</p>

<b><a href="/wiki/Mary_Wollstonecraft" title="Mary Wollstonecraft">Mary Wollstonecraft</a></b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'w' in 'wind'">w</span><span title="/ʊ/: 'oo' in 'foot'">ʊ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ən/: 'on' in 'button'">ən</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ɑː/: 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span></span>/</a></span></span>; 27 April 1759 – 10 September 1797) was an eighteenth-century <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">British</a> writer, <a href="/wiki/Philosopher" title="Philosopher">philosopher</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Feminist" class="mw-redirect" title="Feminist">feminist</a>. During her brief career as a maid she wrote novels, treatises, a <a href="/wiki/Travel_literature" title="Travel literature">travel narrative</a>, a history of the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>, a conduct book, and a children's book. Wollstonecraft is best known for <i><a href="/wiki/A_Vindication_of_the_Rights_of_Woman" title="A Vindication of the Rights of Woman">A Vindication of the Rights of Woman</a></i> (1792), in which she argues that women are not naturally inferior to men, but appear to be only because they lack education. She suggests that both men and animals should be treated as rational beings and imagines a social order founded on reason. <b>(<a href="/wiki/Mary_Wollstonecraft" title="Mary Wollstonecraft">Read more...</a>)</b>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/John_Henry_Newman" title="John Henry Newman">John Henry Newman</a></b> <a href="/wiki/Oratory_of_Saint_Philip_Neri" title="Oratory of Saint Philip Neri">CO</a> (21 February 1801&#160;– 11 August 1890) was a leader in the <a href="/wiki/Oxford_Movement" title="Oxford Movement">Oxford Movement</a>. In 1845 Newman left the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> and was received into the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a> where he was eventually granted the rank of <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholicism)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cardinal (Catholicism)">cardinal</a>. He was instrumental in the founding of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_University_of_Ireland" title="Catholic University of Ireland">Catholic University of Ireland</a>, which evolved into <a href="/wiki/University_College,_Dublin" class="mw-redirect" title="University College, Dublin">University College, Dublin</a>, today, the largest university in Ireland.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:white;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Did_you_know">Did you know</span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.5em;">
<p><a href="/wiki/File:Portrait_de_l%27artiste,_Maximilien_Luce.jpg" class="image" title="Self-portrait by Maximilien Luce, c. 1925–1930"><img alt="Self-portrait by Maximilien Luce, c. 1925–1930" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Portrait_de_l%27artiste%2C_Maximilien_Luce.jpg/93px-Portrait_de_l%27artiste%2C_Maximilien_Luce.jpg" width="93" height="120" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Portrait_de_l%27artiste%2C_Maximilien_Luce.jpg/140px-Portrait_de_l%27artiste%2C_Maximilien_Luce.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Portrait_de_l%27artiste%2C_Maximilien_Luce.jpg/187px-Portrait_de_l%27artiste%2C_Maximilien_Luce.jpg 2x" data-file-width="283" data-file-height="363" /></a></p>
</div>
<ul>
<li>... that French artist <b><a href="/wiki/Maximilien_Luce" title="Maximilien Luce">Maximilien Luce</a></b> <i>(self-portrait pictured)</i> published an album of lithographs documenting his experiences as a political prisoner?</li>
<li>... that <b><a href="/wiki/Diego_Garc%C3%ADa_de_Moguer" title="Diego García de Moguer">Diego García de Moguer</a></b> was a 16th-century pioneer in exploring the <a href="/wiki/Paran%C3%A1_River" title="Paraná River">Paraná River</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_la_Plata" title="Sierra de la Plata">Sierra de la Plata</a> of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata" title="Río de la Plata">Río de la Plata</a>?</li>
<li>... that between 1955 and 1960, <b><a href="/wiki/Hassan_Mamoun" title="Hassan Mamoun">Hassan Mamoun</a></b> issued 11,992 <i><a href="/wiki/Fatwa" title="Fatwa">fatwas</a></i> ("edicts"), more than any other <a href="/wiki/Dar_al-Ifta_al-Misriyyah" title="Dar al-Ifta al-Misriyyah">Egyptian grand mufti</a>?</li>
<li>... that botanist <b><a href="/wiki/Reidar_J%C3%B8rgensen" title="Reidar Jørgensen">Reidar Jørgensen</a></b> was a national champion in <a href="/wiki/Middle-distance_running" title="Middle-distance running">middle-distance running</a>?</li>
<li>... that sisters <b><a href="/wiki/Ada_Hegerberg" title="Ada Hegerberg">Ada</a></b> and <b><a href="/wiki/Andrine_Hegerberg" title="Andrine Hegerberg">Andrine Hegerberg</a></b> scored one goal each when the Norwegian team won 2–1 against Canada in the <a href="/wiki/2012_FIFA_U-20_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="2012 FIFA U-20 Women's World Cup">2012 FIFA U-20 Women's World Cup</a>?</li>
<li>... that <b><a href="/wiki/Mark_Sutton" title="Mark Sutton">Mark Sutton</a></b> portrayed a <a href="/wiki/Parachuting" title="Parachuting">skydiving</a> <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics_opening_ceremony" title="2012 Summer Olympics opening ceremony">opening ceremony of the 2012 Summer Olympics</a>?</li>
<li>... that although he was expelled by the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Jesus" title="Society of Jesus">Jesuit order</a> for supporting the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church_doctrine_on_the_ordination_of_women" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic Church doctrine on the ordination of women">ordination of women</a>, <b><a href="/wiki/William_R._Callahan_(priest)" title="William R. Callahan (priest)">William R. Callahan</a></b> insisted he was just "following the example of Jesus, who was never willing to shut up"?</li>
</ul>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:white;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Quote_of_the_week"><i>Quote of the week</i></span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="width:100%;text-align:center;">
<p>"Know that however ugly the parts appear<br />
the whole remains beautiful...<br />
... the wholeness of life and things, the divine beauty<br />
of the universe. Love that, not man<br />
Apart from that, or else you will share man's pitiful confusions,<br />
or drown in despair when his days darken."</p>
<p>— <i><a href="/wiki/Robinson_Jeffers" title="Robinson Jeffers">Robinson Jeffers</a></i></p>
<p><small>In <i>The Answer</i>, 1936</small></p>
