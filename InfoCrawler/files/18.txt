<p><b>Applied mathematics</b> is a branch of <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a> that deals with <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_model" title="Mathematical model">mathematical methods</a> that find use in <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">business</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industry</a>. Thus, applied mathematics is a combination of <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_science" class="mw-redirect" title="Mathematical science">mathematical science</a> and specialized knowledge. The term "applied mathematics" also describes the <a href="/wiki/Profession" title="Profession">professional specialty</a> in which mathematicians work on practical problems by formulating and studying mathematical models. In the past, practical applications have motivated the development of mathematical theories, which then became the subject of study in <a href="/wiki/Pure_mathematics" title="Pure mathematics">pure mathematics</a> where abstract concepts are studied for their own sake. The activity of applied mathematics is thus intimately connected with research in pure mathematics.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Historically, applied mathematics consisted principally of <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_analysis" title="Mathematical analysis">applied analysis</a>, most notably <a href="/wiki/Differential_equations" class="mw-redirect" title="Differential equations">differential equations</a>; <a href="/wiki/Approximation_theory" title="Approximation theory">approximation theory</a> (broadly construed, to include <a href="/wiki/Representation_(mathematics)" title="Representation (mathematics)">representations</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asymptotic_analysis" title="Asymptotic analysis">asymptotic</a> methods, <a href="/wiki/Calculus_of_variations" title="Calculus of variations">variational methods</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Numerical_analysis" title="Numerical analysis">numerical analysis</a>); and applied <a href="/wiki/Probability" title="Probability">probability</a>. These areas of mathematics related directly to the development of <a href="/wiki/Newtonian_physics" class="mw-redirect" title="Newtonian physics">Newtonian physics</a>, and in fact, the distinction between mathematicians and physicists was not sharply drawn before the mid-19th century. This history left a pedagogical legacy in the United States: until the early 20th century, subjects such as <a href="/wiki/Classical_mechanics" title="Classical mechanics">classical mechanics</a> were often taught in applied mathematics departments at American universities rather than in <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a> departments, and <a href="/wiki/Fluid_mechanics" title="Fluid mechanics">fluid mechanics</a> may still be taught in applied mathematics departments.<sup id="cite_ref-Stolz2002_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stolz2002-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Quantitative_finance" class="mw-redirect" title="Quantitative finance">Quantitative finance</a> is now taught in mathematics departments across universities and <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_finance" title="Mathematical finance">mathematical finance</a> is considered a full branch of applied mathematics.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">Engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> departments have traditionally made use of applied mathematics.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions">Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

Fluid mechanics is often considered a branch of applied mathematics and mechanical engineering.</div>

<p>Today, the term "applied mathematics" is used in a broader sense. It includes the classical areas noted above as well as other areas that have become increasingly important in applications. Even fields such as <a href="/wiki/Number_theory" title="Number theory">number theory</a> that are part of <a href="/wiki/Pure_mathematics" title="Pure mathematics">pure mathematics</a> are now important in applications (such as <a href="/wiki/Cryptography" title="Cryptography">cryptography</a>), though they are not generally considered to be part of the field of applied mathematics <i>per se</i>. Sometimes, the term "<a href="/wiki/Applicable_mathematics" title="Applicable mathematics">applicable mathematics</a>" is used to distinguish between the traditional applied mathematics that developed alongside physics and the many areas of mathematics that are applicable to real-world problems today.</p>
<p>There is no consensus as to what the various branches of applied mathematics are. Such categorizations are made difficult by the way mathematics and science change over time, and also by the way universities organize departments, courses, and degrees.</p>
<p>Many mathematicians distinguish between "applied mathematics," which is concerned with mathematical methods, and the "applications of mathematics" within science and engineering. A <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biologist</a> using a <a href="/wiki/Matrix_population_models" title="Matrix population models">population model</a> and applying known mathematics would not be <i>doing</i> applied mathematics, but rather <i>using</i> it; however, mathematical biologists have posed problems that have stimulated the growth of pure mathematics. Mathematicians such as <a href="/wiki/Henri_Poincar%C3%A9" title="Henri Poincaré">Poincaré</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Arnold" title="Vladimir Arnold">Arnold</a> deny the existence of "applied mathematics" and claim that there are only "applications of mathematics." Similarly, non-mathematicians blend applied mathematics and applications of mathematics. The use and development of mathematics to solve industrial problems is also called "industrial mathematics".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The success of modern numerical mathematical methods and software has led to the emergence of <a href="/wiki/Computational_mathematics" title="Computational mathematics">computational mathematics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computational_science" title="Computational science">computational science</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Computational_engineering" title="Computational engineering">computational engineering</a>, which use <a href="/wiki/Supercomputer" title="Supercomputer">high-performance computing</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Simulation" title="Simulation">simulation</a> of phenomena and the solution of problems in the sciences and engineering. These are often considered interdisciplinary.</p>

<p>Historically, mathematics was most important in the <a href="/wiki/Natural_sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural sciences">natural sciences</a> and <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>. However, since <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, fields outside the physical sciences have spawned the creation of new areas of mathematics, such as <a href="/wiki/Game_theory" title="Game theory">game theory</a> and <a href="/wiki/Social_choice_theory" title="Social choice theory">social choice theory</a>, which grew out of economic considerations.</p>
<p>The advent of the computer has enabled new applications: studying and using the new computer technology itself (<a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>) to study problems arising in other areas of science (computational science) as well as the mathematics of computation (for example, <a href="/wiki/Theoretical_computer_science" title="Theoretical computer science">theoretical computer science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_algebra" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer algebra">computer algebra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Numerical_analysis" title="Numerical analysis">numerical analysis</a>). <a href="/wiki/Statistics" title="Statistics">Statistics</a> is probably the most widespread <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_science" class="mw-redirect" title="Mathematical science">mathematical science</a> used in the <a href="/wiki/Social_sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Social sciences">social sciences</a>, but other areas of mathematics, most notably <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economics</a>, are proving increasingly useful in these disciplines.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status_in_academic_departments">Status in academic departments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Status in academic departments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Academic institutions are not consistent in the way they group and label courses, programs, and degrees in applied mathematics. At some schools, there is a single mathematics department, whereas others have separate departments for Applied Mathematics and (Pure) Mathematics. It is very common for Statistics departments to be separated at schools with graduate programs, but many undergraduate-only institutions include statistics under the mathematics department.</p>
<p>Many applied mathematics programs (as opposed to departments) consist of primarily cross-listed courses and jointly appointed faculty in departments representing applications. Some Ph.D. programs in applied mathematics require little or no coursework outside mathematics, while others require substantial coursework in a specific area of application. In some respects this difference reflects the distinction between "application of mathematics" and "applied mathematics".</p>
<p>Some universities in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a> host departments of <i>Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> but it is now much less common to have separate departments of pure and applied mathematics. A notable exception to this is the <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Mathematics,_University_of_Cambridge" title="Faculty of Mathematics, University of Cambridge">Department of Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a>, housing the <a href="/wiki/Lucasian_Professor_of_Mathematics" title="Lucasian Professor of Mathematics">Lucasian Professor of Mathematics</a> whose past holders include <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Newton" title="Isaac Newton">Isaac Newton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Babbage" title="Charles Babbage">Charles Babbage</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Lighthill" title="James Lighthill">James Lighthill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Dirac" title="Paul Dirac">Paul Dirac</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Hawking" title="Stephen Hawking">Stephen Hawking</a>.</p>
<p>Schools with separate applied mathematics departments range from <a href="/wiki/Brown_University" title="Brown University">Brown University</a>, which has a large Division of Applied Mathematics that offers degrees through the <a href="/wiki/Doctorate" title="Doctorate">doctorate</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clara_University" title="Santa Clara University">Santa Clara University</a>, which offers only the <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">M.S.</a> in applied mathematics.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Research universities dividing their mathematics department into pure and applied sections include <a href="/wiki/MIT" class="mw-redirect" title="MIT">MIT</a>. <a href="/wiki/Brigham_Young_University" title="Brigham Young University">Brigham Young University</a> also has an Applied and Computational Emphasis (ACME), a program that allows student to graduate with a Mathematics degree, with an emphasis in Applied Math. Students in this program also learn another skill (Computer Science, Engineering, Physics, Pure Math, etc.) to supplement their applied math skills.</p>

<p>Applied mathematics is closely related to other mathematical sciences.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scientific_computing">Scientific computing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Scientific computing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Scientific_computing" class="mw-redirect" title="Scientific computing">Scientific computing</a> includes applied mathematics (especially <a href="/wiki/Numerical_analysis" title="Numerical analysis">numerical analysis</a>), <a href="/wiki/Computing_science" class="mw-redirect" title="Computing science">computing science</a> (especially <a href="/wiki/High-performance_computing" class="mw-redirect" title="High-performance computing">high-performance computing</a>), and mathematical modelling in a scientific discipline.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Computer_science">Computer science</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Computer science">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">Computer science</a> relies on <a href="/wiki/Logic" title="Logic">logic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Algebra" title="Algebra">algebra</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Combinatorics" title="Combinatorics">combinatorics</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations_research_and_management_science">Operations research and management science</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Operations research and management science">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Operations_research" title="Operations research">Operations research</a> and <a href="/wiki/Management_science" title="Management science">management science</a> are often taught in faculties of engineering, business, and public policy.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Statistics">Statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Applied mathematics has substantial overlap with the discipline of statistics. <a href="/wiki/Statisticians" class="mw-redirect" title="Statisticians">Statistical theorists</a> study and improve statistical procedures with mathematics, and statistical research often raises mathematical questions. Statistical theory relies on <a href="/wiki/Probability_theory" title="Probability theory">probability</a> and <a href="/wiki/Optimal_decision" title="Optimal decision">decision theory</a>, and makes extensive use of scientific computing, analysis, and <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_optimization" title="Mathematical optimization">optimization</a>; for the <a href="/wiki/Design_of_experiments" title="Design of experiments">design of experiments</a>, statisticians use <a href="/wiki/Algebraic_statistics" title="Algebraic statistics">algebra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Combinatorial_design" title="Combinatorial design">combinatorial design</a>. Applied mathematicians and <a href="/wiki/Statistician" title="Statistician">statisticians</a> often work in a department of mathematical sciences (particularly at colleges and small universities).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Actuarial_science">Actuarial science</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Actuarial science">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Actuarial_science" title="Actuarial science">Actuarial science</a> applies probability, statistics, and economic theory to assess risk in insurance, finance and other industries and professions.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mathematical_economics">Mathematical economics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Mathematical economics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mathematical_economics" title="Mathematical economics">Mathematical economics</a> is the application of mathematical methods to represent theories and analyze problems in economics. The applied methods usually refer to nontrivial mathematical techniques or approaches. Mathematical economics is based on statistics, probability, mathematical programming (as well as other <a href="/wiki/Computational_economics" title="Computational economics">computational methods</a>), operations research, game theory, and some methods from mathematical analysis. In this regard, it resembles (but is distinct from) <a href="/wiki/Financial_mathematics" class="mw-redirect" title="Financial mathematics">financial mathematics</a>, another part of applied mathematics.</p>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Mathematics_Subject_Classification" title="Mathematics Subject Classification">Mathematics Subject Classification</a> (MSC), mathematical economics falls into the <a href="/wiki/Mathematics_Subject_Classification#Applied_mathematics_.2F_other" title="Mathematics Subject Classification">Applied mathematics/other</a> classification of category 91:</p>
<dl>
<dd>Game theory, economics, social and behavioral sciences</dd>
</dl>
<p>with <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://msc2010.org/mscwiki/index.php?title=MSC2010">MSC2010</a> classifications for '<a href="/wiki/Game_theory" title="Game theory">Game theory</a>' at codes <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://msc2010.org/mscwiki/index.php?title=91Axx">91Axx</a> and for 'Mathematical economics' at codes <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://msc2010.org/mscwiki/index.php?title=91Bxx">91Bxx</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_disciplines">Other disciplines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Other disciplines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The line between applied mathematics and specific areas of application is often blurred. Many universities teach mathematical and statistical courses outside the respective departments, in departments and areas including business, <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">psychology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scientific_computation" class="mw-redirect" title="Scientific computation">scientific computation</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_physics" title="Mathematical physics">mathematical physics</a>.</p>
