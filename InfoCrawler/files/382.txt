<p><b>Bavaria-Ingolstadt</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Bayern-Ingolstadt</i></span> or <i>Oberbayern-Ingolstadt</i>) was a duchy which was part of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> from 1392 to 1447.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bavaria-Ingolstadt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the death of <a href="/wiki/Stephen_II,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Stephen II, Duke of Bavaria">Stephen II</a> in 1375, his sons <a href="/wiki/Stephen_III,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Stephen III, Duke of Bavaria">Stephen III</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frederick,_Duke_of_Bavaria-Landshut" class="mw-redirect" title="Frederick, Duke of Bavaria-Landshut">Frederick</a>, and <a href="/wiki/John_II,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="John II, Duke of Bavaria">John II</a> jointly ruled <a href="/wiki/Bavaria-Landshut" title="Bavaria-Landshut">Bavaria-Landshut</a>. After seventeen years, the brothers decided to formally divide their inheritance. John received <a href="/wiki/Bavaria-Munich" title="Bavaria-Munich">Bavaria-Munich</a>, Stephen received Bavaria-Ingolstadt, while Frederick kept what remained of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria-Landshut" title="Bavaria-Landshut">Bavaria-Landshut</a>.</p>
<p>After Stephen's death in 1413, <a href="/wiki/Louis_VII,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Louis VII, Duke of Bavaria">Louis VII</a> assumed his father's throne. In 1429 parts of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria-Straubing" title="Bavaria-Straubing">Bavaria-Straubing</a> were united with Bavaria-Ingolstadt. Louis reigned until his own son, <a href="/wiki/Louis_VIII,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Louis VIII, Duke of Bavaria">Louis VIII</a>, usurped his throne in 1443 and delivered him to their enemy, <a href="/wiki/Henry_XVI,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Henry XVI, Duke of Bavaria">Henry XVI</a>, duke of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria-Landshut" title="Bavaria-Landshut">Bavaria-Landshut</a>. Louis VIII died two years later. Louis VII died in captivity. With no heir, Bavaria-Ingolstadt was returned to Bavaria-Landshut.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bavaria-Ingolstadt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bavaria-Ingolstadt was cobbled together from diverse, non-contiguous territories in Bavaria. The capital was Ingolstadt and included the territories around it: <a href="/wiki/Schrobenhausen" title="Schrobenhausen">Schrobenhausen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aichach" title="Aichach">Aichach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Friedberg,_Bavaria" title="Friedberg, Bavaria">Friedberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rain_am_Lech" class="mw-redirect" title="Rain am Lech">Rain am Lech</a> and <a href="/wiki/H%C3%B6chst%C3%A4dt_an_der_Donau" title="Höchstädt an der Donau">Höchstädt an der Donau</a>. In addition, Bavaria Ingolstadt incorporated the following towns:</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Wasserburg_am_Inn" title="Wasserburg am Inn">Wasserburg am Inn</a></li>

</ul>
