<p>The <b>Hochschule für Musik Nürnberg</b> (formerly <i>Hochschule für Musik Nürnberg-Augsburg</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/College_or_university_school_of_music" class="mw-redirect" title="College or university school of music">music conservatoire</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> (with a secondary building in <a href="/wiki/Augsburg" title="Augsburg">Augsburg</a>), <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Hochschule">The Hochschule</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hochschule_f%C3%BCr_Musik_N%C3%BCrnberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The Hochschule">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Hochschule für Musik Nürnberg is the result of the merging of the <i>Meistersinger-Konservatoriums</i> in Nuremberg and the <i>Leopold-Mozart-Konservatorium</i> in Augsburg in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-web_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-web-1">[1]</a></sup> The Meistersinger-Konservatorium dates back from 1821 when Johannes Scharrer founded the <i>Städtische Singschule</i>, which later became the State Music School (1883) and from 1972 as the "Fachakademie für Musik und Meistersinger-Konservatorium".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_courses">The courses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hochschule_f%C3%BCr_Musik_N%C3%BCrnberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The courses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The hochschule offers degrees and postgraduate qualifications in all orchestral instruments, jazz, popular music, singing, opera, music education, conducting and composition.<sup id="cite_ref-web_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-web-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
