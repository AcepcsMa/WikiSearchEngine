<p>The <b>Kunstverein Nürnberg</b> (art association <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, official name "Kunstverein Nürnberg - Albrecht Dürer Gesellschaft e.V.") is a venue for exhibitions of contemporary art. The association was founded in 1792 and is Germany's oldest arts association.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The association's exhibition space is situated in a building designed 1930 by architect <a href="/w/index.php?title=Otto_Ernst_Schweizer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Otto Ernst Schweizer (page does not exist)">Otto Ernst Schweizer</a>, well known for buildings such as the <a href="/wiki/Ernst-Happel-Stadion" title="Ernst-Happel-Stadion">Ernst-Happel-Stadion</a>.</p>
<p>The gallery shows a wide range of international contemporary artistic positions.</p>
<p>Past exhibitions (selection):</p>
<ul>
<li>2015 <i>Transparencies</i> - Neil Beloufa, Juliette Blightman, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Gander" title="Ryan Gander">Ryan Gander</a>, Calla Henkel &amp; Max Pitegoff, <a href="/wiki/David_Horvitz" title="David Horvitz">David Horvitz</a>, Metahaven, <a href="/wiki/Katja_Novitskova" title="Katja Novitskova">Katja Novitskova</a> and Yuri Pattison <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>2011 <a href="/wiki/Phyllida_Barlow" title="Phyllida Barlow">Phyllida Barlow</a> - Cast <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>2010 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shahryar_Nashat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Shahryar Nashat (page does not exist)">Shahryar Nashat</a> - Line up</li>
</ul>
