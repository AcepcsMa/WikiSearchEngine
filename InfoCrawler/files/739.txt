<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:#000094;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Bavaria"><big>Bavaria</big></span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">

<p><b><a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a></b> (German: <i><span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Freistaat_Bayern.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d9/Freistaat_Bayern.ogg" class="internal" title="Freistaat Bayern.ogg">Freistaat <b>Bayern</b></a></span>&#160;<small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Freistaat_Bayern.ogg" title="File:Freistaat Bayern.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span></i>; <a href="/wiki/International_Phonetic_Alphabet" title="International Phonetic Alphabet">IPA</a>: <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[fraɪ.ʃtaːt ˈbaɪ.ɐn]</span> English: <b>Free State of Bavaria</b>), with an area of 70,548 square kilometres (27,200&#160;sq&#160;mi) and almost 12.5 million inhabitants, is located in the southeast of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and is the largest federated <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i><a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany">state</a> (Bundesland)</i></span> of Germany by area. Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> in <a href="/wiki/Upper_Bavaria" title="Upper Bavaria">Upper Bavaria</a>.</p>
<p>Modern Bavaria also includes parts of the historical regions of <a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swabia" title="Swabia">Swabia</a>. Neighbouring states within Germany are <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a></span>, <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><a href="/wiki/Hesse" title="Hesse">Hesse</a></span>, <a href="/wiki/Thuringia" title="Thuringia">Thuringia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a>. International borders are shared with Austria and the Czech Republic. The <a href="/wiki/Bavarian_Alps" title="Bavarian Alps">Bavarian Alps</a> form the border with Austria, and within the range is the highest peak in Germany, the <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><a href="/wiki/Zugspitze" title="Zugspitze">Zugspitze</a></span>. Switzerland can be reached directly by crossing <a href="/wiki/Lake_Constance" title="Lake Constance">Lake Constance</a>. Two major rivers flow through the state, the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> (<span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Donau</i></span>) and the <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><a href="/wiki/Main_(river)" title="Main (river)">Main</a></span>.</p>
<p>The major cities in Bavaria are <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> (<span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>München</i></span>), <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> (<span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Nürnberg</i></span>), <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><a href="/wiki/Augsburg" title="Augsburg">Augsburg</a></span>, <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><a href="/wiki/W%C3%BCrzburg" title="Würzburg">Würzburg</a></span>, <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><a href="/wiki/Regensburg" title="Regensburg">Regensburg</a></span>, <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><a href="/wiki/Ingolstadt" title="Ingolstadt">Ingolstadt</a></span>, <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><a href="/wiki/F%C3%BCrth" title="Fürth">Fürth</a></span> and <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><a href="/wiki/Erlangen" title="Erlangen">Erlangen</a></span>.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:#000094;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_article">Selected article</span></h2>

<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:Ludwig_I_of_Bavaria.jpg" class="image" title="Louis I."><img alt="Louis I." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Ludwig_I_of_Bavaria.jpg/100px-Ludwig_I_of_Bavaria.jpg" width="100" height="143" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Ludwig_I_of_Bavaria.jpg/150px-Ludwig_I_of_Bavaria.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Ludwig_I_of_Bavaria.jpg/200px-Ludwig_I_of_Bavaria.jpg 2x" data-file-width="631" data-file-height="900" /></a></div>
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bavaria" title="Kingdom of Bavaria">Kingdom of Bavaria</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Königreich Bayern</i></span>) was a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> state that existed from 1806–1918. Elector <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_I_Joseph_of_Bavaria" title="Maximilian I Joseph of Bavaria">Maximilian I Joseph</a> of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Wittelsbach" title="House of Wittelsbach">House of Wittelsbach</a> became the first King of Bavaria in 1806. The monarchy would remain held by the Wittelsbachs until the kingdom's dissolution in 1918. Most of Bavaria's modern-day borders were established after 1814 with the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1814)" title="Treaty of Paris (1814)">Treaty of Paris</a>, in which Bavaria ceded <a href="/wiki/County_of_Tyrol" title="County of Tyrol">Tyrol</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vorarlberg" title="Vorarlberg">Vorarlberg</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Empire" title="Austrian Empire">Austrian Empire</a> while receiving <a href="/wiki/Aschaffenburg" title="Aschaffenburg">Aschaffenburg</a> and parts of <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Hesse" title="Grand Duchy of Hesse">Hesse-Darmstadt</a>. As a state within the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a>, the kingdom was second in size only to the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a>. Since the <a href="/wiki/Unification_of_Germany" title="Unification of Germany">unification of Germany</a> in 1871, Bavaria has remained part of Germany to the present day.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:#000094;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_biography">Selected biography</span></h2>

<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:Diesel_1883.jpg" class="image" title="Rudolf Diesel"><img alt="Rudolf Diesel" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Diesel_1883.jpg/100px-Diesel_1883.jpg" width="100" height="119" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Diesel_1883.jpg/150px-Diesel_1883.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Diesel_1883.jpg/200px-Diesel_1883.jpg 2x" data-file-width="378" data-file-height="450" /></a></div>
<p><b>Rudolf Christian Karl Diesel</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈʁuːdɔlf ˈkʁɪstjan ˈkaʁl ˈdiːzəl]</a></span>; March 18, 1858 – last seen alive September 29, 1913) was a <a href="/wiki/French_People" class="mw-redirect" title="French People">French</a>/<a href="/wiki/German_people" class="mw-redirect" title="German people">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Inventor" title="Inventor">inventor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineer" class="mw-redirect" title="Mechanical engineer">mechanical engineer</a>, famous for the invention of the <a href="/wiki/Diesel_engine" title="Diesel engine">diesel engine</a>.</p>
<p>Diesel was born in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> in 1858 as the second of three children to Theodor and Elise Diesel. Diesel's parents were German-born immigrants living in France. Theodor Diesel, a bookbinder by trade, had left his home town of <a href="/wiki/Augsburg" title="Augsburg">Augsburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bavaria" title="Kingdom of Bavaria">Kingdom of Bavaria</a>, in 1848. He met his wife, Elise Strobel, daughter of a <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> merchant, in Paris in 1855 and himself became a leathergoods manufacturer there.</p>

<p>The Watzmann (Austro-Bavarian: Watzmo) is the third highest [1] mountain in Germany (Zugspitze is the highest at 2,962m, Hochwanner the second at 2,746m). The three main peaks (right side in image) are Hocheck (2651 m), Mittelspitze (Middle Peak, 2,713m) and Südspitze (South Peak, 2,712m).</p>

</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #aaaaaa; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: white;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">

<ul>
<li>...that there is a <b>Bavarian citizenship</b> (as opposed to a <a href="/wiki/German_citizenship" class="mw-redirect" title="German citizenship">German citizenship</a>)? Actually, the Bavarian constitution (Bavaria has a separate constitution that exists alongside the German constitution) explicitly provides for it in articles 6 and 7. Specifically, you become a Bavarian citizen by birth, by marriage or by being naturalized.</li>
</ul>
