<p><b>St. Jakob</b> (St James the Greater) is a medieval church of the former free imperial city of <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is dedicated to <a href="/wiki/James,_son_of_Zebedee" title="James, son of Zebedee">Saint James the Greater</a>. The church was badly damaged during the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> and later restored.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St._Jakob,_Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The church was founded on 20 February 1209 by <a href="/wiki/Otto_IV,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Otto IV, Holy Roman Emperor">Otto IV, Holy Roman Emperor</a>. The small Romanesque chapel was demolished about 80 years later and the church rebuilt. The church soon came into the possession of the <a href="/wiki/Knights_Hospitaller" title="Knights Hospitaller">Knights of St John</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> By <a href="/wiki/Frederick_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Frederick II, Holy Roman Emperor">Frederick II</a>, the order was equipped with more and more possessions. The church still contains the <a href="/wiki/Reredos" title="Reredos">reredos</a> on the high altar which dates from 1360-1370.</p>
<p>During the Reformation, the Church of St. James was one of the Protestant Churches. In 1531 a preacher was intended, but the church remained in possession of the <a href="/wiki/Teutonic_Knights" class="mw-redirect" title="Teutonic Knights">Catholic Teutonic Order</a>.</p>
<p>In 1632 during the Thirty Years' War <a href="/wiki/Gustavus_Adolphus_of_Sweden" title="Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden">King Gustav Adolf</a> expropriated the Teutonic Order, and handed the church to the city of Nuremberg and which carried out an extensive renovation. By the provisions of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Westphalia" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Westphalia">Treaty of Westphalia</a> returned to the Teutonic Order in 1648. The Order kept its hold until 1809, when <a href="/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Napoleon I of France">Napoleon Bonaparte</a> ordered its dissolution. In 1810 St. Jakob became the third Protestant parish church of <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>.</p>
