<p>The <b>Nuremberg Palace of Justice</b> <i>(German Justizpalast)</i> is a building complex in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, Bavaria, Germany. It was constructed from 1909 to 1916 and houses the appellate court (<i>Oberlandesgericht</i>), the regional court (<i>Landgericht</i>), the local court (<i>Amtsgericht</i>) and the public prosecutor's office (<i><a href="/wiki/Staatsanwaltschaft" class="mw-redirect" title="Staatsanwaltschaft">Staatsanwaltschaft</a></i>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nuremberg_trials">Nuremberg trials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Palace_of_Justice,_Nuremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nuremberg trials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The building was the location of the <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_trials" title="Nuremberg trials">Nuremberg trials</a> that were held from 1945 to 1949 for the main surviving German war criminals of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. The Palace of Justice was chosen as the site of the trials because it was almost undamaged, offered sufficient space and included a large prison complex. The choice of the city of Nuremberg was symbolic as the <a href="/wiki/Nazis" class="mw-redirect" title="Nazis">Nazis</a> had held large <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_Rallies" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuremberg Rallies">Nuremberg Rallies</a> in the city.</p>
<p>The trials took place in courtroom number 600, situated in the eastern wing of the palace of Justice. The courtroom is still used, especially for murder trials. At the end of the Nuremberg Trials the courtroom was refurbished, and is now smaller. A wall that had been removed during the trials in order to create more space was re-erected. In addition, the judges' bench was turned 90 degrees and is no longer situated in front of the window, but stands where the witness box was placed during the trials.</p>
<p>From the year 2000 on, Courtroom 600 could be visited by tourists, during weekends. On December 2008, the courtroom was closed to the public due to construction works creating a permanent exhibition. The <i>Memorial Nuremberg Trials</i> hosted by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_Municipal_Museums&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nuremberg Municipal Museums (page does not exist)">Nuremberg Municipal Museums</a> was opened in November 2010.</p>
