<p><b>InterCity</b> (commonly abbreviated <i><b>IC</b></i> on timetables and tickets) is the classification applied to certain long-distance <a href="/wiki/Passenger_train" class="mw-redirect" title="Passenger train">passenger train</a> services in Europe. Such trains (in contrast to regional, local, or commuter trains) generally call at major <a href="/wiki/Railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Railway station">stations</a> only.</p>
<p>The Inter-City Rapid Transit Company was an <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a> <a href="/wiki/Interurban" title="Interurban">interurban</a> company, which began operations in 1930 as it had purchased its route from the Northern Ohio Traction &amp; Light Company. It remained in operation till 1940.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The use of <i>Inter-City</i> was reborn in the United Kingdom: A daily <a href="/wiki/The_Inter-City" title="The Inter-City">train of that name</a> was introduced in 1950, running between the cities of <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a>. This usage can claim to be the origin of all later usages worldwide.</p>
<p>In 1966 <a href="/wiki/British_Rail" title="British Rail">British Rail</a> introduced the brand InterCity for all of its express train routes, and in 1986 the term was adopted by the <a href="/wiki/InterCity_(British_Rail)" title="InterCity (British Rail)">InterCity sector of British Rail</a>. Following the privatisation of the railways in Great Britain the term is no longer in official use there, although many people still refer to fast long-distance services as InterCity trains. The brand still exists though, and according to <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Transport" title="Department for Transport">DfT</a> is owned by them.</p>
<p>In West Germany, the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Bundesbahn" title="Deutsche Bundesbahn">Deutsche Bundesbahn</a> first used the name (then written <i>Intercity</i>) in 1968, denoting special first-class services on the <a href="/wiki/F-Zug" class="mw-redirect" title="F-Zug">F-Zug</a> train network. Many of the <a href="/wiki/DB_Class_VT_11.5" title="DB Class VT 11.5">Class VT 11.5</a> <a href="/wiki/Diesel_multiple_units" class="mw-redirect" title="Diesel multiple units">diesel multiple units</a> formerly used on the <a href="/wiki/Trans_Europ_Express" title="Trans Europ Express">TEE</a> network were converted for early Intercity services.</p>
<p>In Switzerland, the InterCity brand replaced SwissExpress in the 1982 schedule.</p>
<p>In Norway, <i>intercity</i> (later also written <i>InterCity</i>) trains were introduced in 1975 on the Vestfold Line (the <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a>-<a href="/wiki/Skien" title="Skien">Skien</a> service), later also on the Østfold Line (Oslo-<a href="/wiki/Halden" title="Halden">Halden</a>). They were (relatively) fast trains on distances up to 2–3 hours. Today, the name is used not on the trains, but on the main lines from Oslo to Skien, <a href="/wiki/Lillehammer" title="Lillehammer">Lillehammer</a>, and Halden – and also on the Ringerike Line, which is under construction from Oslo to Hønefoss.</p>
<p>An international variant of the InterCity are the <a href="/wiki/EuroCity" title="EuroCity">EuroCity</a> (EC) trains which were introduced in May 1987. EuroCity trains consist of high-standard, <a href="/wiki/Air_conditioned" class="mw-redirect" title="Air conditioned">Air conditioned</a> coaches and are usually subject to on-board border controls. EuroCity trains are run by a variety of operators, for example EuroCity trains running in Germany can be made up by rolling stock of either the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Federal_Railways" title="Swiss Federal Railways">SBB</a> (Switzerland), <a href="/wiki/%C3%96BB" class="mw-redirect" title="ÖBB">ÖBB</a> (Austria) and the <a href="/wiki/SNCF" title="SNCF">SNCF</a> (France), but also less commonly by the Czech <a href="/wiki/%C4%8CD" class="mw-redirect" title="ČD">ČD</a> and the Hungarian <a href="/wiki/M%C3%81V" class="mw-redirect" title="MÁV">MÁV</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="InterCity_by_country">InterCity by country</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=InterCity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: InterCity by country">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Austria">Austria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=InterCity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Austria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Federal_Railways" title="Austrian Federal Railways">Austrian Federal Railways</a> (ÖBB) have operated IC services since 1991. However, contrary to most other countries, these are often little more than <a href="/wiki/Regional_rail" title="Regional rail">regional rail</a>, as most long-distance, high-standard trains in Austria are likely to be <a href="/wiki/EuroCity" title="EuroCity">EuroCity</a> (EC) services, even when not leaving the Austrian borders (named <i>ÖBB-EuroCity</i> until 2011). Modernised stock of <a href="/wiki/Eurofima_coach" title="Eurofima coach">Eurofima coaches</a> is used under the brand name <i>ÖBB-InterCity</i> (OIC) mainly on the Austrian <a href="/wiki/Western_Railway_(Austria)" title="Western Railway (Austria)">Western</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southern_Railway_(Austria)" title="Southern Railway (Austria)">Southern Railways</a> from <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> to <a href="/wiki/Salzburg" title="Salzburg">Salzburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Villach" title="Villach">Villach</a>.</p>
<p>The ÖBB also deployed <a href="/wiki/Electric_multiple_unit" title="Electric multiple unit">electric multiple unit</a> trains, from 2006 also three <a href="/wiki/ICE_T" title="ICE T">ICE T</a> (Class 411, named <i>ÖBB 4011</i>) trainsets in cooperation with the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Bahn" title="Deutsche Bahn">Deutsche Bahn</a>, currently running from Vienna to <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a> via <a href="/wiki/Linz" title="Linz">Linz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Passau" title="Passau">Passau</a>. ÖBB <a href="/wiki/High-speed_rail" title="High-speed rail">high-speed rail</a> service is provided by <a href="/wiki/Railjet" title="Railjet">Railjet</a> (RJ) trains.</p>
<p>Since 2011, there is an hourly express train service on the Western Railway operated by <a href="/wiki/WESTbahn" title="WESTbahn">WESTbahn</a>, the only long-distance competitor of the ÖBB.</p>
<p>IC-Services:</p>
<ul>
<li>Western Railway
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>–<a href="/wiki/Linz" title="Linz">Linz</a>–<a href="/wiki/Salzburg" title="Salzburg">Salzburg</a> (trains are operating from 5 am to 10 pm, hourly)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Southern Railway
<ul>

<p>InterCity trains link all major cities of Belgium. Some of them serve also destinations outside the country. The IC between <a href="/wiki/Li%C3%A8ge" title="Liège">Liège</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a> travels at 200&#160;km/h on the <a href="/wiki/HSL_2" title="HSL 2">HSL 2</a>. <p>Most of the IC are served by trains of state-owned operator <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Cesk%C3%A9_dr%C3%A1hy" title="České dráhy">České dráhy</a> with speeds up to 160&#160;km/h but they have been converted into category Express in 2012 timetable. The other is served by trains of private operator <a href="/wiki/RegioJet" title="RegioJet">RegioJet</a> with speed up to 160&#160;km/h. No surcharge is applied to IC trains but RegioJet runs reservation compulsory trains. Third private passenger operator <a href="/wiki/LEO_Express" title="LEO Express">LEO Express</a> introduced express InterCity train service between Prague and Ostrava in November 2012, with speeds up to 160&#160;km/h.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The state-owned operator <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Cesk%C3%A9_dr%C3%A1hy" title="České dráhy">České dráhy</a> also serves line <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>–<a href="/wiki/Ostrava" title="Ostrava">Ostrava</a> (with some connections extended to <a href="/wiki/Bohum%C3%ADn" title="Bohumín">Bohumín</a> and <a href="/wiki/Franti%C5%A1kovy_L%C3%A1zn%C4%9B" title="Františkovy Lázně">Františkovy Lázně</a>) with <a href="/wiki/Pendolino" title="Pendolino">Pendolino</a> trainsets under designation "<i><a href="/wiki/Supercity_Pendolino" class="mw-redirect" title="Supercity Pendolino">SuperCity</a></i>", which conforms to IC standard. A compulsory reservation is applied on these trains.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Denmark">Denmark</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=InterCity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Denmark">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Intercity network of the <a href="/wiki/Danish_State_Railways" class="mw-redirect" title="Danish State Railways">Danish State Railways</a> consists of IC trains and their faster version, <i>Lyntog</i> (Lightning Train), which is identical but with fewer stops. Each train type operates hourly between the eastern terminus at Copenhagen and westwards to <a href="/wiki/Odense" title="Odense">Odense</a>–<a href="/wiki/%C3%85rhus" class="mw-redirect" title="Århus">Århus</a>–<a href="/wiki/%C3%85lborg" class="mw-redirect" title="Ålborg">Ålborg</a>, and less frequently to alternative destinations in <a href="/wiki/Jutland" title="Jutland">Jutland</a>. These are run by <a href="/wiki/IC3" title="IC3">IC3</a> diesel materiel since most of the network is not electrified. There are also electrical IC trains run by <a href="/wiki/IR4" class="mw-redirect" title="IR4">IR4s</a> in an hourly schedule from Copenhagen westwards to <a href="/wiki/Odense" title="Odense">Odense</a> and alternately <a href="/wiki/Esbjerg" title="Esbjerg">Esbjerg</a>/<a href="/wiki/S%C3%B8nderborg" title="Sønderborg">Sønderborg</a>. This means during most of the day there are three trains an hour between Copenhagen and Odense. Quite unusual in the world, some trains will consist of both electrically and diesel-powered units coupled together. Being the only option for long-distance and some short-distance travel, there is no surcharge for IC and Lyntog. They have a maximum speed of 180&#160;km/h. Additionally, there a few IR trains during Friday and Sunday peak hours between Copenhagen and Århus. These are locomotive-run and have <a href="/wiki/Bilevel_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Bilevel car">bilevel cars</a>. The IC3 trains are planned to be replaced by new <a href="/wiki/IC4" title="IC4">IC4</a> trains, originally in 2001. They first ran with passengers in 2008, but haven't nearly replaced the <a href="/wiki/IC3" title="IC3">IC3</a> yet.</p>

<p>In Finland, <a href="/wiki/VR_Group" title="VR Group">VR</a> has operated InterCity trains between major Finnish cities since August 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-tm-13-1988_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tm-13-1988-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vr-ic_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vr-ic-4">[4]</a></sup> The first routes were Helsinki–Vaasa and Helsinki–Imatra,<sup id="cite_ref-tm-13-1988_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tm-13-1988-3">[3]</a></sup> which later expanded to all major cities and include for instance Helsinki–Tampere–Oulu–Rovaniemi, Helsinki–Turku, Helsinki–Iisalmi and Helsinki–Joensuu. InterCity trains have become the standard as the Finnish long distance rail travel mainstay, and their predecessors, <a href="/wiki/VR_blue_carriages" title="VR blue carriages">blue-carriaged</a> express trains, are being withdrawn off the schedules.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The train tickets include an additional surcharge compared to ordinary express trains: 17 to 27% depending of the journey length.</p>
<p>VR operates both ordinary InterCity trains (IC) and entirely double-decker trains (IC²).<sup id="cite_ref-vr-ic2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vr-ic2-6">[6]</a></sup> An ordinary IC train usually consists of 3 to 4 double-decker cars and 3 to 5 ordinary IC cars. In addition the train has a restaurant car.<sup id="cite_ref-vr-ic_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vr-ic-4">[4]</a></sup> IC² trains consist only of double-decker cars and have no separate restaurant car with a sales trolley moving about in the train.<sup id="cite_ref-vr-ic2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vr-ic2-6">[6]</a></sup> However, due to high demand, VR ordered double-decker restaurant cars from Transtech in early 2011. The first carriages should be operational in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> IC cars have separately ventilated smoking cabins instead of smokers' compartments; IC² trains are smoke-free.<sup id="cite_ref-vr-ic2_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vr-ic2-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The trains run at a maximum speed of 140–160&#160;km/h for single-decker trains<sup id="cite_ref-vr-ic_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vr-ic-4">[4]</a></sup> and up to 200 kilometres per hour (120&#160;mph) for double-deckers,<sup id="cite_ref-vr-ic2_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vr-ic2-6">[6]</a></sup> which was VR's aim in 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-tm-13-1988_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tm-13-1988-3">[3]</a></sup> Only the <i><a href="/wiki/VR_Class_Sm3" title="VR Class Sm3">Pendolino</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Karelian_Trains_Class_Sm6" class="mw-redirect" title="Karelian Trains Class Sm6">Allegro</a></i> trains are faster than InterCity trains in Finland.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=InterCity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006, the <a href="/wiki/SNCF" title="SNCF">SNCF</a> rebranded Corail, Téoz and Lunéa services as '<a href="/wiki/Intercit%C3%A9s" title="Intercités">Intercités</a>', a brand for all of their 'classic' services day and night.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=InterCity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Intercity_(Deutsche_Bahn)" title="Intercity (Deutsche Bahn)">Intercity (Deutsche Bahn)</a></div>
<p>In Germany, the InterCity network was launched in 1971 to accompany and eventually replace the <a href="/wiki/Trans_Europ_Express" title="Trans Europ Express">Trans Europ Express</a> trains. At first, IC services were first-class only, often using TEE stock and the then-new <a href="/wiki/DB_Class_103" title="DB Class 103">Class 103</a> locomotives. Trains ran bi-hourly. DB paid a royalty fee to <a href="/wiki/British_Rail" title="British Rail">BR</a> for many years for the use of the <a href="/wiki/Brand_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Brand name">brand name</a>.</p>
<p>In 1978, it was decided to expand the IC network to services with both first and second class, and so the new scheme, called <i><b>IC '79</b></i> was launched in 1979 with the motto <i>"Jede Stunde, jede Klasse"</i> ("every hour, every class") to emphasize its new structure. Large numbers of air-conditioned open coach cars, the <i>Bpmz 291</i>, were built for InterCity services, which at first were using the TEE colour scheme. In 1985, with many of the TEE trains gone and the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/InterRegio" title="InterRegio">InterRegio</a>, the network was expanded again, now covering virtually any major city of then-<a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>. It faced further changes after the <a href="/wiki/German_reunification" title="German reunification">German reunification</a> and the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/InterCityExpress" class="mw-redirect" title="InterCityExpress">InterCityExpress</a> in the early 1990s.</p>
<p>Today, after the abolition of the InterRegio in 2002, most long distance connections in Germany are either IC or ICE trains; they most commonly offer at least bi-hourly service. Maximum speed for an IC is 200&#160;km/h.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ireland">Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=InterCity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/InterCity_(Iarnr%C3%B3d_%C3%89ireann)" title="InterCity (Iarnród Éireann)">InterCity (Iarnród Éireann)</a></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iarnr%C3%B3d_%C3%89ireann" title="Iarnród Éireann">Iarnród Éireann</a> introduced the brand name InterCity in 1984, replacing the previous name of <i>Mainline</i>, which had been introduced in 1976. Initially applied to services operated by <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Mark_3" title="British Rail Mark 3">British Rail Mark 3</a> trains, it was later extended to include all services not part of the <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Suburban_Rail" title="Dublin Suburban Rail">Dublin Suburban Rail</a> network. Today the brand encompasses services between <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cork_(city)" title="Cork (city)">Cork</a>, <a href="/wiki/Galway" title="Galway">Galway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Limerick" title="Limerick">Limerick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Waterford" title="Waterford">Waterford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sligo" title="Sligo">Sligo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Westport,_County_Mayo" title="Westport, County Mayo">Westport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rosslare_Europort_railway_station" title="Rosslare Europort railway station">Rosslare Europort</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ballina,_County_Mayo" title="Ballina, County Mayo">Ballina</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ennis" title="Ennis">Ennis</a>, as well as some regional services. A new InterCity logo was introduced in 2006, though the vast majority of rolling stock bore the original script logo and orange-tan livery until the final Mark 3 set was withdrawn in 2009. Since Ireland completed its replacement of the old Mark 2 and Mark 3 stock in 2012, it has one of the most modern InterCity fleets in the world.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Railways" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Ireland Railways">Northern Ireland Railways</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iarnr%C3%B3d_%C3%89ireann" title="Iarnród Éireann">Iarnród Éireann</a> both formerly operated trains on the <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>–<a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a> line under the InterCity brand, however this was replaced with the revived <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_(train)" class="mw-redirect" title="Enterprise (train)">Enterprise</a> brand name upon the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/De_Dietrich_Ferroviaire" title="De Dietrich Ferroviaire">De Dietrich Ferroviaire</a> rolling stock in 1997. This, coupled with the subsequent withdrawal of most coaching stock bearing the logo and the rebrand to the Translink name, means that the InterCity brand has largely disappeared from Northern Ireland Railways.</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/Italian_Railways" class="mw-redirect" title="Italian Railways">Italy</a>, InterCity trains constitute a capillary network that links the main cities across the peninsula. There are over 80 day (Intercity) and night (Intercity Notte) services. The major north-south connections are Turin-Lecce (night trains only), Milan-Bari, Bolzano/Bozen-Bari, Venice-Bari, Milan-Rome and Trieste-Naples. In the south, Intercity connections consist of Rome-Palermo, Rome-Bari, Rome-Naples and Rome-Reggio di Calabria. In the north, Intercity connections consist of Turin-Genoa, Milan-Genoa, Milan-Livorno and Venice-Florence.</p>
<p>The fleet consists in first and second class wagons with open-seat or six-seat compartments. Night trains include sleeper compartments of three types: bunker for four travellers, two-bed "double" sleeper and one-bed "single" sleeper. There are, however, no showering facilities on all Intercity Notte trains. The newer wagons, called InterCity Plus, have their interiors renovated and sockets for PC or mobile phones added to each compartment. However Excelsior cabins only shown on the Rome To Sicily night train claim to have their own toilet and shower in the cabin, information from Trenitalia web pages&#160;:</p>
<p>The cars Excelsior, available on some trains Night, offer you services comparable to those of a high-level Hotel, every vehicle available cabins&#160;: • Single: a cabin with a bed, • Double rooms&#160;: one twin cabin, • Doubles: a large suite with double bed with all the amenities. Each cabin Excelsior E4 instead offers 4 beds for travel from 1 to 4 persons&#160;: Single: a cabin with a bed, Doubles&#160;: one twin cabin, Triple&#160;: a three-bed cabin Quadruple&#160;: a four-bed cabin All cabins are equipped with shower and wash basin with private toilet . High cab there is a reception with bar service. Excelsior single and double cabins are available from all sales channels, while the triple and quadruple only at the ticket and authorized travel agencies . If travel in these cars you must have a personal identification document.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Netherlands">Netherlands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=InterCity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Netherlands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="thumbcaption">

Dutch Intercity network</div>

<p>Intercity trains are very common in the Netherlands, but may differ from each other. While some intercity trains only call at larger stations, some other may also call at smaller stations or at all intermediate stations along a short stretch of its route. Hence, some stations may be served by one intercity train while another may pass it. For example, the Intercity trains between <a href="/wiki/Arnhem_Centraal_railway_station" title="Arnhem Centraal railway station">Arnhem Central station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Utrecht_Centraal_railway_station" title="Utrecht Centraal railway station">Utrecht Central station</a> alternately serve <a href="/wiki/Driebergen-Zeist_railway_station" title="Driebergen-Zeist railway station">Driebergen-Zeist</a> or <a href="/wiki/Veenendaal-De_Klomp_railway_station" title="Veenendaal-De Klomp railway station">Veenendaal-De Klomp</a> (and both when the frequency is lowered from 4tph to 2tph in the evening). Often, intercity trains run as local trains along the very end of its route. For example, the <a href="/wiki/Nijmegen_railway_station" title="Nijmegen railway station">Nijmegen</a> to <a href="/wiki/Den_Helder_railway_station" title="Den Helder railway station">Den Helder</a> intercity runs as regular intercity until Alkmaar, serving larger stations only, but running as local train between Alkmaar and Den Helder, serving all intermediate stations.</p>
<p>The average distance between station is smaller than most IC trains in most other countries, but frequencies are higher. All lines are served at least with 2tph, but many busy stretches are served have 4tph. From Utrecht Central there are even two different services to Amsterdam, one that serves the <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Centraal_station" title="Amsterdam Centraal station">central station</a> and two others and another that serves the <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Zuid_station" title="Amsterdam Zuid station">south station</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Schiphol_Airport_railway_station" title="Schiphol Airport railway station">airport</a>. From December 2017 the frequency between Amsterdam, Utrecht and Eindhoven will be further increased to 6tph.</p>
<p>Rolling stock used mostly for intercity services in the Netherlands is <a href="/wiki/NS_VIRM" title="NS VIRM">VIRM</a>. <a href="/wiki/NS_DD-AR#Modernisation" title="NS DD-AR">DD-AR</a> trains have been converted to be used as <i>New Intercity Double deckers</i> and have been introduced to parts of the intercity network. Also, older <a href="/wiki/NS_Intercity_Materieel" title="NS Intercity Materieel">ICM</a> (formerly known as Koploper) EMUs are frequently seen on intercity routes. <a href="/wiki/Trains_in_the_Netherlands#Coaches" title="Trains in the Netherlands">ICRm coaches</a>, pulled by a <a href="/wiki/Bombardier_TRAXX" class="mw-redirect" title="Bombardier TRAXX">TRAXX</a> locomotive, can be seen on international stretches and the <a href="/wiki/Intercity_Direct" title="Intercity Direct">Intercity Direct</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Romania">Romania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=InterCity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Romania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Currently InterCity lines link the capital <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a> to <a href="/wiki/Bra%C8%99ov" title="Brașov">Brașov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arad,_Romania" title="Arad, Romania">Arad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oradea" title="Oradea">Oradea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cluj-Napoca" title="Cluj-Napoca">Cluj-Napoca</a>, <a href="/wiki/T%C3%A2rgu_Mure%C8%99" title="Târgu Mureș">Târgu Mureș</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gala%C8%9Bi" title="Galați">Galați</a> (all once daily), <a href="/wiki/Suceava" title="Suceava">Suceava</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ia%C8%99i" title="Iași">Iași</a> (twice daily), <a href="/wiki/Piatra_Neam%C8%9B" title="Piatra Neamț">Piatra Neamț</a> (once daily), <a href="/wiki/Bac%C4%83u" title="Bacău">Bacău</a> (4x daily), <a href="/wiki/Ploie%C8%99ti" title="Ploiești">Ploiești</a> (6x daily), <a href="/wiki/Craiova" title="Craiova">Craiova</a> (twice daily) and <a href="/wiki/Timi%C8%99oara" title="Timișoara">Timișoara</a> (once daily). There are also international trains branded as InterCity between the Hungarian capital, <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a> and some <a href="/wiki/Transylvania" title="Transylvania">Transylvanian</a> cities such as <a href="/wiki/Cluj-Napoca" title="Cluj-Napoca">Cluj-Napoca</a>, <a href="/wiki/T%C3%A2rgu_Mure%C8%99" title="Târgu Mureș">Târgu Mureș</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bra%C8%99ov" title="Brașov">Brașov</a>, and others. These trains usually have specific names (<a href="/wiki/Harghita" class="mw-redirect" title="Harghita">Harghita</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ady_Endre" class="mw-redirect" title="Ady Endre">Ady Endre</a>, etc.).</p>
<p>In the case of IC service running partially or totally on electrified lines as well as international IC-s locomotive-hauled trains are used. For some trains running exclusively on unelectrified lines (mainly in northern <a href="/wiki/Transilvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Transilvania">Transilvania</a>) <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens</a> <a href="/wiki/Desiro" class="mw-redirect" title="Desiro">Desiro</a> DMU-s are preferred. These trainsets look similar to standard Desiro DMU-s used for regional trains but have a different interior design with more comfortable seating.</p>
<p>All IC trains use 1st and 2nd class coaches with air conditioning, having automatic doors and a higher level of comfort than InterRegio trains. In order to compete with regional airlines, <a href="/w/index.php?title=CFR_C%C4%83l%C4%83tori&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="CFR Călători (page does not exist)">CFR Călători</a> introduced special <b>Business class</b> coaches on the IC trains running between <a href="/wiki/Oradea" title="Oradea">Oradea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timi%C8%99oara" title="Timișoara">Timișoara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a>. The new class can be divided into two slightly different premium service levels, <i>Business Exclusiv</i> (1A) and <i>Business Standard</i> (1B). All of these coaches have wireless internet and wall sockets in order to permit the use of laptops, 4 channel audio system, bar, Exclusiv further providing larger space per customer, special leather seating and LCD screens for each seat.</p>
<p>Maximum speed is 140&#160;km/h on Bucharest-Campina, 120&#160;km/h on all other lines.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Serbia">Serbia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=InterCity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Serbia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Currently InterCity lines link the capital <a href="/wiki/Belgrade" title="Belgrade">Belgrade</a> to <a href="/wiki/Novi_Sad" title="Novi Sad">Novi Sad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Subotica" title="Subotica">Subotica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prijepolje" title="Prijepolje">Prijepolje</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Slovakia">Slovakia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=InterCity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Slovakia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Slovak Republic">Slovak Republic</a>, InterCity trains run between the capital <a href="/wiki/Bratislava" title="Bratislava">Bratislava</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ko%C5%A1ice" title="Košice">Košice</a> (some IC trains continued from Bratislava to <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>). In the past, the names of some of these trains used to be sold for commercial use (IC <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0ari%C5%A1" title="Šariš">Šariš</a> named by beer producer, IC <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zelmer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zelmer (page does not exist)">Zelmer</a> named by an electronic devices producer, IC <a href="/w/index.php?title=Slovenka_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Slovenka (magazine) (page does not exist)">Slovenka</a> named by a magazine, IC Mora, IC Gorenje, both named after kitchen appliances producers). In 2012, no IC train had commercial name. All IC trains are named by natural monuments (IC Tatran, IC Kriváň, IC Gerlach, IC Rysy, IC Chopok, IC Ďumbier). All IC trains are subject to <a href="/w/index.php?title=Compulsory_reservation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Compulsory reservation (page does not exist)">compulsory reservation</a> to make comfort as high as possible. The seat reservation is included in the ticket price. The price varies, depending on the day of travel, and the time of reservation (the sooner you buy a ticket, the lower the price).</p>
<p>In the past, there were also 3 other InterCity services: IC 400/407 Donau (Danube) between Bratislava and Vienna, IC 532/533 <a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%A1koczi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rákoczi (page does not exist)">Rákoczi</a> from Košice to <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a> and IC 536/537 <a href="/wiki/Horn%C3%A1d" title="Hornád">Hornád</a> from Košice to <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A9cs" title="Pécs">Pécs</a>. On this train passengers could travel without a reservation or surcharge, since it is impossible to use them for domestic transport in Slovakia.</p>
<p>All InterCity trains consist of new comfortable <a href="/wiki/Passenger_car_(rail)" title="Passenger car (rail)">cars</a> which are fully <a href="/wiki/Air_conditioning" title="Air conditioning">air conditioned</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/First_class_travel#Railways" title="First class travel">First class</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Restaurant_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Restaurant car">restaurant car</a> are available on all IC trains.</p>
<p>Maximum speed between <a href="/wiki/Bratislava" title="Bratislava">Bratislava</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nov%C3%A9_Mesto_nad_V%C3%A1hom" title="Nové Mesto nad Váhom">Nové Mesto nad Váhom</a> has been increased in the recent years and is now 160&#160;km/h. On the rest of the route, maximum speed varies from 80 to 140&#160;km/h. There is a large reconstruction under way between Nové Mesto nad Váhom and Žilina. After the reconstruction is finished, it will be possible to increase maximum speed of this part of the route to 160&#160;km/h.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Slovenia">Slovenia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=InterCity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Slovenia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Intercity trains in Slovenia mainly serve domestic routes, like running from Ljubljana (capital) to Celje, Ptuj, and Maribor. Comfort in Slovenian Intercity trains is not as good as in other countries. Coaches serving main routes are usually old. Some IC trains do not offer first class and restaurant carriage. The type of cars is basically the same as the cars of ÖBB, however, the cars are slightly older with no sign of renovation. Additionally, there are also available older Pendolino electric multiple tilting units ETR 310 (SŽ series 310) labeled as ICS lines connecting Slovenian largest cities, Ljubljana and Maribor and the Mediterranean region of Slovenia, Koper.</p>

<p>During the 1980s, InterCity denoted trains of the highest standard in Sweden, serving as a fast and comfortable connection between major Swedish cities. Trains used to be set up of the most modern cars, always including a restaurant car. During the 1990s the <a href="/wiki/SJ_AB" title="SJ AB">Swedish State Railways</a> introduced the new <a href="/wiki/X_2000" title="X 2000">X 2000</a> units which replaced the InterCity trains in this role. Since then, the InterCity trains serve an auxiliary role, calling at smaller stations and providing a cheaper alternative for the costly X 2000. They are set up of modernised cars pulled by <a href="/wiki/SJ_Rc" title="SJ Rc">Rc6 locomotives</a> with a maximum speed of 160&#160;km/h. They serve the following lines:</p>

Some trains continue from Borlänge via <a href="/wiki/Gagnef" title="Gagnef">Gagnef</a> – <a href="/wiki/Leksand" title="Leksand">Leksand</a> – <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A4ttvik" title="Rättvik">Rättvik</a> towards <a href="/wiki/Mora,_Sweden" title="Mora, Sweden">Mora</a>;</li>

<p>Swiss InterCity services started in 1982, replacing the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Swiss_Express&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Swiss Express (page does not exist)">Swiss Express</a> on the line Geneva-St. Gallen. There is no surcharge for InterCity services in Switzerland and the rolling stock consists of three types of formations:<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Standard type IV carriages (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>EW IV</i></span>, Einheitswagen IV) and driving trailers of type IC, the same as on <a href="/wiki/InterRegio" title="InterRegio">InterRegio</a> services. A normal train composition consists of a <a href="/wiki/Re_460" class="mw-redirect" title="Re 460">Re 460</a> locomotive, 8 to 12 carriages and a driving trailer. Seating capacity is up to 900, depending on the train composition. Most carriages have a small compartment for storing bicycles and/or skis at one end of the carriage; this end is marked by a bicycle drawn on the outside of the closest doors. The type IV carriages are the least wheelchair friendly as they require climbing the stairs to enter. Overall, 533 carriages and driving trailers are in operation.</li>
<li>Double-decker <a href="/wiki/IC_2000" title="IC 2000">IC 2000</a> carriages for lines with heavy commuter load. These trains are also powered by a <a href="/wiki/Re_460" class="mw-redirect" title="Re 460">Re 460</a> locomotive, can be composed of up to 9 carriages with one driving trailer and offer close to 1,000 seats. At present, about 320 carriages are in use.</li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Tilting_train" title="Tilting train">tilting train</a> category called <a href="/wiki/SBB-CFF-FFS_RABDe_500" title="SBB-CFF-FFS RABDe 500">ICN</a> for lines with many curves. These trains are <a href="/wiki/Electric_multiple_unit" title="Electric multiple unit">electric multiple units</a> and hence require no locomotive. One such multiple unit consists of 7 carriages and has a <a href="/wiki/Seating_capacity" title="Seating capacity">seating capacity</a> of 480 passengers: 125 in first class, 332 in second class, and 23 in the dining car (which is part of second class). The fleet of ICN trains comprise 44 units.</li>
</ul>
<p>If needed, two ICN trains can be combined to double the passenger capacity. In addition, several type IV carriages can be attached to an IC 2000 train set on the other side of the locomotive, especially if a group reservation requires extra carriages.</p>
<p>All carriages are air conditioned. Each train formation has first and second class carriages and, in most cases, a restaurant/bistro carriage. The availability of power sockets is constantly improved, sometimes even in second class. The maximal speed of all train types is 200&#160;km/h, except for about half of type IV carriages that have not yet been upgraded and have a maximum speed of 160&#160;km/h.</p>

</ul>
<p>as well as the following routes served by tilting <a href="/wiki/SBB-CFF-FFS_RABDe_500" title="SBB-CFF-FFS RABDe 500">ICNs</a>:</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Darnytsia_Railway_Station" title="Darnytsia Railway Station">Kyiv Darnytsia</a> – <a href="/wiki/Lviv_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Lviv railway station">Lviv</a> – <a href="/wiki/Truskavets" title="Truskavets">Truskavets</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Darnytsia_Railway_Station" title="Darnytsia Railway Station">Kyiv Darnytsia</a> – <a href="/wiki/Odessa" title="Odessa">Odessa</a></li>
</ul>
<p>There were trains from Kharkiv and Dnipro to Crimea and from Kyiv to Donetsk. They were suspended in 2014, shortly after Ukraine have lost control of some of its territories. Currently, a shortened connection to Donbas is in service:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kyiv_Passenger_Railway_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Kyiv Passenger Railway Station">Kyiv</a> – <a href="/wiki/Sloviansk" title="Sloviansk">Sloviansk</a> – <a href="/wiki/Kostyantynivka" class="mw-redirect" title="Kostyantynivka">Kostyantynivka</a></li>
</ul>
<p>These services are operated by Hyundai Rotem <a href="/wiki/HRCS2_multiple_unit" title="HRCS2 multiple unit">HRCS2</a>, Škoda UZ class 675, <a href="/wiki/KVBZ" title="KVBZ">KVBZ</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ekr1&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ekr1 (page does not exist)">Ekr1</a> "Tarpan" trains, and <a href="/wiki/KVBZ" title="KVBZ">KVBZ</a> "Ukrajina" locomotive-hauled train <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.uz.gov.ua/passengers/scheme_of_passenger_car/354920/">[3]</a>.</p>
<p>Intercity (70&#160;km/h – 90&#160;km/h) train services in Ukraine <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://intercity.uz.gov.ua/?page_id=27">[4]</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Darnytsia_Railway_Station" title="Darnytsia Railway Station">Kyiv Darnytsia</a> – <a href="/wiki/Lviv_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Lviv railway station">Lviv</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kyiv_Passenger_Railway_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Kyiv Passenger Railway Station">Kyiv</a> – <a href="/wiki/Kryvyi_Rih" title="Kryvyi Rih">Kryvyi Rih</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kharkiv_Railway_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Kharkiv Railway Station">Kharkiv</a> – <a href="/wiki/Kyiv_Passenger_Railway_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Kyiv Passenger Railway Station">Kyiv</a> – <a href="/wiki/Vinnytsia" title="Vinnytsia">Vinnytsia</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kharkiv_Railway_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Kharkiv Railway Station">Kharkiv</a> – <a href="/wiki/Donetsk" title="Donetsk">Donetsk</a> (Suspended in 2014)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dnipro" title="Dnipro">Dnipro</a> – <a href="/wiki/Donetsk" title="Donetsk">Donetsk</a> (Suspended in 2014)</li>

<p>Intercity and Intercity+ trains are operated by Ukrainian fastspeed railway company railway company <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://intercity.uz.gov.ua/?page_id=27">[5]</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=InterCity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/InterCity_(British_Rail)" title="InterCity (British Rail)">InterCity (British Rail)</a></div>
<p>The term "Inter-City" was first used by state railway company <a href="/wiki/British_Rail" title="British Rail">British Rail</a> in 1966, to <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_brand_names" title="British Rail brand names">brand</a> all its longer-distance, higher-speed services (the hyphen was dropped shortly afterwards, changing the name to "InterCity"). The brand was closely associated with a new design of carriage, the <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Mark_2" class="mw-redirect" title="British Rail Mark 2">Mk2</a> which revolutionised levels of comfort on the system. The system was hugely successful and became one of the world's few profitable public railway services.</p>
<p>Today, Britain's railways having been privatised, InterCity trains in the UK are operated by many different train companies including <a href="/wiki/Abellio_Greater_Anglia" class="mw-redirect" title="Abellio Greater Anglia">Abellio Greater Anglia</a>, <a href="/wiki/CrossCountry" title="CrossCountry">CrossCountry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Railway_(train_operating_company)" title="Great Western Railway (train operating company)">Great Western Railway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Trains" title="Virgin Trains">Virgin Trains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Trains_East_Coast" title="Virgin Trains East Coast">Virgin Trains East Coast</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Midlands_Trains" title="East Midlands Trains">East Midlands Trains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hull_Trains" title="Hull Trains">Hull Trains</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_Railway" title="Grand Central Railway">Grand Central</a>. Most of these companies operate their services from one of the many London termini, with <a href="/wiki/CrossCountry" title="CrossCountry">CrossCountry</a> being the exception, running from <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> to <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>. If travelling via London it is often necessary to change stations: a journey from <a href="/wiki/Norwich" title="Norwich">Norwich</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a> would require a transfer from <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Street_station" title="Liverpool Street station">London Liverpool Street</a> to <a href="/wiki/London_Paddington" class="mw-redirect" title="London Paddington">London Paddington</a> station via the <a href="/wiki/London_Underground" title="London Underground">London Underground</a>.</p>
<p>Currently, InterCity trains in United Kingdom use <a href="/wiki/InterCity_125" title="InterCity 125">InterCity 125</a> (Class 43 power car + 8/9 Mark 3 coaches, also named as HST), <a href="/wiki/InterCity_225" title="InterCity 225">InterCity 225</a> (Class 91 + 9 Mark 4 coaches), <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_90" title="British Rail Class 90">Class 90</a> + 8/9 Mark 3 coaches + <a href="/wiki/Driving_Van_Trailer" title="Driving Van Trailer">Driving Van Trailer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bombardier_Voyager_family" title="Bombardier Voyager family">Voyager family</a> classes <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_220" title="British Rail Class 220">220</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_221" title="British Rail Class 221">221</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_222" title="British Rail Class 222">222</a> (4–7 coach DEMUs), <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_180" title="British Rail Class 180">class 180</a> (5 coach DMUs) and <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_390" title="British Rail Class 390">class 390</a> (9 or 11 coach EMUs) on the Intercity lines.</p>
<p>The British Government have named their project to replace the <a href="/wiki/InterCity_125" title="InterCity 125">InterCity 125</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Intercity_Express_Programme" title="Intercity Express Programme">Intercity Express Programme</a>.</p>
