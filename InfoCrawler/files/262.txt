<p><i><b>Nuremberg</b></i> is a 2000 <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a>/<a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> television docudrama, based on the book <i>Nuremberg: Infamy on Trial</i> by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_E._Persico" title="Joseph E. Persico">Joseph E. Persico</a>, that tells the story of the <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_trials" title="Nuremberg trials">Nuremberg trials</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_summary">Plot summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_(2000_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Part_one">Part one</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_(2000_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Part one">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the close of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hermann_G%C3%B6ring" title="Hermann Göring">Hermann Göring</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brian_Cox_(actor)" title="Brian Cox (actor)">Brian Cox</a>) surrenders to the Americans and enjoys the hospitality of a <a href="/wiki/U.S._Army_Air_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Army Air Force">U.S. Army Air Force</a> base. <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Rosenman" class="mw-redirect" title="Samuel Rosenman">Samuel Rosenman</a> (<a href="/wiki/Max_von_Sydow" title="Max von Sydow">Max von Sydow</a>), acting on the orders of <a href="/wiki/U.S._President" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. President">U.S. President</a> <a href="/wiki/Harry_S._Truman" title="Harry S. Truman">Harry S. Truman</a>, recruits <a href="/wiki/United_States_Supreme_Court" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Supreme Court">U.S. Supreme Court</a> Justice <a href="/wiki/Robert_H._Jackson" title="Robert H. Jackson">Robert H. Jackson</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alec_Baldwin" title="Alec Baldwin">Alec Baldwin</a>) to prepare a war crimes tribunal against Göring and the surviving <a href="/wiki/Nazi" class="mw-redirect" title="Nazi">Nazi</a> leadership. Göring, <a href="/wiki/Albert_Speer" title="Albert Speer">Albert Speer</a> (<a href="/wiki/Herbert_Knaup" title="Herbert Knaup">Herbert Knaup</a>) and others are arrested for <a href="/wiki/War_crime" title="War crime">war crimes</a> and imprisoned in a <a href="/wiki/U.S._Army" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Army">U.S. Army</a> stockade at <a href="/wiki/Mondorf-les-Bains" title="Mondorf-les-Bains">Bad Mondorf</a> in <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>. Jackson, his assistant Elsie Douglas (<a href="/wiki/Jill_Hennessy" title="Jill Hennessy">Jill Hennessy</a>), and his prosecution team fly to <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. Psychologist <a href="/wiki/Gustave_Gilbert" title="Gustave Gilbert">Gustave Gilbert</a> (<a href="/wiki/Matt_Craven" title="Matt Craven">Matt Craven</a>) arrives at the stockade with prisoner <a href="/wiki/Hans_Frank" title="Hans Frank">Hans Frank</a> (Frank Moore), who has attempted <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a>.</p>
<p>Jackson negotiates with <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied</a> representatives <a href="/wiki/David_Maxwell-Fyfe" class="mw-redirect" title="David Maxwell-Fyfe">Sir David Maxwell-Fyfe</a> (<a href="/wiki/Christopher_Plummer" title="Christopher Plummer">Christopher Plummer</a>), General <a href="/wiki/Iona_Nikitchenko" title="Iona Nikitchenko">Iona Nikitchenko</a> (Len Doncheff) and <a href="/wiki/Henri_Donnedieu_de_Vabres" title="Henri Donnedieu de Vabres">Henri Donnedieu de Vabres</a> (<a href="/wiki/Paul_H%C3%A9bert" title="Paul Hébert">Paul Hébert</a>) to ensure a unified prosecution. Jackson selects the <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_Palace_of_Justice" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuremberg Palace of Justice">Nuremberg Palace of Justice</a> for the site of the trials and reconstruction work commences. Göring and the others are stripped of their rank and transferred to the prison in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>, where they come into conflict with the guards under the command of the strict Colonel <a href="/wiki/Burton_C._Andrus" title="Burton C. Andrus">Burton C. Andrus</a> (<a href="/wiki/Michael_Ironside" title="Michael Ironside">Michael Ironside</a>). Major <a href="/wiki/Airey_Neave" title="Airey Neave">Airey Neave</a> (Geoffrey Pounsett) serves Göring, Speer and the others with their indictments. U.S. judge <a href="/wiki/Francis_Biddle" title="Francis Biddle">Francis Biddle</a> (<a href="/wiki/Len_Cariou" title="Len Cariou">Len Cariou</a>) arrives to take control of the court but reluctantly passes the honour at Jackson’s insistence. Following the <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a> of prisoner <a href="/wiki/Robert_Ley" title="Robert Ley">Robert Ley</a> (<a href="/wiki/Julien_Poulin" title="Julien Poulin">Julien Poulin</a>), round-the-clock watches are posted and Gilbert is appointed prisoner liaison.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Lawrence,_1st_Baron_Oaksey" title="Geoffrey Lawrence, 1st Baron Oaksey">Sir Geoffrey Lawrence</a> (David Francis) opens the trial with all defendants pleading not guilty, and Jackson gives a stirring opening statement. At lunch a jovial Göring holds court over the other defendants while Speer begins to show signs of remorse. Maxwell-Fyfe puts forward an emotive eyewitness account of the Nazis' <a href="/wiki/Genocide" title="Genocide">genocidal</a> policies toward <a href="/wiki/Jew" class="mw-redirect" title="Jew">Jews</a> and others, while Jackson reads out dry documentation. As the court begins to tire of Jackson’s meticulous approach, Maxwell-Fyfe urges pushing on to the witness interviews, which reveal the horrors of the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_concentration_camp" class="mw-redirect" title="Nazi concentration camp">concentration camps</a>. The court is shaken by documentary footage of the camps; even Göring appears unsettled.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Part_two">Part two</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_(2000_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Part two">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Speer explains Göring’s dominance to Gilbert and insists that his control over the others must be broken. Göring takes the stand and begins speaking to the <a href="/wiki/German_people" class="mw-redirect" title="German people">German people</a>. Jackson, at Gilbert’s suggestion, has Göring isolated. Under <a href="/wiki/Cross-examination" title="Cross-examination">cross-examination</a>, Göring outmanoeuvers and humiliates Jackson, who later accuses Biddle of giving Göring free rein in court. Douglas talks Jackson out of tendering his resignation, and the two share a kiss. Under advice from Maxwell-Fyfe, Jackson returns to confront Göring with evidence of his crimes against the Jews and successfully dismisses the defendant’s denials.</p>
<p>At a <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> party, the German housekeeper refuses to serve the <a href="/wiki/Soviet" class="mw-redirect" title="Soviet">Soviets</a>, but Douglas rescues the situation before slipping away with Jackson. Gilbert visits the defendants and, under Jackson’s advice, attempts to convince them to take responsibility for their crimes. Andrus relaxes the prison rules for Christmas, and Göring shares a friendly drink with his guard, Lt. Tex Wheelis (Scott Gibson). The cross-examination of the defendants intensifies and the defence calls <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_H%C3%B6%C3%9F" class="mw-redirect" title="Rudolf Höß">Rudolf Höß</a> (<a href="/wiki/Colm_Feore" title="Colm Feore">Colm Feore</a>), who casually reveals the horrors of <a href="/wiki/Auschwitz" class="mw-redirect" title="Auschwitz">Auschwitz</a>. Speer is implicated in the <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">enslavement</a> of foreign workers by fellow defendant <a href="/wiki/Fritz_Sauckel" title="Fritz Sauckel">Fritz Sauckel</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ken_Kramer" title="Ken Kramer">Ken Kramer</a>) and in response accepts collective responsibility for the crimes of the Nazi regime.</p>
<p>Gilbert interviews Göring’s wife <a href="/wiki/Emmy_G%C3%B6ring" title="Emmy Göring">Emmy</a> (<a href="/wiki/Susan_Glover" title="Susan Glover">Susan Glover</a>), who reveals that <a href="/wiki/Hitler" class="mw-redirect" title="Hitler">Hitler</a> had ordered them all executed, which led to the family’s surrender. Jackson is moved by Gilbert’s <a href="/wiki/Summation" title="Summation">summation</a> of his examinations — that the source of the evil behind <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> was a complete lack of <a href="/wiki/Empathy" title="Empathy">empathy</a> — to give an impassioned closing statement. Göring uses his final statement to condemn the trial, and is sentenced along with several others to death by <a href="/wiki/Hanging" title="Hanging">hanging</a>. Speer uses his final statement to commend the tribunal and is sentenced to 20 years in prison. Göring commits <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a> after his request to be executed by <a href="/wiki/Firing_squad" class="mw-redirect" title="Firing squad">firing squad</a> is denied. Andrus presides over the executions of the others while Jackson and Douglas head home.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_inaccuracies">Historical inaccuracies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuremberg_(2000_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Historical inaccuracies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Göring did not drive to an American base and surrender - he sent an aide to Brigadier General Robert I. Stack, offering to surrender to Eisenhower personally; subsequently, an American detachment was sent through the German lines to find him and bring him to a secure American position.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Also, <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Keitel" title="Wilhelm Keitel">Wilhelm Keitel</a> was described in the film as an admiral during the defendants' sentencing. He was in fact a field marshal and would not have been identified with naval rank.</p>
<p>In the film Jackson describes the Nuremberg's Justice Palace as "the same building where Nuremberg Laws were decreed to deprive all the German Jews all of their rights". In reality, the Nuremberg Laws were introduced by the Reichstag at a special meeting at the annual Nuremberg Rally of the NSDAP. Nuremberg's Justice Palace was, as it has always been, a regional court for the local area and the building had no association with the annual Party Rally during Nazi era.</p>
<p>Justice Jackson is portrayed as initially failing in his cross-examination of Gӧring and emerging triumphant on the second day. In reality, the cross-examination was a disaster and severely damaged Jackson's reputation. This situation was recovered by Maxwell Fyfe.</p>
<p>The verdicts and sentences were pronounced together with all defendants present. In reality, verdicts and sentences were pronounced separately and the defendants were called one at a time into the courtroom to learn their sentence. Andrus was not present at the executions.</p>
<p>When the defendants were indicted by Major Neave they all made verbal statements. In reality these statements were collected by Captain <a href="/wiki/Gustave_Gilbert" title="Gustave Gilbert">Gustave Gilbert</a>. He asked the defendants to write their first reactions on a copy of the indictments.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the film <a href="/wiki/Albert_Speer" title="Albert Speer">Albert Speer</a> was arrested when he was giving a lecture to American soldiers. In reality Speer was arrested together with <a href="/wiki/Karl_D%C3%B6nitz" title="Karl Dönitz">Karl Dönitz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Jodl" title="Alfred Jodl">Alfred Jodl</a> in <a href="/wiki/Flensburg" title="Flensburg">Flensburg</a> where they had set up a provisional government.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the film Captain Gilbert is graciously given the right to talk to the prisoners by Col. Andrus in exchange for a library and an exercise field. In reality Gilbert was specifically appointed to talk to the prisoners by the US military. The idea was that Andrus was informed by Gilbert about the state of mind of the prisoners. <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
