			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Nuremberg Symphony Orchestra</h1>

<p>The <b>Nuremberg Symphony Orchestra</b> (German: <b>Nürnberger Symphoniker</b>) is a German orchestra based in <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</a>. Its principal concert venue is the <i>Meistersingerhalle</i>. The orchestra's current <i>Intendant</i> (Managing and Artistic Director) is Lucius A. Hemmer, since September 2003. Since 2009, its chief conductor is <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Shelley" title="Alexander Shelley">Alexander Shelley</a>.</p>
<p>The orchestra began in 1946 as the <a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconia</a> State Orchestra (<i>Fränkisches Landesorchester</i>), with Erich Kloss as its first chief conductor. In the early 1950s, the orchestra accrued international acclaim for their recordings of the sound tracks to <a href="/wiki/Quo_Vadis_(1951_film)" title="Quo Vadis (1951 film)">Quo Vadis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ben-Hur_(1959_film)" title="Ben-Hur (1959 film)">Ben Hur</a> by <a href="/wiki/Mikl%C3%B3s_R%C3%B3zsa" title="Miklós Rózsa">Miklós Rózsa</a>.</p>
<p>The orchestra took its current name in 1963 for the dedication of the newly built <i>Meistersingerhalle</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Meistersingerhalle_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Meistersingerhalle-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1993, they won a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> in the category <i>Best Pop Instrumental Performance</i> for the soundtrack of <a href="/wiki/Beauty_and_the_Beast_(1991_film)" title="Beauty and the Beast (1991 film)">Beauty and the Beast</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Grammy_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grammy-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2008, the Nuremberg Symphony Orchestra has a new rehearsal and concert hall with a seating capacity of 515, the <i>Neuer Musiksaal</i>. During the summer, it also presents an open-air concert series in the <i>Serenadenhof</i>, the southern courtyard of the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_party_rally_grounds#Congress_Hall" title="Nazi party rally grounds">Congress Hall</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Alexander_Shelley" title="Alexander Shelley">Alexander Shelley</a> has been the Principal Conductor of the orchestra since 2009. He is scheduled to conclude his Nuremberg at the end of the 2016-2017 season. In January 2017, the orchestra announced the appointment of Kahchun Wong as its next chief conductor, effective with the 2018-2019 season.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>After serving as festival orchestra of the Heidenheim Opera Festival for 25 years, the Nuremberg Symphony took over the same position at Frankonian Summer Festival in 2013. The orchestra has recorded both for the Bavarian radio (<a href="/wiki/Bayerischer_Rundfunk" title="Bayerischer Rundfunk">Bayerischer Rundfunk</a>) and for commercial CD labels. Every summer, the orchestra participates in Europe's largest open air classical music event, <i>Klassik Open Air</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Stats3_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stats3-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
