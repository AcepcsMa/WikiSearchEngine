			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Christof Perick</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Christof Prick</b> (born 1946), who uses the name <b>Christof Perick</b> in English-speaking countries, is a German <a href="/wiki/Orchestra" title="Orchestra">orchestra</a> <a href="/wiki/Conducting" title="Conducting">conductor</a>. His father was the concertmaster of the Hamburg Philharmonic.</p>
<p>Perick's conducting posts include:</p>
<ul>
<li>1974-1977: Saarländisches Staatsorchester Saarbrücken</li>
<li>1977-1985: <a href="/wiki/Badische_Staatskapelle" title="Badische Staatskapelle">Badische Staatskapelle</a></li>
<li>1992-1995: <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Chamber_Orchestra" title="Los Angeles Chamber Orchestra">Los Angeles Chamber Orchestra</a></li>
<li>1993-1996: Niedersaechsisches Staatsorchester und Staatsoper (Hannover)</li>
<li>2001-2010: <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Symphony_Orchestra" title="Charlotte Symphony Orchestra">Charlotte Symphony Orchestra</a></li>
<li>2006-2011: Bayerisches Staatstheater <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrnberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Nürnberg">Nuremberg</a></li>
</ul>
<p>His work in contemporary music has included conducting the premiere of <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Rihm" title="Wolfgang Rihm">Wolfgang Rihm</a>'s opera <i>Oedipus</i> in October 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Perick became Music Director of the <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Symphony_Orchestra" title="Charlotte Symphony Orchestra">Charlotte Symphony Orchestra</a> in 2001. He stepped down from this post in 2010<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> but has continued his association with the orchestra as conductor laureate in the 2010-2011 season.<sup id="cite_ref-CharlotteObserver_2010-04-16_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CharlotteObserver_2010-04-16-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 2006 to 2011, Perick was Music Director of the Bayerisches Staatstheater <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrnberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Nürnberg">Nuremberg</a>, including the post of principal conductor of the Nürnberg Philharmonic. In addition to his conducting posts, since 1999, he has been a professor of conducting at the Staatliche Hochschule für Musik in Hamburg. In July 2015, the <a href="/wiki/Beethoven_Orchester_Bonn" title="Beethoven Orchester Bonn">Beethoven Orchester Bonn</a> announced the appointment of Perick as its interim <i>Generalmusikdirektor</i> for the 2016-2017 season.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Perick's recordings include an 1988 recording of <a href="/wiki/Franz_Schmidt" title="Franz Schmidt">Franz Schmidt</a>'s rarely heard opera <i><a href="/wiki/Notre_Dame_(opera)" title="Notre Dame (opera)">Notre Dame</a></i>.</p>
<p>Perick and his wife Ulrike, an actress, make their home in Germany.</p>
