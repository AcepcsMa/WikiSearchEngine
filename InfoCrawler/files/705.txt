				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Chaya Arbel</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <span lang="he" dir="rtl" xml:lang="he">חיה ארבל</span>‎‎&#160;; 1921 – 2007) was an Israeli <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">composer</a>. She is one of Israel's best known female classical composers and the recipient of the ACUM Prize.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chaya_Arbel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arbel studied piano and composition in Germany, until she immigrated to the <a href="/wiki/British_mandate_of_Palestine" class="mw-redirect" title="British mandate of Palestine">British mandate of Palestine</a> (1936), where she worked in agriculture at <a href="/wiki/Kibbutz" title="Kibbutz">kibbutz</a> <a href="/wiki/HaMa%27apil" title="HaMa'apil">HaMa'apil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jwa_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jwa-1">[1]</a></sup> Only 25 years later, Arbel could resume her composition studies, with professor <a href="/wiki/Leon_Schidlowsky" title="Leon Schidlowsky">Leon Schidlowsky</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Arbel remained a <a href="/wiki/Kibbutz" title="Kibbutz">kibbutz</a> member for the rest of life. She won the Israeli ACUM Prize for <i>Dialogue for Clarinet and Orchestra</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-jwa_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jwa-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
