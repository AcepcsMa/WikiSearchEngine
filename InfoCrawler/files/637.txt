<p><b>Bundesautobahn&#160;6</b> (translates from <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> as <i>Federal Motorway&#160;6</i>, short form <b>Autobahn&#160;6</b>, abbreviated as <b>BAB&#160;6</b> or <b>A&#160;6</b>), also known as <b>Via Carolina</b> (between <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrnberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Nürnberg">Nürnberg</a> and the Czech border continuing to <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>) is a 477&#160;km (296.4&#160;mi) long German <a href="/wiki/Autobahn" title="Autobahn">autobahn</a>. It starts at the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> border near <a href="/wiki/Saarbr%C3%BCcken" title="Saarbrücken">Saarbrücken</a> in the west and end at the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech</a> border near <a href="/wiki/Waidhaus" title="Waidhaus">Waidhaus</a> in the east.</p>
<p>The first plans for the A&#160;6 were laid out in 1935, construction on several parts began in 1938. In 1940, construction near <a href="/wiki/Mannheim" title="Mannheim">Mannheim</a> was stopped when the bridge across the <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a> collapsed, killing many workers. A new bridge, the <a href="/wiki/Theodor_Heuss_Bridge_(Frankenthal)" title="Theodor Heuss Bridge (Frankenthal)">Theodor Heuss Bridge (Frankenthal)</a>, was opened in 1953. Other parts of the A&#160;6 were completed in 1941. A part near <a href="/wiki/Kaiserslautern" title="Kaiserslautern">Kaiserslautern</a> was used as an airstrip by the <a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Luftwaffe</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. After the war, it was taken over by US forces and became the <a href="/wiki/Ramstein_Air_Base" title="Ramstein Air Base">Ramstein Air Base</a>, while the A&#160;6 was re-built south of the air base.</p>

<p>In the 1960s, construction was continued. One new section cut through the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a>, requiring a major redesign of the race track which resulted in the construction of the <i>Motodrom</i> stadium.</p>
<p>Much like its southern counterpart, the <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_8" title="Bundesautobahn 8">A&#160;8</a>, the A&#160;6 is relatively old and has received little upgrading, making it difficult for it to handle today's traffic. The section around <a href="/wiki/Mannheim" title="Mannheim">Mannheim</a> is currently being widened and modernised from a four-lane to a six-lane motorway requiring the construction of a new bridge over the River <a href="/wiki/Neckar" title="Neckar">Neckar</a>.</p>
<p>About the Kocher Viaduct (German: Kochertalbrücke) near Schwäbisch Hall, the Autobahn 6 crosses the Kocher valley between Heilbronn and Nuremberg. With its maximum height of 185&#160;m (607&#160;ft) above the valley bottom, it is the highest viaduct in Germany.</p>

<p>The direct motorway connection between Prague and <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> was completed when the last missing section between junction <a href="/wiki/Amberg" title="Amberg">Amberg</a>-Ost and interchange <i><a href="/wiki/Upper_Palatinate_Forest" class="mw-redirect" title="Upper Palatinate Forest">Oberpfälzer Wald</a></i> was inaugurated on 10 September 2008.</p>
