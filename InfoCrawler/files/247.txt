<div style="display: inline-block; margin-left: 4px; width: 284px; vertical-align: middle;"><b><a href="/wiki/William_Faulkner" title="William Faulkner">William Faulkner</a></b> (1897–1962) was an <a href="/wiki/American_literature" title="American literature">American writer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prize</a> laureate from <a href="/wiki/Oxford,_Mississippi" title="Oxford, Mississippi">Oxford, Mississippi</a>. Although he wrote novels, short stories, a play, poetry, essays, and screenplays, he is primarily known for his works set in the fictional <a href="/wiki/Yoknapatawpha_County" title="Yoknapatawpha County">Yoknapatawpha County</a>. Faulkner's work was published widely during the 1920s and 1930s, but he remained relatively unknown until receiving the 1949 <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Literature" title="Nobel Prize in Literature">Nobel Prize in Literature</a>. Two of his works, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Fable" title="A Fable">A Fable</a></i> (1954) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Reivers" title="The Reivers">The Reivers</a></i> (1962), won the <a href="/wiki/Pulitzer_Prize_for_Fiction" title="Pulitzer Prize for Fiction">Pulitzer Prize for Fiction</a>. Today, Faulkner is one of the most celebrated writers in <a href="/wiki/American_literature" title="American literature">American literature</a>.<small style="display: block; margin-top: 0.8em;">Photograph: <a href="/wiki/Carl_Van_Vechten" title="Carl Van Vechten">Carl Van Vechten</a>; restoration: <a href="/wiki/User:Adam_Cuerden" title="User:Adam Cuerden">Adam Cuerden</a></small>
