<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:black;"><span class="mw-headline" id="The_History_Portal"><big>The History Portal</big></span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #C27E3A; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #EEDCC8;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<p><br /></p>
<div style="float:right;margin-left:1em">
<center><a href="/wiki/This_is_the_Place_Monument" title="This is the Place Monument">This is the Place Monument</a></center>
<p><a href="/wiki/This_is_the_Place_Monument" title="This is the Place Monument"><img alt="This is the Place Monument" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/This_Is_The_Place_Heritage_Park.jpg/200px-This_Is_The_Place_Heritage_Park.jpg" width="200" height="294" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/This_Is_The_Place_Heritage_Park.jpg/300px-This_Is_The_Place_Heritage_Park.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/This_Is_The_Place_Heritage_Park.jpg/400px-This_Is_The_Place_Heritage_Park.jpg 2x" data-file-width="680" data-file-height="1000" /></a><br /></p>
</div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/History" title="History">History</a></b> is the discovery, collection, organization, analysis, and presentation of information about past events. History can also mean a continuous, typically chronological record of important or public events or of a particular trend or institution. Scholars who write about history are called <a href="/wiki/Historian" title="Historian">historians</a>. It is a field of <a href="/wiki/Knowledge" title="Knowledge">knowledge</a> which uses a <a href="/wiki/Narrative" title="Narrative">narrative</a> to examine and analyse the sequence of events, and it sometimes attempts to objectively investigate the patterns of cause and effect that determine events. Historians debate the nature of history and its usefulness. This includes discussing the study of the discipline as an end in itself and as a way of providing "perspective" on the problems of the present. The stories common to a particular <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a> but not supported by external sources (such as the legends surrounding <a href="/wiki/King_Arthur" title="King Arthur">King Arthur</a>) are usually classified as <a href="/wiki/Cultural_heritage" title="Cultural heritage">cultural heritage</a> rather than as the "disinterested investigation" needed by the discipline of history. Events of the past prior to written record are considered <a href="/wiki/Prehistory" title="Prehistory">prehistory</a>.</p>
<p>Amongst scholars, fifth century BC <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Greek</a> historian <a href="/wiki/Herodotus" title="Herodotus">Herodotus</a> is considered to be the "father of history"; the methods of Herodotus along with his contemporary <a href="/wiki/Thucydides" title="Thucydides">Thucydides</a> form the foundations for the modern study of history. Their influence (along with other historical traditions in other parts of their world) has spawned many different interpretations of the nature of history which has developed over the centuries and are continuing to change. The modern study of history has many different fields, including those that focus on certain regions and those that focus on certain topical or thematic elements of historical investigation. Often, history is taught as part of primary and secondary education, and the academic study of history is a major discipline in university studies.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:black;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_article">Selected article</span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #C27E3A; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #EEDCC8;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:left;margin-right:1em"><a href="/wiki/File:Travis_letter_page_1_adj.jpg" class="image" title="The first page of William Barret Travis's letter, To the People of Texas &amp; All Americans in the World"><img alt="The first page of William Barret Travis's letter, To the People of Texas &amp; All Americans in the World" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Travis_letter_page_1_adj.jpg/150px-Travis_letter_page_1_adj.jpg" width="150" height="244" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Travis_letter_page_1_adj.jpg/225px-Travis_letter_page_1_adj.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Travis_letter_page_1_adj.jpg/300px-Travis_letter_page_1_adj.jpg 2x" data-file-width="432" data-file-height="704" /></a></div>
<b><a href="/wiki/To_the_People_of_Texas_%26_All_Americans_in_the_World" title="To the People of Texas &amp; All Americans in the World">To the People of Texas &amp; All Americans in the World</a></b> is an <a href="/wiki/Open_letter" title="Open letter">open letter</a> written on February 24, 1836, by <a href="/wiki/William_B._Travis" title="William B. Travis">William B. Travis</a>, commander of the <a href="/wiki/Texian" class="mw-redirect" title="Texian">Texian</a> forces at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Alamo" title="Battle of the Alamo">Battle of the Alamo</a>, to settlers in <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Texas" title="Mexican Texas">Mexican Texas</a>. The letter is renowned as a "declaration of defiance" and a "masterpiece of American patriotism", and forms part of the history education of Texas schoolchildren.
<p>On February 23, the <a href="/wiki/Alamo_Mission" class="mw-redirect" title="Alamo Mission">Alamo Mission</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="San Antonio, Texas">San Antonio, Texas</a> had been besieged by Mexican forces led by General <a href="/wiki/Antonio_L%C3%B3pez_de_Santa_Anna" title="Antonio López de Santa Anna">Antonio López de Santa Anna</a>. Fearing that his small group of men could not withstand an assault, Travis wrote this letter seeking reinforcements and supplies from supporters. The letter closes with Travis's vow of "Victory or Death!", an emotion which has been both praised and derided by historians.</p>
<p>The letter was initially entrusted to courier Albert Martin, who carried it to the town of <a href="/wiki/Gonzales,_Texas" title="Gonzales, Texas">Gonzales</a> some seventy miles away. Martin added several postscripts to encourage men to reinforce the Alamo, and then handed the letter to Launcelot Smithers. Smithers added his own postscript and delivered the letter to its intended destination, <a href="/wiki/San_Felipe,_Texas" title="San Felipe, Texas">San Felipe de Austin</a>. Local publishers printed over 700&#160;copies of the letter. It also appeared in the two main Texas newspapers and was eventually printed throughout the United States and Europe. Partially in response to the letter, men from throughout Texas and the United States began to gather in Gonzales. Between 32 and 90 of them reached the Alamo before it fell; the remainder formed the nucleus of the army which eventually defeated Santa Anna at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_San_Jacinto" title="Battle of San Jacinto">Battle of San Jacinto</a>.</p>

<h2 style="font-family:sans-serif; font-size:100%; font-weight:bold; border: none; margin: 0; padding:0; padding-bottom:.1em; color:black;"><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_biography">Selected biography</span></h2>
</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #C27E3A; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #EEDCC8;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:left;margin-right:1em"><a href="/wiki/File:Pericles_Pio-Clementino_Inv269.jpg" class="image" title="Bust of Pericles bearing the inscription “Pericles, son of Xanthippus, Athenian”."><img alt="Bust of Pericles bearing the inscription “Pericles, son of Xanthippus, Athenian”." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Pericles_Pio-Clementino_Inv269.jpg/150px-Pericles_Pio-Clementino_Inv269.jpg" width="150" height="301" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Pericles_Pio-Clementino_Inv269.jpg/225px-Pericles_Pio-Clementino_Inv269.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Pericles_Pio-Clementino_Inv269.jpg/300px-Pericles_Pio-Clementino_Inv269.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1275" data-file-height="2560" /></a></div>
<b><a href="/wiki/Pericles" title="Pericles">Pericles</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Περικλῆς</span>, <i>Periklēs</i>, "surrounded by glory"; c. 495 – 429 BC) was a prominent and influential <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/statesman" class="extiw" title="wikt:statesman">statesman</a>, orator, and general of <a href="/wiki/History_of_Athens" title="History of Athens">Athens</a> during the city's <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Pericles" class="mw-redirect" title="Age of Pericles">Golden Age</a>—specifically, the time between the <a href="/wiki/Greco-Persian_Wars" title="Greco-Persian Wars">Persian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peloponnesian_War" title="Peloponnesian War">Peloponnesian</a> wars. He was descended, through his mother, from the powerful and historically influential <a href="/wiki/Alcmaeonidae" title="Alcmaeonidae">Alcmaeonid</a> family.
<p>Pericles had such a profound influence on Athenian society that <a href="/wiki/Thucydides" title="Thucydides">Thucydides</a>, his contemporary historian, acclaimed him as "the first citizen of Athens". Pericles turned the <a href="/wiki/Delian_League" title="Delian League">Delian League</a> into an Athenian empire and led his countrymen during the first two years of the Peloponnesian War. The period during which he led Athens, roughly from 461 to 429&#160;BC, is sometimes known as the "<a href="/wiki/Age_of_Pericles" class="mw-redirect" title="Age of Pericles">Age of Pericles</a>", though the period thus denoted can include times as early as the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Wars" class="mw-redirect" title="Persian Wars">Persian Wars</a>, or as late as the next century.</p>
<p>Pericles promoted the arts and literature; this was a chief reason Athens holds the reputation of being the educational and cultural centre of the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">ancient Greek</a> world. He started an ambitious project that generated most of the surviving structures on the <a href="/wiki/Acropolis_of_Athens" title="Acropolis of Athens">Acropolis</a> (including the <a href="/wiki/Parthenon" title="Parthenon">Parthenon</a>). This project beautified the city, exhibited its glory, and gave work to the people. Furthermore, Pericles fostered <a href="/wiki/Athenian_democracy" title="Athenian democracy">Athenian democracy</a> to such an extent that critics call him a <a href="/wiki/Populism" title="Populism">populist</a>.</p>

</div>
<div style="border: 1px solid #C27E3A; border-top-width: 1px; vertical-align: top;background: #EEDCC8;opacity: 1; color: black; text-align: left; margin: 0 0 10px; padding: 1em;padding-top: .3em;">
<div style="float:right; margin-left:0.5em;">
<p><a href="/wiki/File:Beak_Doctor.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Beak Doctor.jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Beak_Doctor.jpg/56px-Beak_Doctor.jpg" width="56" height="100" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Beak_Doctor.jpg/84px-Beak_Doctor.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Beak_Doctor.jpg/112px-Beak_Doctor.jpg 2x" data-file-width="361" data-file-height="642" /></a></p>
</div>
<ul>
<li>... that <b><a href="/wiki/Giovanni_de_Ventura" title="Giovanni de Ventura">Giovanni de Ventura</a></b>, a <b><a href="/wiki/Plague_doctor" title="Plague doctor">plague doctor</a></b> who may have worn a <b><a href="/wiki/Beak_doctor_costume" class="mw-redirect" title="Beak doctor costume">beak doctor costume</a></b> <i>(pictured)</i>, was restricted by <b><a href="/wiki/Plague_doctor_contract" title="Plague doctor contract">a covenant</a></b> to treat only infectious patients? In the nose of the mask, there were types of plants that were used to filter the sickness from the wearer.</li>
<li>... that in some <b><a href="/wiki/Archaic_Greek_alphabets" title="Archaic Greek alphabets">archaic Greek alphabets</a></b>, an Ε could look like a Β, a Β like a C, a Γ like an Ι, an Ι like a Σ, or a Σ like an Μ?</li>
<li>... that the <a href="/wiki/State_Administration_of_Cultural_Heritage" title="State Administration of Cultural Heritage">Chinese government</a> has published a <b><a href="/wiki/List_of_Chinese_cultural_relics_forbidden_to_be_exhibited_abroad" title="List of Chinese cultural relics forbidden to be exhibited abroad">list of sixty-four important cultural relics</a></b> that are forbidden to be exhibited outside of China?</li>
<li>... that the 1886 novel <i><b><a href="/wiki/Albertine_(1886_novel)" class="mw-redirect" title="Albertine (1886 novel)">Albertine</a></b></i> expedited the abolition of public prostitution in Norway?</li>

<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Coronation_of_Napoleon_I" title="Coronation of Napoleon I">Coronation of Napoleon I</a></b> as <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_the_French" title="Emperor of the French">Emperor of the French</a> took place on December 2, 1804. After a <a href="/wiki/French_constitutional_referendum,_1804" title="French constitutional referendum, 1804">referendum</a> showed overwhelming French support for the action, Napoleon designed an elaborate coronation ceremony, France's first since before the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>, with a combination of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> pageantry and the purported memory of <a href="/wiki/Charlemagne" title="Charlemagne">Charlemagne</a>. During the ceremony, as <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_VII" title="Pope Pius VII">Pope Pius VII</a> readied the crown for Napoleon, the future emperor turned around, took it from the pope, and placed it on his own head, an unprecedented action of <a href="/wiki/Separation_of_church_and_state" title="Separation of church and state">state over church</a>.</p>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/October_28" title="October 28">October 28</a></b>: <a href="/wiki/Calendar_of_saints" title="Calendar of saints">Feast day</a> of <b><a href="/wiki/Jude_the_Apostle" title="Jude the Apostle">Jude the Apostle</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Western_Christianity" title="Western Christianity">Western Christianity</a>)</p>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/312" title="312">312</a> – <a href="/wiki/Civil_wars_of_the_Tetrarchy" title="Civil wars of the Tetrarchy">Civil wars of the Tetrarchy</a>: <a href="/wiki/Constantine_the_Great" title="Constantine the Great">Constantine the Great</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/Maxentius" title="Maxentius">Maxentius</a> at the <b><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Milvian_Bridge" title="Battle of the Milvian Bridge">Battle of the Milvian Bridge</a></b> in Rome.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1453" title="1453">1453</a> – <b><a href="/wiki/Ladislaus_the_Posthumous" title="Ladislaus the Posthumous">Ladislaus the Posthumous</a></b> was crowned <a href="/wiki/List_of_Bohemian_monarchs" title="List of Bohemian monarchs">King of Bohemia</a>, although <a href="/wiki/George_of_Pod%C4%9Bbrady" title="George of Poděbrady">George of Poděbrady</a> remained in control of the government.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1919" title="1919">1919</a> – The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">U.S. Congress</a> passed the <b><a href="/wiki/Volstead_Act" title="Volstead Act">Volstead Act</a></b> over President <a href="/wiki/Woodrow_Wilson" title="Woodrow Wilson">Woodrow Wilson</a>'s veto, reinforcing <a href="/wiki/Prohibition_in_the_United_States" title="Prohibition in the United States">Prohibition in the United States</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1925" title="1925">1925</a> – The <b><a href="/wiki/Tutankhamun%27s_mask" title="Tutankhamun's mask">funerary mask of Tutankhamun</a></b> <i>(pictured)</i>, possibly made for <a href="/wiki/Neferneferuaten" title="Neferneferuaten">Queen Nefertiti</a>, was uncovered for the first time in 3,250 years.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2013" title="2013">2013</a> – The first terrorist attack in Beijing's recent history took place when three members of the <a href="/wiki/Turkistan_Islamic_Party" title="Turkistan Islamic Party">Turkistan Islamic Party</a> <b><a href="/wiki/2013_Tiananmen_Square_attack" title="2013 Tiananmen Square attack">drove a vehicle into a crowd</a></b>.</li>
